| 10 | **Øre** | Scandinavian currency unit. Minimal, sharp, design-forward. |

### 🏆 MY PICK: **FORGE**

**Tagline:** *Built, not born.*

**Why:** It's active. It implies effort, craft, intentionality. It distances us from the "lumberjack/Viking" cliché while keeping masculine energy. It's one syllable, easy to say, easy to logo, impossible to confuse. "Forge Beard Oil" just *sounds* right. It positions grooming as a *practice*, not vanity.

### Brand Voice & Tone Guide

**Voice:** Confident, direct, slightly dry humor. Think: your friend who knows his shit but doesn't lecture you.

**Tone principles:**
- **Never preachy.** We don't tell men they NEED to groom. We speak to men who already do.
- **Never bro-y.** No "CRUSH YOUR BEARD GAME BRO." We're past that.
- **Slightly irreverent.** We can joke. We're not a medical brand.
- **Knowledgeable without being nerdy.** We explain ingredients when it matters, skip the chemistry lecture.
- **Short sentences.** Punchy copy. Respect their time.

**Voice examples:**
- ✅ "Your beard deserves better than drugstore wax. So do you."
- ✅ "Three drops. Work it in. Walk out."
- ✅ "We put jojoba in everything because jojoba works. Simple."
- ❌ "Unleash your inner warrior with our premium beard elixir!"
- ❌ "Hey king 👑 ready to level up your grooming game?"

### Visual Identity Direction

**Color Palette:**
- Primary: **Charcoal (#2D2D2D)** — not black, warmer
- Secondary: **Burnt Copper (#B87333)** — metallic warmth, the forge
- Accent: **Bone White (#F5F0EB)** — clean, warm off-white
- Tertiary: **Slate (#6B7B8D)** — for secondary text

**Typography:**
- Headlines: **Grotesque sans-serif** (think: Druk, Neue Haas, or similar) — bold, industrial, tight tracking
- Body: **Clean sans-serif** (Inter, Söhne) — readable, modern
- NO serif fonts. NO script fonts. NO "vintage" typography.

**Photography Style:**
- Natural light, slightly desaturated
- Close-up texture shots (beard hair, oil drops, wood grain)
- Real men, not models. Age lines are fine. Grey hairs are fine.
- Environments: workshops, kitchens, bathrooms — real spaces, not studios
- NO shirtless gym shots. NO "man staring pensively into distance."
- Think Aesop meets Carhartt, not Axe meets GQ.

**Packaging:**
- Amber glass bottles (beard oil) — classic, protects from UV
- Matte black tins (balm) — tactile, pocketable
- Kraft + black labels — minimal text, logo debossed not printed
- Feels like something you'd find in a good barbershop, not a pharmacy

## 2. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

### Top DTC Beard/Grooming Brands

| Brand | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| **Beardbrand** | Incredible content (YouTube OG), strong community, premium positioning | Prices creeping too high, brand feels "lifestyle guru" now, overwhelming SKU count |
| **Honest Amish** | OG natural/organic, Amazon dominance, trust | Zero brand personality, packaging is dated, no DTC presence worth noting |
| **Bossman Brands** | Great scents (Jelly Beard Oil is innovative), strong Amazon | Brand feels loud/aggressive, weak content strategy |
| **Cremo** | Mass-market awareness, retail distribution, affordable | Generic, no soul, you forget you bought it |
| **Scotch Porter** | Underserved market (Black men's grooming), clean ingredients, good design | Niche focus limits TAM, inconsistent content |
| **Grave Before Shave** | Cult following, bold branding, fun | Too edgy for mainstream, packaging looks like a tattoo parlor |
| **Zeus Beard** | Good product range, reasonable prices | Forgettable brand, trying to be everything |
| **Bevel** | Walker & Company pedigree, Target distribution, design-forward | Pivoted broad, lost beard focus |
| **Duke Cannon** | Hilarious brand voice, gift-market dominance | Products are mid, more marketing than substance |
| **Manscaped** | Marketing machine, massive awareness | Not really beard-focused, juvenile tone aging poorly |

### Gaps We Exploit

1. **The "Aesop of Beard Care" gap.** Nobody owns the design-forward, premium-but-not-pretentious, minimalist position. Beardbrand is closest but went too lifestyle-guru. There's a massive white space for a brand that looks like it belongs on a nice bathroom shelf next to Aesop and Byredo, but is priced at $25-35, not $60.

2. **The content format gap.** Beardbrand owns long-form YouTube. Nobody owns short-form (TikTok/Reels) in beard care with genuinely good, non-cringe content. Most beard TikTok is either "5 PRODUCTS EVERY MAN NEEDS" listicles or before/after transformations. There's room for funny, relatable, quick-hit content.

3. **The ingredient transparency gap.** Everyone says "natural" but nobody educates well. There's room for a brand that's casually transparent — not preachy-clean-beauty, just honest.

4. **The gifting gap done right.** Duke Cannon owns "funny gift for dad" but nobody owns "actually good gift for a guy who cares." Our kits should be the thing a girlfriend/wife buys because it looks good AND works.

5. **The subscription gap.** Beard oil runs out every 4-6 weeks predictably. Nobody has nailed a simple, no-BS subscription the way other DTC brands have.

## 3. CUSTOMER AVATAR

### Meet "Marcus" — The FORGE Customer

**Demographics:**
- Age: 28-36 (sweet spot within 25-40 range)
- Income: $55K-$110K
- Location: Urban/suburban — Austin, Denver, Nashville, Portland, Raleigh
- Education: College degree or skilled trade
- Relationship: In a relationship or recently settled down

**Psychographics:**
- Has a beard because he *likes* having one, not to make a statement
- Cares about quality but isn't obsessive — he'll research once, then repeat-buy
- Buys fewer, better things (BIFL mindset creeping in)
- Drinks craft beer or good bourbon, but doesn't make it his personality
- Wears Hoka/New Balance, Patagonia/Carhartt WIP — function-meets-style
- Listens to Joe Rogan occasionally but isn't a "Joe Rogan guy"
- Has a skincare routine (even if it's basic) — this isn't his first grooming product

**Online Behavior:**
- **Reddit:** r/beards, r/malegrooming, r/BuyItForLife, r/frugalmalefashion
- **YouTube:** Beardbrand (casually), tool/workshop channels, cooking channels
- **TikTok/IG:** Follows a few grooming creators, lots of food/comedy/sports content
- **Podcasts:** Business/culture pods, not "men's lifestyle" pods specifically
- Checks reviews before buying but trusts a good-looking website + a few Reddit comments more than 500 Amazon reviews

**Purchase Triggers:**
1. Current product ran out and he's annoyed by it (beard feels dry/scratchy)
2. Girlfriend/wife complains about beard scratchiness
3. Saw a TikTok/Reel that casually showed a product looking premium
4. Friend recommended it
5. Gift-giving season (he puts it on his own wishlist, or partner finds it)
6. Seasonal change — winter dryness is the #1 trigger

**What He WON'T Respond To:**
- "Be a better man" messaging
- Hyper-masculine imagery
- Influencer hard-sells
- Complicated multi-step routines
- "Clean beauty" virtue signaling

## 4. PRODUCT LINE STRATEGY

### Phase 1: Launch (Day 1) — 4 SKUs

| Product | Size | Price | Why |
|---------|------|-------|-----|
| **Beard Oil — "Daily"** | 1 oz / 30ml | $28 | The anchor product. Jojoba + argan base, light cedarwood/bergamot scent. Not overpowering. |
| **Beard Balm — "Hold"** | 2 oz / 60ml | $24 | Shea butter + beeswax. Light hold, conditions. Same scent family. |
| **The Starter Kit** | Oil + Balm + Comb | $44 (saves $15) | Gift-friendly. This is the entry point for most customers. |
| **Beard Wash — "Strip"** | 4 oz / 120ml | $18 | Gentle, won't strip oils. Tea tree + peppermint. 2-3x/week use. |

**Single scent at launch.** This is intentional. One signature scent = brand recognition. No choice paralysis. We become "that brand that smells like ___."

**The comb:** Include a branded sandalwood comb in the kit. Costs us ~$2, perceived value is $15. It's the most Instagrammable item and becomes a daily brand touchpoint.

### Phase 2: Months 3-6 — Add 3-4 SKUs

| Product | Price | Rationale |
|---------|-------|-----------|
| **Second scent: "Unscented"** | Same | For sensitive skin / guys who wear cologne. High demand on Reddit. |
| **Beard Butter** | $26 | Between oil and balm. Growing category. Differentiator from competitors stuck in oil/balm binary. |
| **The Full Kit** | $68 | Oil + Balm + Butter + Wash + Comb. The "I want everything" option. |
| **Subscription option** | 15% off | Launch subscribe-and-save. Oil + Wash are the natural recurring items. |

### Phase 3: Months 6-12 — Expansion

| Product | Rationale |
|---------|-----------|
| **Third scent (seasonal/limited)** | Creates urgency, tests new scent profiles. Drop model. |
| **Mustache Wax** | Small SKU, high margin, completes the line |
| **Pre-Shave Oil / Shave Cream** | Expand from beard-only into grooming broadly |
| **Travel Kit** | TSA-friendly sizes of core products. Mini versions. |
| **Merch drop (hat/tee)** | Only if community is strong. Don't force it. |

**Year 1 target:** 8-12 SKUs max. Stay focused. Better to have 8 products that are all excellent than 20 that are fine.

### Pricing Strategy
- Premium-accessible: 20-40% above Amazon/mass-market, 30% below luxury (Aesop/Le Labo)
- Kits are the margin play — bundle discount feels generous but margin per order is higher
- Free shipping at $40 (just above single-product price, nudges to kit)

## 5. CONTENT STRATEGY

### Platform Priority
1. **TikTok** (primary growth engine)
2. **Instagram Reels** (cross-post + carousel education)
3. **Email** (retention, revenue driver)
4. **YouTube Shorts** (cross-post TikTok, low effort)
5. **YouTube long-form** (month 3+, only if capacity exists)

### Content Pillars (5)

1. **THE ROUTINE** (40%) — Quick grooming demos, "how I use it" in real settings (bathroom, before a date, at work). NOT tutorials — just casual use.
2. **THE FACTS** (20%) — Ingredient breakdowns, myth-busting, "why your beard itches" education. Fast, punchy, opinionated.
3. **THE CULTURE** (15%) — Beard humor, relatable moments, "girlfriend vs. beard" content, seasonal beard content.
4. **THE CRAFT** (15%) — Behind-the-scenes: how we make it, packaging choices, scent development. Builds trust + brand depth.
5. **THE COMMUNITY** (10%) — UGC reposts, customer transformations, "show us your FORGE" content.

### Posting Cadence
- **TikTok:** 5x/week (daily if capacity exists)
- **IG Reels:** 4x/week (cross-post best TikTok + 1 native)
- **IG Stories:** Daily (casual, behind-scenes)
- **IG Carousels:** 2x/week (educational, save-worthy)
- **Email:** 2x/week (1 content/value, 1 promotional)

### 25 Specific Post Ideas

**THE ROUTINE:**
1. "3 drops. 30 seconds. That's it." — Real-time beard oil application, no talking, just ASMR-style with text overlay
2. "What's in my Dopp kit" — Unzip, show FORGE products + 2-3 other items, keep it quick
3. "Morning routine but make it honest" — Messy bathroom, coffee, 30-second beard routine
4. "The difference one product makes" — Split screen: beard before oil vs. after oil, same day
5. "She said my beard was scratchy so I fixed it" — Apply balm, cut to her touching his face, approving

**THE FACTS:**
6. "Stop washing your beard every day. Here's why." — 30-sec explainer with simple graphics
7. "Beard oil is just two ingredients. Here's what matters." — Carrier oil + essential oil breakdown
8. "The ingredient I put in everything" — Close-up of jojoba, why it mimics natural sebum
9. "Your beard isn't patchy. It's just new." — Growth timeline expectations, empathetic tone
10. "Beard dandruff isn't dandruff" — It's beardruff (dry skin), here's the fix

**THE CULTURE:**
11. "POV: You grew a beard and now every uncle has an opinion" — Trending sound + text overlay
12. "Things girlfriends say about beards" tier list — Film with actual girlfriend/partner
13. "Winter beard vs. summer beard" — Side by side seasonal comparison
14. "The beard nod" — When two bearded guys acknowledge each other. Universal experience.
15. "Stages of growing a beard" — The itch phase, the awkward phase, the glory phase

**THE CRAFT:**
16. "Why we chose amber glass" — Quick 15-sec explanation: UV protection, feels premium, recyclable
17. "Making our signature scent" — POV mixing cedarwood + bergamot, close-up pours
18. "I tested 47 formulas before this one" — Show the rejected prototypes, land on the final
19. "What $28 beard oil actually costs to make" — Transparent cost breakdown (builds trust)
20. "Every detail matters" — Slow-mo of debossed label, bottle weight, dropper action

**THE COMMUNITY:**
21. "We asked 100 guys their #1 beard tip" — Stitch responses, end with ours
22. "Reacting to your beard routines" — Duet/stitch UGC, genuine reactions
23. "First impressions of FORGE" — Unboxing reactions from real customers
24. "Your beard glow-ups are insane" — Compilation of customer before/afters
25. "The best beard at [local event]" — Street-style interviews at a market/brewery/event

### Content Production Notes
- Film in batches: 1 filming day = 2 weeks of content
- Founder face on camera is the #1 growth lever on TikTok. A face builds trust.
- First 1 second = hook. Text overlay or surprising visual. No "Hey guys welcome to..."
- Keep 15-30 seconds. Only go to 60 if the content truly demands it.
- Use trending sounds but DON'T chase trends that don't fit the brand

## 6. INFLUENCER STRATEGY

### Creator Tiers & Targets

**Tier 1: Micro-influencers (5K-50K followers) — Primary focus**
- **Type:** Guys who happen to have beards + a following. NOT "beard influencers."
- **Categories:** Fitness, cooking, woodworking, coffee, menswear, dad content, outdoor
- **Why:** Their audience trusts them. A beard product recommendation from a woodworking creator feels organic. From a "beard page" it feels like an ad.
- **Volume:** 30-50 creators in first 3 months
- **Deal:** Product gifting only. Send full kit + handwritten note. No contract. No expectation. 70% will post organically if the product + packaging is good.

**Tier 2: Mid-tier (50K-250K) — Selective**
- **Type:** Grooming/lifestyle creators who review products
- **Volume:** 5-10 creators in first 3 months
- **Deal:** Gifting + $200-500 per post/Reel. Affiliate code with 15% commission.

**Tier 3: Barbers & Barbershops — Secret weapon**
- **Type:** Barbers with TikTok/IG presence who film cuts
- **Why:** Ultimate credibility. "My barber uses this" > any influencer recommendation
- **Volume:** 10-20 barbershops
- **Deal:** Free product for shop use + retail partnership (they sell in shop at margin)

### What NOT to Do
- Don't work with anyone who has "🔗 DM for collabs" in their bio as their primary identity
- Don't require approval of content — it kills authenticity
- Don't use influencer platforms/agencies yet — manual outreach converts 3x better at this stage
- Don't pay anyone over $1,000 until you have revenue data showing influencer-driven sales

## 7. LAUNCH CAMPAIGN PLAN

### Pre-Launch: Weeks -8 to -1 (Build the List)

**Goal:** 2,000-5,000 email subscribers before launch day.

**Landing Page (Week -8):**
- Simple page: Logo, tagline, one product shot, email capture
- Headline: "FORGE is coming. Get first access."
- Offer: "First 500 subscribers get 20% off launch day + free shipping"
- Build on Shopify with a pre-launch theme or use Carrd → Klaviyo

**Referral Mechanic (Week -7):**
- Use SparkLoop or Viral Loops
- "Refer 3 friends → get a free comb with your order"
- "Refer 10 friends → get The Starter Kit free on launch day"
- This turns 1,000 signups into 3,000+

**Content Engine Starts (Week -6):**
- Start posting TikTok/Reels with founder's face — NO selling yet
- Content: "I'm building a beard care brand from scratch" journey content
- Show formulation, packaging decisions, scent testing
- Build-in-public creates emotional investment before product exists

**Influencer Seeding (Week -4):**
- Ship Tier 1 gifting packages to 30 creators
- Include a card: "We launch [date]. If you want to share, use #ForgedNotBorn"
- Time it so they receive products 2-3 weeks before launch (gives them time to try it)

**Email Sequence Pre-Launch (Week -2 to launch):**
- Email 1 (Week -2): "We launch in 14 days. Here's what we've been building." (Story-driven)
- Email 2 (Week -1): "The Starter Kit. Here's what's in it." (Product reveal)
- Email 3 (Day -2): "48 hours. Your discount code is inside." (Urgency)
- Email 4 (Day -1): "Tomorrow. Set your alarm." (Hype)

### Launch Week: Days 1-7

**Day 1 — Launch Day:**
- 6 AM: Email blast to full list — "We're live. Use code FORGED20 for 20% off."
- 8 AM: Founder TikTok/Reel — "We just launched. Link in bio."
- All day: IG Stories showing orders coming in (social proof)
- Respond to every single comment, DM, and email personally

**Day 2-3:**
- Send "thank you" to everyone who ordered Day 1 (personal video from founder if under 200 orders)
- Post UGC of first unboxings as they arrive
- Run "Launch Special: Starter Kit" as a pinned TikTok/Reel

**Day 4-5:**
- Publish the "Why I Started FORGE" long-form story (IG carousel + email)
- Influencer posts start dropping (coordinate timing)
- Introduce the subscription option softly in email

**Day 6-7:**
- "Last 48 hours for launch pricing" urgency push
- Founder goes live on TikTok/IG — Q&A, applies products live, takes questions
- Close out launch week with a "Thank You" email sharing stats (orders, states shipped to, etc.)

### First 30 Days: Post-Launch

**Week 2:**
- Follow up with all customers: "How's the oil? Reply to this email." (Collect reviews)
- Start running Meta ads at $20-50/day — retarget website visitors + lookalike from email list
- Begin A/B testing ad creatives (founder talking-head vs. product close-up vs. UGC)

**Week 3:**
- Launch on Amazon (basic listing, use Shopify reviews as social proof)
- Pitch 2-3 local barbershops for wholesale
- Content focus: customer reactions, reviews, "day in the life with FORGE"

**Week 4:**
- Analyze: What sold most? Kit vs. individual? Subscription uptake?
- Double down on top-performing content formats
- Begin planning Phase 2 products based on customer feedback
- Set up post-purchase email flow (Day 3: How to use, Day 14: Reorder reminder, Day 30: Subscribe & save pitch)

## SUMMARY: THE FORGE PLAYBOOK

1. **Brand:** FORGE — "Built, not born." Charcoal + copper. Industrial-minimal. Confident voice, no bro energy.
2. **Gap:** Nobody owns the Aesop-meets-Carhartt position in beard care. We do.
3. **Customer:** 28-36, has a beard he maintains, buys quality, finds us on TikTok or Reddit.
4. **Products:** Launch with 4 SKUs (oil, balm, wash, kit). One signature scent. Expand slowly.
5. **Content:** TikTok-first, founder-faced, 5x/week. Routine, facts, culture, craft, community.
6. **Influencers:** Micro-creators who happen to have beards > beard influencers. Gift 30, pay 5-10.
7. **Launch:** Build a 3K+ email list over 8 weeks, launch with 20% off, hit $15K+ in 30 days.

**The north star:** Every decision should pass the "shelf test." Would this product, this post, this email look out of place next to Aesop, Byredo, or Patagonia? If yes, rethink it. We're building a brand men are *proud* to have on their counter, not one they hide under the sink.
