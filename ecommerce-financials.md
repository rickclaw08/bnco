## 2. Unit Economics Model

### The Formula
```
Gross Profit per Unit = Selling Price - COGS - Shipping - Transaction Fees
Net Profit per Unit   = Gross Profit - (Marketing Cost per Acquisition)
```

### Physical Product Example (AOV = $45)
| Line Item | Amount | % of Price |
|---|---|---|
| Selling Price | $45.00 | 100% |
| COGS (product + packaging) | -$13.50 | 30% |
| Shipping (avg) | -$5.50 | 12% |
| Payment processing (2.9% + $0.30) | -$1.61 | 3.6% |
| Platform transaction fee (~0.5%) | -$0.23 | 0.5% |
| **Gross Margin** | **$24.16** | **53.7%** |
| Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) | -$12.00 | 26.7% |
| **Net Margin per Order** | **$12.16** | **27%** |

### Digital Product Example (AOV = $29)
| Line Item | Amount | % of Price |
|---|---|---|
| Selling Price | $29.00 | 100% |
| COGS (hosting/delivery) | -$0.50 | 1.7% |
| Payment processing | -$1.14 | 3.9% |
| **Gross Margin** | **$27.36** | **94.3%** |
| CAC | -$8.00 | 27.6% |
| **Net Margin per Order** | **$19.36** | **66.8%** |

### Minimum Viable Margins
- **Physical products:** Target ≥50% gross margin, ≥20% net margin
- **Digital products:** Target ≥85% gross margin, ≥50% net margin
- **Below 40% gross on physical = danger zone** (no room for marketing or mistakes)

## 3. Break-Even Analysis

### Fixed Monthly Costs (Months 1-6 Avg)
| Expense | Monthly |
|---|---|
| Platform + tools | $65 |
| Marketing spend | $500-1,500 |
| Miscellaneous (returns, software) | $100 |
| **Total Fixed** | **$665-1,665** |

### Break-Even Calculation
```
Break-Even Units = Fixed Costs / Net Profit per Unit
```

| Scenario | Fixed Costs | Net/Unit | Break-Even Orders/Mo | Revenue Needed |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Physical, lean marketing** | $665 | $12.16 | **55 orders** | $2,475/mo |
| **Physical, growth marketing** | $1,665 | $12.16 | **137 orders** | $6,165/mo |
| **Digital, lean marketing** | $665 | $19.36 | **35 orders** | $1,015/mo |
| **Digital, growth marketing** | $1,665 | $19.36 | **86 orders** | $2,494/mo |

### Reality Check
- 55 physical orders/mo = ~2/day → **very achievable by month 3-4**
- 35 digital orders/mo = ~1/day → **achievable by month 2-3**
- Key lever: **reduce CAC over time** via organic traffic, email, and repeat customers

## 4. Pricing Strategy Framework

### Three Approaches

| Strategy | How It Works | Best For | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cost-Plus** | COGS × markup (2-3x) | Commodities, simple products | Leaves money on table |
| **Value-Based** | Price = perceived value to customer | Unique/branded products, digital | Requires market research |
| **Competitive** | Match or undercut competitors | Crowded markets | Race to bottom |

### 🏆 Recommendation: Value-Based with Competitive Guardrails

1. **Research competitors** — find the price range for similar products
2. **Price in the top 40%** of that range (not cheapest, not most expensive)
3. **Add perceived value** — bundling, branding, experience, guarantees
4. **Test with A/B pricing** — try $29 vs $35 vs $42 in first 30 days

### Pricing Rules of Thumb
- **Never compete on price alone** as a bootstrapped store — you'll lose to scale
- **Charm pricing works:** $29 > $30, $47 > $50
- **Bundles increase AOV 20-35%** — offer a "starter kit" or "complete package"
- **Free shipping threshold** at 1.5x your AOV drives larger carts
- **Digital: price for value, not cost.** A $5 ebook feels cheap. A $29 guide feels valuable.
