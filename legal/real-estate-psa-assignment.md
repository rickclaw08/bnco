# REAL ESTATE PURCHASE AND SALE AGREEMENT

> **DRAFT - NOT YET REVIEWED BY LEGAL COUNSEL**

---

**This Real Estate Purchase and Sale Agreement ("Agreement")** is entered into as of [Date] ("Effective Date") by and between:

**Seller:** [Seller_Name], an individual residing at [Seller_Address] ("Seller")

**Buyer:** ClawOps LLC, an Ohio limited liability company, and/or its assigns, with its principal place of business in Cincinnati, Ohio ("Buyer")

---

## 1. PROPERTY

1.1 **Property Description.** Seller agrees to sell and Buyer agrees to purchase the following real property located in Hamilton County, Ohio:

**Address:** [Property_Address]

**Parcel Number:** [Parcel_Number]

**Legal Description:** [Legal_Description] (or as recorded in the Hamilton County Recorder's Office)

Together with all improvements, fixtures, appurtenances, and rights belonging thereto (collectively, the "Property").

---

## 2. PURCHASE PRICE AND PAYMENT

2.1 **Purchase Price.** The total purchase price for the Property shall be **$[Purchase_Price]** ("Purchase Price").

2.2 **Earnest Money Deposit.** Within three (3) business days of the Effective Date, Buyer shall deposit **$[Earnest_Money_Amount]** ("Earnest Money") with [Title_Company_Name] ("Escrow Agent") as earnest money. Default: $500 unless otherwise specified.

2.3 **Balance at Closing.** The balance of the Purchase Price, less the Earnest Money, shall be paid at Closing by certified funds, cashier's check, or wire transfer.

---

## 3. INSPECTION PERIOD

3.1 **Due Diligence Period.** Buyer shall have fourteen (14) calendar days from the Effective Date ("Inspection Period") to conduct inspections, investigations, and due diligence on the Property at Buyer's expense.

3.2 **Scope of Inspections.** Buyer may conduct any inspections Buyer deems necessary, including but not limited to:

- Physical inspection of the Property and all improvements
- Environmental assessments
- Title examination
- Survey
- Appraisal
- Review of zoning, building code compliance, and permits
- Review of utility connections and outstanding liens
- Lead-based paint inspection (if applicable)

3.3 **Access.** Seller shall provide Buyer and Buyer's agents, contractors, and inspectors with reasonable access to the Property during the Inspection Period for the purpose of conducting inspections. Buyer shall provide Seller with at least 24 hours' notice before any inspection.

3.4 **Right to Terminate.** If Buyer is not satisfied with the results of any inspection, for any reason or no reason, Buyer may terminate this Agreement by providing written notice to Seller on or before the expiration of the Inspection Period. Upon termination, the Earnest Money shall be promptly refunded to Buyer, and neither Party shall have further obligation under this Agreement.

3.5 **Waiver.** If Buyer does not deliver a termination notice within the Inspection Period, Buyer shall be deemed to have waived the right to terminate under this Section, and the Earnest Money shall become non-refundable except as otherwise provided in this Agreement.

---

## 4. TITLE AND CLOSING

4.1 **Marketable Title.** Seller shall convey marketable fee simple title to the Property by general warranty deed, free and clear of all liens, encumbrances, easements, and restrictions, except for:

- (a) Current real property taxes and assessments not yet due and payable
- (b) Recorded easements and restrictions of record that do not materially impair the use of the Property
- (c) Any encumbrances accepted in writing by Buyer

4.2 **Title Commitment.** Seller shall provide or cause to be provided a title commitment or preliminary title report within ten (10) days of the Effective Date. Buyer shall have until the end of the Inspection Period to object to any title defects.

4.3 **Title Defects.** If Buyer objects to any title defect, Seller shall have ten (10) days to cure the defect. If Seller is unable or unwilling to cure, Buyer may: (a) accept title as-is; (b) terminate this Agreement and receive a refund of the Earnest Money; or (c) pursue any available legal remedies.

4.4 **Closing Date.** Closing shall occur within thirty (30) calendar days of the Effective Date, or such other date as the Parties may agree in writing ("Closing Date").

4.5 **Closing Location.** Closing shall take place at the offices of [Title_Company_Name], or at such other location as the Parties may agree, or by mail-away closing.

4.6 **Closing Costs.** Unless otherwise agreed in writing:

- Seller shall pay for: deed preparation, Seller's share of prorated taxes, any existing liens or judgments against the Property, and Seller's closing/settlement fees
- Buyer shall pay for: title insurance (owner's policy), recording fees, Buyer's closing/settlement fees, and any lender-required fees
- Real property taxes shall be prorated as of the Closing Date

---

## 5. SELLER REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

Seller represents and warrants to Buyer as of the Effective Date and as of the Closing Date:

5.1 **Authority.** Seller has full legal authority and capacity to enter into this Agreement and to convey the Property.

5.2 **Ownership.** Seller is the sole owner of the Property (or has obtained all necessary consents from co-owners).

5.3 **No Undisclosed Liens.** To the best of Seller's knowledge, there are no undisclosed liens, assessments, or claims against the Property other than those identified in this Agreement or the title commitment.

5.4 **No Litigation.** There are no pending or threatened lawsuits, claims, or governmental actions affecting the Property, except as disclosed to Buyer in writing.

5.5 **Property Condition.** Seller has disclosed to Buyer all known material defects in the Property's condition, including but not limited to structural issues, water damage, mold, pest infestation, and environmental contamination.

5.6 **No Violations.** To the best of Seller's knowledge, the Property is not in violation of any applicable building codes, zoning ordinances, or governmental regulations.

5.7 **Utilities.** All utilities serving the Property are connected and functional, or Seller has disclosed any known issues.

5.8 **Survival.** Seller's representations and warranties shall survive Closing for a period of twelve (12) months.

---

## 6. PROPERTY CONDITION

6.1 **As-Is Sale.** Except for the representations and warranties set forth in Section 5, Buyer is purchasing the Property in its current "AS-IS, WHERE-IS" condition, with all faults, and without any additional representations or warranties by Seller regarding the condition of the Property.

6.2 **Risk of Loss.** Risk of loss or damage to the Property shall remain with Seller until Closing. If the Property is materially damaged or destroyed before Closing, Buyer may: (a) terminate this Agreement and receive a refund of the Earnest Money; or (b) proceed with Closing and receive an assignment of any insurance proceeds.

---

## 7. ASSIGNMENT

7.1 **Right to Assign.** Buyer may assign this Agreement, and/or Buyer's rights and obligations hereunder, to any third party without the prior consent of Seller. Seller acknowledges and agrees that this Agreement is freely assignable by Buyer.

7.2 **Notice of Assignment.** Buyer shall provide written notice to Seller of any assignment prior to Closing, including the name and contact information of the assignee.

7.3 **Buyer's Liability.** In the event of assignment, Buyer shall remain liable for performance under this Agreement unless Seller provides a written release of Buyer's obligations.

---

## 8. DEFAULT AND REMEDIES

8.1 **Buyer's Default.** If Buyer fails to close on the Property without legal excuse, Seller's sole and exclusive remedy shall be to retain the Earnest Money as liquidated damages. Seller waives all other remedies, including specific performance.

8.2 **Seller's Default.** If Seller fails to close on the Property or breaches any material representation, warranty, or obligation under this Agreement, Buyer may: (a) seek specific performance compelling Seller to close; (b) terminate this Agreement and receive a refund of the Earnest Money plus reimbursement of Buyer's documented out-of-pocket expenses (up to $2,500); or (c) pursue any other remedies available at law or in equity.

---

## 9. ADDITIONAL PROVISIONS

9.1 **Lead-Based Paint Disclosure.** If the Property was built before 1978, Seller shall provide Buyer with the required Lead-Based Paint Disclosure and the EPA pamphlet "Protect Your Family From Lead in Your Home" as required by federal law (42 U.S.C. 4852d).

9.2 **Property Disclosure.** Seller shall complete and provide to Buyer the Ohio Residential Property Disclosure Form (ORC 5302.30) within five (5) days of the Effective Date.

9.3 **Possession.** Possession of the Property shall be delivered to Buyer at Closing, free of all occupants and personal property, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

9.4 **Cooperation.** Both Parties agree to cooperate fully and execute any additional documents reasonably necessary to complete the transaction.

---

## 10. GENERAL PROVISIONS

10.1 **Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Ohio.

10.2 **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties regarding the purchase and sale of the Property and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and agreements.

10.3 **Amendments.** This Agreement may not be modified except by a written instrument signed by both Parties.

10.4 **Severability.** If any provision is found invalid, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force.

10.5 **Time is of the Essence.** Time is of the essence with respect to all dates and deadlines in this Agreement.

10.6 **Notices.** All notices shall be in writing and delivered by email (with read receipt), certified mail, or hand delivery to the addresses set forth below.

10.7 **Counterparts.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall constitute an original.

10.8 **Binding Effect.** This Agreement shall be binding upon and inure to the benefit of the Parties and their respective heirs, executors, administrators, successors, and assigns.

**This Assignment of Real Estate Purchase and Sale Agreement ("Assignment")** is entered into as of [Assignment_Date] by and between:

**Assignor:** ClawOps LLC, an Ohio limited liability company ("Assignor")

**Assignee:** [Assignee_Name], a [Assignee_Entity_Type] ("Assignee")

## 1. RECITALS

1.1 Assignor entered into a Real Estate Purchase and Sale Agreement dated [Original_PSA_Date] ("Original Agreement") with [Seller_Name] ("Seller") for the purchase of real property located at:

**Address:** [Property_Address]

**Parcel Number:** [Parcel_Number]

1.2 The Original Agreement provides that Buyer (Assignor) may assign its rights and obligations under the Original Agreement to a third party.

1.3 Assignor desires to assign all of its rights, title, and interest in the Original Agreement to Assignee, and Assignee desires to accept such assignment, upon the terms set forth herein.

## 2. ASSIGNMENT

2.1 **Assignment of Rights.** Assignor hereby assigns, transfers, and conveys to Assignee all of Assignor's rights, title, and interest in and to the Original Agreement.

2.2 **Assumption of Obligations.** Assignee hereby accepts the assignment and assumes all of Assignor's obligations under the Original Agreement, including but not limited to the obligation to close on the Property and pay the Purchase Price of **$[Purchase_Price]** as set forth in the Original Agreement.

## 3. ASSIGNMENT FEE

3.1 **Fee Amount.** In consideration for this Assignment, Assignee shall pay Assignor an assignment fee of **$[Assignment_Fee]** ("Assignment Fee").

3.2 **Payment of Assignment Fee.** The Assignment Fee shall be paid as follows:

- (a) A non-refundable deposit of **$[Assignment_Deposit]** is due upon execution of this Assignment.
- (b) The balance of the Assignment Fee shall be paid at Closing via the settlement statement/HUD-1.

3.3 **Non-Refundable.** The Assignment Fee deposit is non-refundable. If Assignee fails to close, Assignor retains the deposit.

## 4. REPRESENTATIONS

4.1 **Assignor's Representations.** Assignor represents and warrants that:

- (a) The Original Agreement is in full force and effect and has not been modified except as disclosed to Assignee;
- (b) Assignor has the right to assign the Original Agreement;
- (c) No default exists under the Original Agreement on the part of Assignor;
- (d) A true and complete copy of the Original Agreement has been provided to Assignee.

4.2 **Assignee's Representations.** Assignee represents and warrants that:

- (a) Assignee has reviewed the Original Agreement and accepts its terms;
- (b) Assignee has the financial capacity and legal authority to close on the Property;
- (c) Assignee has conducted or will conduct its own due diligence on the Property.

## 5. CLOSING

5.1 **Closing Date.** Closing shall occur on or before the Closing Date specified in the Original Agreement, or as otherwise agreed by all Parties (including Seller).

5.2 **Closing Responsibilities.** Assignee is responsible for all Buyer obligations at Closing as set forth in the Original Agreement.

5.3 **Title Company.** Closing shall be conducted by the same title company/escrow agent designated in the Original Agreement, unless all Parties agree otherwise.

## 6. GENERAL PROVISIONS

6.1 **Governing Law.** This Assignment shall be governed by the laws of the State of Ohio.

6.2 **Entire Agreement.** This Assignment, together with the Original Agreement, constitutes the entire agreement between Assignor and Assignee regarding the subject matter hereof.

6.3 **Binding Effect.** This Assignment shall be binding upon and inure to the benefit of the Parties and their respective heirs, executors, administrators, successors, and assigns.

6.4 **Counterparts.** This Assignment may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall constitute an original.

6.5 **Severability.** If any provision is found invalid, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force.
