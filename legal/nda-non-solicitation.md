# NON-DISCLOSURE AND NON-SOLICITATION AGREEMENT

> **DRAFT - NOT YET REVIEWED BY LEGAL COUNSEL**

---

**This Non-Disclosure and Non-Solicitation Agreement ("Agreement")** is entered into as of [Date] ("Effective Date") by and between:

**Company:** ClawOps LLC, an Ohio limited liability company, with its principal place of business in Cincinnati, Ohio ("Company")

**Receiving Party:** [Contractor_Name], an individual residing at [Contractor_Address] ("Receiving Party")

This Agreement is executed concurrently with and incorporated into the Independent Contractor Contract ("ICA") between the Parties of even date.

---

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, Company possesses proprietary and confidential information relating to its AI automation services, real estate wholesaling operations, and related business activities;

WHEREAS, Receiving Party will have access to certain Confidential Information in the course of performing services for Company;

WHEREAS, the Parties desire to protect such Confidential Information and establish restrictions on solicitation and competition;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual promises and covenants contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration (including the opportunity to earn commissions under the ICA), the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

---

## 1. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

1.1 **Confidential Information.** "Confidential Information" means any and all non-public information disclosed by Company to Receiving Party, whether orally, in writing, electronically, or by any other means, including but not limited to:

- (a) **AI and Technology Assets:** AI agent scripts, prompts, prompt engineering methodologies, conversational flows, voice AI configurations, chatbot architectures, and related technical documentation
- (b) **Automation Systems:** GoHighLevel (GHL) workflows, automation playbooks, integration configurations, API implementations, system architectures, and deployment procedures
- (c) **Business Intelligence:** Client lists, prospect lists, lead databases, pipeline data, customer relationship information, account histories, and contact information
- (d) **Financial Information:** Pricing strategies, pricing models, commission structures, revenue data, profit margins, cost structures, and financial projections
- (e) **Proprietary Methodologies:** Sales processes, onboarding procedures, implementation frameworks, training materials, operational playbooks, and standard operating procedures
- (f) **Real Estate Operations:** Property leads, deal flow information, buyer lists, assignment strategies, and acquisition methodologies
- (g) **Strategic Information:** Business plans, marketing strategies, product roadmaps, partnership arrangements, and expansion plans
- (h) **Any other information** that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure

1.2 **Exclusions.** Confidential Information does not include information that:

- (a) Is or becomes publicly available through no fault or action of Receiving Party;
- (b) Was already known to Receiving Party prior to disclosure, as demonstrated by written records;
- (c) Is independently developed by Receiving Party without use of or reference to Confidential Information;
- (d) Is rightfully received from a third party without restriction on disclosure;
- (e) Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order, provided that Receiving Party gives Company prompt written notice and cooperates with Company's efforts to obtain a protective order.

---

## 2. NON-DISCLOSURE OBLIGATIONS

2.1 **Duty of Confidentiality.** Receiving Party shall:

- (a) Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence;
- (b) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of Company;
- (c) Use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing services under the ICA;
- (d) Take all reasonable precautions to prevent unauthorized disclosure, including precautions at least as protective as those Receiving Party uses for its own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

2.2 **Permitted Disclosures.** Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information only to individuals who:

- (a) Have a legitimate need to know the information for purposes directly related to performing the Services;
- (b) Are bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than those set forth in this Agreement.

2.3 **Duration of Confidentiality.** The obligations of confidentiality under this Section 2 shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement and shall continue for a period of three (3) years after the date of disclosure, or, with respect to trade secrets, for as long as such information qualifies as a trade secret under applicable law.

---

## 3. RETURN AND DESTRUCTION OF MATERIALS

3.1 **Upon Termination.** Upon termination or expiration of the ICA, or upon Company's written request at any time, Receiving Party shall promptly:

- (a) Return to Company all documents, files, materials, and media (in any format) containing Confidential Information;
- (b) Permanently delete or destroy all electronic copies, notes, summaries, and derivatives of Confidential Information in Receiving Party's possession;
- (c) Certify in writing within five (5) business days that all such materials have been returned and/or destroyed.

3.2 **No Retention.** Receiving Party shall not retain any copies, summaries, excerpts, or derivatives of Confidential Information after the obligations under Section 3.1 have been fulfilled, except as required by applicable law.

---

## 4. NON-SOLICITATION

4.1 **Client Non-Solicitation.** During the term of the ICA and for a period of twelve (12) months following the termination or expiration of the ICA for any reason ("Restricted Period"), Receiving Party shall not, directly or indirectly:

- (a) Solicit, contact, or attempt to solicit any client or customer of Company for the purpose of providing services that are competitive with Company's services;
- (b) Divert or attempt to divert any business, revenue, or customer relationship from Company;
- (c) Interfere with or disrupt any existing business relationship between Company and its clients or customers.

4.2 **Contractor Non-Solicitation.** During the Restricted Period, Receiving Party shall not, directly or indirectly:

- (a) Solicit, recruit, or attempt to recruit any contractor, agent, or service provider of Company to leave Company's engagement or to work for or with Receiving Party or any third party;
- (b) Encourage or induce any contractor or agent of Company to terminate their relationship with Company.

4.3 **Scope of Non-Solicitation.** The non-solicitation restrictions in this Section 4 apply to:

- (a) All clients and customers with whom Receiving Party had contact or about whom Receiving Party received Confidential Information during the term of the ICA;
- (b) All contractors and agents engaged by Company during the twelve (12) months preceding termination.

---

## 5. NON-COMPETE

5.1 **Restricted Activities.** During the term of the ICA and for a period of six (6) months following the termination or expiration of the ICA for any reason, Receiving Party shall not, directly or indirectly, within the geographic territory of the United States:

- (a) Engage in, own, manage, operate, finance, or participate in any business that provides AI receptionist services or AI-powered automation services to service-based businesses (including but not limited to HVAC, plumbing, electrical, dental, and similar industries);
- (b) Provide consulting, sales, or business development services to any competitor that directly competes with Company's AI receptionist or automation service offerings.

5.2 **Narrow Scope.** The Parties acknowledge and agree that this non-compete provision is narrowly tailored to protect Company's legitimate business interests, specifically:

- (a) The protection of Confidential Information and trade secrets;
- (b) The preservation of client and customer relationships;
- (c) The protection of Company's investment in training, tools, and proprietary methodologies shared with Receiving Party.

5.3 **Exclusions.** This non-compete does not prohibit Receiving Party from:

- (a) Owning less than 2% of the outstanding shares of any publicly traded company;
- (b) Engaging in general sales, marketing, or business development activities unrelated to AI receptionist or AI automation services;
- (c) Engaging in real estate activities unrelated to Company's operations.

---

## 6. REMEDIES

6.1 **Injunctive Relief.** Receiving Party acknowledges and agrees that:

- (a) Any breach of this Agreement would cause irreparable harm to Company for which monetary damages would be an inadequate remedy;
- (b) In the event of any breach or threatened breach, Company shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief (including temporary restraining orders, preliminary injunctions, and permanent injunctions) without the necessity of proving actual damages or posting a bond;
- (c) Such injunctive relief shall be in addition to, and not in lieu of, any other remedies available to Company.

6.2 **Liquidated Damages.** In addition to injunctive relief, in the event of a breach of Sections 4 or 5 of this Agreement, Receiving Party shall pay Company liquidated damages in the amount of twenty-five thousand dollars ($25,000) per breach. The Parties agree that:

- (a) Actual damages for breach of the non-solicitation and non-compete provisions would be difficult or impossible to calculate with precision;
- (b) The liquidated damages amount represents a reasonable estimate of the harm Company would suffer from such a breach;
- (c) This provision is intended as a reasonable measure of damages and not as a penalty.

6.3 **Attorneys' Fees.** In any action to enforce this Agreement, the prevailing Party shall be entitled to recover its reasonable attorneys' fees, court costs, and related expenses from the non-prevailing Party.

6.4 **Tolling.** The restricted periods set forth in Sections 4 and 5 shall be tolled during any period in which Receiving Party is in violation of such provisions.

---

## 7. GENERAL PROVISIONS

7.1 **Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Ohio, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

7.2 **Jurisdiction and Venue.** The Parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in Hamilton County, Ohio, for any action arising out of this Agreement.

7.3 **Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held to be unenforceable, the court shall modify such provision to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable, and the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

7.4 **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, together with the ICA and its exhibits, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties regarding the subject matter hereof.

7.5 **Amendments.** This Agreement may not be amended except by a written instrument signed by both Parties.

7.6 **Waiver.** No waiver of any provision of this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing and signed by the waiving Party. No waiver shall constitute a continuing waiver or a waiver of any other provision.

7.7 **Survival.** The confidentiality obligations in Section 2 and the return-of-materials obligations in Section 3 shall survive the expiration of the non-solicitation and non-compete periods.

7.8 **Acknowledgment.** Receiving Party acknowledges that:

- (a) Receiving Party has read and understands this Agreement;
- (b) Receiving Party has had the opportunity to consult with legal counsel before signing;
- (c) The restrictions in this Agreement are reasonable and necessary to protect Company's legitimate business interests;
- (d) Receiving Party's agreement to these terms is a material condition of the ICA.
