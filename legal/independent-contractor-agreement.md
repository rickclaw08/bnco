# INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR CONTRACT

> **DRAFT - NOT YET REVIEWED BY LEGAL COUNSEL**

---

**This Independent Contractor Contract ("Contract")** is entered into as of [Date] ("Effective Date") by and between:

**Company:** ClawOps LLC, an Ohio limited liability company, with its principal place of business in Cincinnati, Ohio ("Company")

**Contractor:** [Contractor_Name], an individual residing at [Contractor_Address] ("Contractor")

Company and Contractor are each referred to individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

---

## 1. PURPOSE AND RELATIONSHIP

1.1 **Independent Contractor Status.** Contractor is engaged as an independent contractor and not as an employee, partner, joint venturer, or agent of the Company. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create an employer-employee relationship. Contractor shall receive an IRS Form 1099-NEC for all compensation paid under this Agreement.

1.2 **No Employment Benefits.** Contractor acknowledges and agrees that Contractor is not entitled to any employee benefits, including but not limited to health insurance, retirement plans, paid vacation, sick leave, workers' compensation, or unemployment insurance. Contractor is solely responsible for all such matters.

1.3 **Tax Obligations.** Contractor is solely responsible for all federal, state, and local taxes arising from compensation received under this Agreement, including self-employment taxes, income taxes, and any other applicable taxes. Company will not withhold any taxes from payments to Contractor.

---

## 2. SCOPE OF SERVICES

2.1 **Services.** Contractor shall provide the following services to Company ("Services"):

- Sales, lead generation, and client acquisition for ClawOps LLC products and services, including but not limited to AI receptionist solutions, revenue automation services, custom AI agent development, and Automation-as-a-Service offerings
- Lead sourcing and qualification for real estate wholesaling opportunities
- Client relationship development and pipeline management
- Such other sales and business development activities as may be mutually agreed upon in writing

2.2 **Results-Based Engagement.** This engagement is results-based. Contractor's compensation is tied entirely to performance outcomes as described in Exhibit A (Commission Schedule), attached hereto and incorporated by reference. Contractor is not compensated on an hourly or salaried basis.

2.3 **No Minimum Hours.** Company does not require Contractor to work any minimum number of hours. Contractor shall determine the method, manner, and means of performing the Services, subject to the terms of this Agreement.

---

## 3. COMPENSATION

3.1 **Commission-Only.** Contractor's sole compensation for Services rendered under this Agreement shall be commissions as set forth in Exhibit A (Commission Schedule).

3.2 **No Base Pay.** Contractor acknowledges and agrees that there is no base salary, hourly wage, draw, or guaranteed minimum compensation under this Agreement.

3.3 **Payment Terms.** All commission payments shall be made in accordance with the schedule and terms set forth in Exhibit A.

---

## 4. TERM AND TERMINATION

4.1 **Initial Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for a period of twelve (12) months ("Initial Term").

4.2 **Auto-Renewal.** Upon expiration of the Initial Term, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive twelve (12) month periods ("Renewal Terms") unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least thirty (30) days prior to the end of the then-current term.

4.3 **Termination for Convenience.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time, with or without cause, by providing the other Party with thirty (30) days' prior written notice.

4.4 **Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

- (a) Materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within ten (10) days of receiving written notice of the breach;
- (b) Engages in fraud, dishonesty, gross negligence, or willful misconduct;
- (c) Becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or has a bankruptcy petition filed against it.

4.5 **Effect of Termination.** Upon termination:

- (a) Contractor shall immediately cease performing Services and representing themselves as affiliated with the Company;
- (b) Contractor shall return all Company property, materials, and Confidential Information within five (5) business days;
- (c) Company shall pay Contractor any earned but unpaid commissions in accordance with Exhibit A, subject to the clawback provisions therein;
- (d) Sections 5, 6, 7, and 8 of this Agreement, along with the NDA and Non-Solicitation Agreement, shall survive termination.

---

## 5. TOOLS, EQUIPMENT, AND EXPENSES

5.1 **Contractor's Tools.** Contractor shall provide, at Contractor's own expense, all tools, equipment, and resources necessary to perform the Services, including but not limited to computer hardware, software, telephone, internet access, and office space.

5.2 **Company-Provided Access.** Company may, at its discretion, provide Contractor with access to certain Company systems, platforms, and tools (such as CRM software) solely for use in performing the Services. Such access does not create an employment relationship and may be revoked at any time.

5.3 **Expenses.** Unless expressly agreed in writing, Contractor is solely responsible for all expenses incurred in connection with the performance of the Services.

---

## 6. SCHEDULE AND CONTROL

6.1 **Flexible Schedule.** Contractor shall set Contractor's own schedule and hours of work. Company does not control when, where, or how Contractor performs the Services, provided that Contractor meets reasonable performance standards and deadlines.

6.2 **No Exclusivity.** Unless otherwise agreed in writing, Contractor is free to engage in other business activities, provided such activities do not violate the NDA, Non-Solicitation, or Non-Compete obligations set forth in the separate NDA and Non-Solicitation Agreement executed concurrently with this Agreement.

---

## 7. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

7.1 **Work Product.** All work product, deliverables, and materials created by Contractor in the course of performing the Services ("Work Product") shall be the sole and exclusive property of the Company.

7.2 **Assignment.** Contractor hereby irrevocably assigns to Company all right, title, and interest in and to the Work Product, including all intellectual property rights therein.

7.3 **Further Assurances.** Contractor agrees to execute any documents and take any actions reasonably requested by Company to effectuate the assignments described in this Section.

---

## 8. INDEMNIFICATION AND LIABILITY

8.1 **Contractor Indemnification.** Contractor shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Company and its members, managers, officers, employees, and agents from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or related to:

- (a) Contractor's performance of the Services;
- (b) Any breach of this Agreement by Contractor;
- (c) Contractor's failure to comply with applicable laws, including tax obligations;
- (d) Any claim that Contractor is an employee of Company.

8.2 **Limitation of Liability.** In no event shall Company's total liability to Contractor under this Agreement exceed the total commissions paid to Contractor during the twelve (12) month period immediately preceding the claim.

---

## 9. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

9.1 **Contractor Representations.** Contractor represents and warrants that:

- (a) Contractor has the legal right and authority to enter into this Agreement;
- (b) Contractor will comply with all applicable federal, state, and local laws and regulations;
- (c) Contractor is not bound by any agreement that would prevent Contractor from fulfilling Contractor's obligations under this Agreement;
- (d) All information provided by Contractor to Company is true and accurate.

---

## 10. GENERAL PROVISIONS

10.1 **Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Ohio, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

10.2 **Dispute Resolution.** Any disputes arising under or in connection with this Agreement shall first be submitted to mediation in Hamilton County, Ohio. If mediation fails, disputes shall be resolved by binding arbitration in Hamilton County, Ohio, in accordance with the rules of the American Arbitration Association.

10.3 **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, together with Exhibit A and the NDA and Non-Solicitation Agreement, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior agreements, representations, and understandings.

10.4 **Amendments.** This Agreement may not be amended or modified except by a written instrument signed by both Parties.

10.5 **Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

10.6 **Waiver.** The failure of either Party to enforce any provision of this Agreement shall not constitute a waiver of such provision or the right to enforce it at a later time.

10.7 **Notices.** All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered via email (with confirmation of receipt) or certified mail to the addresses set forth below or to such other address as either Party may designate in writing.

10.8 **Assignment.** Contractor may not assign or transfer this Agreement or any rights or obligations hereunder without the prior written consent of Company. Company may assign this Agreement freely.

10.9 **Counterparts.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument.
