# EXHIBIT A - COMMISSION SCHEDULE

> **DRAFT - NOT YET REVIEWED BY LEGAL COUNSEL**

---

**This Commission Schedule ("Exhibit A")** is attached to and incorporated into the Independent Contractor Contract ("ICA") between ClawOps LLC ("Company") and [Contractor_Name] ("Contractor"), effective as of [Date].

---

## 1. COMMISSION STRUCTURE OVERVIEW

Contractor participates in the ClawOps Dual Stream Commission Model. Contractor earns a commission of fifteen percent (15%) across two revenue streams for all business generated by Contractor. This structure is designed to reward Contractor for initial deal closure and real estate transaction sourcing.

---

## 2. STREAM 1: SETUP AND IMPLEMENTATION FEES

2.1 **Commission Rate.** Contractor shall earn a commission of fifteen percent (15%) of all setup, implementation, and one-time project fees collected by Company from clients acquired by Contractor.

2.2 **Qualifying Services.** This stream applies to all one-time fees, including but not limited to:

| Service | Typical Fee | Contractor Commission (15%) |
|---------|-------------|----------------------------|
| AI Receptionist Setup | $3,000 | $450 |
| Revenue Ops Sprint | $5,000 | $750 |
| Custom AI Agent Build | $7,500 | $1,125 |

*Note: Actual fees may vary by engagement. Commission is calculated on the actual fee collected, not the listed price.*

2.3 **Payment Trigger.** Commission becomes payable upon Company's receipt of the full setup/implementation fee from the client.

---

## 3. STREAM 2: REAL ESTATE ASSIGNMENT FEES

3.1 **Commission Rate.** Contractor shall earn a commission of fifteen percent (15%) of all real estate assignment fees collected by Company on deals sourced by Contractor.

3.2 **Qualifying Transactions.** This stream applies to:

- Wholesale real estate assignment fees
- Finder's fees on real estate transactions sourced by Contractor
- Any other real estate transaction revenue directly attributable to Contractor's sourcing efforts

3.3 **Example Calculation:**

| Assignment Fee | Contractor Commission (15%) |
|---------------|----------------------------|
| $5,000 | $750 |
| $10,000 | $1,500 |
| $15,000 | $2,250 |
| $25,000 | $3,750 |

3.4 **Payment Trigger.** Commission becomes payable upon Company's receipt of the assignment fee at closing.

---

## 4. PAYMENT TERMS

4.1 **Payment Schedule.** All commissions shall be paid NET 15, meaning within fifteen (15) calendar days after Company receives the corresponding payment from the client or closing agent.

4.2 **Payment Method.** Commissions shall be paid via direct deposit, ACH transfer, or such other method as the Parties may agree upon in writing.

4.3 **Commission Statements.** Company shall provide Contractor with a monthly commission statement detailing:

- (a) Client name and service type for each transaction
- (b) Total revenue collected
- (c) Commission earned
- (d) Commission paid
- (e) Any adjustments, clawbacks, or deductions

4.4 **Disputes.** If Contractor disputes any commission calculation, Contractor must submit a written dispute within thirty (30) days of receiving the applicable commission statement. Company shall review and respond within fifteen (15) business days.

---

## 5. CLAWBACK PROVISION

5.1 **60-Day Clawback.** If a client acquired by Contractor cancels their service or terminates their agreement with Company within sixty (60) days of initial enrollment, the commission paid to Contractor for that client's setup/implementation fee shall be refunded to Company.

5.2 **Clawback Mechanics.** The refunded amount may be:

- (a) Deducted from Contractor's next commission payment(s); or
- (b) Invoiced to Contractor for repayment within thirty (30) days.

5.3 **Partial Refunds.** If Company issues a partial refund to a client, Contractor's commission shall be recalculated based on the net amount retained by Company.

---

## 6. LEAD ATTRIBUTION

6.1 **First-Touch Attribution Model.** ClawOps uses a first-touch attribution model for commission purposes. The contractor who first sources or introduces a lead to Company owns that lead for commission purposes.

6.2 **Attribution Rules:**

- (a) The contractor who first identifies, contacts, or introduces a prospective client or real estate lead to Company is the "sourcing contractor" for that lead;
- (b) Attribution is established at the point of first documented contact, CRM entry, or written notification to Company;
- (c) Once attribution is established, it remains with the sourcing contractor regardless of subsequent interactions by other contractors or Company personnel;
- (d) Attribution follows the lead, not the deal. If a lead initially pursued for one service converts to a different service, the sourcing contractor retains attribution.

6.3 **Attribution Disputes.** In the event of a dispute over lead attribution:

- (a) The Party with the earliest documented evidence of first contact shall prevail;
- (b) CRM records maintained by Company shall be the primary source of truth;
- (c) Company's Managing Member shall make the final determination, which shall be binding.

6.4 **Lead Registration.** Contractor is strongly encouraged to register all leads promptly in the Company CRM (GoHighLevel) or by written notice to Company. Failure to register a lead may impact Contractor's ability to establish attribution.

## 8. ADDITIONAL TERMS

8.1 **No Commission on Company-Sourced Business.** Contractor is not entitled to commissions on clients or deals sourced by Company, its members, or other contractors, unless Contractor is the documented first-touch source.

8.2 **Client Transfers.** If Company reassigns a client relationship from one contractor to another (due to termination, performance, or mutual agreement), the original sourcing contractor retains the commission entitlement for that client.

8.3 **Modifications.** This Exhibit A may be modified only by written agreement signed by both Parties. Any modification shall apply prospectively and shall not affect commissions already earned.

8.4 **Termination Impact.** Upon termination of the ICA:

- (a) Contractor's right to earn new commissions ceases immediately;
- (b) Any unpaid earned commissions shall be paid within thirty (30) days of termination.
