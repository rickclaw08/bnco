# CLAWOPS SYSTEMS ACCESS AND ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY

**Document 11 of the ClawOps Contractor Onboarding Packet**

---

This Systems Access and Acceptable Use Policy ("Policy") is entered into as of [Date] by and between:

**ClawOps** ("Company"), and

**[Contractor Name]** ("Contractor").

This Policy supplements and is incorporated by reference into the Independent Contractor Agreement ("ICA") between Company and Contractor. In the event of any conflict between this Policy and the ICA, the more restrictive provision shall govern.

---

## 1. DEFINITIONS

**1.1** "Company Systems" means all technology platforms, software, tools, applications, accounts, and digital resources provided by, licensed by, or operated on behalf of the Company, including but not limited to:

- (a) Customer Relationship Management platforms (including GoHighLevel and any successor or replacement CRM);
- (b) AI voice and calling platforms (including VAPI and any successor or replacement systems);
- (c) Dashboards, analytics tools, and reporting interfaces;
- (d) AI workflows, automation sequences, and workflow builders;
- (e) Scripts, call flows, rebuttals, objection-handling guides, and sales playbooks;
- (f) Communication tools (including but not limited to email systems, messaging platforms, SMS tools, and VoIP systems);
- (g) Lead capture systems, booking systems, and scheduling tools;
- (h) File storage, document management, and collaboration platforms; and
- (i) Any other digital tool, platform, or system to which Company grants Contractor access in connection with the Services.

**1.2** "Company Data" means all data, records, files, contacts, leads, customer information, prospect information, call recordings, analytics, reports, configurations, and any other information residing in, generated by, or accessible through Company Systems.

**1.3** "Credentials" means usernames, passwords, API keys, tokens, access codes, multi-factor authentication devices, and any other authentication mechanism used to access Company Systems.

**1.4** "Services" has the meaning set forth in the ICA.

---

## 2. APPROVED USE ONLY

**2.1** Contractor shall use Company Systems solely and exclusively for the purpose of performing the Services as defined in the ICA.

**2.2** Any use of Company Systems for personal purposes, for the benefit of any third party, or for any purpose not directly related to the performance of the Services is strictly prohibited.

**2.3** Contractor shall comply with all applicable terms of service, acceptable use policies, and license agreements governing Company Systems, as communicated by Company from time to time.

---

## 3. NO CREDENTIAL SHARING

**3.1** Contractor shall not share, disclose, transfer, lend, or otherwise make available any Credentials to any person, entity, or automated system, regardless of the reason.

**3.2** This prohibition applies to all individuals, including but not limited to family members, friends, business associates, subcontractors, and other contractors of the Company.

**3.3** Contractor is solely responsible for maintaining the confidentiality and security of all Credentials issued to Contractor.

**3.4** Contractor shall notify Company immediately upon discovering or suspecting that any Credentials have been compromised, disclosed, or accessed by an unauthorized party.

---

## 4. NO SCRAPING, BULK EXPORTING, OR AUTOMATED DATA EXTRACTION

**4.1** Contractor shall not scrape, crawl, harvest, bulk export, mass download, or use any automated tool, script, bot, or process to extract data from Company Systems.

**4.2** Contractor shall not use APIs, browser extensions, third-party integrations, or any other mechanism to extract data from Company Systems in bulk, unless expressly authorized in writing by Company.

**4.3** This prohibition applies regardless of whether the data is Company Data, third-party data, or publicly accessible data within Company Systems.

---

## 5. NO COPYING OF PROPRIETARY MATERIALS

**5.1** Contractor shall not copy, reproduce, download, screenshot, photograph, transcribe, or otherwise duplicate any scripts, call flows, rebuttals, objection-handling guides, sales playbooks, AI prompt templates, workflow configurations, automation sequences, or databases from Company Systems to any personal device, personal account, or personal storage of any kind.

**5.2** This prohibition applies to all formats, including but not limited to digital files, printed copies, handwritten notes, audio recordings, and photographs.

---

## 6. NO UNAUTHORIZED STORAGE OF COMPANY DATA

**6.1** Contractor shall not store, maintain, or replicate Company Data on any personal device, personal cloud account, personal email account, personal file storage service, external hard drive, USB drive, or any other storage medium not expressly authorized by Company.

**6.2** Contractor shall not create, maintain, or operate any unauthorized database, spreadsheet, contact list, or tracking system containing Company Data outside of Company Systems (commonly referred to as a "shadow CRM"). This prohibition is absolute and applies regardless of Contractor's intent or purpose.

**6.3** All Company Data shall reside exclusively within Company Systems unless Company provides prior written authorization for a specific, limited exception.

---

## 7. NO PERSONAL FORWARDING OF COMPANY DATA

**7.1** Contractor shall not forward, transfer, transmit, share, or send Company Data to any personal email address, personal messaging account, personal file transfer service, or any recipient or destination not expressly authorized by Company.

**7.2** This prohibition applies to all methods of transmission, including but not limited to email, text message, instant messaging, social media, file sharing platforms, cloud storage services, and physical transfer of storage media.

---

## 8. NO DISABLING OF SECURITY AND MONITORING TOOLS

**8.1** Contractor shall not disable, circumvent, interfere with, modify, or attempt to defeat any logging, audit trail, monitoring, tracking, security tool, access control, or compliance mechanism implemented by Company on Company Systems.

**8.2** Contractor shall not use any tool, technique, or method designed to obscure, mask, or conceal Contractor's activity within Company Systems, including but not limited to VPNs used to disguise location (unless expressly required by Company for security purposes), private browsing modes used to avoid logging, or proxy services.

---

## 9. COMPANY RIGHT TO MONITOR

**9.1** Contractor acknowledges and agrees that Company has the right, at any time and without prior notice, to monitor, log, audit, review, and analyze Contractor's use of and activity within Company Systems.

**9.2** Monitoring may include, without limitation, review of access logs, login history, data access patterns, export activity, communication content within Company Systems, and any other activity within Company Systems.

**9.3** Contractor has no expectation of privacy with respect to any activity conducted within or through Company Systems.

---

## 10. COMPANY RIGHT TO REVOKE ACCESS

**10.1** Company reserves the right to revoke, suspend, restrict, or modify Contractor's access to any or all Company Systems at any time, for any reason or for no reason, with or without prior notice.

**10.2** Revocation of access may be temporary or permanent, partial or complete, at Company's sole discretion.

**10.3** Revocation of access to Company Systems does not, by itself, constitute termination of the ICA, although it may affect Contractor's ability to perform the Services.

---

## 11. RETURN AND DELETION OBLIGATIONS

**11.1** Upon termination or expiration of the ICA, upon revocation of access to Company Systems, or upon written request by Company (whichever occurs first), Contractor shall immediately:

- (a) Cease all use of Company Systems;
- (b) Return to Company all Company Data, materials, documents, and property in Contractor's possession or control;
- (c) Permanently delete and destroy all copies of Company Data, Credentials, proprietary materials, and any other Company information from all personal devices, accounts, and storage media; and
- (d) Provide written certification to Company confirming that all obligations under this Section 11 have been fully satisfied.

**11.2** The obligations in this Section 11 survive termination of the ICA and this Policy.

---

## 12. CONSEQUENCES OF VIOLATION

**12.1** Any violation of this Policy may result in one or more of the following, at Company's sole discretion:

- (a) Immediate revocation of access to Company Systems;
- (b) Immediate termination of the ICA for cause;
- (c) Forfeiture of any unpaid commissions or compensation;
- (d) Legal action for damages, injunctive relief, or other remedies available at law or in equity; and
- (e) Referral to law enforcement authorities if applicable.

**12.2** The remedies set forth in this Section 12 are cumulative and not exclusive. Company's exercise of any remedy does not waive Company's right to pursue any other remedy.

**12.3** Contractor acknowledges that a breach of this Policy may cause irreparable harm to Company for which monetary damages would be an inadequate remedy, and that Company shall be entitled to seek injunctive or other equitable relief without the necessity of proving actual damages or posting a bond.
