## ACKNOWLEDGMENT OF INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR STATUS

By signing below, Contractor acknowledges, confirms, and agrees to each of the following statements:

---

### 1. NOT AN EMPLOYEE

Contractor is an independent contractor and is NOT an employee of ClawOps. No provision of the ICA, this Acknowledgment, or any other agreement between Contractor and Company creates an employment relationship, joint venture, partnership, or agency relationship between the parties. Contractor has entered into the ICA voluntarily and with full understanding of Contractor's independent contractor status.

---

### 2. NO SALARY OR HOURLY WAGES

Contractor will not receive a salary, hourly wage, or any form of guaranteed periodic compensation from Company. Contractor's compensation is limited exclusively to the commission structure set forth in the ICA, which consists of a percentage of one-time setup fees as described therein.

---

### 3. NO OVERTIME COMPENSATION

Contractor is not entitled to overtime pay, premium pay, shift differentials, or any other form of compensation based on hours worked. The Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) and analogous state and local wage-and-hour laws applicable to employees do not apply to Contractor's engagement with Company.

---

### 4. NO EMPLOYEE BENEFITS

Contractor is not entitled to and will not receive any employee benefits from Company, including but not limited to:

- (a) Health insurance;
- (b) Dental insurance;
- (c) Vision insurance;
- (d) Retirement plans or contributions (including 401(k), IRA matching, or pension);
- (e) Paid time off (PTO), vacation, or holiday pay;
- (f) Sick leave or paid sick days;
- (g) Short-term or long-term disability insurance;
- (h) Life insurance;
- (i) Employee assistance programs;
- (j) Tuition reimbursement or professional development stipends;
- (k) Stock options, equity grants, or profit-sharing; and
- (l) Any other fringe benefit, perk, or program offered to employees of any entity.

---

### 5. NO WORKERS' COMPENSATION COVERAGE

Company does not provide workers' compensation insurance coverage for Contractor. Contractor is not covered by any workers' compensation policy maintained by Company, except to the extent required by applicable law in Contractor's jurisdiction. If applicable law requires Company to provide workers' compensation coverage to independent contractors, Company will comply with such requirements, and Contractor will be notified in writing.

---

### 6. NO UNEMPLOYMENT INSURANCE

Company does not pay unemployment insurance premiums on behalf of Contractor. Contractor is not eligible for unemployment insurance benefits through Company. Contractor acknowledges that Contractor's engagement with Company does not establish eligibility for state or federal unemployment compensation.

---

### 7. TAX RESPONSIBILITY

Contractor is solely responsible for the reporting and payment of all federal, state, and local taxes arising from compensation received under the ICA, including but not limited to:

- (a) Federal income tax;
- (b) State and local income tax;
- (c) Self-employment tax (Social Security and Medicare);
- (d) Estimated quarterly tax payments; and
- (e) Any other tax obligation imposed by any governmental authority.

Company will not withhold taxes from Contractor's compensation. Company will issue IRS Form 1099-NEC (or successor form) to Contractor as required by law. Contractor is encouraged to consult with a qualified tax professional regarding Contractor's tax obligations.

---

### 8. INSURANCE RESPONSIBILITY

Contractor is solely responsible for obtaining and maintaining, at Contractor's own expense, any insurance coverage Contractor deems necessary or advisable, including but not limited to:

- (a) Health insurance;
- (b) General liability insurance;
- (c) Professional liability (errors and omissions) insurance;
- (d) Disability insurance; and
- (e) Any other insurance required by applicable law or deemed appropriate by Contractor.

---

### 9. BUSINESS LICENSES AND REGISTRATIONS

Contractor is solely responsible for obtaining and maintaining any business licenses, permits, registrations, or certifications required by applicable federal, state, or local law for Contractor to lawfully operate as an independent contractor and to perform the Services under the ICA.

---

### 10. NO ALTERATION OF ICA TERMS

This Acknowledgment is intended to confirm Contractor's understanding of Contractor's independent contractor status and the absence of employment benefits. Nothing in this Acknowledgment creates, modifies, amends, or supersedes any term or condition of the ICA. In the event of any inconsistency between this Acknowledgment and the ICA, the terms of the ICA shall govern.
