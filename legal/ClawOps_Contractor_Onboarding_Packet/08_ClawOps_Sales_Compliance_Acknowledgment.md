## Purpose

This Sales Compliance Acknowledgment is a standalone agreement between Contractor and Company. By signing below, Contractor confirms that Contractor has read, understands, and agrees to comply with every provision in this document. Failure to comply may result in immediate termination for cause and personal liability as described in Section 15.

---

## Section 1. Telemarketing Law Compliance

Contractor acknowledges and agrees to comply with all applicable federal, state, and local telemarketing laws and regulations, including but not limited to:

- The Telemarketing Sales Rule (TSR), 16 C.F.R. Part 310
- The Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA), 47 U.S.C. Section 227
- All applicable state telemarketing statutes, registration requirements, and licensing obligations

Contractor will not initiate any outbound sales communication in violation of these laws. Contractor is solely responsible for understanding the telemarketing regulations applicable in each jurisdiction where Contractor conducts sales activity on behalf of Company.

---

## Section 2. Do Not Call (DNC) List Compliance

Contractor shall comply with all federal and state Do Not Call (DNC) requirements, including but not limited to:

a. The National Do Not Call Registry maintained by the Federal Trade Commission (FTC)
b. Company-maintained internal Do Not Call lists
c. State-specific Do Not Call registries applicable to jurisdictions where Contractor conducts outreach

Contractor shall not contact any individual or business that appears on any applicable DNC list. If Contractor is unsure whether a contact is restricted, Contractor must verify with Company before initiating communication.

---

## Section 3. Opt-Out and Unsubscribe Handling

Contractor shall immediately honor any opt-out, unsubscribe, or do-not-contact request received from any prospect or lead. Upon receiving such a request, Contractor must:

a. Cease all further communication with the requesting party
b. Log the opt-out request in the Company-approved CRM system within the same business day
c. Report the request to Company if received outside normal CRM workflow

Contractor shall never attempt to re-engage a prospect who has opted out without prior written authorization from Company.

---

## Section 4. Call Recording Compliance

Contractor acknowledges that Company may record calls for quality assurance, training, and compliance purposes. Contractor further acknowledges:

a. Certain jurisdictions require one-party consent for call recording, while others require all-party (two-party) consent
b. Contractor is responsible for knowing the consent requirements applicable in both Contractor's location and the location of the party being contacted
c. Contractor must provide legally required disclosures or obtain consent before any recorded communication, in accordance with applicable law
d. Contractor shall not disable, circumvent, or interfere with any Company-authorized recording systems

---

## Section 5. Truthful Representations Only

Contractor shall make only truthful, accurate, and non-misleading statements about Company, its services, its technology, and its business relationships. Contractor shall not:

a. Misrepresent the nature, scope, capabilities, or pricing of any Company service
b. Misrepresent Contractor's relationship with Company (e.g., claiming to be an employee, partner, or officer)
c. Make any statement that could reasonably be interpreted as deceptive, misleading, or fraudulent
d. Misrepresent the identity or nature of AI-powered tools or services offered by Company

---

## Section 6. No Unauthorized Earnings Promises

Contractor shall not make any representations to prospects or potential clients regarding specific earnings, revenue, savings, or financial outcomes that prospects may achieve by purchasing Company services, unless such representations have been expressly approved in writing by Company. This prohibition includes:

a. Specific dollar amounts or percentage increases in revenue
b. Guaranteed return on investment (ROI) claims
c. Comparisons to competitors' results without Company-approved data
d. Testimonials or case studies not authorized by Company

**Initials: ______**

---

## Section 7. No Unauthorized Legal Claims

Contractor shall not make any legal representations, warranties, or binding commitments on behalf of Company. Contractor is not authorized to:

a. Enter into contracts, agreements, or binding arrangements on behalf of Company
b. Make statements regarding Company's legal obligations, liabilities, or compliance status
c. Provide legal advice or legal interpretations to prospects or clients
d. Represent or imply that Company's services satisfy specific regulatory requirements on behalf of the prospect's business

---

## Section 8. No Guarantee Claims About Results

Contractor shall not guarantee or promise specific results, outcomes, or performance metrics to any prospect or client. This includes, without limitation:

a. Guaranteed lead volumes, appointment rates, or conversion rates
b. Guaranteed revenue increases or cost reductions
c. Guaranteed response times, uptime, or service availability beyond Company's published terms
d. Any "money-back guarantee" or refund commitment not authorized in writing by Company

---

## Section 9. Approved Scripts and Workflows Only

Contractor shall use only Company-approved scripts, talking points, workflows, and sales materials when conducting outreach or sales activities on behalf of Company. Contractor shall not:

a. Create or use unauthorized scripts, presentations, or marketing materials
b. Modify Company-approved materials without prior written authorization
c. Distribute sales materials through unauthorized channels or platforms
d. Represent unauthorized materials as Company-approved content

Company reserves the right to update approved scripts and workflows at any time. Contractor is responsible for using the most current versions provided by Company.

---

## Section 10. AI-Assisted and AI-Generated Voice Workflow Controls

Contractor acknowledges that Company utilizes AI-assisted and AI-generated voice workflows in its service offerings and sales processes. Contractor agrees to:

a. Disclose the use of AI-generated or AI-assisted voice technology to prospects and clients where required by applicable law or regulation
b. Follow all Company-provided protocols for identifying AI-generated communications
c. Not misrepresent AI-generated communications as live human interactions where such misrepresentation would violate applicable law
d. Immediately report to Company any prospect or client concerns, objections, or complaints related to AI voice technology
e. Stay current on Company-provided training regarding AI disclosure obligations as laws and regulations evolve

Contractor acknowledges that AI disclosure requirements vary by jurisdiction and may change. Contractor is responsible for complying with the most current legal standards applicable to Contractor's sales territory.

**Initials: ______**

---

## Section 11. Escalation Requirement

Contractor must immediately escalate the following events to Company through designated escalation channels (email, CRM ticket, or direct contact with assigned Company representative):

a. Any complaint from a prospect, lead, or client regarding Company or its services
b. Any threat of legal action, regulatory complaint, or formal dispute
c. Receipt of any legal notice, cease-and-desist letter, subpoena, or government inquiry
d. Any opt-out, unsubscribe, or do-not-contact request that cannot be processed through standard CRM workflow
e. Any suspected data breach, unauthorized access, or security incident involving Company systems or client data
f. Any compliance-related event, including contact with individuals on DNC lists or recording consent issues

"Immediately" means within two (2) hours of Contractor becoming aware of the event, or sooner if the nature of the event demands urgent response. Failure to escalate is grounds for termination for cause.

## Section 12. No Off-Book Campaigns or Side Deals

Contractor shall not conduct any sales campaigns, outreach programs, lead generation activities, or promotional efforts on behalf of Company (or using Company branding, tools, or client data) that have not been expressly authorized by Company. This includes:

a. Side deals or private arrangements with prospects or clients
b. Referral fee arrangements not authorized by Company
c. Cross-selling or upselling services not offered by Company
d. Running campaigns through personal channels (social media, personal email, personal phone) without Company authorization

---

## Section 13. No Exporting Leads or Client Data

Contractor shall not export, download, copy, screenshot, or otherwise extract lead data, client data, prospect lists, contact information, or any other Company data from Company systems for any purpose, including but not limited to:

a. Personal use or personal business development
b. Transfer to a third party, competitor, or future employer
c. Creation of personal backup copies outside Company-approved systems
d. Use after termination of Contractor's engagement with Company

All leads, client data, and prospect information are and remain the exclusive property of Company.

---

## Section 14. No Use of Personal Tools Outside Company-Approved Platforms

Contractor shall conduct all sales activity, client communication, and data handling exclusively through Company-approved platforms and tools. Contractor shall not:

a. Use personal email accounts, personal phone numbers (unless authorized), or personal messaging applications for Company business
b. Store Company data on personal devices, personal cloud accounts, or unauthorized third-party platforms
c. Use unauthorized CRM, dialer, or communication tools for Company-related activities
d. Install unauthorized software or browser extensions on devices used to access Company systems

---

## Section 15. Consequences of Violation

Violation of any provision in this Sales Compliance Acknowledgment may result in:

a. **Immediate termination for cause** of the Independent Contractor Agreement, without notice or cure period, at Company's sole discretion
b. **Forfeiture of unpaid commissions** associated with transactions tainted by the violation, as permitted by applicable law
c. **Indemnification obligation**, whereby Contractor agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Company, its officers, directors, agents, and affiliates from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, fines, penalties, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from or related to Contractor's violation of this Acknowledgment
d. **Referral to regulatory or law enforcement authorities** where Contractor's conduct may constitute a violation of applicable law

The remedies described in this Section are cumulative and do not limit any other rights or remedies available to Company under the Independent Contractor Agreement, at law, or in equity.
