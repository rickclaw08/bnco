# CLAWOPS DATA SECURITY ADDENDUM

**Document 12 of the ClawOps Contractor Onboarding Packet**

---

This Data Security Addendum ("Addendum") is entered into as of [Date] by and between:

**ClawOps** ("Company"), and

**[Contractor Name]** ("Contractor").

This Addendum supplements and is incorporated by reference into the Independent Contractor Agreement ("ICA") and the Systems Access and Acceptable Use Policy ("Use Policy") between Company and Contractor.

---

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

**1.1** The purpose of this Addendum is to establish minimum data security obligations that Contractor must follow when accessing, using, or handling Company Systems and Company Data (as defined in the Use Policy).

**1.2** This Addendum applies to all devices, networks, and environments from which Contractor accesses Company Systems, whether owned by Contractor, Company, or any third party.

**1.3** The security requirements set forth herein represent minimum standards. Contractor is encouraged to implement additional security measures beyond those required by this Addendum.

---

## 2. PASSWORD REQUIREMENTS

**2.1** Contractor shall use strong, unique passwords for all Company Systems accounts. At minimum, each password must:

- (a) Contain at least twelve (12) characters;
- (b) Include a combination of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters; and
- (c) Not contain easily guessable information such as the Contractor's name, birthdate, or common dictionary words.

**2.2** Contractor shall not reuse passwords across Company Systems or between Company Systems and personal accounts.

**2.3** Contractor shall not store passwords in plain text, unencrypted files, sticky notes, or any other unsecured format. Use of a reputable password manager is strongly recommended.

**2.4** Contractor shall change passwords immediately upon any suspicion of compromise and shall notify Company promptly.

---

## 3. MULTI-FACTOR AUTHENTICATION (MFA)

**3.1** Contractor shall enable and maintain multi-factor authentication (MFA) on all Company Systems accounts that support MFA.

**3.2** Acceptable MFA methods include authenticator applications (such as Google Authenticator, Authy, or Microsoft Authenticator) and hardware security keys. SMS-based MFA is acceptable only when no other method is available.

**3.3** Contractor shall not disable, bypass, or circumvent MFA on any Company Systems account for any reason.

**3.4** If Contractor loses access to an MFA device, Contractor shall notify Company immediately to arrange secure re-authentication.

---

## 4. DEVICE HYGIENE

**4.1** Any device used by Contractor to access Company Systems must meet the following minimum requirements:

- (a) **Operating System Updates:** The device must run a currently supported operating system with all available security patches and updates installed within seven (7) days of release;
- (b) **Antivirus and Antimalware:** The device must have reputable antivirus and antimalware software installed, active, and set to update automatically;
- (c) **Screen Lock:** The device must be configured to lock automatically after no more than five (5) minutes of inactivity, requiring a password, PIN, or biometric authentication to unlock; and
- (d) **Encryption:** Full-disk encryption must be enabled on any device that stores or accesses Company Data.

**4.2** Contractor shall not use jailbroken, rooted, or otherwise modified devices to access Company Systems.

---

## 5. PHISHING AWARENESS

**5.1** Contractor shall exercise caution with all electronic communications and shall not click on suspicious links, open unexpected attachments, or respond to requests for Credentials or sensitive information from unverified sources.

**5.2** Contractor shall verify the authenticity of any communication that requests login credentials, financial information, or access to Company Systems, even if the communication appears to originate from Company or a known contact.

**5.3** Contractor shall report suspected phishing attempts, social engineering attempts, or other suspicious communications to Company within twenty-four (24) hours of discovery.

---

## 6. SECURE DATA STORAGE

**6.1** Contractor shall not store Company Data on any unencrypted device, removable media (including but not limited to USB drives, external hard drives, SD cards, and optical discs), or unauthorized cloud storage service.

**6.2** Company Data shall reside exclusively within Company Systems unless Company provides prior written authorization for a specific, limited exception, as further described in the Use Policy.

**6.3** Contractor shall not print Company Data unless required for the performance of the Services, and any printed materials containing Company Data must be securely destroyed (shredded or incinerated) when no longer needed.

---

## 7. NETWORK SECURITY

**7.1** Contractor shall not access Company Systems over public Wi-Fi networks (including but not limited to those found in coffee shops, airports, hotels, libraries, and co-working spaces) unless Contractor uses a virtual private network (VPN) or equivalent encrypted connection approved by Company.

**7.2** Contractor's home or office network used to access Company Systems should employ WPA3 or WPA2 encryption with a strong, unique password. Open or WEP-encrypted networks are prohibited.

**7.3** Contractor shall not access Company Systems from shared or public computers.

---

## 8. BREACH REPORTING

**8.1** Contractor shall notify Company within twenty-four (24) hours of discovering or reasonably suspecting any of the following:

- (a) Unauthorized access to Company Systems or Company Data;
- (b) Loss or theft of any device used to access Company Systems;
- (c) Compromise of any Credentials;
- (d) Malware infection on any device used to access Company Systems;
- (e) Any other event that may compromise the security, confidentiality, or integrity of Company Data.

**8.2** Notification shall be made by contacting Company at the email address or phone number designated by Company for security incidents. Contractor shall document the notification in writing and retain a copy.

**8.3** Failure to report a known or suspected breach within the twenty-four (24) hour window constitutes a material violation of this Addendum.

---

## 9. INCIDENT ESCALATION PROCEDURE

**9.1** Upon discovering or suspecting a security incident, Contractor shall take the following steps in order:

- (a) **Contain:** Immediately cease the activity that may have caused or contributed to the incident. Do not attempt to "fix" the issue independently unless doing so is necessary to prevent further data exposure;
- (b) **Document:** Record the following information as accurately as possible: (i) date and time of discovery, (ii) nature of the incident, (iii) systems and data affected or potentially affected, (iv) actions taken before and after discovery, and (v) any evidence available (screenshots, error messages, log entries);
- (c) **Notify:** Contact Company's designated security contact within twenty-four (24) hours as described in Section 8;
- (d) **Preserve:** Do not delete, alter, or destroy any evidence, logs, files, or communications related to the incident; and
- (e) **Await Instructions:** Follow all further instructions from Company regarding the incident response.

---

## 10. COOPERATION WITH COMPANY AFTER INCIDENT

**10.1** Following any security incident, Contractor shall cooperate fully with Company and any third parties engaged by Company (including forensic investigators, legal counsel, and law enforcement) in the investigation, remediation, and resolution of the incident.

**10.2** Cooperation includes, without limitation:

- (a) Providing access to affected devices for forensic examination;
- (b) Providing all documentation created under Section 9;
- (c) Participating in interviews and providing truthful, complete information;
- (d) Implementing remedial security measures as directed by Company; and
- (e) Preserving all relevant evidence for the duration of the investigation and any subsequent legal proceedings.

**10.3** Contractor shall not make any public statement, disclosure, or notification regarding any security incident without prior written authorization from Company.

---

## 11. SURVIVAL

**11.1** The obligations set forth in this Addendum, including but not limited to breach reporting, cooperation, and data security obligations, shall survive the termination or expiration of the ICA, the Use Policy, and this Addendum.

**11.2** Survival continues for so long as Contractor retains any Company Data or has knowledge of Company Data sufficient to pose a security risk, and in no event for less than two (2) years following the termination or expiration of the ICA.
