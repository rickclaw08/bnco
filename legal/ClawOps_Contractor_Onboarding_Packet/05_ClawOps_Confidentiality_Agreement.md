# CONFIDENTIALITY, TRADE SECRETS, AND PROPRIETARY RIGHTS AGREEMENT

**DRAFT - FOR LEGAL REVIEW ONLY**

---

## PARTIES

This Confidentiality, Trade Secrets, and Proprietary Rights Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of [Effective Date] ("Effective Date") by and between:

**ClawOps** ("Company"), a technology and consulting firm providing AI-powered voice systems, lead capture, booking, CRM, and communications infrastructure to trades contractors across North America;

and

**[Contractor Name]** ("Contractor"), an individual independent contractor engaged by Company under the Independent Contractor Agreement dated [Effective Date] ("ICA," File 04).

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, Company possesses valuable confidential and proprietary information, including but not limited to client data, sales methodologies, AI agent configurations, pricing structures, and business strategies;

WHEREAS, Contractor will have access to such information in the course of performing Services under the ICA;

WHEREAS, protection of this information is essential to Company's business and competitive position;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual obligations set forth herein and the engagement under the ICA, the parties agree as follows:

---

## 1. PURPOSE

This Agreement sets forth Contractor's obligations regarding the protection of Company's Confidential Information, trade secrets, and proprietary rights. This Agreement supplements and is incorporated by reference into the ICA (File 04).

## 2. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

"Confidential Information" means all non-public business, technical, financial, operational, client, and prospect information disclosed to, or accessible by, Contractor through the engagement, whether disclosed orally, in writing, electronically, or by observation. Confidential Information includes, without limitation:

(a) **Client and Prospect Data.** Client lists, lead lists, prospect databases, contact information, account histories, engagement records, and pipeline data;

(b) **Pricing and Financial Data.** Fee structures, commission rates, Setup Fee amounts, revenue data, profit margins, cost structures, financial projections, and billing records;

(c) **Sales Materials.** Sales scripts, call flows, rebuttals, objection handlers, closing techniques, outreach sequences, and conversion methodologies;

(d) **AI and Technology Assets.** AI agent prompts, voice configurations, workflow designs, automation sequences, integration architectures, API keys, credentials, and system configurations;

(e) **CRM and Operational Data.** CRM records, pipeline data, conversion metrics, performance analytics, dashboards, and reporting structures;

(f) **Marketing and Strategy.** Marketing strategies, campaign plans, content calendars, branding materials, competitive analyses, and market research;

(g) **Business Plans.** Strategic plans, growth projections, operational procedures, expansion strategies, and partnership negotiations;

(h) **Software and Code.** Proprietary software, source code, integrations, scripts, tools, and technical documentation;

(i) **Vendor and Supplier Information.** Vendor relationships, supplier terms, licensing agreements, and third-party contracts;

(j) **Personnel Data.** Employee and contractor compensation details, performance records, and organizational structure;

(k) **Marked Information.** Any information marked "Confidential," "Proprietary," or with similar designation; and

(l) **Reasonably Understood.** Any information that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure.

## 3. EXCLUSIONS FROM CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

Confidential Information does not include information that Contractor can demonstrate by competent written evidence:

(a) Was publicly available at the time of disclosure, or becomes publicly available thereafter, through no fault or breach by Contractor;

(b) Was already known to Contractor prior to the engagement, as evidenced by written records predating the Effective Date;

(c) Was independently developed by Contractor without use of or reference to any Confidential Information, as evidenced by contemporaneous written records; or

(d) Was received by Contractor from a third party who had no obligation of confidentiality to Company and who disclosed such information without restriction.

The burden of proving any exclusion rests with Contractor.

## 4. TRADE SECRETS

"Trade Secrets" means the subset of Confidential Information that meets the statutory requirements for trade secret protection under the Ohio Uniform Trade Secrets Act (Ohio Rev. Code 1333.61 et seq.) and the federal Defend Trade Secrets Act of 2016 (18 U.S.C. 1836 et seq.). Trade Secrets include, without limitation, Company's proprietary AI agent configurations, sales methodologies, client databases, and pricing algorithms to the extent they derive independent economic value from not being generally known and are subject to reasonable efforts to maintain their secrecy.

## 5. NON-DISCLOSURE OBLIGATIONS

Contractor shall not, during the term of the ICA or at any time thereafter (subject to Section 10), disclose, publish, communicate, or make available any Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of Company. Contractor shall use the same degree of care to protect Company's Confidential Information as Contractor uses to protect Contractor's own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

## 6. NON-USE OBLIGATIONS

Contractor shall use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing Services under the ICA. Contractor shall not use Confidential Information for any personal benefit, for the benefit of any third party, or for any purpose other than the performance of Services.

## 7. STANDARD OF CARE

Contractor shall protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care that Contractor uses to protect Contractor's own confidential information of similar nature and importance, but in no event less than a reasonable standard of care. Contractor shall take all reasonable precautions to prevent unauthorized disclosure, including:

(a) Storing Confidential Information only on secure, password-protected, and encrypted devices;

(b) Not transmitting Confidential Information over unsecured channels;

(c) Not leaving Confidential Information accessible in shared or public spaces; and

(d) Immediately reporting any suspected breach to Company.

## 8. PERMITTED DISCLOSURES

Contractor may disclose Confidential Information only:

(a) To Company-approved personnel who have a legitimate need to know in connection with the performance of Services, provided such personnel are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those contained herein; or

(b) As required by applicable law, regulation, subpoena, or legal process, provided that Contractor shall (i) give Company prompt written notice of such requirement (to the extent legally permitted), (ii) cooperate with Company in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy, and (iii) disclose only the minimum amount of Confidential Information required.

## 9. NO REVERSE ENGINEERING

Contractor shall not reverse engineer, decompile, disassemble, or otherwise attempt to derive the design, structure, or source code of any Company technology, software, AI models, or systems, whether or not such technology constitutes Confidential Information.

## 10. DURATION OF OBLIGATIONS

(a) **Confidential Information.** Contractor's obligations regarding Confidential Information (other than Trade Secrets) shall continue for a period of [3] years following the effective date of termination or expiration of the ICA.

(b) **Trade Secrets.** Contractor's obligations regarding Trade Secrets shall continue for as long as the information qualifies as a trade secret under applicable law, which may be indefinite.

## 11. RETURN AND DESTRUCTION

Upon termination or expiration of the ICA, or upon Company's written request at any time:

(a) Contractor shall promptly return to Company all physical materials containing or reflecting Confidential Information;

(b) Contractor shall permanently delete all electronic copies of Confidential Information from all devices, accounts, and storage media within Contractor's possession or control;

(c) Contractor shall certify in writing, within ten (10) business days of termination or request, that all Confidential Information has been returned or destroyed in accordance with this Section;

(d) **Archival Exception.** Contractor may retain one (1) archival copy of this Agreement and the ICA solely for Contractor's legal compliance purposes, provided such copy is stored securely and remains subject to the confidentiality obligations herein. This exception does not extend to client data, lead lists, scripts, AI configurations, or any other operational Confidential Information.

## 12. COMPANY PROPERTY

All Confidential Information is and shall remain the exclusive property of Company. Nothing in this Agreement grants Contractor any license, ownership interest, or other right in or to any Confidential Information, except the limited right to use such information for the purpose of performing Services under the ICA during the term of the engagement.

## 13. INJUNCTIVE RELIEF

Contractor acknowledges that any breach or threatened breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm to Company for which monetary damages would be an insufficient remedy. Accordingly, Company shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief, specific performance, or other equitable remedies from any court of competent jurisdiction, without the necessity of posting a bond or other security and without the need to prove actual damages. Such remedies shall be in addition to, and not in lieu of, any other remedies available at law or in equity.

## 14. DEFEND TRADE SECRETS ACT - FEDERAL WHISTLEBLOWER IMMUNITY NOTICE

Pursuant to the Defend Trade Secrets Act of 2016 (18 U.S.C. 1833(b)), Contractor is hereby notified that an individual shall not be held criminally or civilly liable under any federal or state trade secret law for the disclosure of a trade secret that is made: (i) in confidence to a federal, state, or local government official, either directly or indirectly, or to an attorney, and solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law; or (ii) in a complaint or other document filed in a lawsuit or other proceeding, if such filing is made under seal.

Additionally, an individual who files a lawsuit for retaliation by an employer for reporting a suspected violation of law may disclose the employer's trade secrets to the attorney and use the trade secret information in the court proceeding if the individual: (a) files any document containing the trade secret under seal; and (b) does not disclose the trade secret except pursuant to court order.

## 15. WHISTLEBLOWER CARVEOUT

Nothing in this Agreement restricts or prohibits Contractor from:

(a) Reporting potential violations of federal, state, or local law or regulation to any governmental agency or entity, including but not limited to the Department of Justice, the Securities and Exchange Commission, the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission, or any other federal, state, or local governmental agency or commission;

(b) Making disclosures that are protected under the whistleblower provisions of federal, state, or local law or regulation; or

(c) Cooperating with any governmental investigation or inquiry, including providing documents or other information, without notice to Company.

Contractor is not required to notify Company before making any such report, disclosure, or cooperation.

## 16. REMEDIES

The rights and remedies provided in this Agreement are cumulative and not exclusive of any other rights or remedies available at law or in equity.

## 17. INDEMNIFICATION

Contractor shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Company and its officers, directors, members, managers, employees, agents, and affiliates from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or related to any breach of this Agreement by Contractor.

## 18. SURVIVAL

The obligations set forth in this Agreement shall survive the termination or expiration of the ICA in accordance with Section 10 and shall remain in full force and effect regardless of the reason for termination.

## 19. GOVERNING LAW

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Ohio, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

## 20. VENUE

Any legal action or proceeding arising under or related to this Agreement shall be brought exclusively in the state or federal courts located in Hamilton County, Ohio. The parties hereby consent to the personal jurisdiction of such courts and waive any objection to venue therein.

## 21. SEVERABILITY

If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect. The invalid provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid and enforceable while preserving the parties' original intent.

## 22. ENTIRE AGREEMENT (AS TO SUBJECT MATTER)

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, and representations, whether written or oral, relating to confidentiality. This Agreement supplements and is incorporated into the ICA (File 04). In the event of a conflict between this Agreement and the ICA regarding confidentiality obligations, the more protective provision shall control.

## 23. AMENDMENT

This Agreement may not be amended, modified, or supplemented except by a written instrument signed by both parties.

## 24. WAIVER

The failure of either party to enforce any provision of this Agreement shall not constitute a waiver of that party's right to enforce that provision or any other provision in the future.

## 25. COUNTERPARTS

This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument. Electronic signatures shall have the same legal effect as original signatures.
