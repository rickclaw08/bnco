# CLAWOPS ELECTRONIC SIGNATURE AND ELECTRONIC RECORDS CONSENT

**Document 14 of the ClawOps Contractor Onboarding Packet**

---

This Electronic Signature and Electronic Records Consent ("Consent") is executed as of [Date] by:

**[Contractor Name]** ("Contractor").

This Consent is made in connection with the Independent Contractor Agreement ("ICA") and all related documents between Contractor and **ClawOps** ("Company").

---

## 1. PURPOSE

**1.1** The purpose of this Consent is to obtain Contractor's agreement to conduct business electronically with Company, including the execution of documents by electronic signature and the delivery and receipt of records, notices, and communications in electronic form.

**1.2** This Consent applies to the ICA and all agreements, addenda, policies, acknowledgments, notices, disclosures, and other documents and communications related to the contractual relationship between Contractor and Company (collectively, "Documents").

---

## 2. CONSENT TO ELECTRONIC SIGNATURES

**2.1** Contractor consents to sign Documents electronically using any method accepted by Company, including but not limited to typed name and date, click-to-accept, digital signature platforms (such as DocuSign, HelloSign, PandaDoc, or similar services), and email confirmation.

**2.2** Contractor agrees that Contractor's electronic signature on any Document is valid, binding, and enforceable, and carries the same legal effect as a handwritten signature on a paper document.

**2.3** Contractor acknowledges that by providing an electronic signature, Contractor intends to be legally bound by the terms of the Document so signed, to the same extent as if Contractor had signed a physical copy with ink.

---

## 3. CONSENT TO ELECTRONIC RECORDS AND COMMUNICATIONS

**3.1** Contractor consents to receive all Documents, records, notices, disclosures, and communications from Company in electronic form, including but not limited to:

- (a) The ICA and all exhibits, schedules, and amendments;
- (b) Policies, addenda, and acknowledgments (including all documents in the Contractor Onboarding Packet);
- (c) Commission statements, payment notices, and financial records;
- (d) Notices of termination, modification, or amendment;
- (e) Legal notices and disclosures required by applicable law; and
- (f) Any other communication related to the contractual relationship.

**3.2** Electronic delivery may be made by email, through a secure online portal, via a document signing platform, or by any other electronic method selected by Company.

**3.3** Electronic records and communications provided under this Consent satisfy any legal requirement that such records or communications be provided in writing.

---

## 4. EMAIL ADDRESS ACCURACY

**4.1** Contractor is solely responsible for providing Company with a current, valid, and regularly monitored email address.

**4.2** Contractor shall notify Company promptly of any change to Contractor's email address. Until Company receives notice of a change, Company may rely on the last email address provided by Contractor.

**4.3** Contractor acknowledges that failure to maintain a current email address may result in Contractor not receiving important Documents, notices, or communications, and that Company shall not be liable for any consequences resulting from Contractor's failure to maintain a current email address.

---

## 5. RIGHT TO REQUEST PAPER COPIES

**5.1** Contractor may request a paper copy of any Document that was provided or signed electronically by submitting a written request to Company.

**5.2** Company may provide paper copies at its discretion, or as required by applicable law. Company reserves the right to charge a reasonable fee for the production and delivery of paper copies, except where prohibited by law.

**5.3** A request for paper copies does not constitute withdrawal of this Consent and does not affect the validity of any electronically signed Document.

---

## 6. WITHDRAWAL OF CONSENT

**6.1** Contractor may withdraw this Consent at any time by providing written notice to Company. Withdrawal must be submitted in writing (email is acceptable) to Company's designated contact.

**6.2** Withdrawal of consent is effective upon Company's receipt and acknowledgment of the withdrawal notice. Withdrawal does not affect the legal validity or enforceability of any Document electronically signed or delivered prior to the effective date of withdrawal.

**6.3** Contractor acknowledges that withdrawal of this Consent may affect Contractor's ability to continue the engagement with Company, as Company's onboarding, communication, and documentation processes rely on electronic methods. Withdrawal may result in delays, additional administrative requirements, or, in Company's sole discretion, termination of the ICA.

---

## 7. HARDWARE AND SOFTWARE REQUIREMENTS

**7.1** To access, receive, and retain electronic Documents and communications, Contractor must have the following:

- (a) A computer, tablet, or smartphone with internet access;
- (b) A current, supported web browser (such as Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Apple Safari, or Microsoft Edge);
- (c) Software capable of viewing PDF files (such as Adobe Acrobat Reader or equivalent);
- (d) An active email account capable of sending and receiving email with attachments; and
- (e) Sufficient storage capacity to save electronic Documents or the ability to print them.

**7.2** Contractor is solely responsible for acquiring, maintaining, and updating the hardware and software necessary to meet these requirements.

---

## 8. LEGAL VALIDITY OF ELECTRONIC SIGNATURES

**8.1** Contractor acknowledges and agrees that electronic signatures carry the same legal weight, validity, and enforceability as handwritten signatures on paper documents.

**8.2** Contractor shall not challenge the validity, enforceability, or admissibility of any Document solely on the basis that it was signed electronically or delivered in electronic form.

---

## 9. APPLICABLE LAW

**9.1** This Consent is governed by and shall be interpreted in accordance with the Electronic Signatures in Global and National Commerce Act (ESIGN Act), 15 U.S.C. Sections 7001 through 7006, and the Uniform Electronic Transactions Act (UETA) as adopted by the applicable state jurisdiction.

**9.2** To the extent that any state has enacted its own version of the UETA or other legislation governing electronic signatures and electronic records, such legislation shall apply in addition to the ESIGN Act.

**9.3** Nothing in this Consent is intended to waive any right that cannot be waived under the ESIGN Act, UETA, or other applicable law.

---

## 10. RETENTION RECOMMENDATION

**10.1** Contractor is strongly encouraged to download, save, or print copies of all electronically signed Documents and all electronic records and communications received from Company.

**10.2** Contractor should retain these copies for Contractor's personal records for at least the duration of the contractual relationship and for a minimum of four (4) years following termination or expiration of the ICA, or for such longer period as may be required by applicable tax or regulatory law.

**10.3** Company is not responsible for maintaining copies of Documents on behalf of Contractor, although Company may do so at its discretion.
