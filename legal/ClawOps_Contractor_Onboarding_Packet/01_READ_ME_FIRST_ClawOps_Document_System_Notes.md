## 1. What This Packet Covers

This packet is the complete onboarding and protection document system for ClawOps' 1099 independent contractor sales force. It contains twenty (20) documents covering:

- The master contractor engagement agreement
- Commission structure and payout terms
- Confidentiality and trade secret protections
- Non-solicitation restrictions
- Sales compliance requirements
- Systems access and data security obligations
- Contractor intake and profile collection
- Equipment and infrastructure verification
- Electronic signature and records consent
- Internal operating procedures for document handling, offboarding, and dispute resolution
- Classification guidance and internal control checklists

Every document in this packet is designed to work together as a unified system. Defined terms are consistent across all files. Compensation logic is locked to a single rule set. Cross-references point to real documents within this packet.

## 2. What This Packet Does Not Cover

This packet does not include:

- W-2 employee onboarding documents
- Employee benefit enrollment forms
- Payroll authorization or withholding forms
- Workers' compensation enrollment
- Real estate transaction documents of any kind
- Client-facing service agreements
- LLC formation or operating agreement documents
- State-specific regulatory filings or registrations

If ClawOps transitions any contractor to W-2 employee status, a separate employee onboarding process must be created with independent legal review. See File 18 (W-2 Employee Conversion Warning Memo) for guidance.

## 3. Classification Model

This packet assumes a 1099 independent contractor engagement model. Every document is drafted to reflect that the contractor:

- Controls the manner and means of performing services
- Is not subject to company-directed schedules, quotas, or mandatory working hours
- Provides their own equipment and workspace
- Is responsible for their own taxes, insurance, and business expenses

**This classification depends on actual practice, not just contract language.** If ClawOps' day-to-day management of contractors begins to resemble employer-employee control, the contracts alone will not protect the company. Review File 03 (1099 Classification Caution Memo) for detailed guidance.

## 4. Legal Review Requirement

**These documents are drafts prepared for internal use and review. They are not a substitute for legal counsel.**

Before using this packet in production:

- Have a licensed attorney in your operating state review all documents
- If onboarding contractors in multiple states, obtain multistate legal review, as non-solicitation enforceability, commission payout timing, and contractor classification standards vary significantly by jurisdiction
- Pay particular attention to Files 04, 05, 06, 07, and 08, which carry the highest legal exposure

## 5. Compensation Consistency Warning

The compensation rule in this packet is:

> **15% of the one-time setup/implementation fee on the contractor's own closed deals, payable only after ClawOps receives cleared funds.**

This rule appears in the Independent Contractor Agreement (File 04), the Commission Exhibit (File 07), the Sales Compliance Acknowledgment (File 08), and the Global Definitions Checklist (File 19).

**If you change the compensation structure, you must update every file that references compensation, commission, or payout terms.** Changing one exhibit without updating the master agreement and acknowledgment forms creates internal contradictions that weaken enforcement and invite disputes.

Do not mix prior compensation language (including any references to recurring monthly commissions, residual payments, or multi-stream models) into this packet or any future amendments without a full cross-document update.

## 6. Version Control

When amending any document in this packet:

- Update the version number on the amended document
- Update the Master Document Index (File 02)
- Re-run the Global Definitions Checklist (File 19)
- Re-run the Final Audit Report (File 20)
- Store superseded versions separately with clear date labels
- Never use a prior-version exhibit with a current-version agreement
