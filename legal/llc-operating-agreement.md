# OPERATING AGREEMENT OF CLAWOPS LLC

## AN OHIO LIMITED LIABILITY COMPANY

---

**This Operating Agreement ("Agreement")** is entered into and effective as of [Date] ("Effective Date") by the undersigned Member of ClawOps LLC, an Ohio limited liability company ("Company").

---

## ARTICLE 1: FORMATION AND NAME

1.1 **Formation.** The Company was formed as an Ohio limited liability company by the filing of Articles of Organization with the Ohio Secretary of State on [Filing_Date], in accordance with the Ohio Revised Code Chapter 1706 (Ohio Revised Limited Liability Company Act).

1.2 **Name.** The name of the Company is ClawOps LLC. The Company may conduct business under its legal name or any assumed name properly registered with the State of Ohio.

1.3 **Principal Office.** The principal office of the Company shall be located in Cincinnati, Ohio, or at such other location as the Member may designate from time to time.

1.4 **Registered Agent.** The Company shall maintain a registered agent and registered office in the State of Ohio as required by the Ohio Revised Code.

1.5 **Duration.** The Company shall have perpetual existence unless dissolved in accordance with this Agreement or applicable law.

---

## ARTICLE 2: PURPOSE AND POWERS

2.1 **Purpose.** The Company is organized for the purpose of engaging in:

- (a) Artificial intelligence automation services, including AI receptionist solutions, conversational AI, and intelligent workflow automation
- (b) Software development, integration, and technology consulting
- (c) Revenue operations consulting and business process automation
- (d) Real estate wholesaling, acquisition, assignment, and related real estate investment activities
- (e) Any and all other lawful business activities as determined by the Member

2.2 **Powers.** The Company shall have all powers permitted under the Ohio Revised Code, including but not limited to the power to:

- (a) Enter into contracts and agreements
- (b) Own, lease, and dispose of real and personal property
- (c) Borrow money and grant security interests
- (d) Hire employees and engage independent contractors
- (e) Open and maintain bank accounts
- (f) Sue and be sued in the Company's name
- (g) Engage in any other activity necessary or incidental to the foregoing

---

## ARTICLE 3: MEMBER

3.1 **Sole Member.** The sole Member of the Company is:

| Member | Ownership Interest | Capital Contribution |
|--------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Brand Lio | 100% | $[Initial_Capital_Amount] |

3.2 **Additional Members.** Additional members may be admitted to the Company only with the written consent of the existing Member and upon execution of an amended Operating Agreement reflecting the new membership structure.

---

## ARTICLE 4: MANAGEMENT

4.1 **Member-Managed.** The Company shall be member-managed. The Member shall have full authority to manage and control the business and affairs of the Company.

4.2 **Authority.** The Member shall have the authority to:

- (a) Make all decisions regarding the business, operations, and affairs of the Company
- (b) Execute contracts, agreements, leases, and other instruments on behalf of the Company
- (c) Open and manage bank accounts and financial accounts
- (d) Hire, manage, and terminate employees and contractors
- (e) Acquire, hold, and dispose of property
- (f) Borrow money and incur obligations on behalf of the Company
- (g) Take any and all actions necessary or appropriate to carry out the purposes of the Company

4.3 **Officers.** The Member may appoint officers (such as President, Secretary, Treasurer, or other titles) to assist in the management of the Company. Officers shall serve at the pleasure of the Member and shall have such duties and authority as delegated by the Member. The appointment of officers shall not change the member-managed nature of the Company.

4.4 **Third-Party Reliance.** Any person or entity dealing with the Company may rely upon the authority of the Member (or any officer duly appointed by the Member) to bind the Company without further inquiry.

---

## ARTICLE 5: CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS

5.1 **Initial Contribution.** The Member has made an initial capital contribution to the Company in the amount of $[Initial_Capital_Amount], which may consist of cash, property, services, or a combination thereof.

5.2 **Additional Contributions.** The Member may make additional capital contributions to the Company at any time and in any amount, at the Member's sole discretion. No additional contributions are required.

5.3 **No Interest.** No interest shall be paid on capital contributions.

5.4 **Capital Account.** The Company shall maintain a capital account for the Member in accordance with applicable Treasury Regulations and generally accepted accounting principles.

5.5 **No Withdrawal of Capital.** The Member shall not withdraw any portion of the Member's capital contribution except as provided in Article 6 (Distributions) or upon dissolution of the Company.

---

## ARTICLE 6: ALLOCATIONS AND DISTRIBUTIONS

6.1 **Allocations.** All profits, losses, gains, deductions, and credits of the Company shall be allocated 100% to the Member.

6.2 **Distributions.** Distributions of available cash or property may be made to the Member at such times and in such amounts as the Member determines, in the Member's sole discretion.

6.3 **Limitations on Distributions.** No distribution shall be made if, after giving effect to the distribution:

- (a) The Company would be unable to pay its debts as they become due in the ordinary course of business; or
- (b) The Company's total assets would be less than its total liabilities.

6.4 **Tax Distributions.** The Member may cause the Company to distribute amounts sufficient to cover the Member's estimated tax liability arising from the Company's income, at such times as are reasonably necessary for the timely payment of estimated taxes.

---

## ARTICLE 7: TAX AND ACCOUNTING

7.1 **Tax Classification.** As a single-member LLC, the Company shall be treated as a disregarded entity for federal income tax purposes, unless the Member elects otherwise by filing the appropriate form with the Internal Revenue Service.

7.2 **Fiscal Year.** The fiscal year of the Company shall be the calendar year (January 1 through December 31).

7.3 **Accounting Method.** The Company shall use the cash method of accounting, unless the Member determines that the accrual method is more appropriate for the Company's operations.

7.4 **Records.** The Company shall maintain complete and accurate books and records of all Company transactions, assets, liabilities, and financial condition at its principal office.

7.5 **Tax Returns.** The Member shall be responsible for the timely filing of all tax returns required of the Company.

---

## ARTICLE 8: LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

8.1 **Limited Liability.** The Member shall not be personally liable for the debts, obligations, or liabilities of the Company solely by reason of being a member, except as otherwise provided by the Ohio Revised Code or as the Member may separately agree.

8.2 **Asset Protection.** The Company shall maintain separation between Company assets and the Member's personal assets. The Member shall:

- (a) Maintain a separate bank account for the Company
- (b) Execute all contracts and obligations in the name of the Company, not in the Member's personal name
- (c) Maintain adequate capitalization for the Company's operations
- (d) Observe all formalities required to maintain the Company's separate legal identity
- (e) Not commingle personal funds with Company funds

8.3 **Indemnification.** The Company shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Member and any officers, employees, or agents of the Company from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or incurred in connection with the Company's business, except for losses resulting from the Member's willful misconduct or gross negligence.

8.4 **Insurance.** The Company may purchase and maintain insurance on behalf of the Member and any officers, employees, or agents against liability asserted against them in their capacity as such, whether or not the Company would have the power to indemnify them against such liability.

---

## ARTICLE 9: TRANSFER OF INTERESTS

9.1 **Transfer by Member.** The Member may freely transfer, assign, or encumber all or any portion of the Member's interest in the Company.

9.2 **Admission of Transferees.** A transferee of the Member's interest shall be admitted as a member of the Company only upon the written consent of the transferring Member and execution of an amended Operating Agreement.

---

## ARTICLE 10: DISSOLUTION AND WINDING UP

10.1 **Events of Dissolution.** The Company shall be dissolved upon the occurrence of any of the following events:

- (a) The written decision of the Member to dissolve the Company
- (b) The entry of a decree of judicial dissolution under the Ohio Revised Code
- (c) Any event that makes it unlawful for the Company to continue its business
- (d) The death or incapacity of the Member, unless the Member's estate, personal representative, or successor elects to continue the Company within ninety (90) days

10.2 **Winding Up.** Upon dissolution, the Company's affairs shall be wound up by the Member (or the Member's designee, estate, or personal representative, as applicable). The winding up shall include:

- (a) Collecting all assets of the Company
- (b) Paying or making provision for all debts, liabilities, and obligations of the Company
- (c) Distributing remaining assets to the Member

10.3 **Order of Distribution.** Upon winding up, the Company's assets shall be distributed in the following order:

- (a) First, to creditors of the Company (including the Member to the extent the Member is a creditor), in satisfaction of the Company's debts and liabilities
- (b) Second, to the establishment of any reserves the Member deems necessary for contingent or unforeseen liabilities
- (c) Third, to the Member in respect of the Member's capital account balance and membership interest

10.4 **Articles of Dissolution.** Upon completion of winding up, the Member (or authorized representative) shall file Articles of Dissolution with the Ohio Secretary of State.

---

## ARTICLE 11: MISCELLANEOUS

11.1 **Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Ohio, specifically the Ohio Revised Limited Liability Company Act (Ohio Revised Code Chapter 1706).

11.2 **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement of the Member with respect to the Company and supersedes all prior agreements, whether written or oral.

11.3 **Amendments.** This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by the Member.

11.4 **Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

11.5 **Headings.** The headings in this Agreement are for convenience only and shall not affect the interpretation of this Agreement.

11.6 **Binding Effect.** This Agreement shall be binding upon and inure to the benefit of the Member and the Member's heirs, executors, administrators, successors, and assigns.
