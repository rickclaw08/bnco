Got questions? We have answers.

OpenClaw is an open-source AI agent framework that lets you run autonomous agents on your own hardware. These agents can browse the web, write code, manage files, send messages, and integrate with your existing tools. AutoPilot is our managed service that handles the setup and optimization so you do not have to.
Not at all. The entire point of AutoPilot is that we handle the technical side. For the Free tier you will follow our step-by-step guide. For Pro and Enterprise, our team does everything: installation, configuration, optimization, and ongoing maintenance.
Pro gives you a single managed agent with monthly optimization and email support. Enterprise unlocks multi-agent orchestration, custom workflow development, priority support with 4-hour response times, weekly strategy calls, and a dedicated account manager. Enterprise is built for teams that need multiple agents working together on complex tasks.
Yes. You can upgrade or downgrade at any time. Changes take effect at the start of your next billing cycle. If you upgrade mid-cycle, we will prorate the difference.
Your agents run on your own hardware or cloud infrastructure. OpenClaw is self-hosted, which means your data stays on your machines. We manage the setup and configuration remotely, but the actual agent processes run wherever you choose to host them.
No contracts, no commitments. AutoPilot is month-to-month. Cancel anytime and your agents keep running with the configuration we set up. You just will not have ongoing managed support and optimization after cancellation.
OpenClaw agents can integrate with virtually any tool that has an API or web interface. Common integrations include Slack, Discord, Telegram, Gmail, Google Calendar, GitHub, Notion, Trello, Zapier, and hundreds more. On the Enterprise plan, we build custom integrations tailored to your specific tech stack.
Security is foundational to everything we build. Since your agents run on your own infrastructure, your data never leaves your control. We use encrypted communication for all remote management, follow least-privilege access principles, and provide regular security audits for Enterprise customers.
