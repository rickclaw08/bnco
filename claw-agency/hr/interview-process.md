# ClawOps Interview Process and Scoring Rubric

## Overview

Our interview process is designed to be efficient, respectful of candidates' time, and effective at identifying people who will thrive at ClawOps. The entire process should take no more than 2 weeks from first contact to decision.

---

## Process Stages

### Stage 1: Application Review (Same day or next day)

**Time:** 5-10 minutes per candidate
**Who:** Hiring lead

**Review:**
- Resume/CV for relevant experience
- Portfolio, GitHub, or work samples
- Cover note or application message

**Decision:** Advance / Reject / Hold
**Goal:** Filter to top 20-30% of applicants

---

### Stage 2: Async Screen (Candidate spends 20-30 min)

**Time:** 24-48 hour turnaround
**Who:** Hiring lead

Send the candidate a role-specific async prompt via email. They respond in writing.

**Prompts by Role:**

**AI Engineer:**
"Describe how you would build a customer support chatbot that can access a company's knowledge base of 500+ documents. Include your architecture choices, tools, and how you would handle questions the system cannot answer. Keep it under 500 words."

**Automation Specialist:**
"A client receives 50+ invoices per day via email in various formats (PDF, image, plain text). They want these automatically extracted, categorized, and entered into their accounting system. Describe your approach, tools, and how you would handle errors. Keep it under 500 words."

**Sales Rep:**
"A mid-size e-commerce company tells you they are 'interested in AI but not sure where to start.' Walk us through how you would handle that discovery conversation and what questions you would ask. Keep it under 500 words."

**Evaluation:** Score using rubric below. Advance candidates scoring 3+ average.

---

### Stage 3: Live Interview (45-60 min)

**Time:** Scheduled within 1 week of async screen
**Who:** Hiring lead + one team member

**Structure:**

| Segment | Duration | Focus |
|---------|----------|-------|
| Intro and rapport | 5 min | Make them comfortable, explain ClawOps |
| Background deep-dive | 15 min | Walk through relevant experience |
| Technical/role exercise | 20 min | Role-specific (see below) |
| Culture and values | 10 min | Alignment with how we work |
| Candidate questions | 10 min | Let them interview us |

**Technical Exercises:**

**AI Engineer:**
- Walk through a past AI project in detail (architecture decisions, challenges, outcomes)
- Live problem: "Here is a failing LLM prompt. How would you debug and improve it?" (share screen, think aloud)

**Automation Specialist:**
- Walk through a complex automation they built
- Live problem: "Here is a broken n8n/Make workflow. What is wrong and how would you fix it?"

**Sales Rep:**
- Role-play: Conduct a 10-minute discovery call (interviewer plays the prospect)
- Discuss how they handle objections and lost deals

---

### Stage 4: Paid Trial Project (For contractors/significant hires)

**Time:** 3-5 hours of work, 1 week to complete
**Compensation:** Full rate for their time

Assign a real but non-critical task from the current pipeline. Evaluate:
- Quality of output
- Communication during the project (questions asked, updates given)
- Timeliness
- How they handle ambiguity

---

### Stage 5: Reference Check

**Time:** 15 min per reference, 1-2 references
**Who:** Hiring lead

**Questions:**
1. How did you work with [candidate]? What was the project?
2. What were their biggest strengths?
3. What areas could they improve?
4. How did they handle challenges or disagreements?
5. Would you hire or work with them again?

---

## Scoring Rubric

Rate each dimension 1-5:

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Does not meet expectations |
| 2 | Below expectations |
| 3 | Meets expectations |
| 4 | Exceeds expectations |
| 5 | Exceptional |

### Technical Roles (AI Engineer, Automation Specialist)

| Dimension | Weight | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Technical skill | 25% | Depth of knowledge, problem-solving ability |
| Relevant experience | 20% | Past projects and their complexity/impact |
| Communication | 20% | Clarity, responsiveness, ability to explain concepts |
| Independence | 15% | Self-directed, asks good questions, manages own work |
| Culture fit | 10% | Alignment with ClawOps values and work style |
| Growth potential | 10% | Curiosity, learning speed, ambition |

### Sales Role

| Dimension | Weight | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Sales ability | 25% | Discovery, presentation, closing skills |
| Technical understanding | 20% | Can they explain AI/automation to non-technical buyers? |
| Communication | 20% | Clarity, persuasion, listening skills |
| Drive and motivation | 15% | Self-starter, track record of results |
| Culture fit | 10% | Alignment with ClawOps values and work style |
| Network and reach | 10% | Existing connections, industry knowledge |

---

## Decision Framework

| Weighted Average | Recommendation |
|-----------------|----------------|
| 4.0+ | Strong hire. Move fast. |
| 3.5 - 3.9 | Hire if no red flags. |
| 3.0 - 3.4 | Consider with reservations. Discuss as a team. |
| Below 3.0 | Do not advance. |

## Communication Standards

- Respond to all applicants within 48 hours
- Rejected candidates get a respectful, brief email (not ghosted)
- Provide feedback to candidates who made it to Stage 3+
- Keep candidates informed of timeline at every stage
