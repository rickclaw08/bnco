# Contractor Sourcing Guide

## Overview

This guide covers our process for finding, vetting, and onboarding freelance talent to support ClawOps client projects. As an AI-first agency, we leverage human contractors for specialized work that requires domain expertise, creative judgment, or tasks outside our current automation capabilities.

## Sourcing Platforms

### Development Talent

**Upwork**
- Best for: Full-stack developers, specialized backend work, mobile development
- Rate range: $40-150/hour depending on experience and location
- Vetting: Review portfolio, conduct technical screening, start with small test project
- Pros: Large talent pool, built-in time tracking, payment protection
- Cons: Platform fees (10-20%), variable quality

**Toptal**
- Best for: Senior developers, complex architecture work, emergency support
- Rate range: $80-200/hour
- Vetting: Pre-vetted by platform (top 3% claim), still conduct internal review
- Pros: High quality baseline, fast onboarding
- Cons: Premium pricing, less flexibility on contracts

**Gun.io**
- Best for: Vetted freelance developers, project-based work
- Rate range: $75-175/hour
- Vetting: Pre-screened developers, review work history
- Pros: Quality over quantity, good for urgent needs
- Cons: Smaller pool than Upwork

**GitHub Jobs / Dev Communities**
- Best for: Niche technical skills, open source contributors
- Rate range: Negotiable, typically $60-140/hour
- Vetting: Review GitHub activity, contributions, technical blog posts
- Pros: Find passionate specialists, often cutting-edge skills
- Cons: More work to source, less standardized process

### Design Talent

**Dribbble**
- Best for: UI/UX designers, visual identity, web design
- Rate range: $50-120/hour
- Vetting: Portfolio review, design challenge (paid), check client references
- Pros: High-quality creative work, strong portfolios
- Cons: Many designers prefer project rates over hourly

**Behance**
- Best for: Brand designers, motion graphics, illustration
- Rate range: $45-110/hour
- Vetting: Portfolio depth, case study review, style alignment
- Pros: Free to browse, diverse styles, Adobe integration
- Cons: Not transaction-focused, need separate contract process

**Contra**
- Best for: Independent creatives, flexible arrangements
- Rate range: $50-130/hour
- Vetting: Profile review, intro call, trial project
- Pros: No platform fees, commission-free
- Cons: Smaller network, less protection infrastructure

### Copywriting & Content

**Contently**
- Best for: Long-form content, thought leadership, technical writing
- Rate range: $0.50-2.00/word or $75-150/hour
- Vetting: Writing samples, domain expertise check, editing review
- Pros: Professional writer network, content management tools
- Cons: Platform markup, primarily content marketing focused

**ClearVoice**
- Best for: Blog content, SEO writing, social media copy
- Rate range: $0.30-1.50/word
- Vetting: Sample review, SEO knowledge test, tone matching
- Pros: Fast turnaround, good for volume content
- Cons: Quality varies, may need heavy editing

**Direct Outreach (LinkedIn, Twitter)**
- Best for: Niche B2B writers, technical subject matter experts
- Rate range: Negotiable, $100-200/hour for specialists
- Vetting: Published work review, writing test (paid), reference check
- Pros: Find exact expertise needed, build long-term relationships
- Cons: Time-intensive sourcing, custom contracts required

## Rate Guidelines

### When to Pay Premium (Top 25%)

- Tight deadlines or complex requirements
- Specialized technical knowledge required
- High-stakes client projects
- Long-term strategic work

### When to Use Mid-Range (50th Percentile)

- Standard web development or design
- Content with clear briefs and templates
- Defined scope with good documentation
- Repeat project types

### When to Negotiate Lower

- Junior-friendly tasks with learning opportunity
- Large volume commitments
- Long-term retainer arrangements
- Geographic arbitrage (respectfully)

## Vetting Process

### Phase 1: Initial Screen (15 minutes)

**Review:**
- Portfolio or work samples
- Client reviews and ratings
- Communication quality in initial message
- Availability and timezone fit

**Red flags:**
- Generic proposals that ignore project details
- Unrealistic timelines or promises
- Poor written communication
- No verifiable past work

### Phase 2: Interview (30-45 minutes)

**Assess:**
- Technical or creative approach to our specific need
- Problem-solving methodology
- Communication style and responsiveness
- Cultural fit with ClawOps values (efficiency, transparency, quality)

**Questions to ask:**
- "Walk me through your process for [relevant task type]"
- "How do you handle feedback and revisions?"
- "What's your typical turnaround time for [deliverable]?"
- "Tell me about a challenging project and how you solved it"

### Phase 3: Paid Test Project

**Structure:**
- Small, realistic task (2-5 hours of work)
- Clear deliverables and success criteria
- Fair compensation at proposed rate
- Defined deadline

**Evaluate:**
- Quality of deliverable
- Timeliness and communication
- Attention to requirements
- Proactive problem-solving

**Decision point:**
- Pass: Proceed to project contract
- Conditional: Provide feedback, offer revision opportunity
- No fit: Polite decline with constructive feedback

## Onboarding Contractors

### Documentation Required

- W-9 or W-8BEN (for tax reporting)
- Signed contractor agreement (use ClawOps standard template)
- NDA if working with sensitive client data
- Payment details (PayPal, bank transfer, or platform payment)

### Access & Tools

**Provide:**
- Project brief and context
- Relevant brand assets or design systems
- Communication channel (Slack guest, email thread, or platform)
- File sharing access (Dropbox, Google Drive, GitHub)

**Do NOT provide:**
- Access to internal systems beyond project scope
- Client contact information without approval
- Unnecessary sensitive information

### Kickoff Checklist

- [ ] Share project brief and requirements document
- [ ] Clarify deliverables and timeline
- [ ] Establish communication cadence (daily updates, weekly calls, etc.)
- [ ] Define review/feedback process
- [ ] Set milestones and payment schedule
- [ ] Introduce to relevant ClawOps agent or point of contact

## Managing Contractors

### Communication Best Practices

- Set clear expectations on response times
- Use async communication by default (respect their schedule)
- Provide consolidated feedback (avoid dribs and drabs)
- Be specific and actionable in revision requests
- Acknowledge good work and timely delivery

### Quality Control

- Review work promptly (don't bottleneck their progress)
- Use structured feedback templates
- Track revision rounds (2-3 is normal, more indicates scope/brief issues)
- Document patterns for future briefs

### Payment

- Pay on time, every time (builds reputation and loyalty)
- Use platform protection when available
- For direct contracts, use milestone-based payments
- Keep records of all invoices and payments

## Building a Bench

### Maintain a Talent Pool

**Track contractors in Airtable/Notion:**
- Name and contact info
- Skills and specialties
- Hourly rate and availability
- Past project performance scores
- Preferred project types
- Timezone and language

**Stay in touch:**
- Quarterly check-ins with top performers
- Share relevant opportunities even if not immediate
- Provide testimonials or LinkedIn recommendations
- Consider retainer arrangements for consistent needs

### Scaling Relationships

**From project to retainer:**
- After 3-5 successful projects, discuss ongoing arrangements
- Offer guaranteed hours for rate discount
- First priority on new projects
- Deeper integration with ClawOps processes

## Red Flags to Watch

**During project:**
- Missed deadlines without proactive communication
- Defensive response to feedback
- Scope creep or unapproved additional charges
- Quality decline after initial test project
- Unresponsiveness or communication blackouts

**Action steps:**
- Address issues directly and immediately
- Document concerns
- Set clear improvement expectations
- If unresolved, exit relationship professionally

## Platform-Specific Tips

### Upwork

- Use milestones for fixed-price projects
- Weekly billing limits for hourly work
- Monitor time tracking screenshots if concerned
- Leave detailed reviews to build our company reputation

### Direct Contracts

- Use standard ClawOps contractor agreement (legal-reviewed)
- Consider contractor insurance requirements for large projects
- Net 15 or Net 30 payment terms
- Include IP assignment clauses

## Diversity & Inclusion

**Actively seek:**
- Geographic diversity (global talent pool)
- Underrepresented groups in tech/creative fields
- Non-traditional backgrounds and skill paths
- Junior talent with high potential

**Create inclusive process:**
- Skills-based evaluation over credentials
- Flexible working arrangements
- Clear, jargon-free job descriptions
- Bias check on requirements (is that really needed?)

## Metrics to Track

- Time to fill (posting to contract signed)
- First project success rate
- Repeat engagement rate
- Average contractor rating (our rating of them)
- Cost per project type
- Diversity metrics
