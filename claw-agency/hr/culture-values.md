# ClawOps Culture and Values

## Who We Are

ClawOps is a lean, remote-first AI automation agency. We build intelligent systems that help businesses work smarter. Our team is small by design and excellent by choice. We move fast, ship real products, and take pride in work that actually works.

---

## Our Values

### 1. Ship It
We bias toward action. Perfect is the enemy of done. Get it working, get it in front of the client, then iterate. A working prototype today beats a polished deck next week.

### 2. Own Your Work
Every person at ClawOps is responsible for their output. No one is going to check your homework. If you see a problem, fix it. If you are stuck, speak up. Ownership means both pride in your wins and accountability for your misses.

### 3. Stay Curious
AI moves fast. What worked last month might be obsolete next month. We expect everyone to stay sharp, experiment with new tools, and bring ideas to the table. Learning is not optional; it is part of the job.

### 4. Communicate Clearly
We are remote and async-first. That means your writing matters. Say what you mean. Be direct. Update people before they have to ask. Over-communication beats under-communication every time.

### 5. Respect the Client
Our clients trust us with their businesses. We honor that trust by delivering quality work, being honest about timelines, and never overselling what AI can do. Under-promise, over-deliver.

### 6. Keep It Simple
The best solution is usually the simplest one that works. Do not over-engineer. Do not add complexity for its own sake. If a no-code tool solves the problem, use it. Save the fancy architecture for when it is genuinely needed.

---

## How We Work

### Remote and Async-First
- We do not require fixed hours. We require results.
- Default to async communication (messages, docs, recorded videos).
- Meetings are for decisions, not updates. Keep them short and rare.
- Respect time zones. Do not expect instant replies outside working hours.

### Transparency
- Share context freely. The more everyone knows, the better we all work.
- Financial transparency with the team on project margins and company health.
- Decisions are explained, not just announced.

### Feedback
- Give feedback directly and kindly. Do not let issues fester.
- Receive feedback gracefully. It is data, not an attack.
- Regular check-ins (not micromanagement) to ensure alignment.

### Tools Over Meetings
- Use project management tools to track work.
- Document decisions in writing so anyone can catch up.
- Record important calls for those who could not attend.

---

## What We Expect From Team Members

1. **Deliver on commitments.** If you say you will do something by Friday, do it by Friday. If that changes, communicate early.

2. **Be proactive.** Do not wait to be told what to do. See a gap? Fill it. Have an idea? Share it.

3. **Help each other.** We are a small team. When someone is stuck, lend a hand. When you are stuck, ask for one.

4. **Protect the client relationship.** Every interaction with a client reflects on all of us.

5. **Keep learning.** Dedicate time each week to learning something new. Share what you learn with the team.

---

## What You Can Expect From ClawOps

1. **Fair compensation.** We pay competitive rates and share in the upside as we grow.

2. **Autonomy.** We hired you because you are good. We will not micromanage you.

3. **Growth opportunities.** As ClawOps grows, so do the opportunities for everyone on the team.

4. **Honest leadership.** We will be straight with you about where the company stands and where it is going.

5. **Respect for your time.** We will not waste it with unnecessary meetings, busywork, or politics.

---

## What We Will Not Tolerate

- Dishonesty with clients or team members
- Missing deadlines without communication
- Disrespect or hostility toward anyone
- Cutting corners on quality that affects clients
- Leaking confidential information

---

## The ClawOps Test

When making a decision, ask yourself:

1. **Does this help the client?**
2. **Would I be proud to show this work?**
3. **Am I being honest about what I know and what I do not know?**

If the answer is yes to all three, you are on the right track.
