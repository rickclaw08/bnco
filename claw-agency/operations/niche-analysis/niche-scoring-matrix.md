# Niche Scoring Matrix - Home Services Sub-Verticals

*Created: 2026-03-02 | Author: Harper (COO)*

## Evaluation Criteria

Each sub-niche scored 1-10 across five factors critical to our AI call-handling product.

| Factor | HVAC | Plumbing | General Contractors | Roofing | Electrical |
|--------|------|----------|-------------------|---------|------------|
| After-Hours Call Volume | 9 | 8 | 3 | 4 | 6 |
| Average Ticket Value | 9 | 7 | 8 | 9 | 6 |
| Call Urgency (emergency %) | 9 | 9 | 2 | 5 | 7 |
| Seasonality (predictable spikes) | 10 | 5 | 4 | 7 | 4 |
| Number of Businesses (TAM) | 9 | 8 | 10 | 6 | 7 |
| **TOTAL** | **46** | **37** | **27** | **31** | **30** |

## Why These Scores

### HVAC (Score: 46) - CLEAR WINNER

- **After-Hours Call Volume (9):** HVAC emergencies spike at night, weekends, and holidays. AC dies at 2 AM in July, furnace quits at 11 PM in January. These are the calls nobody answers.
- **Average Ticket Value (9):** Emergency HVAC repairs range $300-$1,200. System replacements run $5,000-$15,000+. A single missed emergency call averages $800-$1,500 in lost revenue when you factor in the repair plus the potential full system sale that grows from it.
- **Call Urgency (9):** Roughly 40-60% of inbound calls to HVAC companies are urgent or emergency. People don't "browse" for HVAC, they call when something breaks.
- **Seasonality (10):** The most predictable demand spikes in all of home services. Summer cooling rush (June-August) and winter heating rush (November-February). We can time our outreach perfectly.
- **TAM (9):** ~120,000+ HVAC businesses in the US. Heavily fragmented, mostly 1-20 employee shops. Perfect for our product.

### Plumbing (Score: 37) - STRONG SECONDARY

- High emergency call volume but less seasonal predictability
- Lower average ticket ($200-$800 for most calls)
- Strong 24/7 need, but less concentrated demand windows
- Good expansion niche after HVAC is locked down

### General Contractors (Score: 27) - POOR FIT

- Project-based work with long sales cycles
- Low call urgency, mostly estimate requests
- Call handling is less time-sensitive
- Harder to prove ROI per call

### Roofing (Score: 31) - MODERATE

- Storm-driven spikes create temporary call floods
- High ticket value but infrequent calls per customer
- Seasonal but unpredictable (weather-dependent, not calendar-dependent)
- Smaller total market

### Electrical (Score: 30) - MODERATE

- Mix of emergency and scheduled work
- Lower average ticket on most calls
- Less defined "pain window" than HVAC
- Decent TAM but scattered urgency patterns

## Decision

**PRIMARY NICHE: HVAC**
**SECONDARY NICHE (Phase 2): Plumbing**

HVAC wins on every dimension that matters for an AI call-handling product. The combination of high urgency, high ticket value, massive seasonal spikes, and a giant fragmented market makes it the obvious choice. It is not close.
