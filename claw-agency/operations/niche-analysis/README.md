## Executive Summary

**Decision: Go all-in on HVAC as our primary niche.**

After scoring all five home services sub-verticals across urgency, ticket value, call volume, seasonality, and market size, HVAC wins by a wide margin (46/50 vs. Plumbing's 37/50). It is not a close call.

HVAC contractors have the highest pain around missed calls because their customers call in emergencies (broken AC in July, dead furnace in January). The caller's urgency is our leverage. They will not leave a voicemail and wait until Monday. They call the next company on Google. Every missed call is $700-$1,200 in lost revenue for the shop.

## Key Numbers

- **120,000+ HVAC businesses** in the US, mostly 1-20 employee shops
- **$700-$1,200** average revenue per missed call (blended across emergency, service, and replacement)
- **40-60%** of inbound HVAC calls are urgent/emergency
- **60-80%** of after-hours callers who hit voicemail call a competitor instead of leaving a message
- **$14,000-$48,000/month** in lost revenue during peak season for a shop missing 5-10 calls/week

---

## The Moat

Nobody is doing HVAC-specific AI call handling. The market has:
- Generic AI receptionists (Smith.ai, Goodcall, Rosie AI) that don't speak HVAC
- Traditional answering services that take messages but don't book jobs
- ServiceTitan adding AI features but not dedicated call answering

Our positioning: **the AI dispatcher that speaks HVAC, books jobs into your system, and never misses a call.** Not a generic bot. Not a message-taker. A trained dispatcher that works 24/7/365.

---

## Recommended Brand: "Scale Your Shop" by ClawOps

Mirrors Wyatt's "Scale Your Clinic" model. "Shop" is the industry-native word HVAC contractors use for their business. Specific enough to resonate, broad enough to expand into plumbing later.
