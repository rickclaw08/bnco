# Brand Positioning - "Scale Your Shop" by ClawOps

*Created: 2026-03-02 | Author: Harper (COO)*

---

## The Wyatt Roderick Playbook Applied

Wyatt niched into medical clinics with "Scale Your Clinic." Single vertical, clear branding, specific language. Every piece of content, every ad, every landing page screams "we understand clinics."

We do the same for HVAC.

---

## Recommended Brand Positioning

### Primary Brand: **ClawOps - HVAC Edition** (or sub-brand: "Scale Your Shop")

**Why "Shop":**
- HVAC contractors call their business a "shop." Not a firm, not a company, not a practice. A shop.
- "Scale Your Shop" immediately signals: "these people know my world"
- It is specific enough to resonate but broad enough to eventually cover plumbing (Phase 2)
- Parallel to "Scale Your Clinic" but distinctly blue-collar

**Alternative names considered:**
- "Never Miss a Call" - too generic, doesn't signal HVAC
- "ClawOps HVAC" - functional but no personality
- "Dispatch AI" - too tech-focused, intimidating to owner-operators
- "Scale Your Shop" - WINNER. Aspirational, industry-native language

---

## Positioning Statement

**For HVAC shop owners who are losing revenue to missed calls, ClawOps is the AI dispatcher that answers every call like a trained CSR, books jobs directly into your system, and never takes a sick day. Unlike generic answering services, we speak HVAC and we work 24/7/365, especially when your phones blow up during peak season.**

## Messaging Framework

### Headline Options (for landing pages, ads, cold outreach)
1. "Stop Losing $700 Every Time Your Phone Rings and Nobody Picks Up"
2. "Your Answering Service Takes Messages. We Book Jobs."
3. "Peak Season is Coming. Is Your Phone Ready?"
4. "What If You Never Missed Another Emergency Call?"
5. "The AI Dispatcher That Speaks HVAC"

### Core Value Props (in order of impact)
1. **Never miss a call again.** Day, night, weekends, holidays, peak season overflow. Every call gets answered.
2. **Books jobs, not messages.** Integrates with ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Jobber. Puts appointments on the board.
3. **Speaks your language.** Trained on HVAC terminology, common issues, triage protocols. Not a generic bot.
4. **Flat monthly rate.** No per-call surprises when summer hits and call volume triples.
5. **ROI in the first week.** One booked emergency call covers a month of service.

### Objection Handlers
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "My customers want to talk to a real person" | "85% of callers who hit voicemail after hours just call the next company on Google. They'd rather talk to AI than talk to nobody." |
| "I already have an answering service" | "Your answering service takes a message and emails it to you at 7 AM. The customer booked with your competitor at 11 PM. We book the job on the spot." |
| "AI sounds robotic" | "Our AI is trained specifically on HVAC conversations. It asks about system type, symptoms, and urgency, then books. Most callers don't know it's AI." |
| "It's too expensive" | "You're losing $700+ per missed call. At $X/month, you need ONE recovered call to be profitable. How many calls did you miss last week?" |
| "I'm too small for this" | "You're exactly who this is for. Big shops have 3 CSRs. You have yourself and a voicemail box. We level the playing field." |

## Content Strategy Pillars

### Pillar 1: "The Cost of Missed Calls" (Problem Awareness)
- Blog posts with real math on lost revenue
- Social posts: "Your competitor answered that 9 PM call. Did you?"
- Video testimonials from HVAC owners who tracked their missed calls

### Pillar 2: "Peak Season Prep" (Seasonal Urgency)
- Timed content pushes before summer and winter
- "Is your phone ready for the first heat wave?"
- Peak season survival guides with ClawOps as the solution

### Pillar 3: "Modern Shop Operations" (Aspiration)
- Content about running a professional, scalable HVAC business
- "The difference between a $500K shop and a $2M shop is systems"
- Position ClawOps as part of the modern tech stack alongside ServiceTitan

### Pillar 4: "HVAC Owner Stories" (Social Proof)
- Case studies, testimonials, before/after metrics
- "How [Shop Name] recovered $8,000/month in missed revenue"
- Facebook group engagement with real results

## Brand Voice (HVAC-Specific)

**Do say:**
- Shop, technician, dispatcher, service call, diagnostic, system replacement
- "Your phone is your pipeline"
- "Book the call, not a message"
- "Peak season ready"

**Don't say:**
- Office, employees, clients (say customers)
- Generic SaaS language ("synergy," "leverage," "stakeholders")
- Anything that sounds like you have never been in a shop

**Tone:** Direct, no-BS, proof-focused. These are business owners who have been burned by overpromising vendors. Show the math, show the results, skip the fluff.
