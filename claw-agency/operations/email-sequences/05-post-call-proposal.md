# Post-Call Proposal Send

**When to send:** Within 2-4 hours after the discovery call. Speed matters.
**Tone:** Recap the conversation, present the solution, make it easy to say yes.

---

## Template

**Subject:** [First Name], here's the plan we discussed

Hi [First Name],

Thanks for the great conversation today. I'm excited about what we can build for [Company Name].

Here's a recap of what we discussed and our proposed solution:

**The Problem:**
[1-2 sentences summarizing their specific pain point, in their own words if possible]

**What We'll Build:**
[Bullet list of specific deliverables, tailored to what you discussed on the call]
- [Deliverable 1]
- [Deliverable 2]
- [Deliverable 3]

**Timeline:** [X weeks] from kickoff to delivery

**Investment:** $[Amount]
- 50% upfront to begin work
- 50% on delivery and your approval

**What's Included:**
- [X] rounds of revisions
- [X] days of post-launch support
- Full documentation and handoff
- [Any other relevant inclusions]

**Next Steps:**
1. Reply "let's go" (or any questions you have)
2. I'll send over the agreement and invoice
3. We kick off within [X] business days of payment

I've attached [proposal document / scope of work] with full details.

If you have any questions or want to adjust anything, just let me know. Happy to hop on another quick call if that's easier.

Looking forward to working together.

Best,
[Your Name]
ClawOps

---

## Notes

- Always customize the deliverables section based on the actual call
- Use their exact language when describing the problem
- Keep pricing simple and clear. No hidden fees, no confusing tiers
- If they mentioned budget constraints on the call, address them directly
- Send the proposal as a clean PDF attachment, not just inline text
