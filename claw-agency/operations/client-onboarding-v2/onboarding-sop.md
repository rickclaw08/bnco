# Client Onboarding SOP

**Goal:** Signed contract to project kickoff in 48 hours or less.
**Owner:** Harper (COO) | Last updated: 2026-02-21

---

## Overview

Every new client goes through 7 steps. No exceptions, no shortcuts. This SOP exists so delivery never stalls because someone forgot to set up a Slack channel or send a welcome email.

---

## Step 1: Contract Signed (Hour 0)

**Trigger:** Client signs proposal/contract (via Stripe invoice, DocuSign, or manual confirmation).

Actions:
1. Confirm payment received or payment terms locked (Net-15, deposit, etc.)
2. Log the client in the Client Tracker (Notion or Google Sheet)
   - Client name
   - Company
   - Contact email and phone
   - Project type
   - Contract value
   - Payment schedule
   - Start date
3. Assign a project lead (Brand or contractor, depending on capacity)
4. Create a project folder in Google Drive: `Clients/[ClientName]/[ProjectName]`
5. Send the Welcome Email (see template below) within 1 hour of signing

---

## Step 2: Internal Setup (Hours 0-4)

Actions:
1. Create a dedicated Slack/Discord channel: `#client-[shortname]`
2. Create the project board (Notion, Trello, or Linear) with default columns:
   - Backlog
   - In Progress
   - In Review
   - Done
3. Add the client's key details to the project board header
4. Set up a shared Google Drive folder with the client (if applicable)
5. Schedule the Kickoff Call within 24-48 hours of contract signing
6. Send the Kickoff Call calendar invite with the agenda (see template below)

---

## Step 3: Pre-Kickoff Prep (Hours 4-24)

Actions:
1. Review the signed proposal/scope of work
2. Prepare a preliminary task breakdown based on the scope
3. Identify any access, credentials, or assets needed from the client
4. Draft a "What We Need From You" checklist to present at kickoff
5. Review the client's existing systems (website, CRM, tools) if applicable
6. Prepare discovery questions specific to their project

---

## Step 4: Kickoff Call (Hours 24-48)

Actions:
1. Run the Kickoff Call using the agenda template (see below)
2. Record the call (with permission)
3. Confirm project scope, deliverables, and timeline
4. Walk through the "What We Need From You" checklist
5. Set expectations for communication cadence:
   - Weekly status updates (async, written)
   - Weekly check-in call (optional, 15-30 min)
   - Slack/email response times (within 4 business hours)
6. Agree on a go-live or delivery date
7. Confirm the client's single point of contact

---

## Step 5: Post-Kickoff (Within 4 Hours of Call)

Actions:
1. Send the Kickoff Summary Email (recap of decisions, next steps, deadlines)
2. Update the project board with confirmed tasks and deadlines
3. Request all outstanding access/credentials from the client
4. Assign first sprint tasks to the project lead
5. Set a reminder for the first status update (3-5 business days out)

---

## Step 6: First Status Update (Day 3-5)

Actions:
1. Send the first Weekly Status Update (see template below)
2. Flag any blockers or items waiting on the client
3. Confirm the project is tracking to the agreed timeline

---

## Step 7: Ongoing Delivery

Actions:
1. Weekly Status Updates every Friday by 3 PM ET
2. Weekly Report sent to the client every Friday by 5 PM ET (see template below)
3. Internal project review every Monday morning
4. Escalation protocol: if a blocker is unresolved for 48+ hours, escalate to Harper

---

## Escalation Rules

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Client unresponsive for 48+ hours | Send a follow-up email + Slack message. If no response in 24 more hours, escalate to Harper. |
| Scope creep detected | Document the request, flag it to the client as out-of-scope, propose a change order. |
| Delivery at risk | Notify Harper immediately. Replan sprint. Communicate revised timeline to client within 24 hours. |
| Quality issue found post-delivery | Acknowledge within 2 hours. Fix within 24 hours. Send a root cause summary. |
