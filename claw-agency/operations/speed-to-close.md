# Speed-to-Close Playbook - ClawOps

From first contact to signed contract in under 48 hours. Every step, every email, every document.

**Target:** Close deals in 24-48 hours  
**Success Rate Goal:** 40%+ close rate on qualified leads  
**Revenue Impact:** Fast close = fast cash flow = $100K goal achieved

---

## THE PHILOSOPHY

Speed creates urgency. Urgency creates momentum. Momentum closes deals.

The longer a deal drags, the colder it gets. We do not let deals get cold. We strike fast and we strike clean.

**Core Principles:**
1. Respond in minutes, not hours
2. Ask for the sale early and often
3. Remove every barrier to "yes"
4. Make signing frictionless
5. Assume the sale

---

## HOUR 0: FIRST CONTACT (0-15 minutes)

### Lead Source
- Inbound form fill
- Referral introduction
- Cold outreach reply
- Social DM
- Discovery call request

### Immediate Action
**Response Time:** Under 15 minutes during business hours, under 2 hours after hours

**First Response Template:**

```
Hi [First Name],

Thanks for reaching out! I just saw your message about [specific pain point/request].

Quick question: Are you looking to get this moving in the next week or two, or just exploring options?

Either way, happy to help. Want to jump on a quick 15-minute call today or tomorrow?

Here is my calendar: [Link]

Or if you prefer, just hit reply with:
1. What you are trying to automate/fix
2. Your timeline
3. Your budget range

I will send you a proposal by end of day.

[Your Name]
ClawOps
[Phone] | [Calendar]
```

**Key Moves:**
- Acknowledge specific pain point (shows you read their message)
- Qualify urgency immediately
- Offer two paths: call or async (meet them where they are)
- Promise fast turnaround
- Make next step effortless

## HOUR 1-4: DISCOVERY & QUALIFICATION

### The Discovery Call (15-30 minutes)

**Agenda:**
1. Intro (2 min): Who we are, what we do
2. Their Story (8 min): What is the problem? What have they tried? What happens if they do nothing?
3. Our Solution (5 min): How we solve it (high level, no technical weeds)
4. Logistics (5 min): Timeline, budget, decision-makers
5. Close (5 min): Next steps, proposal timeline

**Qualification Questions:**
- What is the pain level? (1-10 scale)
- What is this costing you right now? (time, money, opportunity)
- When do you need this solved by?
- What is your budget range for a solution?
- Who else needs to approve this?
- If we can solve this in [X] weeks for [Y] budget, would you move forward?

**Red Flags (Disqualify Fast):**
- No urgency ("just exploring")
- Budget too low (<$5K for standard projects)
- No decision-making authority ("I need to check with...")
- Vague problem description (they do not know what they want)
- Previous bad agency experience (high maintenance risk)

**Green Lights (Fast Track):**
- Clear, specific pain point
- Timeline under 30 days
- Budget aligned ($10K+)
- Decision-maker on the call
- Past success with automation
- Referral from existing client

**End of Call Close:**
"Based on what you shared, I am confident we can solve this. I will send you a proposal by [specific time today/tomorrow]. If it looks good, we can kick off as early as [date]. Sound good?"

## HOUR 4-12: PROPOSAL CREATION & SEND

### Proposal Document Structure

**File Format:** PDF, branded, professional  
**Length:** 3-5 pages max (nobody reads long proposals)  
**Sections:**

#### 1. COVER PAGE
- Their company name
- Project title
- "Prepared for [Name] by ClawOps"
- Date
- Proposal valid for 7 days (creates urgency)

#### 2. THE PROBLEM (1/2 page)
Restate their pain in their own words.

"You are currently spending 20+ hours per week on manual data entry. This is costing you approximately $2,000/month in labor and causing frequent errors that damage customer relationships."

#### 3. THE SOLUTION (1 page)
What we will build. Be specific but not technical.

"We will build a custom AI-powered automation system that:
- Extracts data from [source] automatically
- Validates and cleans data using AI
- Inputs data into [system] without human intervention
- Sends alerts for exceptions
- Reduces manual work by 90%"

#### 4. DELIVERABLES (1/2 page)
Exactly what they get.

- Custom automation workflows
- Integration with [systems]
- Dashboard for monitoring
- Documentation
- 2 weeks of post-launch support
- Training for your team

#### 5. TIMELINE (1/4 page)
Week-by-week breakdown.

- Week 1: Discovery & Setup
- Week 2-3: Development
- Week 4: Testing & Launch

Launch Date: [Specific Date]

#### 6. INVESTMENT (1/2 page)
Pricing, clear and simple.

**Project Fee:** $[Amount]  
**Payment Terms:** 50% to start, 50% on completion  
**What is Included:** [List]  
**What is NOT Included:** [List, be clear]

**Optional Add-Ons:**
- [Add-on 1]: $[Price]
- [Add-on 2]: $[Price]

#### 7. NEXT STEPS (1/4 page)
Make it dead simple to say yes.

"To get started:
1. Reply 'YES' to this email
2. Sign the attached agreement (5 minutes)
3. Submit 50% deposit via [payment link]

We will kick off within 24 hours of receiving your signed agreement."

#### 8. GUARANTEE (1/4 page)
Remove risk.

"If we do not deliver what is outlined in this proposal, you get a full refund. No questions asked."

## HOUR 12-24: FOLLOW-UP & OBJECTION HANDLING

### Follow-Up Sequence

**4 Hours After Proposal:**  
Slack/Text (if you have their number):

"Hey [First Name], just sent over the proposal. Let me know if you have any questions!"

**12 Hours After Proposal:**  
Email:

**Subject:** Quick Question on the Proposal

**Body:**

```
Hi [First Name],

Just checking in on the proposal I sent earlier.

Do you have any questions or concerns I can address?

Happy to hop on a quick call if easier: [Calendar Link]

[Your Name]
```

**24 Hours After Proposal:**  
Phone Call + Voicemail:

"Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] from ClawOps. I sent over the proposal for [Project Name] and wanted to see if you had any questions. Give me a call back at [Phone] or just reply to my email. Thanks!"

**48 Hours After Proposal:**  
Final Email:

**Subject:** [First Name], Should I Close Your File?

**Body:**

```
Hi [First Name],

I have not heard back from you on the proposal for [Project Name].

Should I assume you are not interested right now, or are you still considering it?

If timing is an issue, we can adjust the start date. If budget is a concern, we can explore options.

Just let me know either way so I can plan our team's capacity.

[Your Name]
```

This is a breakup email. It either gets a response or you move on.

## COMMON OBJECTIONS & RESPONSES

### "I need to think about it."

**Response:**  
"Totally understand. What specifically do you need to think through? Budget, timeline, or something about the solution itself?"

(Uncover the real objection, then address it.)

### "I need to run this by my [boss/partner/team]."

**Response:**  
"Makes sense. What concerns do you think they will have? I am happy to jump on a call with them, or I can send you a one-pager you can share."

(Offer to be part of the conversation.)

### "The price is too high."

**Response:**  
"I get it. Just so I understand, is the price completely out of reach, or is it more about timing/payment terms?

If payment terms help, we can break it into 3 installments instead of 2. Or we can reduce scope and start with [smaller version] for $[lower amount]."

(Give them a path to yes, even if it is smaller.)

### "Can you send me some references/case studies?"

**Response:**  
"Absolutely. I will send those over in the next hour. But quick question: If the references check out, are you ready to move forward? Just want to make sure we are aligned."

(Always confirm the real decision criteria.)

### "We are evaluating other agencies."

**Response:**  
"Smart move. What are the other agencies proposing, and what is most important to you in making the decision? I want to make sure you are comparing apples to apples."

(Position yourself as a consultant, not a vendor.)

## HOUR 24-48: CLOSING THE DEAL

### The Close Call

If they have not signed after 24 hours, get them on the phone.

**Script:**

"Hi [First Name], thanks for hopping on. I know you are busy, so I will keep this quick.

I sent over the proposal [X] hours ago. I am curious, what is holding you back from moving forward?

[Listen]

[Address objection]

Here is what I suggest: Let us get you on the calendar to kick off [specific date]. Worst case, if something changes, we can adjust. But if we wait too long, that timeline slips and you are stuck with [their current pain] for another month.

Does [start date] work for you?"

**Assumptive Close:**  
"Great! I am going to send you the agreement and payment link right now. Once those are in, we will kick off [date]. Sound good?"

## SIGNATURE & PAYMENT PROCESS

### Tools
- **E-Signature:** DocuSign, PandaDoc, HelloSign
- **Payment:** Stripe, PayPal, ACH transfer, wire

### Agreement Document Checklist
- Scope of work (clear deliverables)
- Timeline (start/end dates)
- Payment terms (50/50, 30/30/40, etc.)
- What is included / not included
- Cancellation/refund policy
- Signatures

### Payment Flow
1. Client signs agreement
2. Payment link auto-sent (or included in agreement)
3. Payment received
4. Kickoff email auto-triggered
5. Client added to Slack/portal
6. Project officially starts

**Speed Matters:**  
Process signatures and payments the same day. Do not let deals sit "pending" overnight.
