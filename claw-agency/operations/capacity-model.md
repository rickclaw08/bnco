# Capacity Planning Model - ClawOps

How many clients can we handle? What breaks first? When do we hire?

**Purpose:** Know our limits before we hit them. Scale smart, not chaotic.

---

## BOTTLENECK ANALYSIS

### Where We Break First

**1. Development Time (PRIMARY BOTTLENECK)**
- Lead developer is the constraint
- Complex builds require deep focus time
- Context switching kills productivity
- Mitigation: Templatize common workflows, hire contractor for overflow

**2. Client Communication**
- Discovery calls, updates, revisions
- Each client needs 2-4 hours/week of sync time
- Mitigation: Async-first communication, client portal, batched updates

**3. Sales/Onboarding**
- CEO time spent on sales is time not spent on delivery
- Each deal requires 5-10 hours (discovery, proposal, negotiation)
- Mitigation: Templatize sales process, hire part-time BDR at $3K/month

**4. Quality Assurance**
- Testing takes time, rushing causes bugs
- Bugs cause support load
- Mitigation: Automated testing frameworks, QA checklist

## CAPACITY THRESHOLDS

### Phase 1: Founders Only (Months 1-3)
- **Max Revenue:** $50K-$75K/month
- **Max Clients:** 3-5 active projects
- **Signs of Strain:**
  - Missed deadlines
  - Late-night work becoming norm
  - Client communication delays
  - No time for sales

**Action:** Do not take more than 4 active projects until you hire.

---

### Phase 2: First Hire (Months 3-6)
**Hire:** Junior Developer OR Project Manager  
**Cost:** $5K-$8K/month (contractor or part-time)  
**Impact:** +50% delivery capacity  
**New Max Revenue:** $75K-$125K/month  
**New Max Clients:** 5-8 active projects

**Why PM over Developer?**
- PM frees up founder time for sales and high-level delivery
- PM handles client comms, updates, project tracking
- Founder dev stays focused on building

**Why Developer over PM?**
- If sales pipeline is full and delivery is the bottleneck
- Founder is strong at client management
- Technical work is piling up

**Decision Rule:** Hire PM first if founders are spending >50% of time on client comms. Hire developer first if technical backlog is >4 weeks.

---

**Team:**
- 2 Founders

**Total Team Cost:** $20K-$30K/month

## HIRING TRIGGERS

### When to Hire Developer #2
- Consistent 4+ week technical backlog
- Founders working 60+ hour weeks
- Declining project quality
- Turning down deals due to capacity

**Hire:** Full-time or contractor, $6K-$10K/month

---

### When to Hire Project Manager
- 5+ active clients
- Client communication taking >20 hours/week
- Founder time split <50% on delivery
- Missing client updates/deadlines

**Hire:** Part-time or full-time, $4K-$7K/month

---

### When to Hire Sales/BDR
- Inbound pipeline drying up
- Founders spending <10 hours/week on sales
- Deal close rate dropping below 30%
- Need to hit $100K+ consistently

**Hire:** Commission-based or part-time, $3K base + 10% commission

## RISK MITIGATION

### What Happens If We Overcommit?
1. Missed deadlines
2. Rushed, buggy work
3. Unhappy clients
4. Refund requests
5. Bad reviews
6. Burned-out team

**Prevention:**
- Track hours per project religiously
- Say no to deals that exceed capacity
- Build 20% buffer into all timelines
- Outsource overflow work to trusted contractors

---

## OUTSOURCING STRATEGY

### When to Outsource
- Overflow work (above capacity)
- Non-core tasks (design, copywriting, QA)
- One-time specialized skills (Zapier expert, Airtable consultant)

### Trusted Contractor Pool
Build a bench of 3-5 contractors for:
- Frontend dev
- Backend dev
- Automation specialists (Make, Zapier, n8n)
- QA testers
- Designers

**Rates:** $50-$100/hour depending on skill level  
**Usage:** Pay only when needed, no fixed costs

---

## THE CAPACITY RULE

**Never exceed 80% capacity for more than 2 weeks in a row.**

If you are consistently at 80%+ utilization, it is time to hire or turn down deals.

Staying at 100% capacity is a recipe for burnout and bad work.
