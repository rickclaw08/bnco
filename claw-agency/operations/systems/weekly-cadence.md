# ClawOps Weekly Operations Cadence

## Philosophy

Consistency beats intensity. We do a little bit of operations every day so nothing becomes overwhelming. This cadence keeps us aligned, proactive, and learning continuously.

---

## Monday: Plan & Align

**Time Block: 9:00 AM - 10:00 AM EST**

### Team Standup (30 min)
*Location: Slack #operations or Google Meet*

**Format:**
- What did you ship last week?
- What are you tackling this week?
- Any blockers or dependencies?
- Celebration: One win from last week (personal or professional)

**Keep it moving:**
- 3-5 minutes per person max
- Blockers get noted, detailed discussion happens after
- Use a timer (seriously)

### Weekly Planning (30 min)
*Solo work in Notion*

**Everyone does:**
1. Review your Notion task list
2. Set 3-5 priorities for the week (not 20, be honest)
3. Block calendar time for deep work
4. Flag anything that might slip
5. Update project statuses

**COO (Harper) does additionally:**
- Review all active projects
- Check resource allocation (who's overloaded?)
- Identify risks for the week
- Prepare agenda for Wednesday ops review

---

## Tuesday: Execute & Support

**No standing meetings.** Full day for execution.

### Client Check-in Window (anytime)
- Respond to all client messages from yesterday
- Post updates in client Slack channels
- Quick 15-min sync calls if needed (but async preferred)

### Time Tracking Checkpoint (end of day)
**5 min task:**
- Review today's time entries in Clockify
- Clean up descriptions
- Verify project codes are correct
- Stop any running timers you forgot about

---

## Wednesday: Review & Adjust

**Time Block: 2:00 PM - 3:00 PM EST**

### Operations Review (45 min)
*COO leads, all team members join*
*Location: Slack thread or quick Google Meet*

**Agenda:**
1. **Project health checks** (15 min)
   - Green/Yellow/Red status on each active project
   - Anything going off track?
   - Client satisfaction pulse check

2. **Pipeline & capacity** (15 min)
   - New leads or opportunities?
   - Current utilization (are we overbooked?)
   - Upcoming project starts/ends

3. **Blockers & escalations** (10 min)
   - What's stuck and needs help?
   - Any client issues brewing?
   - Tool or process problems?

4. **Process improvement** (5 min)
   - One thing that's working well
   - One thing to try differently

**Output:** Update Notion project statuses, create action items

### Admin Time (15 min)
*Solo work*
- Pay any bills or invoices
- File any paperwork
- Respond to administrative emails
- Update financial tracking

---

## Thursday: Execute & Communicate

**No standing meetings.** Full day for execution.

### Client Communication Day
**Theme: Proactive updates, not reactive firefighting**

**Morning (1 hour):**
- Draft status updates for all active projects
- Prepare any demos or materials needed
- Review upcoming client meetings this week

**Afternoon (ongoing):**
- Send updates to clients
- Schedule next check-ins
- Update client-facing Notion pages (if applicable)

### Documentation Hour (3:00 PM - 4:00 PM)
**Pick one:**
- Update an SOP that's outdated
- Document a new process you figured out
- Write a technical guide for a repeated task
- Create a template for something you do often

*Don't skip this. Future you will be grateful.*

---

## Friday: Review, Reflect, Wrap

**Time Block: 3:00 PM - 5:00 PM EST**

### Weekly Review & Retro (1 hour)
*All team members*
*Location: Slack thread (async) or Google Meet*

**Format:**

1. **Wins of the week** (10 min)
   - What shipped?
   - What went better than expected?
   - Shout-outs to team members

2. **Metrics review** (15 min)
   - Utilization rate this week
   - Project progress vs plan
   - Client sentiment
   - Revenue vs target

3. **Lessons learned** (15 min)
   - What didn't go as planned?
   - What would we do differently?
   - What surprised us?

4. **Next week preview** (10 min)
   - Major milestones coming
   - Client meetings scheduled
   - Resource constraints?

5. **Process check** (10 min)
   - Is this cadence working?
   - Any tools or workflows to adjust?
   - New needs emerging?

**Output:** Update Notion weekly review page, action items for next week

### Friday Shutdown Ritual (30 min)
**Everyone does individually:**

1. **Close out time tracking**
   - Review the whole week in Clockify
   - Clean up any messy entries
   - Verify totals make sense
   - Note any unbillable time patterns

2. **Inbox zero attempt**
   - Clear Slack notifications
   - Respond to or snooze emails
   - Archive or delete as needed

3. **Notion cleanup**
   - Mark completed tasks as Done
   - Archive finished projects
   - Create next week's task list
   - Update any stale information

4. **Brain dump**
   - Write down anything on your mind for Monday
   - Clear your head for the weekend
   - Note any personal items to handle

**Celebrate.** Week done. No work after 5pm Friday.

## Daily Habits (All Days)

### Morning Routine (15 min)
**Before diving into work:**
1. Check Slack for urgent messages
2. Review calendar for today
3. Check Notion for today's priorities
4. Start Clockify timer for first task

### End of Day Routine (10 min)
**Before logging off:**
1. Stop all Clockify timers
2. Update task statuses in Notion
3. Note tomorrow's top 3 priorities
4. Clear desk (physical or digital)

### Client Response Time Targets
- **Urgent/blocking**: Within 2 hours during business hours
- **Normal requests**: Within 24 hours
- **Non-urgent**: Within 48 hours

---

## Monthly Rhythms

### First Monday of Month
**Strategic Planning (1 hour)**
- Review last month's performance
- Set goals for current month
- Adjust resource allocation
- Check on quarterly objectives

### Mid-Month Check (15th-ish)
**Pulse Check (30 min)**
- Are we on track for monthly goals?
- Any course corrections needed?
- Client renewal conversations starting?

### Last Friday of Month
**Financial Close (1 hour)**
- Review revenue vs target
- Reconcile expenses
- Export reports from Clockify
- Prepare invoices for clients
- Update financial tracking in Notion

---

## Quarterly Rhythms

### End of Quarter
**Strategic Review (Half day)**
- Full retrospective on the quarter
- Client satisfaction surveys
- Team feedback session
- Update annual plan
- Plan next quarter's priorities
- Tool evaluation: still using the right stack?

---

## Holiday Weeks & Time Off

**Adjust, don't skip:**
- Move Monday planning to Tuesday if needed
- Skip or shorten Wednesday ops review
- Friday review can be async in Slack
- Always do time tracking cleanup (financial hygiene)

**Coverage plan:**
- Designate backup for client communication
- Clear OOO messages in Slack and email
- Hand off urgent items before leaving
- Update project statuses so others can pick up

---

## Red Flags (When to Break the Cadence)

**Call an emergency huddle if:**
- Client is about to churn
- Project is going off rails
- Team member is burning out
- Major opportunity needs quick decision
- Security incident or data breach
- Tool failure blocking work

**Don't wait for Wednesday. Address immediately.**

---

## Making This Work

### First Month: Learning
- Follow this religiously
- Take notes on what feels awkward
- Give it a real shot before changing
- Expect it to feel like overhead at first

### After Month 1: Tuning
- Adjust meeting times to fit team energy
- Modify format based on what's useful
- Keep what works, drop what doesn't
- Update this document with changes

### Long Term: Habits
- These rhythms should become automatic
- Reduce formality as habits form
- Keep the spirit even if format changes
- Revisit quarterly to prevent drift

---

## Why This Cadence?

**Monday**: Start strong and aligned  
**Tuesday**: Long execution day  
**Wednesday**: Mid-week correction point  
**Thursday**: Communication and documentation  
**Friday**: Close clean and learn  

**The pattern:**
- Plan → Execute → Review → Execute → Reflect
- Two full execution days
- Three checkpoints (Mon, Wed, Fri)
- Built-in reflection time
- No meeting-heavy days

---

## Tools Reference

- **Notion**: Planning, task management, documentation
- **Slack**: Standups, quick syncs, daily communication
- **Clockify**: Time tracking and review
- **Google Meet**: Video calls when needed
- **Google Calendar**: Scheduling and reminders

---

## Success Metrics

**This cadence is working if:**
- Projects rarely slip without warning
- Team feels aligned and informed
- Clients feel proactively managed
- Issues get caught early
- We're learning and improving weekly
- Work doesn't bleed into weekends

**This cadence is failing if:**
- Meetings feel like waste of time
- Same issues come up every week without resolution
- Team avoids or skips rituals
- Information still gets lost
- Everything feels reactive

**Fix it or change it. Don't suffer through bad process.**
