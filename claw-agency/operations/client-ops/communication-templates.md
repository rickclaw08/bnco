## Tone Guidelines

### Professional but Friendly
- Use contractions (we're, you're, it's)
- Start sentences with "And" or "But" occasionally
- Emoji acceptable but use sparingly (1-2 per email max)
- Avoid corporate jargon

### Clear and Concise
- Front-load important information
- Use bullet points and formatting
- Break up long paragraphs
- Bold key dates and action items

### Proactive and Transparent
- Address issues before client asks
- Set expectations clearly
- Admit mistakes honestly
- Provide specific next steps

### Response Time Expectations
- Client emails: Within 24 business hours
- Urgent issues: Within 4 hours
- After-hours emergencies: As defined in SLA

## Email Formatting Standards

**Subject Lines:**
- Start with project name for easy filtering
- Be specific about content
- Use brackets for status [URGENT], [Complete], etc.

**Structure:**
- Greeting (Hi [Name], not Dear or Hey)
- Context (1 sentence if needed)
- Main content (organized with headers)
- Clear next steps or call to action
- Friendly sign-off
- Signature with contact info

**Attachments:**
- Mention in body ("See attached...")
- Use descriptive filenames
- Keep under 10MB, use links for larger files
