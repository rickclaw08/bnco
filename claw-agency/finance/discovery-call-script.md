# ClawOps Discovery Call Script

**For:** Rick & Brand
**Purpose:** Qualify prospects, identify the right tier, close deals
**Duration:** 20-30 minutes

---

## Before the Call (2 min prep)

- [ ] Check their website/LinkedIn -- what do they do?
- [ ] Note their company size (employees, revenue if visible)
- [ ] How did they find us? (referral, social, cold outreach?)
- [ ] Have the pricing tiers ready to reference

---

## 1. Opening (2 min)

**Goal:** Build rapport, set the agenda, make them comfortable.

> "Hey [Name], thanks for taking the time. I'm [Rick/Brand] from ClawOps. Before we dive in, let me set expectations -- this is a casual conversation to see if we can help. No hard sell, no pressure. I want to understand what's going on in your business and see if there's a fit. Sound good?"

> "To make the most of our time, I'll ask a few questions about your current setup, then walk you through how we might help. We'll wrap up with clear next steps either way. Cool?"

---

## 2. Discovery -- Identify Pain Points (8-10 min)

**Goal:** Find the bleeding. The more specific the pain, the easier the close.

### The Big Three Questions

**Question 1: The Problem**
> "So tell me -- what's the thing that's eating up the most time in your business right now? The stuff that makes you think, 'there has to be a better way.'"

*Let them talk. Take notes. Don't interrupt.*

**Question 2: The Impact**
> "How much time would you say your team spends on that per week? Roughly."

*Get a number. Even a guess. You need this for ROI.*

**Follow-up:** "And who's doing that work? Is it you, or do you have team members handling it?"

**Question 3: The Cost**
> "If you had to put a dollar amount on it -- either in labor cost or lost revenue from not doing higher-value work -- what would that be per month?"

*If they don't know:* "No worries. Let's rough it out. If it's [X hours/week] at even [$30-50/hr], that's [$X/month] just in direct cost, not counting the opportunity cost."

### Dig Deeper (pick 2-3 as relevant)

- "What tools are you currently using for this?"
- "Have you tried to fix this before? What happened?"
- "If this was solved tomorrow, what would your team do with that freed-up time?"
- "Is this problem getting worse as you grow?"
- "Are there errors or quality issues from doing this manually?"
- "How does this affect your customers or revenue?"

### Red Flags (Disqualify Early)

- They want something "for free to test" -- politely decline, our Starter Sprint IS the test
- They can't articulate any specific problem -- not ready
- They want to "pick your brain" with no intent to buy -- redirect to a paid engagement
- Budget is clearly under $600 -- not a fit right now, stay friendly for later

---

## 3. Qualify Budget and Decision-Making (3 min)

**Goal:** Confirm they can actually buy. Do this naturally, not interrogation-style.

> "So if we found a solution that saved your team [X hours/week], what would that be worth to your business?"

*Their answer tells you which tier to pitch.*

> "And just so I'm talking to the right person -- are you the one who'd green-light this, or is there someone else involved in the decision?"

*If someone else decides:* "Got it. Would it help if I put together a one-page proposal you could share with them?"

> "In terms of timing, is this something you want to solve now, or are you planning for later in the quarter?"

*Urgency matters. "Now" = close today. "Later" = follow up.*

---

## 4. Present the Right Tier (5 min)

**Goal:** Match their problem to exactly one tier. Don't overwhelm with options.

### Decision Matrix

| Signal | Recommend |
|---|---|
| One specific, simple problem. Small team. Budget-conscious. | **Starter Sprint ($600)** |
| Multiple ongoing needs. Growing team. Wants continuous support. | **Growth Retainer ($2K/mo)** |
| Complex multi-step process. Big time sink. Wants a complete overhaul. | **Automation Sprint ($7,500)** |
| Needs technical leadership. No CTO. Strategic + execution. | **Enterprise ($5K/mo)** |

### How to Present Each Tier

**Starter Sprint:**
> "Based on what you've told me, this sounds like a perfect fit for our Starter Sprint. It's a one-week, flat-rate engagement -- $600 -- where we come in, automate [their specific problem], document it, and hand it off. You'll start saving [X hours/week] immediately. Most clients see payback in the first two weeks."

**Growth Retainer:**
> "It sounds like you have a few of these problems, and they'll keep coming as you grow. Our Growth Retainer gives you 20 hours a month of dedicated dev work -- we handle your automations, integrations, and maintenance for $2,000/month. Month-to-month, cancel anytime. Most clients save 2-3x what they pay us within the first 90 days."

**Automation Sprint:**
> "This is a big workflow with a lot of moving parts. Our Automation Sprint is built for exactly this -- a focused 2-4 week engagement where we redesign and automate the entire process. $7,500 flat. Based on [X hours/week] you're spending now, that pays for itself in [Y weeks]."

**Enterprise:**
> "What you really need is a technical leader who can own this -- strategy, architecture, and execution. Our Enterprise tier is a Fractional CTO plus dev team for $5,000/month. That's a fraction of what a full-time CTO costs ($200K+), and you get both the thinking and the doing."

---

## 5. Handle Objections (as needed)

### "It's too expensive."

> "I hear you. Let's look at it differently -- you're spending [X hours/week] on this at [$Y/hr]. That's [$Z/month] in labor cost alone. Our [tier] pays for itself in [timeframe]. The question isn't whether you can afford it -- it's whether you can afford NOT to fix it."

### "We can build it ourselves."

> "Absolutely, you could. The question is -- what's the opportunity cost? If your team spends 4-6 weeks building this, that's 4-6 weeks not spent on [their core business]. We can have it done in [1-4 weeks] so your team stays focused on what makes you money."

### "We need to think about it."

> "Totally fair. What specifically do you want to think through? Is it the scope, the cost, or something else?"

*Address the specific concern. Then:*

> "Tell you what -- I'll send over a formal proposal with everything we discussed, including the ROI breakdown. Take a look, and let's reconnect [specific day]. Sound good?"

### "Can you do it cheaper?"

> "Our pricing reflects the speed and quality we deliver. That said, if budget is tight, the Starter Sprint at $600 is a great way to prove the value before committing to something bigger. One quick win, and you'll see exactly what we can do."

### "We've been burned by agencies before."

> "That's fair, and honestly it's why we structured things the way we did. Month-to-month on retainers, flat-rate on sprints, and you own everything we build. No lock-in, no hostage code. We earn your trust by delivering, not by trapping you in a contract."

### "I need to talk to my partner/boss."

> "Makes total sense. Would it help if I hopped on a quick call with both of you? Or I can put together a one-page proposal you can forward -- it'll have the scope, timeline, ROI, and a direct link to get started."

---

## 6. Close (3 min)

**Goal:** Get a commitment. Either payment or a locked next step.

### The Direct Close (best for Starter Sprint)

> "So here's what I'd suggest -- let's get the Starter Sprint kicked off. I'll send you the payment link right now, and we can have your discovery call scheduled by tomorrow. In a week, [their problem] is solved. Ready to go?"

### The Proposal Close (for larger tiers)

> "I'll send you the full proposal in the next hour. It'll have everything -- scope, timeline, deliverables, ROI, and a direct payment link. If it looks good, you can sign and pay right from the proposal. Can we set a follow-up for [specific day, 2-3 days out] to answer any questions?"

### The Starter-to-Upsell Close

> "If you want to test the waters first, the Starter Sprint is perfect -- $600, one week, one quick win. A lot of our Growth and Enterprise clients started there. Prove the value, then we can talk about the bigger engagement."

---

## 7. After the Call (5 min)

- [ ] Send the proposal or payment link within 1 hour
- [ ] Log the call notes (company, pain points, tier recommended, objections, next steps)
- [ ] Set a follow-up reminder for the agreed date
- [ ] If they went cold, follow up in 48 hours with a brief message
- [ ] If they paid, send a personal "welcome" message and schedule discovery/kickoff

### Follow-Up Templates

**If they said "let me think about it":**
> "Hey [Name], great chatting earlier. Here's the proposal we discussed: [link]. The ROI breakdown is on page 2 -- I think you'll find the numbers compelling. Happy to jump on a quick call if any questions come up. Talk soon."

**48-hour follow-up (no response):**
> "Hey [Name], just checking in on the proposal. Any questions I can answer? Happy to hop on a 5-minute call if that's easier."

**1-week follow-up (still no response):**
> "Hi [Name], wanted to circle back one last time. If the timing isn't right, no worries at all -- just let me know and I'll follow up down the road. If you're ready to move forward, the payment link in the proposal is still active."

## Golden Rules

1. **Listen more than you talk.** 70/30 rule -- they talk 70% of the time.
2. **Use their words.** When they say "nightmare," you say "nightmare." Mirror their language.
3. **Sell the outcome, not the service.** They don't want automation. They want their time back.
4. **Always get a next step.** Never end a call with "we'll be in touch." Get a specific date and action.
5. **One tier, one recommendation.** Don't present all four options. Pick the right one and pitch it with confidence.
6. **The $600 Starter is your secret weapon.** When in doubt, start there. It's the easiest yes, and it leads to bigger deals.
