# ClawOps Pricing Calculator

**Purpose:** Determine what to charge for custom projects outside standard tiers  
**Last Updated:** February 2026

---

## STANDARD TIERS (Reference)

| Tier | Price | What's Included |
|------|-------|-----------------|
| **Starter** | $500 one-time | Single workflow automation, basic setup, 2 revisions |
| **Growth** | $2,000/month | 3-5 workflows, ongoing support, monthly optimization |
| **Enterprise** | $5,000-$15,000/month | Custom AI agents, full automation stack, dedicated support |

---

## HOURLY RATE CALCULATION

**Base Hourly Rate:** $150/hour

**Calculation Logic:**
- Target: $53K net profit in 6 months
- Assume 30% of revenue goes to expenses/overhead
- Need ~$75K gross revenue
- At 20 billable hours/week over 24 weeks = 480 hours
- $75,000 / 480 hours = $156/hour
- Round to $150/hour for simplicity

**When to Use Hourly:**
- Complex custom projects
- Unclear scope
- Discovery/consulting phases
- Emergency fixes

## PROJECT PRICING FRAMEWORK

### Step 1: Estimate Hours

**Workflow Complexity:**
- Simple (email automation, basic Zapier): 3-5 hours
- Medium (multi-step, API integrations): 8-15 hours
- Complex (custom AI agent, multiple systems): 20-40 hours
- Enterprise (full automation stack): 40-100+ hours

**Add Buffer:**
- Add 20% for revisions and client communication
- Add 30% for first-time tools/platforms

### Step 2: Calculate Base Price

**Formula:** Estimated Hours × $150 × Complexity Multiplier

**Complexity Multipliers:**
- Low risk/proven tools: 1.0x
- New tools/learning required: 1.2x
- High stakes/mission critical: 1.3x
- Cutting edge AI/experimental: 1.5x

### Step 3: Package It

**Don't quote hourly to clients.** Convert to fixed-price packages:
- Rounds up to nearest $500 for projects under $5K
- Rounds to nearest $1,000 for projects over $5K

## VALUE-BASED PRICING (Advanced)

**When client ROI is clear, price on value instead of hours:**

**Formula:** (Client's Annual Savings or Revenue Gain) × 10-20%

**Example:**
- Client spends $30K/year on customer service staff
- Your automation saves them 50% = $15K/year savings
- Value-based price: $15,000 × 15% = $2,250 one-time or $3,000-$5,000/year for ongoing

**When to Use:**
- Client has clear cost savings
- Client will gain measurable revenue
- You can prove ROI
- Enterprise clients with budgets

## SUBSCRIPTION CONVERSION

**Turn one-time projects into recurring revenue:**

**Monthly Maintenance Add-On:** 15-20% of project cost
- $1,000 project → $150-$200/month maintenance
- $5,000 project → $750-$1,000/month maintenance
- $10,000 project → $1,500-$2,000/month maintenance

**What's Included:**
- Monthly check-ins
- Updates and optimizations
- Bug fixes
- Performance monitoring
- Email/Slack support

---

## DISCOUNT GUIDELINES

**When to Discount:**
- First 3 clients: 20% off (portfolio building)
- Annual prepay: 15% off
- Referrals: 10% off next project
- Non-profits: 25% off

**When NOT to Discount:**
- Client asks "what's your best price" without reason
- Scope is already lean
- High-value enterprise clients (they expect to pay fairly)
- Rush jobs (charge 30% premium instead)

---

## MINIMUM PROJECT SIZE

**Don't take projects under $500.**

Why:
- Admin overhead isn't worth it
- Attracts wrong clients
- Devalues your expertise

**Exception:** Portfolio building (first 3 clients) or strategic partnerships

---

## RED FLAGS (CHARGE MORE OR WALK AWAY)

**Charge 30-50% more if:**
- "We need this by end of week" (rush fee)
- "Just a quick thing" (scope creep alert)
- Client has fired previous freelancers
- Client asks for spec work before hiring
- "We're a startup, can't pay much but great exposure"

**Walk away if:**
- Client won't define scope
- Client wants to pay after "seeing results"
- Aggressive negotiation before work starts
- Bad vibes (trust your gut)

---

## PAYMENT STRUCTURES

**Project <$2,000:**
- 50% upfront, 50% on delivery

**Project $2,000-$10,000:**
- 30% upfront, 40% at milestone, 30% on delivery

**Project >$10,000:**
- 25% upfront, 25% at milestone 1, 25% at milestone 2, 25% on delivery

**Monthly Retainers:**
- First month upfront
- Auto-billing on 1st of month
- 30-day cancellation notice

## CONFIDENCE RULE

**If you're unsure, estimate high.**

It's easier to deliver under budget than ask for more. Worst case, you make better margin. Best case, client is thrilled you finished early.

**When quoting:** Add 25% to your internal estimate when talking to client.
