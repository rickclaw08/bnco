# Payment Processing Options for ClawOps

**Evaluation Date:** February 2026
**Goal:** Zero upfront cost, minimal fees, professional experience

---

## RECOMMENDED STACK

**Primary:** Stripe
**Secondary:** PayPal Business
**Backup:** Zelle (for US clients, zero fees)

---

## DETAILED COMPARISON

### 1. Stripe (RECOMMENDED PRIMARY)

**Cost to Start:** $0
**Transaction Fees:**
- 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction (online)
- 2.7% + $0.05 per transaction (in-person, if using Terminal)
- 0.8% for ACH bank transfers (3-7 day processing)

**Pros:**
- Professional payment links and invoicing
- Excellent API for automation (perfect for our brand)
- Instant setup, no approval wait
- Built-in recurring billing for subscriptions
- Clean dashboard and reporting
- Free invoicing tools
- Mobile app for tracking

**Cons:**
- Slightly higher fees than some alternatives
- 7-day rolling payout hold for new accounts (first 2 weeks)

**Best For:** $2K/mo and $5-15K enterprise clients (professional image)

**Setup Time:** 15 minutes

---

### 2. PayPal Business

**Cost to Start:** $0
**Transaction Fees:**
- 3.49% + $0.49 per transaction (standard)
- 2.99% + $0.49 for PayPal/Venmo payments
- PayPal Invoicing: Same fees as above

**Pros:**
- Clients already have PayPal accounts (easy for them)
- Instant payments possible
- Free invoicing
- Global reach

**Cons:**
- Higher fees than Stripe
- Less professional appearance
- Known for freezing accounts/funds unexpectedly
- Customer service issues

**Best For:** $500 starter package clients who want convenience

**Setup Time:** 10 minutes

---

### 3. Zelle (BACKUP - US ONLY)

**Cost to Start:** $0
**Transaction Fees:** $0 (FREE)

**Pros:**
- Zero fees
- Instant transfer
- Built into most US banks

**Cons:**
- No paper trail/invoicing built in
- Limited to US banks
- No buyer/seller protection
- Manual reconciliation required
- Less professional

**Best For:** Quick payments from trusted US clients, collecting deposits

**Setup Time:** 5 minutes (if bank supports it)

---

### 4. Wave (Alternative - NOT RECOMMENDED)

**Cost to Start:** $0
**Transaction Fees:** 2.9% + $0.60 per credit card, 1% for bank payments ($1 minimum)

**Pros:**
- Free accounting software included
- Lower fees for bank transfers
- Built for small businesses

**Cons:**
- Less known, potential client trust issues
- Limited automation features
- Slower support

---

### 5. Square (Alternative)

**Cost to Start:** $0
**Transaction Fees:** 3.5% + $0.15 per transaction (online invoices), 2.6% + $0.10 (in-person)

**Pros:**
- Great for in-person if needed
- Simple interface
- Fast setup

**Cons:**
- Higher online fees than Stripe
- Less sophisticated for B2B/recurring billing

---

## MONTH 1 SETUP PLAN

**Week 1:**
1. Set up Stripe account (primary)
2. Create payment link templates for each service tier
3. Set up recurring billing for monthly clients
4. Configure invoice templates in Stripe

**Week 2:**
1. Set up PayPal Business (backup)
2. Link PayPal to same business email
3. Test both systems with small transactions

**Ongoing:**
- Monitor for Zelle availability through business bank account
- Evaluate ACH transfers through Stripe (0.8% fee) for larger invoices

## RECOMMENDATION SUMMARY

**For Launch:**
- Use Stripe as primary processor (professional, automation-ready)
- Accept PayPal for clients who prefer it
- Mention Zelle as option for US clients (save them fees)
- Consider offering 1-2% discount for ACH/Zelle on invoices >$5K

**Action Items:**
1. Sign up for Stripe business account
2. Sign up for PayPal Business account
3. Add payment methods to invoice template
4. Create standard payment links for each tier
5. Test transactions before first client

**Total Setup Cost:** $0
**Total Setup Time:** ~2 hours
**Monthly Fees:** $0 (only pay per transaction)

---

## FUTURE OPTIMIZATION (After $50K revenue)

Consider:
- Stripe ACH for recurring clients (0.8% vs 2.9%)
- Negotiate rates with Stripe (possible at higher volumes)
- Business bank account with built-in invoicing
- Accounting software integration (QuickBooks, Xero)

**For now: Keep it simple. Stripe + PayPal covers 95% of use cases.**
