## Business Structure Options

### Sole Proprietorship (Default — Day 1)
- No formation needed, you ARE the business
- Report income on Schedule C (Form 1040)
- Pay self-employment tax (15.3%) on net profit
- Simple but no liability protection

### Single-Member LLC (Recommended — Form by Month 3)
- **Why:** Liability protection, professional credibility, potential tax benefits
- **Cost:** $50–$500 depending on state (e.g., Wyoming: $100, California: $800/yr min)
- **Tax treatment:** Same as sole prop by default (pass-through)
- **When to form:** Once revenue is consistent ($3K+/mo)

### S-Corp Election (Consider at $50K+ annual profit)
- Elect S-Corp status via Form 2553
- Pay yourself "reasonable salary" → save on self-employment tax
- **Example:** $80K profit → $50K salary + $30K distribution = save ~$4,590 in SE tax
- **When:** Annual profit exceeds $50K reliably

## Tax Obligations

### Federal Taxes
| Tax | Rate | On What |
|-----|------|---------|
| Income tax | 10-37% (brackets) | Net profit |
| Self-employment | 15.3% | Net profit (first $168,600 for SS, unlimited Medicare) |
| **Effective combined** | **~25-40%** | Depending on total income |

### Quarterly Estimated Taxes
**You must pay quarterly if you'll owe $1,000+ in taxes for the year.**

| Quarter | Period | Due Date |
|---------|--------|----------|
| Q1 | Jan–Mar | April 15 |
| Q2 | Apr–May | June 15 |
| Q3 | Jun–Aug | September 15 |
| Q4 | Sep–Dec | January 15 (next year) |

**How to calculate:** Take projected annual profit × 30% ÷ 4
- Example: $68K projected → $68K × 0.30 = $20,400 → $5,100/quarter

**How to pay:** IRS Direct Pay (irs.gov/payments) or EFTPS.gov — Form 1040-ES

### State Taxes
- Varies by state (0% in TX, FL, WY, NV, WA, etc.)
- Check your state's requirements for income tax + business tax
- Some states require separate quarterly estimates

## Deductible Expenses

### 100% Deductible
- Software subscriptions (Make, Zapier, OpenAI API, hosting)
- Domain names and website costs
- Contractor payments (get W-9s, issue 1099s if >$600)
- Business phone/internet (% of personal use)
- Professional development (courses, books, conferences)
- Marketing and advertising costs
- Business insurance
- Legal and accounting fees
- Bank and payment processing fees (Stripe fees!)

### Home Office Deduction
- **Simplified method:** $5/sq ft, up to 300 sq ft = max $1,500
- **Regular method:** Calculate % of home used exclusively for business
- Must be regular and exclusive use

### Equipment (Section 179)
- Computer, monitor, peripherals — deduct full cost in year purchased
- Phone (business % only)

### Meals
- 50% deductible for business meals with clients/prospects

## Record Keeping Requirements

1. **Keep all receipts** (digital is fine — use an app)
2. **Separate bank account** for business (required for LLC, smart regardless)
3. **Track mileage** if driving to client meetings
4. **Issue 1099-NEC** to any contractor paid $600+ by January 31
5. **Retain records for 7 years**

## The 30% Rule
**From every payment received, immediately transfer 30% to a separate savings account for taxes.** This is non-negotiable. Do not touch this money. When quarterly estimates are due, pay from this account.

Example: $2,000 Growth client payment → $600 to tax savings, $1,400 available.
