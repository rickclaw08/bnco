## Executive Summary

The market pivot fundamentally changes ClawOps' financial trajectory in three critical ways:

1. **Recurring revenue is now a core revenue stream** (AI Receptionist + AaaS retainers), not just an upsell afterthought
2. **Price-to-value alignment improves** - we're charging based on outcomes ($5K-$20K/mo in saved missed calls) not hours
3. **The $100K target becomes MORE achievable**, not less - the mix of one-time + recurring revenue creates compounding momentum

**Bottom line:** Under the old model, $100K required 10+ individual project closes. Under the new model, $100K is achievable with 6-8 deals if we lock in recurring revenue early. Projected timeline: **75-90 days from today (mid-May 2026)**.

## Detailed Unit Economics - New Tiers

### Tier 1: AI Readiness Audit ($500)

**Purpose:** Paid discovery. Qualifies serious buyers. Leads into Sprint/Receptionist sales.

| Cost Category | Amount |
|--------------|--------|
| Your time (3 hrs @ $150/hr) | $450 |
| AI tools/analysis | $15 |
| Stripe fees (2.9% + $0.30) | $15 |
| Overhead allocation | $20 |
| **Total Cost** | **$500** |
| **Revenue** | **$500** |
| **Gross Profit** | **$0** |

**Wait - zero profit?** Yes, and that's fine. This is a **lead qualification tool**, not a profit center.

**Real value:** 60-70% of audits convert to a $5K-$25K engagement. Expected value per audit = $500 revenue + $4,200 pipeline value (0.65 conversion x $6,500 avg next deal).

**CAC impact:** By charging $500, we filter out tire-kickers. Only serious buyers pay for discovery. This drops our effective CAC on downstream deals by 40-50%.

## Risk Analysis

### Risk 1: AI Receptionist Churn Higher Than Expected
**Impact:** MRR projection drops; $100K timeline extends
**Probability:** Medium (service businesses are non-technical, may not understand value)
**Mitigation:** Monthly ROI reports showing calls answered, leads captured, revenue attributed. Make the value undeniable.

### Risk 2: Revenue Ops Sprints Harder to Close at $5K-$15K
**Impact:** One-time revenue shortfall
**Probability:** Low-Medium (market research shows strong demand at this price point for vertical-specific solutions)
**Mitigation:** Lead with AI Readiness Audit to pre-qualify. Use audit findings to justify Sprint investment.

### Risk 3: Delivery Capacity on AI Receptionist Volume
**Impact:** Can't onboard 3-4 Receptionist clients/week
**Probability:** High initially, drops as templates mature
**Mitigation:** Build the first 3 manually, then template everything. By client 5, setup should take 5-8 hours, not 15-25.

### Risk 4: Vertical Competition (Conduit AI, Smith.ai)
**Impact:** Price pressure on AI Receptionist
**Probability:** Medium (but we offer customization they don't)
**Mitigation:** Position as "AI receptionist + full automation stack" not just a call answering service. Bundle with CRM, review requests, and follow-up automation.

## Recommendations

1. **Price the AI Receptionist at $2,250 setup + $400/mo minimum.** The $300/mo tier has thin margins (27%). Don't offer it unless absolutely necessary to close a deal.

2. **Kill the old Starter Sprint ($600) immediately.** Replace all references with $500 AI Readiness Audit.

3. **3-month minimum on all recurring services.** AI Receptionist and AaaS both require a 3-month commitment. This protects against early churn and gives time to prove ROI.

4. **Prepay discounts:** Offer 10% off for quarterly prepay, 15% off for annual prepay on recurring services. This accelerates cash flow.

5. **Hire a part-time AI Receptionist specialist by week 6.** At $25-35/hr for 15-20 hrs/week, they can handle 3-4 setups/week while you focus on Sprint/Agent sales.

6. **Track MRR weekly, not monthly.** In a pivot phase, monthly reporting is too slow. Weekly MRR tracking lets you course-correct in real time.
