# High-Ticket Pricing Strategy - ClawOps

**Purpose:** Move beyond $500-$2K deals into $10K-$50K territory through value-based pricing, ROI anchoring, and strategic positioning.

**Target Client:** Companies spending $50K-$500K/year on manual labor that can be automated.

---

## Core Philosophy: Price on Value, Not Hours

**Old way (hourly/project):**
- "This will take 40 hours at $150/hr = $6,000"
- Client thinks: "That's expensive for 40 hours of work"

**New way (value-based):**
- "You're spending $120K/year on manual data entry. We'll reduce that by 80% for $25K one-time + $2K/mo support. That's $96K saved, every year."
- Client thinks: "I'd be stupid NOT to do this"

**The shift:** Stop selling automation. Start selling ROI.

## Pricing Model 1: ROI-Anchored Pricing

### How It Works

1. **Discover their pain in dollars**
   - "How many hours per week does your team spend on [manual task]?"
   - "What's the average hourly cost of those team members?"
   - Calculate: Hours x Cost x 52 weeks = Annual waste

2. **Position your solution as a fraction of the waste**
   - Annual waste: $120,000
   - Your price: $25,000 (21% of annual waste)
   - Their savings: $95,000 in year 1, $120,000 every year after

3. **Frame payback period**
   - "This pays for itself in 2.5 months. After that, it's pure profit to your bottom line."

### ROI Pricing Formula

**Price = (Annual Savings x 0.15 to 0.30) + Complexity Premium**

Examples:

| Client Annual Waste | Savings (80% reduction) | Price (20% of savings) | Payback Period |
|---------------------|------------------------|------------------------|----------------|
| $50,000 | $40,000 | $8,000 | 2.4 months |
| $120,000 | $96,000 | $19,200 | 2.4 months |
| $250,000 | $200,000 | $40,000 | 2.4 months |
| $500,000 | $400,000 | $80,000 | 2.4 months |

**Why 15-30% of first-year savings?**
- Cheap enough to be a no-brainer
- Expensive enough to be taken seriously
- Captures ongoing value (they save every year, we get paid once... unless retainer)

### Complexity Premium

Add to base price for:
- **Multiple system integrations:** +$2,000-$5,000 per system beyond 3
- **Custom AI model training:** +$5,000-$15,000
- **High security/compliance requirements:** +$3,000-$10,000
- **24/7 uptime SLA:** +$2,000/mo ongoing
- **Multiple stakeholder training:** +$1,000 per department

## Pricing Model 2: Outcome-Based (Performance Pricing)

### Structure

**Base Fee + Success Bonus**

Example:
- Base: $15,000 (covers build, guaranteed)
- Success bonus: $10,000 if we achieve 75%+ reduction in manual hours within 90 days
- Total potential: $25,000

**Why clients love this:**
- Lower upfront risk
- Aligns incentives (we only win if they win)
- Justifies higher total price

**Why we love this:**
- Commands premium (can charge 50% more than fixed)
- Filters for serious clients (they have to measure success)
- Creates case studies with hard numbers

### Success Metrics Examples

| Outcome | Base Fee | Success Bonus | Total |
|---------|----------|---------------|-------|
| Reduce support ticket response time by 60% | $12,000 | $8,000 | $20,000 |
| Increase lead qualification rate by 40% | $10,000 | $10,000 | $20,000 |
| Save 100+ hours/month on data entry | $15,000 | $12,000 | $27,000 |
| Achieve 90%+ automation rate on invoice processing | $18,000 | $15,000 | $33,000 |

**Risk mitigation:**
- Define success criteria IN CONTRACT (no ambiguity)
- Set measurement period (30, 60, or 90 days)
- Require client to provide baseline data
- Bonus paid NET-30 after measurement period

## Pricing Model 3: Automation Sprint (Productized Service)

### The Offer

**"30-Day Automation Sprint: We transform one department in your company"**

**What's included:**
- Week 1: Process audit & automation roadmap
- Week 2-3: Build and integrate core automations
- Week 4: Testing, training, and handoff
- 30 days post-launch support

**Fixed scope, fixed price, fixed timeline.**

### Pricing Tiers

| Department | Typical Scope | Price |
|------------|---------------|-------|
| **Customer Support** | Ticket routing, auto-responses, FAQ bot, escalation workflow | $8,000 |
| **Sales** | Lead capture, CRM automation, follow-up sequences, proposal generation | $10,000 |
| **Operations** | Invoice processing, expense tracking, vendor management, reporting | $12,000 |
| **Marketing** | Content scheduling, lead scoring, email sequences, analytics dashboard | $9,000 |
| **HR** | Onboarding workflows, time-off requests, document signing, payroll prep | $11,000 |

**Add-ons:**
- Additional department: +$7,000
- Custom integrations (beyond standard): +$2,000 each
- Ongoing optimization: $2,000/mo after sprint

### Why This Works

1. **Removes decision paralysis** (clear scope, clear outcome)
2. **Premium pricing justified** (specialized expertise, guaranteed timeline)
3. **Scalable delivery** (template-based, repeatable process)
4. **Upsell path** (sprint -> retainer -> full company transformation)

## Pricing Model 4: Growth Retainer (Subscription with Tiers)

### New Retainer Structure

| Tier | Monthly Price | Hours Included | Best For |
|------|---------------|----------------|----------|
| **Essential** | $3,000/mo | 20 hours | 1-2 departments, basic automation |
| **Professional** | $6,000/mo | 45 hours | 3-4 departments, ongoing optimization |
| **Enterprise** | $12,000/mo | 100 hours | Company-wide, dedicated account manager |

**What's included (all tiers):**
- Proactive monitoring and maintenance
- Priority support (2-hour response Essential, 1-hour Pro/Enterprise)
- Monthly strategy call
- Quarterly automation roadmap review
- Slack/email access to team

**What's NOT included (billed separately):**
- New major integrations (3+ days work)
- Custom AI model development
- Third-party tool costs (APIs, licenses)

### Retainer Positioning

**Essential:** "For teams who want automation handled without thinking about it"

**Professional:** "For growing companies that need continuous optimization"

**Enterprise:** "For organizations treating automation as strategic advantage"

### Upsell Path

1. **Start with Automation Sprint** ($10K one-time)
2. **Convert to Essential retainer** ($3K/mo after sprint)
3. **Upgrade to Professional** (after 3-6 months, as needs grow)
4. **Scale to Enterprise** (when automation is across company)

**Lifetime value example:**
- Sprint: $10,000
- Essential x 6 months: $18,000
- Professional x 12 months: $72,000
- Enterprise x 12 months: $144,000
- **Total over 30 months: $244,000** (from one client)

## Price Anchoring: The Presentation Script

### Setup: Always Present 3 Options

**Anchor High -> Land Middle -> Upsell Later**

#### Script for Discovery Call Close

> "Based on what you've shared, I see three ways we can work together:
>
> **Option 1: Full Transformation** - We audit your entire operation, build a comprehensive automation strategy, and implement across all departments. This is a 90-day engagement. Investment: $75,000.
>
> [Pause. Let them process the anchor.]
>
> **Option 2: Automation Sprint** - We focus on your [biggest pain department] and transform it in 30 days. Fixed scope, guaranteed results. Investment: $12,000.
>
> [This now seems reasonable by comparison.]
>
> **Option 3: Starter Project** - We tackle one specific workflow to prove value. Great if you want to test us out first. Investment: $1,500.
>
> [Safety net, but positioned as "small" compared to others.]
>
> **Most clients in your situation start with Option 2**, because it delivers meaningful impact without the commitment of a full transformation. You get quick wins, we prove ourselves, and then we expand from there.
>
> **What feels right to you?**"

### Why This Works

1. **$75K anchor** makes $12K feel cheap (even though $12K is your target)
2. **Option 2 positioned as "most popular"** (social proof, guidance)
3. **Option 3 exists as safety** but you've framed it as "small" (ego barrier)
4. **You've made the decision for them** ("most clients choose...") so they don't have to

### Variations by Client Size

**Small business ($500K-$2M revenue):**
- Anchor: $25,000 (full transformation)
- Target: $8,000 (sprint)
- Safety: $1,500 (starter)

**Mid-market ($2M-$20M revenue):**
- Anchor: $75,000 (full transformation)
- Target: $15,000 (sprint)
- Safety: $3,000 (starter)

**Enterprise ($20M+ revenue):**
- Anchor: $200,000 (full transformation)
- Target: $50,000 (sprint)
- Safety: $10,000 (starter)

**Rule:** Anchor should be 5-10x your target price. Makes target feel like a bargain.

## Objection Handling Scripts

### Objection 1: "That's too expensive"

**Bad response:** "Well, I could do it for less..."

**Good response:**
> "I understand. Let me ask: what's this problem costing you right now?"
>
> [Let them answer. Calculate.]
>
> "So you're spending $X per month on this manually. Our solution is $Y, which pays for itself in [timeframe]. After that, it's pure savings, every month, forever."
>
> "The question isn't whether you can afford to do this. It's whether you can afford NOT to."

### Objection 2: "We need to think about it"

**Bad response:** "Sure, take your time."

**Good response:**
> "Of course. What specifically do you need to think through? [Listen.]"
>
> "Let me address that now: [answer their concern directly]."
>
> "Here's what I recommend: let's start with [Starter/Sprint] so you can see results without the big commitment. If it works, we expand. If not, you still got value. Fair?"

### Objection 3: "Can we do a smaller pilot first?"

**Good response:**
> "Absolutely. That's why we have the Starter tier at $1,500. One workflow, one week, you see our quality."
>
> "BUT... here's what I've learned from 50+ clients: pilots often don't show the real impact. Automating one workflow saves a few hours. Automating a whole department saves hundreds."
>
> "My recommendation: start with the Sprint at $12K. It's still focused (one department), but big enough to actually transform how your team works. If you love it, we go bigger. If you don't, at least you got a working system."

### Objection 4: "We can build this internally"

**Good response:**
> "You absolutely could. Your team is smart. Here's what I'd think through:
>
> 1. **Time:** Building this internally will take 3-5x longer. Your developers have other priorities. This becomes a side project that drags for months.
>
> 2. **Expertise:** We've built this 50 times. Your team is building it for the first time. That means mistakes, rework, and edge cases they didn't anticipate.
>
> 3. **Opportunity cost:** If your lead developer spends 80 hours on this at $150/hr loaded cost, that's $12,000. Same as hiring us. But they're not shipping product features during that time.
>
> **My take:** Your team should build things only YOU can build (your core product). We handle the automation plumbing so they stay focused on what makes you money."

### Objection 5: "We need board/partner approval"

**Good response:**
> "Totally understand. How can I help make that easier?"
>
> **[Offer:]**
>
> "I can join the call/meeting to present directly and answer their questions. Or I can send you a one-page ROI summary they can review. What would work better?"
>
> **[If they resist both:]**
>
> "No problem. Here's what I'll do: I'll send you a proposal with clear ROI breakdown, case study from a similar company, and a simple approval template. Should have everything they need."

### Objection 6: "We've tried automation before and it didn't work"

**Good response:**
> "I hear this a lot. What happened? [Listen carefully.]"
>
> **[Common failure reasons:]**
> - Off-the-shelf tool didn't fit their workflow
> - Consultant built it and disappeared
> - Team didn't adopt it
> - Broke when something changed
>
> **[Your response based on their reason:]**
>
> "Here's how we're different:
> 1. We build custom for YOUR workflow, not force you into a template
> 2. We include 30-60 days support after launch, not abandon you
> 3. We train your team and make adoption easy
> 4. We build with monitoring and alerts, so if something breaks, we know immediately"
>
> "Automation only fails when it's built wrong or supported wrong. We do both right."

## Advanced Pricing Tactics

### Tactic 1: Pay-in-Full Discount

**Offer:** "Pay 100% upfront, get 10% off"

**Why it works:**
- Improves cash flow immediately
- Reduces collections risk
- Filters for serious buyers (if they won't pay up front, they might be trouble)

**When to use:** Deals over $10K where cash flow matters

### Tactic 2: Multi-Department Bundle

**Offer:** "Automate 2 departments for $18K (normally $12K each = $24K). Save $6K."

**Why it works:**
- Increases deal size
- Locks client in deeper (harder to leave after 2 departments automated)
- Actually costs you less (shared infrastructure, already know the company)

**When to use:** Client mentions pain in multiple areas during discovery

### Tactic 3: Annual Prepay on Retainers

**Offer:** "Pay 12 months up front, get 2 months free" (effectively 17% discount)

**Why it works:**
- Locks in $36K-$144K immediately (depending on tier)
- Reduces churn risk (they've already paid)
- Predictable revenue for hiring and scaling

**Math:**
- Essential: $3K x 10 months = $30K (vs $36K monthly)
- Professional: $6K x 10 months = $60K (vs $72K monthly)
- Enterprise: $12K x 10 months = $120K (vs $144K monthly)

**When to use:** Client committed but concerned about monthly budgeting

### Tactic 4: Equity Stakes (For Startups)

**Offer:** "We'll do this at 50% off in exchange for 0.5-1% equity"

**Why it works:**
- Allows startups to afford you (they're cash-poor but equity-rich)
- Upside if they exit
- Positions you as partner, not vendor

**When NOT to use:**
- Bootstrapped companies with no exit path
- Lifestyle businesses
- You need cash now (equity is illiquid)

**Structure:**
- $12K project -> $6K cash + 0.5% equity
- $25K project -> $12.5K cash + 1% equity

**Only do this for 1-2 clients max** (diversification risk)

### Tactic 5: Guarantee (Risk Reversal)

**Offer:** "If we don't save you at least 50 hours/month in the first 90 days, we'll refund 100%"

**Why it works:**
- Removes buyer risk entirely
- Signals confidence
- Almost never gets claimed (if you're good at delivery)

**Requirements:**
- Client must provide baseline data (how much time they spend now)
- Client must track time after implementation
- Refund only if YOU failed, not if they didn't adopt

**When to use:** High-value deals ($15K+) where client is hesitant

## Pricing Psychology Principles

### 1. Never Round Down

**Bad:** $10,000  
**Good:** $12,500

Why? Round numbers feel arbitrary. Specific numbers feel calculated and justified.

### 2. Frame as Monthly Cost, Not Total

**Bad:** "This will cost $24,000"  
**Good:** "This is $2,000/month, less than one full-time employee"

Why? Monthly feels smaller. Comparing to FTE salary makes it feel cheap.

### 3. Use "Investment" Not "Cost"

**Bad:** "The cost is $15,000"  
**Good:** "The investment is $15,000"

Why? Cost = expense = gone. Investment = asset = return.

### 4. Lead with Value, End with Price

**Bad:** "We charge $12,000 for an automation sprint"  
**Good:** "We'll save you 120 hours per month, which at your team's cost is $18,000/year in savings. The investment for the sprint is $12,000."

Why? Anchor value first. Price becomes a fraction of value.

### 5. Give Them 3 Options, Guide to Middle

**Always present:** High / Medium / Low

**Position medium as "most popular" or "best value"**

Why? People avoid extremes. Middle feels safe. But middle is YOUR target price.

## When to Charge More (Premium Triggers)

Charge 50-100% MORE if:

1. **Urgent timeline** ("We need this in 2 weeks")
2. **High-stakes outcome** (IPO prep, acquisition due diligence, major product launch)
3. **Complex compliance** (HIPAA, SOC 2, PCI, government)
4. **24/7 uptime required** (vs business hours)
5. **Multiple time zones** (team distributed, need global coverage)
6. **High-profile client** (using them as case study has huge value)
7. **They mentioned a huge budget** (if they say "we have $100K for this," don't charge $20K)

**Premium pricing script:**
> "Given [urgent timeline / compliance requirements / 24/7 uptime], this moves into premium tier. The investment for this would be $35,000 instead of our standard $18,000. The additional cost covers [specific extra effort/risk]."

**Don't apologize.** State it confidently. If they balk, they weren't serious.

## When to Walk Away (Red Flags)

Don't take the deal if:

1. **They want free work disguised as "trial"**
2. **They can't/won't measure success**
3. **"We'll pay you when we get results"** (outcome-based is OK, but you need SOME base fee)
4. **They bad-mouth their previous vendors** (you'll be next)
5. **Scope creep before signing** ("Can you also add...")
6. **They want to pay NET-60 or later** (cash flow killer)
7. **Price is their ONLY concern** (quality doesn't matter to them)
8. **They can't define the problem clearly** (you'll never satisfy them)

**Walking away protects your time, energy, and reputation.**

## Pricing Roadmap (Next 12 Months)

- Offer all tiers, see what converts

- Get first testimonials
- **Goal:** 10 clients, any tier

- Focus sales on Growth + Enterprise

- Launch Automation Sprint as fixed offering
- Standardize proposals (templates for each tier)
- Build pricing calculator tool
- **Goal:** $30K MRR, 50% margin

### Month 10-12: Premium Positioning

- Raise prices 20-30% across board
- Focus only on $10K+ deals
- Add Enterprise retainer tier ($15K-$25K/mo)
- **Goal:** $50K+ MRR, 60% margin

**By end of year:** Charging 2-3x what you started at, serving fewer clients, making more profit.

## Final Thoughts: Pricing is Positioning

**Cheap = Commodity** (you compete on price, you die on price)

**Expensive = Premium** (you compete on value, you win on results)

**The goal is NOT to be affordable to everyone.** The goal is to be valuable to the right clients.

**Charge what you're worth. Deliver more than you charge. Repeat.**
