## 9. Weekly Finance Routine

**Every Monday morning:**
1. Check Stripe dashboard: new payments, failed charges, cancellations
2. Update MRR calculation
3. Review cash balance
4. Log any new expenses
5. Update this dashboard
6. Review pipeline: follow up on proposals

**Every month-end:**
1. Close the books: finalize income and expenses
2. Calculate profit margin
3. Review churn and client count
4. Update 6-month trends
5. Pay estimated quarterly taxes (if due)
6. Review financial goals: on track or adjust?

1. QuickBooks: Apps > Find Apps > Search "Stripe" > Connect
2. Wave: Settings > Connected Apps > Stripe
3. Automatic: Stripe transactions sync daily

## 11. Red Flags to Watch

**Immediate action needed if:**
- Churn rate exceeds 10% in a month
- Cash runway drops below 2 months
- Profit margin falls below 50%
- 3+ failed payment charges in a week
- MRR declines 2 months in a row

**How to respond:**
- High churn: Survey customers, improve onboarding, add value
- Low cash: Cut non-essential expenses, accelerate sales
- Low margin: Raise prices, reduce costs, stop unprofitable work
- Failed payments: Reach out immediately, update cards, pause service if needed
- MRR decline: Double down on marketing, re-engage past leads, improve retention

## 12. Quarterly Financial Review

## Dashboard Template (Spreadsheet Version)

**Want this in Google Sheets or Excel?**

**Sheet 1: Overview**
- MRR, cash, clients, profit margin, churn (this month vs last month)

**Sheet 2: Monthly P&L**
- Income, expenses, net profit by month (running 12-month view)

**Sheet 3: Client List**
- Client name, tier, MRR, start date, status (active/churned)

**Sheet 4: Pipeline**
- Prospects, deal size, stage, probability, expected close

**Sheet 5: Expenses**
- All expenses categorized (software, operations, marketing, etc.)

**Formulas:**
- MRR: =SUMIF(Clients!Status, "Active", Clients!MRR)
- Churn: =(Churned this month / Clients at start) * 100
- Profit Margin: =(Revenue - Expenses) / Revenue * 100
