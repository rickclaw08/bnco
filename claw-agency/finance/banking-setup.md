# Banking and Accounting Setup for ClawOps

## Overview

Getting your banking and accounting right from day one saves massive headaches later. This guide covers business bank accounts, accounting software, and bookkeeping best practices for a small AI agency.

---

## Part 1: Business Bank Account

### Why You Need a Separate Business Account

**Legal reasons:**
- Keeps personal and business finances separate (critical for tax audits)
- If you form an LLC, it's required to maintain liability protection
- Makes accounting 10x easier

**Practical reasons:**
- Clean bookkeeping (no sorting through personal Starbucks charges)
- Professional appearance (clients pay "ClawOps" not "Brand Smith")
- Easier to track profit and expenses

### Recommended Business Banks for Freelancers/Small Agencies

#### Option 1: Relay (Best for Small Agencies)

**Website:** https://relayfi.com

**Pros:**
- Free business checking (no monthly fees, no minimums)
- Up to 20 free checking accounts (great for separating income, expenses, taxes, savings)
- Integrates with Stripe, QuickBooks, Xero
- Modern app, good mobile experience
- Fast ACH transfers
- Virtual debit cards (create cards for different expense categories)

**Cons:**
- No physical branches (online-only)
- No cash deposits
- Newer bank (less established than traditional banks)

**Best for:** Digital businesses, agencies, freelancers who rarely handle cash

**Setup time:** 10-15 minutes online

**What you need:**
- Business name and EIN (or SSN if sole prop)
- Government ID
- Business address

**Recommendation:** Open 3-4 accounts:
1. **Operating** - Day-to-day income and expenses
2. **Taxes** - Set aside 25-30% of revenue for taxes
3. **Profit** - Your pay and business savings
4. **Expenses** - Buffer for upcoming bills

**If you're solo or very small:** Relay. Free, easy, and the multi-account feature is perfect for separating tax savings and profit.

**If you're tech-savvy and plan to scale:** Mercury. Better for investor relationships, more advanced features.

**If you need physical branches:** Chase. Pay the fee for the peace of mind.

### How to Open a Business Bank Account

**Step 1: Gather documents**
- Government-issued ID (driver's license or passport)
- Social Security Number or EIN (if you have an LLC)
- Business formation documents (articles of organization if LLC)
- Business address
- Doing-business-as (DBA) certificate (if applicable)

**Step 2: Apply online** (Relay, Mercury, Novo) or visit branch (Chase)

**Step 3: Fund the account**
- Initial deposit: $50-100 (depends on bank)
- Link to personal account for transfer

**Step 4: Order debit card** (physical card ships in 7-10 days, virtual card instant)

**Step 5: Connect to Stripe**
- In Stripe: Settings > Bank accounts and scheduling > Add bank account
- Use routing and account numbers from your new business account
- Verify with micro-deposits (1-2 days)

**Step 6: Set up automated transfers**
- After each Stripe payout, transfer 30% to "Taxes" account
- Transfer remaining profit to "Profit" account weekly or monthly

## Part 2: Accounting Software

You need software to track income, expenses, and generate reports for taxes. Don't use spreadsheets alone (too error-prone).

### Option 1: Wave (Best Free Option)

**Website:** https://waveapps.com

**Pricing:** FREE (they make money on optional paid services like payroll and payments)

**Pros:**
- Completely free accounting and invoicing
- Clean, simple interface
- Automatic bank connection (syncs transactions)
- Connects to Stripe
- Generates profit & loss statements, balance sheets
- Tax-ready reports
- Receipt scanning (mobile app)
- Unlimited users

**Cons:**
- Fewer advanced features (no inventory tracking, project accounting)
- Customer support is limited (mostly help docs)
- Payroll and payment processing cost extra

**Best for:** Solo freelancers, very small agencies (1-2 people), businesses with simple finances

**Morgan's take:** If you're just starting and money is tight, Wave is perfect. It's shockingly good for free software. Upgrade later if you need more features.

---

**Website:** https://quickbooks.intuit.com

**Pricing:**

- Industry standard (most accountants use it)

**Cons:**

**Morgan's take:** If you can afford $30-55/month and want professional-grade software, QuickBooks is the safe choice. Your accountant will thank you.

## Part 3: Bookkeeping Setup

Once you have a bank account and accounting software, you need a system to keep everything organized.

### Bookkeeping Basics

**The goal:** Know exactly how much money is coming in, going out, and where it's going.

**Core tasks:**
1. Record all income (Stripe automatically syncs)
2. Record all expenses (connect bank, manually add cash expenses)
3. Categorize transactions (software, marketing, travel, meals, etc.)
4. Reconcile accounts monthly (make sure bank balance matches accounting software)
5. Generate profit & loss statement monthly
6. Save receipts (digital or physical)
7. Track mileage (if you drive for business)

---

### Chart of Accounts (How to Categorize Expenses)

Your accounting software will have default categories. Customize them for your agency:

**Income categories:**
- Service revenue (Stripe payments)
- Setup fees (one-time charges)
- Other income (affiliate commissions, etc.)

**Expense categories:**
- Software subscriptions (Stripe, tools, SaaS)
- Subcontractors (if you hire help)
- Marketing and advertising (ads, content, SEO)
- Office expenses (coworking, supplies)
- Meals and entertainment (client dinners, 50% deductible)
- Travel (flights, hotels, 100% deductible if business)
- Education (courses, books, conferences)
- Professional services (lawyer, accountant)
- Bank fees and interest
- Taxes (estimated quarterly payments)
- Insurance (business liability, health)
- Utilities (phone, internet, if home office)

**Keep categories simple.** Don't create 50 categories. 10-15 is plenty.

---

### Monthly Bookkeeping Routine

**Week 1 of month:**
1. Connect bank and credit card accounts to accounting software (if not already connected)
2. Import last month's transactions
3. Categorize all transactions (software does most automatically)
4. Attach receipts to transactions (scan or upload)

**Week 2 of month:**
1. Reconcile bank account (match software balance to bank statement)
2. Reconcile Stripe account
3. Generate profit & loss statement
4. Review: Did we make money? Where did we overspend?

**Week 3 of month:**
1. Pay any outstanding bills
2. Set aside tax money (25-30% of profit)
3. Update financial dashboard (see financial-dashboard.md)

**Week 4 of month:**
1. Back up accounting data (export or cloud backup)
2. Review next month's budget
3. Plan for any big expenses coming up

**Time required:** 2-4 hours per month if you stay on top of it.

---

### DIY vs Outsource Bookkeeping

**DIY (Do It Yourself):**
- Use Wave, QuickBooks, or FreshBooks
- Spend 2-4 hours per month managing it yourself
- **Cost:** $0-85/month (software only)
- **Best for:** Solo freelancers, early-stage agencies, people who like control

**Outsource to Bookkeeper:**
- Hire a part-time bookkeeper ($200-500/month)
- Or use a service like Bench ($300-500/month)
- They handle categorizing, reconciling, reports
- You just review monthly and focus on business

**Outsource to Full Accounting Service:**
- Services like Pilot ($600-1,000+/month)
- Includes bookkeeping, tax filing, CFO advice
- **Best for:** Agencies making $100k+/year, people who hate numbers

**Morgan's recommendation:**

**Year 1 (under $50k revenue):** DIY with Wave (free) or QuickBooks ($30/month). Learn the basics.

**Year 2 ($50k-100k revenue):** Consider outsourcing to Bench or a local bookkeeper. Frees up 3-4 hours per month for you to focus on sales and delivery.

**Year 3+ ($100k+ revenue):** Absolutely outsource. Your time is worth more than bookkeeping. Use a full-service firm or accountant.

---

### Receipt Management

**IRS rule:** Keep receipts for any business expense over $75. Keep everything over $25 to be safe.

**Best practices:**
1. **Go digital:** Use your accounting software's mobile app to snap photos of receipts immediately
2. **File by month:** Create folders (digital or physical) for each month
3. **Label clearly:** "2026-02 - Office Depot - $47.32 - Supplies"
4. **Store for 7 years:** IRS can audit up to 7 years back (digital is fine, no need for paper)

**Apps for receipt scanning:**
- QuickBooks mobile app
- Wave mobile app
- FreshBooks mobile app
- Expensify (if you need standalone solution)

**Pro tip:** Email receipts to yourself with subject "Receipt - [Category]" and set up auto-forwarding to your accounting software. Many support email-to-receipt features.

---

### Separating Personal and Business Expenses

**Golden rule:** NEVER mix personal and business on the same card or account.

**How to handle gray areas:**

**Home office:** If you work from home, you can deduct a portion of rent, utilities, internet.
- Calculate square footage of office space / total home square footage
- Apply that percentage to rent and utilities
- Example: 150 sq ft office in 1,000 sq ft apartment = 15% deduction

**Phone and internet:** If you use your personal phone for business, deduct the business percentage (e.g., 50% if half your usage is business)

**Meals:** Client meals are 50% deductible. Solo meals while traveling for business are 50% deductible. Regular lunch at your desk is NOT deductible.

**Car:** If you drive for business, track mileage. IRS standard rate: $0.67/mile (2024). Use an app like MileIQ or Everlance.

**When in doubt, ask your accountant.** It's worth a $200 consultation to avoid a $2,000 audit penalty.

## Part 4: Connecting Everything Together

### Step-by-Step Integration

**1. Open business bank account** (Relay, Mercury, or Novo)

**2. Connect Stripe to bank account**
- Stripe > Settings > Bank accounts > Add bank
- Enter routing and account numbers
- Verify micro-deposits in 1-2 days

**3. Set up accounting software** (Wave, QuickBooks, or FreshBooks)

**4. Connect bank to accounting software**
- Accounting software > Banking > Add account
- Log in via Plaid (secure bank connection)
- Transactions auto-import daily

**5. Connect Stripe to accounting software**
- Accounting software > Integrations > Stripe
- Authorize connection
- Revenue auto-imports from Stripe

**6. Set up expense categories** (customize chart of accounts)

**7. Import and categorize transactions**
- Review transactions weekly
- Assign categories
- Attach receipts

**8. Generate monthly reports**
- Profit & loss statement
- Cash flow statement
- Balance sheet (if needed for loans)

**9. Update financial dashboard** (see financial-dashboard.md)

---

### Automation Hacks

**Auto-categorize recurring expenses:**
- Most accounting software learns patterns
- After categorizing "Stripe subscription fee" once, it auto-categorizes future Stripe fees

**Set up bank rules:**
- Create rules like "All transactions from Stripe = Revenue"
- "All transactions from AWS = Software subscriptions"

**Schedule automatic reports:**
- QuickBooks and FreshBooks can email you monthly P&L statements automatically

**Use Zapier for advanced automation:**
- Zap: New Stripe charge → Add row to Google Sheet → Send Slack notification
