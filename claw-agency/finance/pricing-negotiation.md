# ClawOps Pricing Negotiation Guidelines

> When to discount, when to hold firm, and how to package deals

---

## Core Pricing

| Tier | Price | Type |
|------|-------|------|
| Starter | $500 | One-time |
| Growth | $2,000/mo | Recurring |
| Enterprise | $5,000-$15,000/mo | Recurring |

**Golden rule:** Never discount more than 20% on any tier. If they need more than 20% off, they are not the right client.

---

## When to Hold Firm (No Discount)

1. **First client interaction.** Never lead with a discount. Present the value first.
2. **Enterprise tier.** Custom pricing already reflects flexibility. The floor is $5,000/mo.
3. **Clients who negotiate aggressively before seeing a proposal.** Price-focused buyers churn fast.
4. **Scope is already defined and fair.** If the price matches the work, hold firm.
5. **You are at capacity.** High demand = premium pricing.
6. **They are comparing you to freelancers.** Reframe: you are an agency with systems, not a solo contractor.

**What to say:**
- "Our pricing reflects the value and ongoing support included. Let me walk you through the ROI."
- "I understand budget is a factor. Let me show you what clients at this tier typically save in the first 90 days."
- "We can adjust scope to fit your budget, but our rates reflect the quality and reliability we deliver."

---

## When to Offer a Discount

### Acceptable Discount Scenarios

| Scenario | Max Discount | Condition |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| Annual prepay (Growth) | 2 months free (~17%) | Must pay full year upfront |
| Annual prepay (Enterprise) | 1 month free (~8%) | Must pay full year upfront |
| Referral reward | 1 month free for referrer | Referred client must sign and pay |
| Starter-to-Growth conversion | Waive Starter fee | Apply $500 as credit toward first Growth month |
| Case study permission | 10% off first 3 months | Client agrees to public case study with name/logo |
| Beta/early adopter | 15-20% off for 3 months | First 5 clients only, locked to initial term |
| Multi-service bundle | 10% off combined | Client buys 2+ distinct services |
| Nonprofit/social impact | 15-20% off | Verified nonprofit, ongoing only |

### How to Frame Discounts

Never say "discount." Use:
- "Early adopter rate"
- "Annual commitment pricing"
- "Partnership rate"
- "Introductory rate" (with clear expiration)
- "Bundled pricing"

Always attach a condition. Free value without a reason trains clients to always ask for less.

## Package Deals

### Starter + Growth Upgrade Path
- Client starts with Starter ($500)
- If they upgrade to Growth within 30 days, credit the $500 toward first month
- Growth first month becomes $1,500 (effective discount)
- Locks them into recurring revenue

### Growth Annual Plan
- Monthly: $2,000/mo ($24,000/year)
- Annual: $20,000/year (2 months free, paid quarterly or upfront)
- Minimum commitment: 12 months
- Early termination: Remaining balance due at full monthly rate

### Enterprise Custom Bundle
- Base: $5,000/mo for core automation management
- Add-on: Custom AI chatbot build (+$2,000-$5,000 one-time)
- Add-on: Data pipeline/integration (+$1,000-$3,000/mo)
- Add-on: Priority SLA with 4-hour response (+$1,000/mo)
- Bundle 2+ add-ons: 10% off the combined add-on price

### Launch Package (Limited Availability)
- For first 5 clients only
- Growth Plan at $1,600/mo for first 3 months, then $2,000/mo
- Must commit to minimum 6 months
- Includes one free Starter-level automation as onboarding bonus

## Negotiation Tactics

### The Scope Lever
If a client wants a lower price, reduce scope instead of reducing rate.

- "I can bring the monthly to $1,500, but we would manage 1 automation instead of 3."
- "At $3,500/mo instead of $5,000, I would remove the dedicated Slack channel and move to weekly check-ins."

This protects your rate while giving the client a way to fit their budget.

### The Value Reframe
When they say "that is too expensive":

1. Ask: "Compared to what?" (Understand their anchor)
2. Show the in-house alternative cost ($14K-$21K/mo for one engineer)
3. Show the ROI calculation (hours saved x hourly rate)
4. Show the opportunity cost (what are they losing by NOT automating?)

### The Walkaway
Sometimes the best deal is no deal. Walk away when:

- They want more than 20% off with no justification
- They are disrespectful about pricing
- The scope keeps expanding but the budget does not
- Your gut says they will be a problem client
- They want to "try for free first" (offer a paid pilot instead)

### The Paid Pilot
Instead of free trials, offer a paid pilot:

- 2-week pilot at $750 (Starter-adjacent scope)
- Builds one small automation as proof of concept
- Credit toward Growth plan if they continue
- Demonstrates value without giving away free work

## Discount Authority Matrix

| Decision | Who Decides |
|----------|------------|
| 0-10% discount | You (any valid reason) |
| 10-15% discount | You (must meet criteria above) |
| 15-20% discount | You (early adopter or nonprofit only) |
| >20% discount | Do not do it. Re-scope instead. |

---

## Red Flags in Negotiations

Watch for these and proceed with caution:

- "Can you do a free trial?" (Offer paid pilot instead)
- "My budget is only $X" before hearing the pitch (They may not value the service)
- "My last agency charged half that" (Ask why they left)
- "Can we start small and see?" (Fine, but define "small" with clear scope)
- "I will refer you to lots of people" (Referrals are earned, not promised)
- Constantly changing requirements during negotiation
- Wanting to skip the contract/agreement
