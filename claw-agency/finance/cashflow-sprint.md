## Executive Summary: The Cash Flow Reality

**Brutal truth:** Revenue and cash are NOT the same thing.

You can have $100K in "revenue" but $0 in the bank if:
- Clients pay NET-30 (you invoice today, get paid in 30 days)
- Clients pay in installments (50% now, 50% on completion)
- Clients don't pay at all (collections nightmare)

**Cash flow is survival.** Revenue is a vanity metric.

**This model shows:**
1. When cash actually hits your account
2. When expenses need to be paid
3. When you'll have cash crunches (and how to avoid them)
4. How payment terms make or break your business

## Payment Terms Strategy

### Recommended Terms (Optimized for Cash Flow)

| Tier | Payment Structure | Why |
|------|------------------|-----|
| **Starter** | 100% upfront (via Stripe) | Small amount, no negotiation |
| **Sprint** | 50% deposit, 50% on completion | Protects both parties, gets cash fast |
| **Growth Retainer** | Monthly via ACH (auto-charge) | Recurring, predictable |
| **Growth Retainer (prepay)** | 3 months upfront (10% discount) | Big cash injection |
| **Enterprise** | 50% deposit, 40% on completion, 10% NET-15 | Large amounts, need structure |

**Critical rule:** NO NET-30 or NET-60 terms for first 20 clients. You can't afford to wait 30-60 days for cash.

### Payment Processing Timelines

| Method | Client Pays | Cash in Your Account |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|
| Stripe (card) | Day 0 | Day 2 |
| Stripe (ACH) | Day 0 | Day 5-7 |
| Wire transfer | Day 0 | Day 1-2 |
| Check (avoid) | Day 0 | Day 7-10 (plus mail time) |

**Recommendation:** Push clients to ACH or wire for deals over $5K (lower fees, fast settlement).

## Payment Collection Strategy

### Deposits: The Cash Flow Lifeline

**For Sprint and Enterprise deals, ALWAYS collect 50% deposit before starting work.**

**Why:**
1. Protects you if client ghosts mid-project
2. Gets cash in hand immediately (day 1-2 via Stripe)
3. Proves client is serious (if they won't pay deposit, they won't pay final either)

**Script for deposit request:**
> "We require a 50% deposit to begin work. This reserves your spot and covers initial setup costs. The remaining 50% is due upon completion. I'll send the invoice today, and we can kick off as soon as it clears (1-2 business days)."

**Non-negotiable.** If they resist, they're not serious.

---

### Completion Payments: Don't Wait

**For Sprint and Enterprise deals, invoice final 50% THE DAY you deliver.**

**Why:**
1. Client is happiest at delivery (excitement = fast payment)
2. Longer you wait, harder it is to collect
3. Avoids "we'll pay you next month" trap

**Script for completion invoice:**
> "Great news! Your system is live and working beautifully. Here's the final invoice for the remaining 50% ($X). Per our agreement, this is due upon delivery. Let me know if you have any questions!"

**Follow up after 48 hours if unpaid.** After 7 days, pause support until payment clears.

---

### Retainer Auto-Billing: Set It and Forget It

**For Growth retainers, set up auto-charge via Stripe on a fixed date (1st of month or anniversary date).**

**Why:**
1. Client doesn't have to remember to pay
2. You don't have to chase invoices
3. Predictable cash flow (you know exactly when money hits)

**Setup:**
1. Client provides card or bank details
2. Stripe auto-charges monthly
3. If payment fails, Stripe retries 3 times, then notifies you

**Script for setup:**
> "For retainer clients, we auto-charge monthly via Stripe on the [1st/15th]. No invoices to track, no manual payments. Just seamless service. Sound good?"

---

### Late Payments: Act Fast

**If a client misses payment deadline:**

**Day 1:** Friendly reminder email
> "Hi [Name], just a heads-up that invoice #[X] for $[Y] was due yesterday. Let me know if you need me to resend it or if there are any issues!"

**Day 3:** More direct follow-up
> "Hi [Name], following up on invoice #[X] for $[Y]. This is now 3 days overdue. Can you confirm when I can expect payment?"

**Day 7:** Pause work
> "Hi [Name], invoice #[X] is now 7 days overdue. Per our agreement, I've paused work on your account until payment is received. Let me know how I can help resolve this."

**Day 14:** Collections or write-off
- For small amounts ($500-$2K): Write off, ban client
- For large amounts ($5K+): Send to collections or small claims court

**Prevention is better than cure:** Qualify clients better up front. If they haggle on price or delay deposit, walk away.

## Cash Crunch Scenarios (And How to Avoid Them)

### Scenario 1: Slow Sales Start (Weeks 1-4)

**Problem:** No deals close in first month, burning $8K-$10K in expenses and survival costs.

**Solution:**
1. **Have 60 days runway saved BEFORE you start** ($10K-$15K buffer)
2. **Reduce burn:** Live cheaply, minimize marketing spend, use free tools
3. **Close ANYTHING:** Lower price, offer Starter deals, just get cash flowing

**Emergency move:** Part-time job or freelance gigs to cover basics while building.

---

### Scenario 2: Clients Pay NET-30 (You Agreed to Terms)

**Problem:** Close $50K in deals by week 6, but won't see cash until week 10-11. Meanwhile, you have expenses NOW.

**Solution:**
1. **DON'T AGREE TO NET-30** (see payment terms above)
2. If you already did, negotiate early payment (offer 5% discount)
3. Use personal savings to bridge gap
4. Factor invoices (sell invoices to factoring company for 90-95% immediate cash)

**Prevention:** No NET-30 terms until you have $50K+ cash buffer.

---

### Scenario 3: Client Doesn't Pay Completion Invoice

**Problem:** Delivered $12K Sprint, client loves it, but won't pay final $6K ("We'll pay next month").

**Solution:**
1. **Pause support immediately** (per contract terms)
2. Send formal demand letter
3. File small claims if needed
4. Learn: Better client qualification next time

**Prevention:**
- Only work with clients who paid deposit fast (proof they have money)
- Check references (do they pay vendors on time?)
- Include late fees in contract (2% per week overdue)

---

### Scenario 4: Too Many Deals, Not Enough Capacity

**Problem:** Close 8 deals in week 6, but can only deliver 3. Rest are delayed 4-6 weeks, clients angry.

**Solution:**
1. **Hire BEFORE you're underwater** (week 4-5, not week 8)
2. **Delay start dates:** "We can start March 15" (close now, deliver later)
3. **Turn down low-value deals** (focus on high-margin Sprint and Enterprise)
4. **Refund if you can't deliver** (protect reputation)

**Prevention:** Track delivery capacity weekly. Stop selling when you're at 80% capacity until you hire.

---

### Scenario 5: Unexpected Expense (Legal, Tool Costs, Refund)

**Problem:** Client demands refund ($6K), or you get sued, or tool costs spike to $2K/mo.

**Solution:**
1. **Keep 20% cash buffer** (don't spend every dollar)
2. **Insurance:** E&O insurance covers lawsuits ($100/mo, worth it)
3. **Contracts protect you:** Refund only for YOUR failure, not client's change of mind

**Prevention:** 
- Airtight contracts (see legal folder)
- Set expectations clearly (no surprises = no refunds)
- Budget 10-20% more for expenses than you think (things always cost more)

## Cash Flow Optimization Tactics

### Tactic 1: Prepayment Discounts (Accelerate Cash)

**Offer:** "Pay 3 months upfront on Growth retainer, get 10% off"

**Impact:**
- Client pays $5,400 instead of $6,000 (3 x $2K)
- You get $5,400 TODAY instead of $2K/month over 3 months
- Net present value: Worth taking the discount for immediate cash

**When to use:** Weeks 4-8 when you're building cash reserves for hiring

---

### Tactic 2: Upsell at Completion (Capture Excitement)

**Timing:** Day of delivery, when client is thrilled with results

**Script:**
> "Glad you love it! Many clients at this stage add on [retainer/additional department/training]. Want to lock in a 3-month retainer now and keep the momentum going?"

**Impact:** Convert 40-50% of Sprint clients to retainers (doubles LTV)

---

### Tactic 3: Batch Invoicing (Predictable Cash Days)

**Strategy:** Invoice all monthly retainers on the 1st, all project completions on Fridays

**Impact:**
- Predictable cash inflows (you know Monday and 1st = cash hits)
- Easier to forecast and plan expenses
- Simplifies bookkeeping

---

### Tactic 4: Early Payment Incentives

**Offer:** "Pay final invoice within 24 hours, get 5% off"

**Impact:**
- $12K final invoice becomes $11,400 (you lose $600)
- But you get cash TODAY instead of waiting 7-30 days
- Reduces collections risk (bird in hand)

**When to use:** Large invoices ($10K+) where 5% is worth the liquidity

---

### Tactic 5: Subscription Model for Everything

**Convert one-time projects to recurring revenue wherever possible:**

- Sprint ($12K one-time) -> Sprint + 6-month support ($12K + $2K/mo x 6 = $24K)
- Enterprise ($25K one-time) -> Enterprise + 12-month retainer ($25K + $4K/mo x 12 = $73K)

**Impact:**
- Predictable monthly cash flow
- Higher LTV per client
- Easier to forecast hiring and expenses
