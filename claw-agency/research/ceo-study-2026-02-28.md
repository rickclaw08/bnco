### 1. Skinny Funnel Strategy (r/Entrepreneur)
- Don't chase 810 pitches to get 10 clients. Disqualify early and hard.
- Focus energy on deals with real close probability.

### 2. Cold Email Conversion Tactics (r/sales)
- Never send links, images, or attachments in first email. Plain text only.
- Offer to send a demo/lead magnet, wait for positive response, then deliver.
- Give specific times instead of calendar links (fewer friction, higher conversion).
- Position > Personalization. Clear pain point articulation beats generic personalization.
- Follow up with closed-lost prospects every few months with a subtle check-in.
- **Applied to ClawOps**: Our cold emails bounced because of bad addresses, not bad copy. When we get verified addresses, use plain text, no links first touch.

### 3. Text-Only Closing Observations
- Deals under $5K close over text regularly in B2B SaaS.
- The key is removing friction: don't make them click links, don't make them schedule calls.
- Bring the value to them in the conversation itself.
- **Applied to ClawOps**: Keep DM conversations substantive. Answer every question in the chat. Only link to demos after they ask or after building rapport.

### 4. Reddit DM Sales Pattern
- Post > Profile > DM is the conversion path (per earlier CMO research)
- Never pitch in the first message. Ask a question or share genuine insight.
- Good_luggage pattern worked: we helped first, pitched second, he came back with detailed questions = engaged buyer.
