# Discovery Call Script - ClawOps

## Pre-Call Prep (5 minutes)
- [ ] Review their website/LinkedIn
- [ ] Identify their likely pain points based on industry
- [ ] Check any previous touchpoints (email opens, website visits)
- [ ] Set intention: Learn their world, don't pitch yet

---

## Opening (2 minutes)

**"Hey [Name], this is Jordan from ClawOps. How are you doing?"**

*[Let them respond naturally]*

**"Thanks for taking the time. I know you're busy, so I'll keep this focused. I saw [specific detail about their business/recent event/growth], and wanted to learn more about how you're handling [relevant area] as you scale."**

**"Does that work? I have about 15-20 minutes blocked, but happy to adjust if you need more or less time."**

---

## Discovery Questions (10-12 minutes)

### Current State
**"Walk me through a typical day or week in your operation. What does your team spend most of their time on?"**

*Listen for: Repetitive tasks, bottlenecks, manual processes*

---

**"What are the 2-3 biggest time-sinks right now? The things that just eat up hours but have to get done?"**

*Listen for: Administrative work, data entry, customer communication, scheduling*

---

**"How many of those tasks require actual human judgment versus just following a process?"**

*This helps them see the automation opportunity*

---

### Pain & Impact
**"When [task they mentioned] doesn't happen on time or gets dropped, what's the ripple effect?"**

*Listen for: Lost revenue, unhappy customers, team stress, missed opportunities*

---

**"If you could wave a magic wand and eliminate one bottleneck in your business, what would it be and why?"**

*This reveals their #1 priority*

---

**"Have you tried solving this before? What did you try, and what got in the way?"**

*Listen for: Previous automation attempts, budget constraints, technical fears, bad experiences*

---

### Goals & Vision
**"Where do you want the business to be 12 months from now? What growth are you aiming for?"**

*Listen for: Revenue targets, client capacity, team size, new offerings*

---

**"What's stopping you from getting there today? If you had more time/capacity, what would you focus on?"**

*This reveals opportunity cost of current inefficiencies*

---

**"If we could give you back 10-15 hours per week, what would you do with that time?"**

*Helps them visualize the benefit personally*

---

## Qualification Check (Built into conversation)

**Budget Indicator:**
"Most of our clients invest between $500 and $5,000 per month depending on scope. Does that range make sense for solving [their pain point]?"

**Decision-Making:**
"Who else would need to be involved in a decision like this?" 
*If it's just them: Great. If others: "Should we get them on a follow-up call?"*

**Timeline:**
"When would you ideally want to have this solved by? Is this a 'nice to have eventually' or 'we need this now'?"

---

## Transition to Solution (2-3 minutes)

**"Okay, so let me play this back to make sure I'm hearing you right..."**

*Summarize:*
- Their biggest pain points (2-3 specific items)
- The impact those have on revenue/time/stress
- Their goal for the next 12 months
- What's at stake if nothing changes

**"Did I get that right? Anything I missed?"**

---

**"So here's what we do. We build custom AI automation systems that handle [their specific pain points]. For businesses like yours, we typically focus on [1-2 relevant use cases from their industry]."**

**"The way we work: We start with a pilot project focused on your biggest bottleneck, usually get it live within 2-3 weeks, and then expand from there. Most clients see ROI within the first 30-60 days."**

**"Does that approach make sense for what you're trying to solve?"**

---

## Next Steps (2 minutes)

**If they're interested:**

**"Great. Here's what I'd suggest: Let me put together a specific game plan for [their #1 pain point]. I'll map out exactly what we'd automate, how it would work, and what the timeline and investment would look like."**

**"Can we schedule 30 minutes next [specific day/time range] to walk through that? I'll send you the plan beforehand so you can review it."**

*Book the follow-up call immediately*

---

**If they're hesitant:**

**"No pressure at all. It sounds like [summarize their situation]. Even if we don't work together, I'm happy to send you a few quick wins you could implement yourself. Would that be helpful?"**

*Send value regardless, stay in touch*

---

**If they're not qualified (budget/timeline/fit):**

**"I appreciate your time. Based on what you've shared, it sounds like [honest assessment]. We might not be the right fit right now, but I'll keep you in mind if anything changes. Can I check back in [3/6 months]?"**

---

## Closing

**"Thanks again for your time, [Name]. I'll send you a calendar invite for our next call and [follow-up email with notes/value]. Sound good?"**

**"Have a great rest of your [day/week]!"**

---

## Post-Call (Immediately After)

- [ ] Update CRM with notes
- [ ] Send follow-up email within 1 hour
- [ ] Schedule next touchpoint
- [ ] Flag any urgent items or concerns
- [ ] If they mentioned a referral source, send thank you

---

## Key Principles

1. **Listen 70%, talk 30%** - This is discovery, not a pitch
2. **Ask follow-up questions** - "Tell me more about that" is your best friend
3. **Don't jump to solutions too fast** - Understand the full picture first
4. **Take notes during the call** - Shows you care and helps with follow-up
5. **Mirror their language** - Use their words for pain points, not jargon
6. **Be human** - This is a conversation between two business owners, not a script to read robotically

---

## Red Flags to Watch For

- **"We need to think about it"** (without specific next step) = Not urgent enough
- **"Send me information"** = Polite brush-off, they're not engaged
- **Multiple decision-makers not on the call** = You're talking to the wrong person
- **No clear pain point articulated** = They don't know why they're on the call
- **Budget is "we'll figure it out later"** = No budget allocated

**How to handle:** Address directly but kindly. "I want to be respectful of your time. It sounds like this might not be the right priority right now. Should we reconnect when [X is true]?"
