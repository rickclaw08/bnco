# Follow-Up Email Sequence - ClawOps

## Sequence Overview
- **Email 1:** Immediate post-discovery call (same day)
- **Email 2:** Value + case study (Day 3)
- **Email 3:** Quick win idea (Day 7)
- **Email 4:** Social proof + urgency (Day 12)
- **Email 5:** Final check-in / breakup (Day 18)

**Note:** Adjust timing based on their stated timeline and urgency. If they said "we need this now," compress the sequence. If they said "revisit in 3 months," space it out or pause.

---

## Email 1: Post-Discovery Thank You
**Send:** Within 1-2 hours of the call  
**Goal:** Recap conversation, provide value, confirm next steps

**Subject:** Great talking today, [Name]

**Body:**

Hi [Name],

Thanks for taking the time today. I really enjoyed learning about [their business] and how you're handling [specific challenge they mentioned].

Just to recap what we discussed:

**Your current situation:**
- [Pain point 1 they mentioned]
- [Pain point 2 they mentioned]
- [Impact/cost of those issues]

**Your goal:**
- [Their stated objective, e.g., "Add 20% more clients without hiring"]

**What we discussed:**
- [Brief summary of solution/approach]
- [Estimated timeline]
- [Next step, e.g., "I'll send you a custom plan by Friday"]

**In the meantime**, I thought you might find this helpful: [Link to relevant resource, case study, or quick tip related to their specific pain point]

I'll follow up on [specific date] with [what you promised]. If anything comes up before then, just hit reply.

Thanks again,  
Jordan

---

## Email 2: Value + Case Study
**Send:** Day 3 after the call (if no response to Email 1)  
**Goal:** Provide social proof and remind them of value

**Subject:** How [Similar Business] solved [Their Pain Point]

**Body:**

Hey [Name],

Been thinking about your situation with [their specific challenge]. Reminded me of a client we worked with last year.

**The situation:**  
[Client Name/Type] was dealing with [similar pain point]. They were spending [X hours/week] on [task] and it was [specific negative impact].

**What we built:**  
[Brief description of the solution, 2-3 sentences]

**The results:**  
- Saved [X hours/week]
- [Specific metric, e.g., "Response time went from 4 hours to 15 minutes"]
- [Business impact, e.g., "Closed 30% more deals that quarter"]

Full case study here: [link]

Not saying your situation is identical, but thought you'd find it interesting.

Still planning to send over that custom plan for [their business] by [date you promised]. Let me know if you have questions in the meantime.

Jordan

## Email 3: Quick Win Idea
**Send:** Day 7 (if no response)  
**Goal:** Give them something actionable they can do themselves (builds trust, stays top of mind)

**Subject:** Quick win for [Their Business]

**Body:**

[Name],

Even if we don't end up working together, wanted to share something you could implement this week to help with [their pain point].

**The problem:**  
[Their specific issue, e.g., "You mentioned leads wait too long for a response"]

**Quick fix you can do yourself:**  
[Specific, actionable advice, 3-5 steps, e.g.,  
1. Set up an auto-reply email using [tool they already have]  
2. Include [specific elements]  
3. Track [metric] to measure impact]

This won't solve everything, but it'll give you some immediate relief while you figure out the bigger automation picture.

If you want help implementing this (or the full solution we discussed), just let me know.

Hope this helps,  
Jordan

**Why this works:** You're providing value without asking for anything. Positions you as helpful, not pushy.

---

## Email 4: Social Proof + Gentle Urgency
**Send:** Day 12 (if no response)  
**Goal:** Create FOMO and remind them of opportunity cost

**Subject:** Thought you should see this

**Body:**

[Name],

Quick update: We just wrapped a pilot project for another [their industry] business. They started 3 weeks ago and are already seeing [specific result, e.g., "10 hours back per week"].

Here's the thing that stood out to me, they were dealing with the exact same [pain point] you mentioned on our call.

**Their words:** *"[Testimonial quote or specific result story]"*

I know you're still thinking through whether this makes sense for [their business]. No pressure at all.

But I wanted to share this because I'd hate for you to be dealing with [their pain point] six months from now while your competitors are running lean with automation.

If you want to hop on a quick call to revisit this, I've got time this week. Or if now's not the right time, just let me know when makes sense.

Jordan

**P.S.** If you're curious what we built for them, happy to show you a demo. Takes 10 minutes.

---

## Email 5: Breakup Email (Permission to Close the Loop)
**Send:** Day 18-21 (if no response)  
**Goal:** Get a clear yes/no, or permission to follow up later

**Subject:** Should I close your file?

**Body:**

Hey [Name],

I've reached out a few times and haven't heard back, which tells me one of three things:

1. **Not the right time** - You're swamped, and this isn't a priority right now (totally fair)
2. **Not interested** - This isn't the right fit for [their business] (also fair)
3. **My emails are going to spam** - Technology, am I right?

I don't want to be that person who keeps emailing forever, so I'll leave it here.

**If you're still interested**, just reply and we'll pick up where we left off. I'll send over a plan whenever you're ready.

**If now's not the time**, no hard feelings at all. I'll check back in [3/6 months] to see if anything's changed. Sound good?

**If this just isn't a fit**, let me know and I'll stop bothering you. I promise I won't be offended.

Either way, I really appreciated learning about [their business] and wish you the best with [specific thing they were working on].

Jordan

---

**Why this works:**
- Acknowledges reality (they're not responding)
- Gives them an easy out (reduces pressure)
- Often triggers a response because it's respectful and different from typical sales emails
- If they don't respond to THIS, they're really not interested and you can move on guilt-free

## Post-Sequence Strategy

### If They Still Don't Respond:
- Add them to a **long-term nurture list**
- Send valuable content every 4-6 weeks (industry insights, case studies, tips)
- Check in every quarter with a simple "Hey, wanted to see if anything's changed"
- Watch for trigger events (they post about hiring, growth, new funding, etc.) and reach out contextually

### If They Respond Positively:
- Move to proposal/close phase immediately
- Schedule a call within 48 hours
- Strike while the iron is hot

### If They Say "Not Now":
- **Ask when:** "No problem at all. When should I check back in? 3 months? 6 months?"
- **Set a reminder** and actually follow up
- Send them valuable content in the meantime to stay top of mind

## Sequence Customization Tips

### High-Intent Prospects (Said "yes, let's do this"):
- Skip Email 2-5
- Send Email 1, then send proposal/contract within 24-48 hours
- Don't over-communicate when they're ready to buy

### Medium-Intent (Interested but hesitant):
- Use full sequence as written
- Focus on building trust and demonstrating value
- Adjust timing based on their stated urgency

### Low-Intent (Exploring options, no urgency):
- Space out the sequence (every 5-7 days instead of 3-4)
- Focus more on education than closing
- Set expectations for a longer sales cycle

## Email Best Practices

### Keep it short:
- 150 words or less per email
- One main idea per email
- Easy to skim on mobile

### Write like a human:
- No corporate jargon
- Use "you" and "I," not "we" and "our clients"
- Conversational tone (like you're texting a colleague)

### Clear call-to-action:
- One ask per email
- Make it easy to respond ("just reply to this email")
- Low friction (don't ask them to fill out forms or jump through hoops)

### Subject lines that work:
- Personal and specific (use their name or business name)
- Curiosity without clickbait
- Short (50 characters or less for mobile)

### What to avoid:
- ALL CAPS or excessive exclamation points!!!
- Pushy language ("limited time offer," "act now")
- Walls of text
- Multiple links (looks spammy)
- Talking about yourself instead of them

## Tracking & Optimization

**Measure these metrics:**
- Open rates (aim for 40%+)
- Reply rates (aim for 15-20%)
- Conversion to next step (call booked, proposal requested)

**Test and iterate:**
- A/B test subject lines
- Try different value offers in Email 3
- Adjust timing based on response patterns
- Note which emails get replies and double down on that style

**CRM tracking:**
- Tag which sequence email they're on
- Note their engagement level (opened but didn't reply vs. didn't open at all)
- Track responses and adjust follow-up accordingly

- Your authentic voice
