# GHL Blueprint Value Post - HVAC Angle

## Title:
465 AI cold calls to HVAC companies. $60 spent. 0 demos. Here's what I learned.

## Body:

I spent 3 weeks building an AI voice agent on VAPI + GHL to cold call HVAC companies and sell them AI receptionists.

Here are the actual numbers:
- 465 outbound calls
- $60 in VAPI/telephony costs
- 0 demos booked
- 0 closes

What went wrong (so you don't repeat it):

**1. Cold calling HVAC owners with AI to sell them AI is ironic.**
They don't want to talk to a robot. Their customers don't want to talk to a robot. Leading with "hey, want an AI phone system?" to someone who just got an AI cold call is a tough sell.

**2. No one picks up cold calls from unknown numbers.**
Connection rate was under 15%. Of those, most hung up within 10 seconds. The ones who stayed usually said "not interested" or "send me an email."

**3. Direct-to-business is the wrong buyer.**
HVAC owners are busy running crews, not evaluating software. The actual buyer is the marketing agency that already manages their leads, their website, their GHL account.

**What I'm doing differently:**
Building a white-label AI voice receptionist that agencies can resell to their HVAC clients. The agency handles the relationship. I handle the tech. Everyone makes money.

If you're running an agency that serves home service businesses (especially HVAC), I'd be curious to hear how you're handling AI voice for your clients - or if you even are yet.

Not pitching anything. Genuinely want to know what's working in this space.
