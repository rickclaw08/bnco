# Sales Playbook — Claw Agency

## Overview

We sell AI automation services to small and mid-size businesses. Our sales cycle is 7–21 days. Every deal follows: **Lead → Qualify → Discovery → Propose → Close → Onboard.**

---

## 1. Lead Qualification (BANT Framework)

Score each criterion 1–3. Minimum score of 8/12 to pursue.

| Criteria | 1 (Weak) | 2 (Moderate) | 3 (Strong) |
|----------|----------|--------------|-------------|
| **Budget** | No budget discussed, price-sensitive signals | Has budget but unclear allocation | Confirmed budget ≥ our minimum ($500+) |
| **Authority** | Talking to an employee with no buying power | Manager/director, needs approval | Owner, founder, or C-suite — decides alone |
| **Need** | Vague interest in "AI" | Identified a pain point but no urgency | Clear bottleneck costing them time/money NOW |
| **Timeline** | "Maybe next quarter" | Within 60 days | Within 30 days or immediate |

### Disqualification signals (walk away):
- No measurable problem to solve
- Budget under $500 and unwilling to invest
- "Just exploring" with no timeline
- Wants to own/build internally and just wants free consulting

## 2. Discovery Call Script (25–30 minutes)

### Opening (2 min)
> "Hey [Name], thanks for taking the time. I'll keep this tight — my goal is to understand what you're dealing with and see if we can actually help. If we can't, I'll tell you. Sound good?"

### Situation Questions (5 min)
- "Walk me through your current workflow for [area they mentioned]."
- "How many people touch that process?"
- "What tools are you using today?"

### Problem Questions (8 min)
- "Where does it break down?"
- "How much time does your team spend on [manual task] per week?"
- "What happens when it goes wrong — what's the cost?"
- "Have you tried to fix this before? What happened?"

### Impact Questions (5 min)
- "If you could get those [X] hours back every week, what would your team do instead?"
- "What does this problem cost you per month — in dollars, time, or missed opportunities?"
- "How does this affect your ability to scale?"

### Vision & Fit (5 min)
- "Here's what I'm thinking we could do..." *(brief, verbal-only — no screen share yet)*
- "We've done something similar for [reference client]. They went from [before] to [after]."
- "Does that sound like it would move the needle for you?"

### Next Steps (3 min)
- "Here's what I'd suggest: I'll put together a short proposal with scope, timeline, and pricing. Can we block 15 minutes on [date] to walk through it?"
- Confirm decision-maker will be on the next call
- Send calendar invite before hanging up

## 3. Objection Handling

### "It's too expensive."
> "I hear you. Let me ask — what's this problem costing you right now in time or money? ... So you're spending [$X/month] on this. Our solution pays for itself in [timeframe]. It's not a cost, it's an offset."

### "We don't trust AI / it's too new."
> "Totally fair. We're not asking you to trust AI blindly. We build specific, tested automations for your workflow — you approve every step. And we start small so you see results before committing more. Zero risk."

### "We can build it ourselves."
> "You absolutely could. The question is — should you? Your team's time is worth [$X/hr]. We do this every day and can have it running in [timeframe]. Building internally takes 3–5x longer and pulls your team off revenue-generating work."

### "I need to think about it."
> "Of course. What specifically do you want to think through? ... Let me address that now so you have everything you need."

### "We tried automation before and it didn't work."
> "What happened? ... That's actually common with [generic tool / bad vendor]. We do it differently — we build around YOUR workflow, not force you into a template. And we support it ongoing so it doesn't break."

### "Can you just do a free trial / proof of concept?"
> "We don't do free work, but our Starter package at $500 is designed exactly for this — one focused automation so you can see the quality and results before going bigger. Lowest possible risk."

---

## 4. Closing Techniques

### The Assumptive Close
> "Great — I'll send the contract over today. Would you prefer to start next Monday or the following week?"

### The Summary Close
> "So to recap: we're automating [X], which saves you [Y hours/week], and the investment is [Z]. Everything look right? Let's get started."

### The Urgency Close (use sparingly, only when real)
> "We have capacity for two new projects this month. After that, onboarding pushes to [date]. Want to lock in your slot?"

### The Puppy Dog Close (for hesitant buyers)
> "Tell you what — let's start with the Starter package. $500, one automation. If you love it, we scale up. If not, you still got a working automation out of it."

## 5. Follow-Up Cadence

After sending a proposal or after any meeting where next steps are pending:

| Day | Action | Channel |
|-----|--------|---------|
| **Day 1** | Send proposal + personal note: "Here's everything we discussed. Happy to jump on a quick call if any questions." | Email |
| **Day 3** | Follow up: "Hey [Name], wanted to make sure you had a chance to review. Any questions I can answer?" | Email or LinkedIn DM |
| **Day 7** | Value-add touch: share a relevant case study, article, or quick insight related to their problem | Email |
| **Day 14** | Direct ask: "Are you still looking to move forward on this? Happy to adjust scope if needed." | Phone call or voice note |
| **Day 30** | Final touch: "Circling back one last time. If timing isn't right, no worries — I'll check in next quarter." | Email |

### Rules:
- Never follow up more than 5 times without a response
- Always add value — never send "just checking in"
- If they go dark after Day 30, move to nurture list (monthly newsletter / content)
- If they say no, ask: "Mind if I ask what tipped the decision? Helps us improve."

## 6. Sales Metrics to Track

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Discovery calls / week | 5+ |
| Proposal sent rate | 60% of discovery calls |
| Close rate | 30% of proposals |
| Average deal size | $2,000+ |
| Time to close | < 14 days |
| Follow-up response rate | > 40% |

---

## 7. Handoff to Delivery

Once a deal closes:
1. Signed contract + payment received
2. Send client welcome email (template in onboarding/)
3. Schedule kickoff call within 48 hours
4. Create project in PM tool with scope from proposal
5. Introduce client to their point of contact

**Rule: Sales owns the relationship until kickoff is complete.**
