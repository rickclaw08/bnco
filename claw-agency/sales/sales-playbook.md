# Sales Playbook - ClawOps v2.0
**Updated:** February 24, 2026  
**Vertical:** Home Services  
**Positioning:** "Never miss a lead again"

---

## Overview

We sell AI-powered revenue operations to home service businesses (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, landscaping). Our flagship product is the AI Receptionist & Lead Capture System. Our sales cycle is 7-14 days. Every deal follows:

**Lead → Qualify → AI Readiness Audit ($500) → Propose → Close → Deliver**

---

## 1. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**Who we sell to:**
- Home service business (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, landscaping, junk removal, pest control, cleaning)
- Revenue: $500K-$10M/year
- 3-50 employees
- Owner-operated or small management team
- Located in warm-climate metros (TX, FL, AZ, GA)
- Currently missing calls during business hours
- Using basic or no CRM (ServiceTitan, Jobber, Housecall Pro, or pen and paper)

**Who we DON'T sell to:**
- Solopreneurs under $250K revenue (can't afford us, high churn risk)
- Franchises with corporate-mandated tech stacks (can't integrate)
- Businesses that "just want to try AI" with no real problem
- Anyone who pushes back on $500 for the audit (not serious)

## 2. Lead Qualification (Updated BANT)

Score each criterion 1-3. Minimum score of 8/12 to pursue.

| Criteria | 1 (Walk Away) | 2 (Maybe) | 3 (Go) |
|----------|--------------|-----------|---------|
| **Budget** | "That sounds expensive" with no follow-up | Has budget, wants to see ROI first | Already spending $3K+ on answering services or admin staff |
| **Authority** | Office manager, no buying power | Part-owner, needs partner approval | Owner/sole decision-maker |
| **Need** | "We answer most calls" | "We miss some calls but it's manageable" | "I know we're losing leads, I just can't be on the phone on job sites" |
| **Timeline** | "Maybe in a few months" | "After this busy season" | "Yesterday. We need this now." |

### Instant Qualifiers (Skip scoring, these are gold):
- "We're paying Ruby/Smith.ai $X/month and it sucks"
- "I missed 3 calls today while I was on a roof"
- "My competitor just got 50 Google reviews and I have 12"
- "I hired a receptionist but she quit"

### Disqualification Signals:
- Wants free work or proof of concept before any commitment
- Can't articulate a specific problem (just "curious about AI")
- Under $250K revenue (check truck fleet, review count, service area as proxies)
- Wants to build it themselves (send them to n8n tutorials, wish them luck)

## 3. Sales Process

### Step 1: Cold Outreach
- Use templates from outreach-home-services.md
- Target: 50 emails/week to home service businesses
- Channels: Cold email (primary), LinkedIn (secondary), Reddit (long game)
- Goal: Book AI Readiness Audit calls

### Step 2: AI Readiness Audit ($500 - Paid Discovery)

**Before the call:**
- Conduct mystery call to their business (call as a fake customer)
- Note: time to answer, who answered, quality of intake, follow-up received
- Check their Google reviews, website, social presence
- Research their competitors' online presence

**The call (45 minutes):**

**Opening (3 min):**
> "Hey [Name], thanks for doing this. Here's how this works - I'm going to ask you some questions about how leads come in and what happens to them. Then I'm going to show you something that might piss you off a little - we actually called your business as a fake customer to test your response. At the end, I'll give you a clear picture of where you're losing money and what to do about it. Sound good?"

**Current State (10 min):**
- "Walk me through what happens when a customer calls during business hours."
- "What about after hours? Weekends?"
- "How do you follow up with someone who left a voicemail?"
- "How many calls would you guess you miss per week?"
- "What's your average job value?"
- "How are you getting reviews right now?"

**The Mystery Call Reveal (10 min):**
> "So here's what happened when we called [COMPANY] on [DATE] at [TIME]..."
> [Walk through the experience. Be honest but not cruel.]
> "If I was a real customer with a [broken AC / burst pipe / etc], what would I have done? Called the next company on Google."

**Impact (10 min):**
- "Based on what you told me - [X] missed calls/week at an average job of $[Y] - you're leaving roughly $[Z] on the table every month."
- "Your competitors with 200+ Google reviews are getting the leads you're not. How do they have that many? They're automating the ask."
- "If you could answer every call in 2 seconds, 24/7, and automatically book appointments - what would that do to your revenue?"

**Recommendation (10 min):**
> "Here's what I recommend for [COMPANY]..."
> [Present AI Receptionist or Sprint, depending on their situation]
> [Show the pricing, show the ROI math]
> "You're currently losing $[X]/month. This system costs $[Y]/month. That's a [Z]:1 return."

**Close (2 min):**
> "I can have this live in 2 weeks. Want to get started?"

### Step 3: Proposal & Close

If they need to "think about it":
- Send written proposal within 2 hours of the call
- Include: the Revenue Leak Report, recommended solution, pricing, ROI math, timeline
- Follow up next day: "Any questions about the proposal?"
- Follow up Day 3: Share a relevant case study or testimonial
- Day 5: Direct ask - "Ready to stop losing those calls?"

### Step 4: Onboarding

Once closed:
1. Payment received (setup fee)
2. Kickoff call within 48 hours
3. Collect: business info, services offered, pricing, availability, current CRM access
4. Build and test AI system (5-7 business days)
5. Live demo with owner before going live
6. Go live with 2-week tuning period
7. 30-day check-in to optimize and upsell

## 4. Objection Handling (Home Services Specific)

### "I don't trust AI to talk to my customers."
> "Totally fair. Here's the thing - the AI isn't replacing you. It's catching the calls you CAN'T answer because you're on a job site. Right now, those calls go to voicemail and 80% of those people never call back. The AI answers, gets their info, and books the appointment. You still do the estimate, the job, and the relationship. It just makes sure you don't lose the lead."

### "$300/month? I can just hire someone."
> "A part-time receptionist is $2,000-$3,000/month, works 8 hours a day, takes lunch breaks, calls in sick, and quits every 6 months. Our AI works 24/7/365 for $300/month. And it never forgets to ask for a Google review."

### "I'm not a tech person. I don't want something complicated."
> "Perfect - that's exactly who we built this for. You don't touch anything. We set it up, we manage it, we optimize it. You just see more appointments on your calendar and more reviews on Google. If you can check your email, you can use this."

### "My son/nephew/office manager can set this up."
> "Maybe. But will they maintain it? Optimize it? Fix it when it breaks at 2 AM on a Saturday? This is what we do all day. Your son should be doing whatever makes your business the most money - and it's not debugging phone systems."

### "We tried something like this and it didn't work."
> "What did you try? ... Yeah, [Grasshopper / Google Voice / some IVR system] isn't the same thing. Those are phone trees. Ours is an AI that actually has a conversation with the caller - asks what they need, checks your availability, and books the appointment. Want me to call you right now and you can hear the difference?"

### "Business is slow right now, bad timing."
> "When business is slow, every single lead matters even more. You can't afford to miss a $3,000 job because you were under a house. This is actually the best time to set it up - get it dialed in before busy season hits and you're slammed."

## 5. Discovery Call Script (Quick Version - For Inbound Leads)

If someone responds to outreach and wants to learn more (not ready for the full audit):

**15-minute qualification call:**

> "Hey [Name], thanks for getting back to me. Quick question before we dive in - what made you respond to my email?"
> [Listen. Their answer tells you everything.]
>
> "Got it. So how are you handling incoming calls right now when you're on a job?"
> [Listen.]
>
> "And roughly how many calls would you say go to voicemail per week?"
> [Listen. Calculate the math out loud.]
>
> "So at [X] missed calls times your average job of $[Y], that's roughly $[Z]/month in potential revenue that's just... gone. Does that number feel right?"
> [Let them confirm or correct.]
>
> "Here's what I'd suggest. We do a $500 AI Readiness Audit - we actually call your business as a fake customer, measure your response time, and put together a full report on where you're losing leads and exactly how to fix it. Takes about a week, and if you move forward with our system, we credit the $500 toward setup. Sound fair?"
