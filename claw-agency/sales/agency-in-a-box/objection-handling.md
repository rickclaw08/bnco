# Objection Handling Guide — Agency-in-a-Box

## The 10 Objections You'll Hear (And How to Kill Each One)

---

### 1. "GHL already has Voice AI built in. Why would I pay for something else?"

**The Reality Check:**
> "You're right — GHL has Voice AI. And you've probably seen the subreddit. Half the posts are agencies fighting consent errors, ghost opt-outs, Voice AI ignoring custom fields, and workflows breaking when two automations touch the same contact. GHL's Voice AI is a feature they bolted on. Ours is a purpose-built system. We handle the compliance, the voice quality, the CRM sync, and the edge cases so you don't have to debug prompts at 2am. Your time is worth more than fighting GHL support tickets."

**Key stat:** GHL's r/GoHighLevel community (17,000+ members) is flooded with Voice AI troubleshooting posts. This is a known pain point.

---

### 2. "$5,000 is a lot of money upfront."

**The Math Response:**
> "I get it. Let's do the math real quick. If you charge just $400/mo to 10 clients — which is conservative for what this does — that's $4,000/mo in revenue against $1,000/mo in cost to us. $3,000/mo profit. Your $5,000 investment pays back in under 2 months. And after that, it's pure margin. Compare that to hiring one employee at $4,000/mo who can answer maybe 40 calls a day and calls in sick."

**If they push back further:**
> "What's the alternative? Build it yourself? You're looking at $10-15K in development and 3-6 months of your time. Or use GHL's native Voice AI and spend 10+ hours per client troubleshooting. $5,000 for a done-for-you, white-labeled system with ongoing support is the cheapest path to revenue."

---

### 3. "My clients won't pay $400-500/mo for an AI answering their phone."

**The Reframe:**
> "They're not paying for AI. They're paying for never missing a call again. Ask any of your HVAC, dental, or legal clients: what's one new customer worth? $500? $2,000? $5,000? If the AI books just ONE extra appointment per month, it's ROI-positive. Most businesses are missing 30-60% of their calls right now. You're not selling technology — you're selling recovered revenue."

**Anchor it lower if needed:**
> "Start at $297/mo if your market is price-sensitive. You still keep $197/mo per client. 15 clients at that rate = $2,955/mo in pure margin. That's an extra $35K/year from a service that takes zero ongoing effort from you."

---

### 4. "What if my clients don't like talking to AI?"

**The Data Response:**
> "Two things. First: 73% of consumers now prefer interacting with AI for routine tasks like booking appointments (Salesforce State of the Connected Customer, 2025). Second: the AI doesn't announce 'Hi, I'm a robot.' It answers naturally, with a custom voice that matches the business. Most callers don't realize — and don't care — because their question gets answered and their appointment gets booked. The ones who want a human get transferred instantly. Nobody falls through the cracks."

**The positioning:**
> "Frame it to your clients as a 'smart receptionist' or 'virtual front desk,' not 'AI phone bot.' Language matters."

---

### 5. "I can build this myself with Vapi/Retell/Bland and keep 100% of the margin."

**The Time-vs-Money Response:**
> "You absolutely can. And some agencies do. Here's what that looks like: 2-3 months building the voice pipeline, debugging call flows, handling Twilio compliance, building the CRM sync, creating the client dashboard, and then maintaining all of it forever. Every API change, every edge case, every bug — that's your problem.

> Or you can be live in 5 days, deploy to your first client next week, and never touch infrastructure again. The question isn't whether you CAN build it — it's whether building voice AI infrastructure is the best use of your next 500 hours. You're an agency. Your superpower is selling and servicing clients, not maintaining telephony infrastructure."

---

### 6. "What happens if the AI says something wrong or embarrassing to a client's customer?"

**The Safety Net Response:**
> "Three layers of protection. First: every call flow is configured with guardrails — the AI knows exactly what it can and can't say, and escalates to a human when it's unsure. Second: every call is recorded and transcribed. You and your client can review any interaction. Third: we have a 'confidence threshold' — if the AI isn't sure about something, it doesn't guess. It says 'Let me connect you with someone who can help with that' and routes the call. The risk isn't the AI saying something wrong. The risk is the phone ringing to voicemail and the lead going to a competitor."

---

### 7. "I don't have enough clients to justify this."

**The Growth Play:**
> "How many clients do you have right now? Even 5? If you deploy this to just 5 existing clients at $400/mo, that's $2,000/mo in new revenue with $1,500/mo in margin. But here's the real play: this becomes your LEAD MAGNET for new clients.

> Agencies in the GHL ecosystem are fighting over the same services — funnels, websites, automations, review management. Every agency offers that. Almost nobody is offering AI receptionist as a service. You become the agency that guarantees 'every call answered.' That's a differentiator that wins new business, not just a revenue add-on."

---

### 8. "How is this different from a virtual receptionist service like Smith.ai or Ruby?"

**The Comparison:**
> "Three differences. First: COST. Smith.ai charges $292-$1,170/mo retail with no resale margin for you. Our system costs you $100/mo per client and you sell it at whatever you want. Second: SCALE. Human receptionist services have capacity limits, scheduling issues, and turnover. AI handles unlimited concurrent calls, 24/7, with zero sick days. Third: INTEGRATION. Human receptionist services don't plug into GHL. Our system syncs everything — contacts, tags, pipelines, appointments — in real time. Your clients see it all in one place."

---

### 9. "What about non-English callers? My clients serve diverse communities."

**The Multilingual Response:**
> "The AI supports 20+ languages with natural-sounding voices in each. It can detect the caller's language and switch automatically, or be configured for specific languages based on the business. A lot of GHL agencies serve med spas, dental offices, and legal firms in bilingual markets — this handles that natively. Try doing that with a $15/hr receptionist."

---

### 10. "I want to try it on one client first before committing to the full setup."

**The Pilot Program:**
> "Smart. We actually encourage that. Here's what we can do: full setup on your brand, we deploy to your first client together, and you see the results for 30 days. If you're not confident it's going to work for your agency after seeing real calls answered and real appointments booked, we'll work with you to make it right.

> But I'll be honest — the agencies that commit to deploying to 3-5 clients out of the gate see ROI fastest because they spread the setup cost and build momentum. Your call."

## CLOSING FRAMEWORK

When you've handled the objections and the prospect is warm, use this close:

### The "Who's Your Worst Client" Close:
> "Let me ask you this: which of your clients complains the most about not getting enough leads? The one where you KNOW the marketing is working but they say 'we're not getting calls'? What if you pulled their call data and showed them they're missing 40% of inbound calls — and then said 'I can fix this for $400/mo'? Who is that client?"

*Wait for the answer. They always have one. That's your first deployment.*

### The Scarcity Close:
> "We're onboarding 5 agency partners this month. Each partner gets hands-on deployment support for their first 3 clients. After that, it's self-serve onboarding. If you want the white-glove treatment, this week is the time."

### The "What Changes" Close:
> "Look, you can keep doing what you're doing. Your clients' phones will keep ringing to voicemail. You'll keep offering the same services every other GHL agency offers. Or you can add a new revenue stream that none of your competitors have, with technology you don't have to build or maintain. What sounds better?"

---

## BONUS: DISQUALIFICATION TRIGGERS

Walk away from prospects who:
- Have fewer than 3 clients (they need more runway first)
- Want to "think about it" for more than 2 weeks (they're not ready)
- Ask for free trials longer than 30 days (they're tire-kickers)
- Don't currently use GHL or any CRM (wrong market)
- Want to negotiate the per-seat price below $75/mo (margin doesn't work)

Your time is better spent finding agencies that are ready to move.
