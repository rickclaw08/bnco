## Template 1: Website Security Scan Results (Cold Outreach)

**Context:** You ran a scan on their website and found real issues. This is a cold email, but it doesn't feel cold because you're leading with specific, personalized findings about THEIR business. This is the highest-converting cold email in the toolkit.

**When to send:** Monday-Thursday, 8am-10am in their timezone. Never Friday or weekend.

**Prerequisites:** Actually scan their site first. Never fake findings. Use real data or don't send the email.

---

**Subject:** I found 3 issues on [COMPANY_WEBSITE] that are costing you customers

**Alternate subjects:**
- [FIRST_NAME], your website has a problem you probably don't know about
- Quick heads up about [COMPANY_WEBSITE]

---

```
Hi [FIRST_NAME],

I was researching [THEIR_INDUSTRY] businesses in [THEIR_CITY] and came across [COMPANY_NAME]. Ran a quick technical scan on your site out of curiosity and a few things jumped out.

Here's what I found:
```

## Template 4: Follow-Up After No Response (3-Touch Sequence)

**Context:** They didn't reply to your first email. Most sales happen after the follow-up, not the initial outreach. This is a 3-email sequence spaced over 10 days.

---

### Touch 1 - The Bump (3 Days After Original Email)

**Subject:** Re: [ORIGINAL_SUBJECT_LINE]

```
Hi [FIRST_NAME],

Just floating this back to the top of your inbox in case it got buried. I know [THEIR_INDUSTRY] owners are busy, especially this time of year.

The quick version: I found [NUMBER] issues with [COMPANY_WEBSITE/COMPANY_NAME's call handling] that are likely costing you customers. I put together a free analysis - happy to share it if you're interested.

Takes 2 minutes to review. Worth it if even one of the findings saves you a lost customer.

Let me know.

[YOUR_NAME]
```

---

### Touch 2 - The Value Add (7 Days After Original Email)

**Subject:** Quick tip for [COMPANY_NAME] (no reply needed)

```
[FIRST_NAME],

Not going to keep emailing you about the audit, but I did want to share one thing that might help regardless:

[INSERT ONE GENUINELY USEFUL TIP RELATED TO THEIR SITUATION]

Examples:
- "I noticed your Google Business profile doesn't have any Q&As filled out. Adding 5-10 common questions and answers takes 10 minutes and can significantly improve your local search visibility."
- "Your website's mobile menu is cutting off the phone number. Quick CSS fix - any web developer can do it in 5 minutes."
- "You're listed on Yelp but haven't claimed the business. That means Yelp can put competitor ads on YOUR listing page. Claiming it is free."

Free tip, no strings. If you ever want the full audit, the offer stands.

[YOUR_NAME]
```

---

### Touch 3 - The Breakup (10 Days After Original Email)

**Subject:** Closing the loop on [COMPANY_NAME]

```
[FIRST_NAME],

This is my last email on this. I've reached out a couple times about the issues I found with [COMPANY_NAME]'s online presence and call handling, and I'm guessing the timing just isn't right.

Totally respect that. I'll close out my notes on [COMPANY_NAME] and won't follow up again.

If things change down the road - maybe you notice you're missing more calls, or a competitor starts eating your lunch online - my inbox is open. Just reply to any of these emails and I'll pick back up.

Hope business is good either way.

[YOUR_NAME]
ClawOps
[PHONE]
```

**Note:** The breakup email gets the highest response rate of the three. About 15-20% of people who ignored the first two will reply to this one. Loss aversion is a powerful motivator.

## Email Deliverability Rules

1. **Never send more than 30 cold emails per day from one inbox.** Warm up new domains slowly (5/day for week 1, 10/day for week 2, etc.).
2. **Personalize the first line.** Email providers can detect batch-and-blast. Unique openers improve deliverability.
3. **Keep emails under 200 words for cold outreach.** Long emails get skimmed or ignored. Save the detail for follow-ups.
4. **No images in cold emails.** They trigger spam filters. Plain text performs better.
5. **One link maximum in cold emails.** Zero links is even better. Let them reply instead of click.
6. **Use a real reply-to address.** Not a no-reply. Not a generic info@ address. Use your name.
7. **Test subject lines.** Send Version A to 50% of the list, Version B to the other 50%. Use the winner going forward.
8. **Track opens and replies, not clicks.** Opens tell you if your subject line works. Replies tell you if your message works. Clicks are a vanity metric for cold email.
