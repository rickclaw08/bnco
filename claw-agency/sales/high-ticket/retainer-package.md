## Why Retainer vs. Project?

**Projects are great for building something new.** Retainers are for companies that need ongoing support, optimization, and evolution.

Here's what happens without a retainer:
- Automations break when APIs change (Zapier updates, tools deprecate features)
- Your team builds workarounds instead of fixing root issues
- You spend $200/month on tools you're not fully using
- New hires don't understand the automation stack
- "Quick fixes" pile up and become technical debt

Here's what happens with a retainer:
- We proactively monitor and maintain everything
- Your automations get faster and smarter over time
- You have a technical partner to bounce ideas off
- New workflows get built as your business evolves
- You scale without adding headcount

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What if we don't use all 20 hours in a month?**
A: Unused hours roll over for up to 2 months. After that, they expire. (Most clients use 15-25 hours/month once we're up to speed.)

**Q: Can we pause the retainer?**
A: On quarterly contracts, yes (with 30 days notice). On annual contracts, no (but you can bank hours for slower months).

**Q: What if we need more than 20 hours?**
A: You can purchase additional hours at $150/hour, or we can discuss upgrading to a higher-tier retainer (40 hours/month for $9,000/month).

**Q: Do you work with our existing tools, or do we need to switch?**
A: We work with your existing stack. If we identify opportunities to consolidate or switch tools, we'll recommend it, but we never force a change.

**Q: What if we hire someone in-house?**
A: Great! We can train them, hand off documentation, and transition you off the retainer. Many clients keep us on at a lower tier (10 hours/month, $3,000/month) for strategy and overflow work.

**Q: What's your cancellation policy?**
A: Quarterly contracts require 30 days notice. Annual contracts are non-refundable but you can pause for up to 2 months if needed (e.g., during a slow season).
