# Results Guarantee Framework

## Core Promise
"We'll save your team at least 15 hours per week in the first 30 days, or we refund 50% of your investment."

## Why This Works

**Removes risk for the buyer:**
Most executives hesitate on $7,500-$15,000 purchases not because they don't believe it will work, but because they've been burned before by consultants who overpromise and underdeliver.

A guarantee says: "We're confident enough in our work that we'll put our money where our mouth is."

**Filters for the right clients:**
If a prospect doesn't have 15+ hours/week of manual work to automate, they're not a fit anyway. The guarantee helps us qualify in/out faster.

**Creates urgency:**
"Try us risk-free" is easier to say yes to than "trust us with $10K."

## Guarantee Tiers by Package

### High-Ticket Sprint ($7,500-$15,000)

**Guarantee:** "We'll save your team at least 15 hours per week within the first 30 days, or we refund 50% of the sprint fee."

**How we measure:**
- Pre-sprint baseline: We document current time spent on target workflows (time tracking, team interviews, process audits)
- Post-sprint tracking: We track time saved weekly for 30 days using:
  - Time logs from the client's team
  - Before/after metrics (e.g., "order updates took 10 hours/week, now take 2 hours/week")
  - Client sign-off on savings validation

**Refund trigger:**
- If measured savings < 15 hours/week after 30 days AND client has actively used the deployed automations
- Client must provide data (we can't measure if they don't track)
- Refund is 50% of sprint fee ($3,750-$7,500)

**Exclusions (when guarantee does NOT apply):**
- Client didn't implement/use the delivered automations
- Scope changed mid-sprint at client's request
- Client team was unavailable for training or handoff
- External factors (API deprecation, tool changes) outside our control

### Fractional CTO Retainer ($5,000-$6,000/month)

**Guarantee:** "We'll deliver measurable ROI (time saved or cost reduced) equal to at least 2x your monthly retainer fee within 90 days, or we'll work an additional month for free."

**How we measure:**
- Monthly ROI tracking report showing:
  - Hours saved across the team
  - Tool costs eliminated or reduced
  - Hires avoided (e.g., "didn't need to hire an ops coordinator")
  - Revenue protected (e.g., churn reduction, faster renewals)

**ROI calculation example:**
- Client pays $5,000/month = $15,000 over 90 days
- Target ROI: $30,000 in value delivered
- Typical sources:
  - 30 hours/week saved at $50/hour loaded cost = $6,000/month = $18,000 over 90 days
  - Tool consolidation: Eliminated 2 redundant subscriptions = $500/month = $1,500 over 90 days
  - Avoided hire: Didn't need to hire $70K ops coordinator = $17,500 quarterly cost avoided
  - Total: $37,000 in value delivered (2.5x ROI)

**Refund trigger:**
- If measured ROI < 2x monthly fee after 90 days
- Remedy: We work month 4 for free (no additional retainer payment)

**Exclusions:**
- Client didn't provide access to necessary tools/data
- Client team was unresponsive (>1 week delays in feedback/approvals)
- Major business change (pivot, layoffs, leadership turnover)

## How to Position the Guarantee in Sales

### Discovery Call Script

"Before we talk about pricing, let me explain how we de-risk this for you.

Most companies your size have been burned by consultants who promise the moon and deliver a PowerPoint deck. We're not that.

Here's our guarantee: **We'll save your team at least 15 hours per week in the first 30 days, or we refund 50% of the fee.**

We're that confident because we've done this 50+ times. We know what works. And if for some reason we don't hit that target, you get half your money back.

Fair?"

### Objection Handling

**Objection:** "That sounds good, but how do you actually measure the hours saved?"

**Response:** "Great question. On day one, we sit down with your team and document the current state: How long does [process X] take? How many times per week do you do it? We get baseline numbers.

Then, 30 days after launch, we do it again. We compare before and after. If the math doesn't work out, you get a refund. Simple as that.

The key is that you need to actually use what we build. We can't measure savings if the automations sit unused. But if you're engaged and we still don't deliver, you're protected."

**Objection:** "What if the hours saved come from somewhere we didn't expect?"

**Response:** "That's totally fine. The guarantee is 15 hours/week total, not 15 hours/week on one specific workflow. If we thought we'd save 10 hours on order processing but ended up saving 20 hours on reporting, that counts.

The goal is measurable impact, not checking boxes on a scope doc."

**Objection:** "50% refund seems low. Why not 100%?"

**Response:** "Fair question. We've put in real engineering time, delivered working systems, and provided training. If the work was done but the results didn't hit the target, a 50% refund reflects that effort while still protecting you from total loss.

That said, we've only had to invoke the guarantee twice in 3 years, and both times it was because the client's team didn't actually implement what we built. If you're committed to using what we deliver, you won't need the refund."

## Internal Guidelines for Invoking the Guarantee

### When to proactively offer a refund (before client asks):
- We know we underdelivered (scope was wrong, technical issues we couldn't solve, etc.)
- Client is clearly unhappy and the data supports their complaint
- Relationship is salvageable and refund shows good faith

### When to pushback on refund request:
- Client didn't use the delivered automations
- Client was unresponsive during the sprint (missed meetings, didn't provide access, etc.)
- Scope changed significantly mid-project at client's request
- Savings are there but client is disputing the measurement methodology

### Documentation requirements (for every sprint):
- **Pre-sprint baseline report:** Document current time spent, pain points, and success metrics
- **Weekly check-ins during sprint:** Track progress, flag issues early
- **Post-sprint ROI report:** Delivered at 30 days with before/after metrics
- **Client sign-off:** Get written confirmation of results (email is fine)

If a client requests a refund and we don't have this documentation, we have no defense. Track everything.

## Financial Impact of the Guarantee

**Worst-case scenario:**
- 10% of clients invoke the guarantee = 1 in 10 sprints
- Average sprint fee: $10,000
- Refund per invocation: $5,000
- Net revenue per 10 sprints: ($100,000 revenue - $5,000 refund) = $95,000

**Reality (based on our track record):**
- <5% of clients invoke the guarantee (we've had 2 in 3 years)
- Most clients see 2-5x the guaranteed savings
- Guarantee increases close rate by ~20% (more people say yes because risk is lower)

**Math:**
- Without guarantee: 30% close rate on 100 leads = 30 clients = $300K revenue
- With guarantee: 40% close rate on 100 leads = 40 clients = $400K revenue - $10K refunds (5% of 40) = $390K net

**Conclusion:** The guarantee INCREASES revenue by reducing friction, even accounting for refunds.

## Competitor Comparison

Most automation consultants/agencies DON'T offer guarantees because:
1. They're not confident in their ability to deliver measurable results
2. They bill hourly (no incentive to be efficient)
3. They do long-term projects (hard to measure ROI in 30 days)

We can offer a guarantee because:
1. We deliver fast (7 days = tight feedback loop)
2. We focus on measurable outcomes (hours saved, not "better processes")
3. We've done this enough times to know what works

This is a HUGE differentiator in sales conversations.
