## TIMING RULES

| Day | Email | Purpose |
|-----|-------|---------|
| Day 0 | Initial cold email | Open the conversation |
| Day 2 | Follow-up #1 | Simple nudge, slight value add |
| Day 5 | Follow-up #2 | Real value (video, ROI, insight) |
| Day 8-10 | Breakup email | Close the loop with class |

**After the breakup email:** Stop. Do not send another email to this prospect for at least 30 days. If you follow up again after 30 days, reference something new (a case study, a relevant article, a new service) rather than rehashing the old thread.

---

## KEY PRINCIPLES (From the Trillion-Dollar CRO Playbook)

1. **Every follow-up adds value.** Never send "just checking in" or "circling back." Each email should give them a reason to reply.

2. **Urgency comes from capacity, not pressure.** "I have limited spots this week" is real urgency. "ACT NOW BEFORE IT'S TOO LATE" is spam.

3. **Shorter is better on follow-ups.** Your initial email can be 150-200 words. Follow-ups should be 50-100 words. The breakup email should be under 80 words.

4. **Mirror their language.** If their job posting said "data entry specialist," say "data entry." Don't upgrade it to "data operations automation" to sound impressive.

5. **The fortune is in the follow-up.** 80% of sales happen after the 5th contact. Most people give up after 2. But there's a difference between persistent and annoying. Three well-spaced, value-adding follow-ups is persistent. Five emails in five days is annoying.

6. **Track everything.** Log every email sent, every reply, every open (if you're using tracking). Update the pipeline tracker with the current status and next action.

## REPLY HANDLING

### If they reply positively:
> Respond within 1 hour. Book the call immediately. Send a calendar link. Don't let momentum die.

### If they reply with a question:
> Answer the question directly and concisely. Then pivot to a call: "Happy to go deeper on this. Want to hop on a quick 10-minute call?"

### If they reply with "not right now":
> Respect it. Ask when would be better. Set a reminder in the pipeline tracker and follow up on that date.

### If they reply with "not interested":
> Thank them for letting you know. Don't argue. One sentence: "Appreciate you letting me know. If things change, I'm here." Move on.

### If they reply with "unsubscribe" or angry:
> Remove them immediately. Never contact again. Don't take it personally. It's a numbers game.
