# DM-to-Close Playbook
**Date:** March 1, 2026
**Author:** Jordan (CRO), ClawOps
**Context:** 20+ Reddit DM threads, zero closes. This playbook fixes that.

---

## The Core Problem

We're sending DMs that read like cold pitches. Reddit DMs convert when they feel like helpful conversations between two people who share context. The moment it feels transactional, you're dead.

**The fix:** Every DM must answer three questions the prospect is silently asking:
1. "Why should I trust you?"
2. "What's in it for me, specifically?"
3. "How easy is this to do?"

---

## Phase 1: Pre-DM Positioning (Before You Ever Message Anyone)

### 1.1 The Content-First Approach
Before DMing anyone in a subreddit, you should have 2-3 genuine, helpful comments or posts in that community. This gives you a post history that prospects WILL check.

**What to post/comment:**
- Answer questions about missed calls, phone systems, customer service
- Share specific data: "62% of callers won't leave a voicemail - they'll call your competitor instead" (real stat from industry research)
- Tell stories about businesses you've helped (anonymized)
- Give genuinely useful advice without pitching

**Don't:**
- Drop your business name in comments
- Link to your website unprompted
- Start DMs from an account with zero community participation

### 1.2 Profile Optimization
Your Reddit profile is your storefront. When someone gets a DM, the first thing they do is click your username.

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Bio mentions what you do (not a sales pitch, just factual)
- [ ] Recent post history shows you helping people, not selling
- [ ] No desperate "hire me" posts in your history
- [ ] Account is at least 2-4 weeks old with organic activity

## Phase 2: Initial DM Templates by Vertical

### CRITICAL RULE: Personalize Everything
These templates are STARTING POINTS. Every single DM must reference something specific about the prospect - their post, their business, their problem. Generic = deleted.

### 2.1 Dental Offices

**Context:** Dental offices lose an estimated $500-1,000 per missed new patient call. They're hiring receptionists at $2,000+/mo.

**Template:**
```
Hey [name] - saw your post about [specific thing they mentioned].

Quick thought: I work with dental offices on their phone systems. One thing I keep seeing is offices missing calls when the front desk is busy with patients. The crazy part is most callers won't leave a voicemail - they just call the next dentist on Google.

I actually set up a quick demo using your office name and hours. Takes about 2 minutes to hear it. Want me to send it over?

No pitch, no call needed - just thought it might be useful.
```

**Why this works:**
- References their specific post
- Leads with their pain (missed calls), not our feature
- Offers something tangible (demo) without asking for commitment
- "No pitch, no call needed" removes friction
- Under 6 sentences of substance

### 2.2 Electricians / HVAC / Plumbers

**Context:** Emergency service businesses lose $200-500+ per missed call. When someone's AC is out in July, they call the next guy immediately.

**Template:**
```
Hey [name] - your post about [topic] caught my eye.

Random question: do you get a lot of calls after hours or when you're on a job? I work with service businesses on catching those calls, and the data is wild - most people won't leave a voicemail for an emergency job. They just call someone else.

I built a quick thing using your business name that auto-responds to missed calls. Happy to share it if you're curious - takes 2 min to check out. No strings.
```

### 2.3 Digital Marketing Agencies (selling to agency owners)

**Template:**

## Phase 3: The Follow-Up Sequence

### THE GOLDEN RULE: 80% of DM closes happen between follow-up #2 and #5. Most people give up after follow-up #1.

### Follow-Up #1 (Day 3 after initial DM)
**If they haven't responded to your first DM:**

```
Hey [name] - just bumping this in case it got buried. No rush at all.

[Optional: add ONE new piece of value - a stat, a quick insight, etc.]
```

**Keep it SHORT.** 1-2 sentences max. Don't re-pitch.

### Follow-Up #2 (Day 7)
**The Social Proof Drop:**

```
Hey [name] - quick update: just helped a [similar business type] set this up and they caught 12 calls they would've missed in the first week alone.

Still happy to share that demo I mentioned if you're curious. If not, no worries at all.
```

### Follow-Up #3 (Day 14)
**The New Angle:**

```
Hey [name] - thought of you when I saw [relevant article/stat/trend about their industry].

[Share the insight in 1-2 sentences]

Anyway, the offer still stands on that demo. Just lmk.
```

### Follow-Up #4 (Day 21)
**The Permission-to-Say-No:**

```
Hey [name] - I've reached out a few times so I want to be upfront: if this isn't something you need right now, totally fine. I'd rather you tell me to buzz off than keep messaging you. 

If timing's bad but you want me to check back in a few months, just say the word. Either way, no hard feelings.
```

**Why this works:** This is the #1 re-engagement tactic from r/sales research. Giving someone an easy out paradoxically makes them MORE likely to engage. 

### Follow-Up #5 (Day 28)
**The Break-Up Message:**

```
Hey [name] - closing out my notes on this. Figured the timing wasn't right.

If things change down the road, I'm around. Best of luck with [their business/specific thing they posted about].
```

**This is the most important message in the sequence.** Per r/sales consensus, break-up messages generate more replies than any other follow-up because they trigger loss aversion.

## Phase 4: Objection Handling Scripts

### Objection: "What does it cost?"

**BAD response:**
```
It's $X/month.
```
(Never lead with price before establishing value)

**GOOD response:**
```
Great question. Before I throw numbers at you - how many calls do you think you miss per week? Just want to make sure I recommend the right plan for your volume.

Most of our [industry] clients are on our $297/mo plan. Quick math: if you're missing even 5 calls a month and each job/patient/client is worth $200+, that's $1,000+ in lost revenue. The system pays for itself with one recovered call.
```

### Objection: "I need to think about it"

**BAD response:**
```
No problem, take your time! Let me know when you're ready.
```
(This loses the deal 90% of the time)

**GOOD response:**
```
Totally fair. What specifically are you weighing? Happy to answer any questions that would make the decision easier.

If it helps - there's no contract, you can cancel anytime. Most people know within the first week whether it's working for them.
```

### Objection: "Send me more info"

**BAD response:**
```
Sure! Here's our website: [link]
```
(This is where deals go to die)

**GOOD response:**
```
Absolutely. What specifically would be most helpful? I'd rather send you exactly what you need than dump a generic brochure on you.

Actually, the fastest way to see what this does is the demo I set up for your business. Takes 2 minutes to listen to - want me to send that instead?
```

### Objection: "We already have something for that"

**BAD response:**
```
Oh, what are you using? We're way better because...
```

**GOOD response:**
```
Nice - what are you using? Genuinely curious.

[If they tell you]: Oh cool, I know [competitor/solution]. How's it working for you? Any gaps you've noticed?

[Then address gaps if they mention any, or]: Sounds like you're in good shape then. If anything changes or you want to compare, I'm around. No pressure.
```

### Objection: "I don't trust AI / robots answering my phone"

**GOOD response:**
```
Totally get that. A lot of our clients felt the same way before trying it.

Here's the thing: the AI doesn't replace your team. It catches the calls your team CAN'T catch - after hours, during rush, when all lines are busy. Your staff still handles everything during normal business.

Easiest way to see: just listen to the demo I set up for your business. You'll hear exactly what your callers would experience. If it sounds robotic or weird, no hard feelings.
```

### Objection: "I can't afford it right now"

**GOOD response:**
```
I hear you. Budget is tight for everyone right now.

Quick question though: do you have a rough idea of how many calls you miss per month? Just curious because most of our clients find the system pays for itself within the first week.

[If they give a number]: So at [X] missed calls and [estimated job value], that's roughly $[amount] in potential revenue. The system costs $297/mo, so mathematically it's not a cost - it's a revenue recovery tool.

No pressure though. If you want to try it for a week with no commitment, we can do that.
```

## Phase 5: Demo-to-Close Transition

This is where most DM sales die. The prospect says "cool demo" and then... nothing. Here's how to bridge from demo to payment.

### Step 1: After They Listen to the Demo

```
What'd you think? Anything you'd want changed or customized?
```

**Don't pitch yet.** Let them react first. Their response tells you where they are:
- Excited/positive = Ready to move forward
- "That's cool" = Interested but not convinced
- Questions about features = Evaluating, need more info
- Silence = Need more nurturing

### Step 2: The Customization Bridge

Whatever their response, offer to customize:

```
I can tweak [specific thing] based on your [hours/services/preferences]. Takes me about 15 minutes. Then it's basically ready to go live.

Want me to set it up so you can test it with your real number for a week?
```

**This is the key move:** Offering a FREE TEST with their real number. Once they experience missed calls being caught in real-time, they close themselves.

### Step 3: The Activation Close

After the test period:

```
Hey [name] - looked at your stats from this week. [Your AI name] caught [X] calls that would've gone to voicemail. 

[If possible, estimate revenue]: Based on your average [job value/patient value/case value], that's roughly $[amount] you would've missed.

Ready to keep it running? Here's the link to activate: [payment link]

Monthly plan is $297/mo, no contract. Cancel anytime.
```

### Step 4: If They Don't Activate After Test

```
Hey [name] - your trial wrapped up yesterday. Saw the AI caught [X] calls for you.

Wanted to check in: was there anything that didn't work well? Happy to fix it.

If you're on the fence, I can extend the trial another week so you get a full picture. Just lmk.
```

## Phase 6: Urgency Triggers (Non-Sleazy)

### 1. The Math Urgency
```
Quick napkin math: if you miss [X] calls/week at [Y] per job, that's $[amount]/month walking to your competitors. The system is $297/mo. The math does itself.
```

### 2. The Competitor Urgency
```
FYI - I noticed [competitor in their area] just signed up for a similar service. Not trying to create pressure, just figured you'd want to know the landscape is shifting.
```
**Only use if TRUE.** Never fabricate competitor urgency.

### 3. The Capacity Urgency
```
Heads up - I'm capping new setups this month at [X] because each one needs personalized configuration. Not a hard sell, just didn't want you to reach out later and have to wait in line.
```
**Only use if you genuinely have capacity limits.**

### 4. The Cost-of-Waiting Urgency
```
Every week without the system is another [X] missed calls. That's [Y] potential customers going to someone else. Not trying to be dramatic - just want you to have the full picture.
```

### 5. The Social Proof Urgency
```
Just set up [business type] in [city] yesterday. Funny thing - they told me they signed up because their competitor started using something similar and was capturing their overflow calls. The game is changing fast in [industry].
```

## Quick Reference: DM Dos and Don'ts

### DO:
- Reference their specific post/comment/business
- Lead with their problem, not your solution
- Offer something tangible before asking for anything (demo, audit, insight)
- Keep messages under 6 sentences
- Give permission to say no
- Follow up 5-7 times over 4 weeks
- Use the break-up message
- Position as software/subscription, not agency service
- Show math (missed calls x revenue per call = lost revenue)
- Offer a free trial/test period

### DON'T:
- Send the same message to everyone
- Lead with price
- Pitch before demonstrating value
- Give up after 1-2 follow-ups
- Send long messages
- Use corporate language ("I'd love the opportunity to discuss...")
- Badmouth competitors
- Create fake urgency
- Respond to "send me info" with a website link
- Skip the break-up message

## Conversion Metrics to Track

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| DM Response Rate | 15-25% |
| Demo Request Rate (of responders) | 40-60% |
| Demo Listen Rate | 70-80% |
| Trial Activation (after demo) | 30-40% |
| Trial-to-Paid Conversion | 50-70% |
| Overall DM-to-Close Rate | 3-5% |

### Expected Close Rate
Based on research, a 3-5% DM-to-close rate is realistic for $297-500/mo services. That means for every 100 quality DMs (personalized, to the right people), 3-5 should close.

**With 20 active threads, we should expect 1 close if our sequence is right.**

If we're at 0/20, the sequence is broken. The most likely break points:
1. Initial DM isn't compelling enough (no personalized demo/value)
2. Follow-up sequence is too short or too aggressive
3. No trial/test offer to reduce commitment friction
4. Asking for too much too fast (payment before they've experienced value)
