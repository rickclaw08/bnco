# ClawOps Discovery Call Script
## Based on Trillion-Dollar Sales Practices (MEDDIC + Pain-Based Selling)

**Total call time:** 10-15 minutes
**Goal:** Confirm pain, demonstrate solution fit, close or set clear next step

---

## PRE-CALL CHECKLIST (2 minutes before)

- [ ] Review their job posting or initial inquiry
- [ ] Note the specific role they were hiring for (or process they mentioned)
- [ ] Have a relevant automation example ready to screen share
- [ ] Have the Stripe payment link open and ready
- [ ] Open their website in a tab for reference

---

## PHASE 1: OPENING (60 seconds)

**The goal:** Be human. Build rapport. Set expectations for the call.

> "Hey [Name], thanks for hopping on. I know you're busy, so I'll keep this tight. I want to spend about 10 minutes understanding what you're dealing with, and if I can help, I'll show you exactly what that looks like. Sound good?"

**If they say yes (they will):**

> "Perfect. So I saw [reference to how you connected, such as: 'your posting for a data entry specialist' or 'your reply to my email about automating your workflows']. Tell me a little about what's going on with that."

**Key principles:**
- Don't pitch. Not yet.
- Don't talk about ClawOps, AI, or automation. Not yet.
- Let them talk. Your job is to listen for pain.

---

## PHASE 2: PAIN DISCOVERY (3-4 minutes)

**The goal:** Understand the specific process, how much time it takes, and what it costs them.

### Primary Discovery Questions

**Process mapping:**
> "Walk me through how this process works today. Step by step, what does your team actually do?"

**Time quantification:**
> "Roughly how many hours per week does someone spend on this?"

**Frequency:**
> "Is this daily? Weekly? How often does this come up?"

**Error/frustration probe:**
> "What breaks? Where do things fall through the cracks?"

### Deepening Questions (use 2-3 of these based on what you hear)

> "What happens when that doesn't get done on time?"

> "Have you tried to fix this before? What did you try?"

> "If you could wave a magic wand and this problem just disappeared tomorrow, what would that free your team up to do instead?"

> "Is this getting worse as you grow, or has it been about the same?"

> "Who else on your team feels this pain?"

### The Money Question (critical)

> "Just so I understand the full picture: what are you spending on this right now? Whether that's a salary, a contractor, or just the time your team puts in?"

**Why this matters:** You need a dollar figure to anchor your pricing against. If they're paying $40K/year for a data entry person, your $3,500 build looks like a steal.

### LISTEN FOR:
- **Hours wasted** (this is your ROI ammunition)
- **Errors and rework** (reliability angle)
- **Growth bottleneck** ("we can't scale because...")
- **Frustration** (emotional buying signal)
- **Multiple people involved** (bigger impact = bigger deal)

**Write these down. You'll use them in the close.**

---

## PHASE 3: QUALIFYING (1-2 minutes)

**The goal:** Make sure you're talking to the decision maker and there's real urgency.

### Decision Maker Check

> "If we figured out a solution that works, are you the person who'd pull the trigger on this? Or would someone else need to weigh in?"

**If they're not the decision maker:**
> "Totally makes sense. Would it be helpful to loop them into a quick follow-up call? I can walk them through exactly what they'd be getting."

### Timeline Check

> "How soon are you looking to get this handled? Is this a 'this week' kind of thing or more of a 'sometime this quarter' situation?"

**What you want to hear:** Urgency. "We needed this yesterday" is gold. "Sometime in Q3" means this isn't a real opportunity right now.

### Budget Check (subtle)

> "Have you set aside a budget for solving this, or is this more of a 'let's see what it costs' conversation?"

**No matter what they say, you're fine.** You just want to know if there's a financial blocker before you present pricing.

---

## PHASE 4: SOLUTION FRAMING (2-3 minutes)

**The goal:** Connect their specific pain to your specific solution. No generic pitches.

> "Okay, so here's what I'm hearing. You're spending [X hours/week] on [their process], it's costing you roughly [dollar amount in labor], and when things slip, [consequence they mentioned]. Does that sound right?"

**Wait for confirmation. This is important.** You're reflecting their pain back to them so they feel understood.

> "Good. So here's exactly what I'd build for you."

**Now screen share and walk through the automation:**
- Show a similar automation you've built, or mock up their specific flow
- Keep it visual and simple
- Focus on what THEY see (the output, the time saved), not the technical back end

> "This handles [step 1], [step 2], and [step 3] automatically. Your team would just [minimal remaining human step, if any]. Based on what you told me, this would save you roughly [X hours per week], which is about [$Y per month] in labor costs."

**Key principles:**
- Mirror their exact words back. If they said "it's a nightmare," say "this eliminates that nightmare."
- Tie everything to the hours and dollars they gave you in discovery.
- Make it concrete. "This emails your team at 9 AM every Monday with the report" is better than "this automates your reporting."

---

## PHASE 5: PRICING AND CLOSE (2-3 minutes)

**The goal:** Present pricing anchored against their current cost, and close or set a concrete next step.

> "So here's how we work. For what you need, it's a one-time build of [$X]. That includes the full automation, documentation so your team knows how it works, and 30 days of support to make sure everything runs smoothly."

**Pause. Let them process.**

> "To put that in perspective, you mentioned you're spending about [$Y per month] on this right now. The automation pays for itself in [timeframe], and after that it's just savings."

### If They're Ready

> "I can start building this tomorrow and have it live for you by [day]. Want me to send over the payment link right now?"

**Send the Stripe link while on the call.** Don't wait.

### If They Need to Think

> "I totally get it. What specifically do you want to think through? I want to make sure I've answered everything."

**This is not aggressive. This is helpful.** Most "I need to think about it" responses are actually "I have a concern I haven't voiced." Surface it now.

### If They Need Approval from Someone Else

> "Makes sense. Can we set up a quick 10-minute call with them this week? I'll walk them through the ROI so they have everything they need to make a decision."

**Book the call before you hang up.** Do not leave with "I'll talk to them and get back to you."

---

## PHASE 6: OBJECTION HANDLING

### "It's too expensive."

> "I hear you. Let's look at the math together. You told me this process costs you about [$Y per month] in labor right now. The automation is a one-time [$X] investment. So you break even in [timeframe], and everything after that is pure savings. Over 12 months, you're looking at [$Z] in net savings."

### "I need to think about it."

> "Absolutely, take your time. Just so I can help: is there a specific part you're unsure about? The cost, whether it'll actually work, the timeline? I'd rather address it now than have you wondering."

### "Can you do it cheaper?"

> "I keep pricing straightforward because I deliver high quality and I stand behind the work. What I can do is adjust the scope. We could start with just [core piece] at a lower price point, prove it works, and add on from there. Would that work?"

### "I can build this myself / my team can do it."

> "You probably can. The question is whether that's the best use of your time. If it takes your team 40 hours to figure out, at their hourly rate, that's more than what I'd charge. Plus I've built this exact thing before, so I can have it done in 48 hours with no learning curve."

### "I've been burned by freelancers before."

> "I hear that a lot, and I respect the caution. Here's my approach: I scope it clearly upfront so there are no surprises, I deliver within 48-72 hours so you're not waiting around, and I include 30 days of support. If something breaks, I fix it. No extra charge."

### "We're not ready right now."

> "No problem. When do you think you'll be ready to tackle this? I'll follow up then so we can pick up where we left off."

**Get a specific date. Add it to the pipeline tracker.**

---

## POST-CALL ACTIONS (within 30 minutes)

1. **Send a recap email** summarizing what you discussed, the solution, and the price
2. **Include the Stripe payment link** in the email
3. **Update the pipeline tracker** with call notes, stage, next step, and date
4. **If call scheduled with decision maker:** Send a calendar invite immediately
5. **Set a follow-up reminder** for 24 hours if they haven't responded
