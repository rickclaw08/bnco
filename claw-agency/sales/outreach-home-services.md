## Customization Notes by Trade

### HVAC
- Pain point emphasis: "Emergency calls at 2 AM when the AC dies in July"
- Job value reference: $3,000-$15,000 for system replacement
- Seasonal angle: "Summer is coming. How many emergency calls will go to voicemail?"
- Common services: AC repair, furnace install, duct cleaning, maintenance plans

### Plumbing
- Pain point emphasis: "Burst pipe at 11 PM - customer calls 3 plumbers, hires the first one who answers"
- Job value reference: $500-$8,000 for major repairs/repiping
- Emergency angle: Plumbing emergencies can't wait - first responder wins
- Common services: Drain cleaning, water heater, repiping, slab leak, sewer line

### Electrical
- Pain point emphasis: "Panel upgrade quote request - customer calls Monday, you call back Wednesday, they already hired someone"
- Job value reference: $2,000-$10,000 for panel upgrades, rewiring
- Safety angle: Electrical problems feel urgent to homeowners
- Common services: Panel upgrade, rewiring, outlet install, EV charger, generator

### Roofing
- Pain point emphasis: "Storm hits, 50 homeowners call for inspections. You can handle 10 calls. 40 hire someone else."
- Job value reference: $8,000-$25,000 for roof replacement
- Seasonal angle: Storm season = lead flood. AI handles the volume.
- Common services: Inspection, repair, replacement, gutter install, storm damage

### Landscaping / Lawn Care
- Pain point emphasis: "Spring quote requests pour in. You're out mowing. Calls go to voicemail."
- Job value reference: $200-$5,000 for landscape projects, $50-$200/mo for maintenance
- Recurring revenue angle: Miss the first call, miss the monthly contract
- Common services: Mowing, landscape design, irrigation, tree trimming, hardscaping

## Sending Rules

1. **Personalize Email 1 and 3** - Generic = deleted. Reference their Google reviews, their service area, something real.
2. **Email 3 (Mystery Call) only if you actually called them** - Don't fake it. Actually call. The honesty is the hook.
3. **Send between 7-9 AM local time** - Contractors check email before heading to job sites
4. **Tuesday-Thursday only** - Monday is chaos, Friday they're wrapping up
5. **Max 30 emails/day per sending domain** - Stay under spam radar
6. **Use a separate domain** - Not theclawops.com. Something like clawops-ai.com or getclawops.com
7. **Track opens and replies** - Double down on subject lines that work
8. **Respond to replies within 30 minutes** - Speed wins. They emailed you back, they're interested NOW.
