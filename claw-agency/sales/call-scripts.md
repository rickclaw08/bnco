## Discovery Call Script (25-30 minutes)

### Pre-Call Checklist

- [ ] Research their company (website, LinkedIn, recent news)
- [ ] Review their industry pain points from prospect list
- [ ] Prepare 2-3 relevant examples or case studies
- [ ] Have pricing sheet ready (do not share unless asked)
- [ ] CRM open with their contact record
- [ ] Calendar ready for booking next steps

---

### Opening (2 minutes)

> "Hey [Name], appreciate you taking the time. I'll keep this tight. My goal today is to understand what's going on in your business and figure out if we can genuinely help. If we can, great, I'll show you how. If not, I'll be straight about it. Sound fair?"

**Wait for confirmation.** This sets the tone: no-pressure, honest, efficient.

> "Before I dive in, quick question: is there anything specific you're hoping to get out of this call?"

**Why:** Lets them set the agenda. Shows you care about their time.

---

### Situation Questions (5 minutes)

**Goal:** Understand their current state. Don't assume.

> "Walk me through how [relevant process] works at [their company] right now."

> "How many people are involved in that workflow?"

> "What tools or software are you using for that today?"

> "About how many [leads/orders/clients/tickets] do you handle per week?"

> "Is this process the same every time, or does it vary a lot?"

**Tip:** Let them talk. Take notes. Repeat back what you hear to confirm understanding.

---

### Problem Questions (8 minutes)

**Goal:** Uncover pain. Make it concrete and quantifiable.

> "Where does that process break down or slow down?"

> "What's the most time-consuming part?"

> "How much time does your team spend on [manual task] per week? Rough estimate is fine."

> "When things go wrong, what does that look like? What's the fallout?"

> "Have you tried to fix this before? What happened?"

> "What's keeping you from solving this on your own?"

**Key phrase to listen for:** "We've been meaning to fix this" or "It's costing us [time/money]." That's your signal of urgency.

---

### Impact Questions (5 minutes)

**Goal:** Quantify the cost of inaction. Help them see the ROI.

> "If you could get those [X] hours back every week, what would your team focus on instead?"

> "What does this problem cost you per month, in dollars, staff time, or missed opportunities?"

> "How does this affect your ability to grow or take on more [clients/orders/projects]?"

> "If we fixed this in the next 30 days, what would that mean for your business?"

**Tip:** Let them do the math. When they say the number out loud, it becomes real.

---

### Solution Preview (5 minutes)

**Goal:** Paint a picture of the solution without getting too technical.

> "Based on what you've shared, here's what I'm thinking..."

Describe the solution in plain language:
- "We'd connect [Tool A] to [Tool B] so that when [trigger] happens, [action] happens automatically."
- "Your team would go from spending [X hours] on this to basically zero."
- "We did something similar for [reference]. They went from [before state] to [after state] in [timeframe]."

> "Does that sound like it would move the needle for you?"

**Do NOT:**
- Screen share or demo at this stage (save for demo call)
- Quote exact pricing unless directly asked
- Over-explain the technical details

**If they ask about pricing:**
> "Our packages start at $500 for a single automation, and our most popular plan is $2K/month for ongoing automation work. I'll put together a custom recommendation based on what we discussed today."

---

### Close and Next Steps (3 minutes)

> "Here's what I'd suggest as next steps. I'll put together a short proposal with the exact scope, timeline, and pricing. Then we can block 15 minutes on [suggest specific day] to walk through it. Does that work?"

**If they want a demo:**
> "Absolutely. Let's book a 30-minute demo where I can show you exactly how this would work for your setup. Does [day/time] work?"

**Confirm:**
- [ ] Who else needs to be on the next call? (identify decision-makers)
- [ ] What's their timeline for making a decision?
- [ ] Any concerns or questions before we wrap up?

> "Perfect. I'll send a calendar invite and a quick recap email within the hour. Looking forward to it."

**Send calendar invite BEFORE hanging up.**

---

### Post-Call Actions (within 1 hour)

1. Update CRM with call notes, BANT score, next steps
2. Send recap email summarizing what you discussed and confirmed next steps
3. Begin preparing proposal or demo
4. Set follow-up reminder in CRM

## Demo Script (30 minutes)

### Pre-Demo Checklist

- [ ] Build or prepare a demo tailored to their specific use case
- [ ] Test all automations and integrations before the call
- [ ] Have their proposal ready to share at the end
- [ ] Prepare a "before and after" comparison
- [ ] Know who's on the call (decision-makers?)

---

### Opening (3 minutes)

> "Hey [Name], good to see you again. Thanks for making time for this. Today I want to show you exactly how we'd automate [their specific process]. I'll keep it practical, no fluff, just what it would look like in your workflow."

> "Quick check: is [other decision-maker] joining us? And is there anything specific you want to make sure we cover today?"

---

### The "Before" State (3 minutes)

**Goal:** Remind them of the pain. Contrast it with what's coming.

> "So based on our last conversation, here's where things stand today:"

Walk through their current workflow:
- "Step 1: [Manual action] happens, and someone has to [tedious task]"
- "Step 2: Then [another person] manually [action]"
- "Step 3: If something falls through the cracks, [negative consequence]"

> "And you mentioned this takes your team about [X hours/week]. Does that still sound right?"

---

### The Demo (15 minutes)

**Goal:** Show, don't tell. Walk through the automated version of their workflow.

> "Now let me show you what this looks like after we automate it."

**Structure the demo as a story:**

1. **Trigger:** "When [event] happens..."
   - Show the trigger firing (form submission, email received, order placed, etc.)

2. **Automation in action:** "...the system automatically..."
   - Walk through each automated step
   - Show data flowing between tools
   - Highlight where manual work disappears

3. **Output:** "...and your team gets [result]."
   - Show the end result (notification, updated CRM, sent email, generated report)
   - Emphasize time saved

4. **Error handling:** "And if something goes wrong..."
   - Show how the system handles edge cases
   - Show notification/alert for exceptions that need human review

**Tips:**
- Use their actual tool names and process language
- Pause after each step: "Make sense so far?"
- Keep it visual, minimize talking over static screens
- If showing a live build, narrate what you're doing

> "So what used to take [X hours/week] now happens in [seconds/minutes], automatically, every time."

---

### Results and ROI (4 minutes)

> "Let me put some numbers to this."

| Metric | Before | After |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Time spent on [process] | [X] hrs/week | [Y] hrs/week (or zero) |
| Errors/month | [X] | Near zero |
| Response time | [X hours/days] | [X minutes] |
| Monthly cost of manual work | $[X] | $[Y] (or eliminated) |

> "So your investment of [$price] pays for itself by [month X], and after that it's pure savings."

---

### Objection Handling During Demo

**"What if it breaks?"**
> "Great question. Every automation we build includes error handling and alerts. If something unexpected happens, you get notified immediately. Plus, on our Growth plan, we monitor and maintain everything ongoing."

**"Can it handle [edge case]?"**
> "Let me show you..." [demonstrate or explain how you'd handle it]
> If you can't demo it live: "We'd build a custom rule for that. It's straightforward and included in the scope."

**"Our team isn't technical."**
> "That's exactly why we handle everything. Your team doesn't need to touch the backend. They just see the results: fewer manual tasks, faster turnaround."

**"What about our data security?"**
> "Everything runs through the platforms you already use and trust. We don't store your data separately. And our contract includes confidentiality and data handling terms."

---

### The Close (5 minutes)

> "So that's what it looks like. Before we talk next steps, any questions about what you saw?"

**Handle questions, then transition:**

> "Based on everything we discussed, I'd recommend our [Starter/Growth/Enterprise] package. Here's what that includes..."

**Pull up the proposal (share screen or send link):**

- Walk through scope, timeline, and pricing
- Emphasize what's included (support, revisions, training)

**Ask for the decision:**

> "Does this look like what you need? If so, I can send the contract over today and we can kick off [next week / specific date]."

**If they need to discuss internally:**
> "Totally understand. When do you think you'll have a decision? I'll follow up on [that date]. And if any questions come up in the meantime, just email me directly."

**If they want to negotiate:**
> "What part would you like to adjust? Let's see what we can do."

**Never discount below:**
- Starter: $500 (firm)
- Growth: $1,800/mo (absolute floor)
- Enterprise: Scope-dependent, maintain margins

1. Send proposal within 2 hours if not already shared
2. Update CRM with demo notes and deal stage
3. Start the 5-Touch post-proposal follow-up sequence
4. If they said yes: send contract same day
