# Outreach Scripts

Copy-paste ready scripts for every stage of prospect outreach. Personalize the bracketed fields.

---

## 1. Reddit DM (Cold Outreach)

**Use when:** You found a business owner on Reddit (r/smallbusiness, r/entrepreneur, r/HVAC, r/dentistry, industry subs, local subs).

```
Hey [NAME], I saw your post about [BRIEF REFERENCE TO THEIR POST/BUSINESS]. I actually built a custom AI receptionist demo for [BUSINESS_NAME] - it answers calls, books appointments, and handles FAQs specific to your business.

Not trying to sell you anything. I literally already built it. You can try it right now:

Call: (702) 728-4638
[OR: Try the web demo: WIDGET_LINK]

It knows your services, your hours, everything. Took me about 15 minutes to set up. Figured it was easier to show you than explain it.

Let me know what you think.
```

**Shorter version (if their post history suggests they prefer brevity):**

```
Hey [NAME] - I built a custom AI receptionist for [BUSINESS_NAME]. It answers calls, knows your services/hours, and books appointments. Already set up. Call (702) 728-4638 to hear it. No pitch, just wanted to show you what's possible.
```

## 2. Email (Cold Outreach)

**Use when:** You have their business email from their website.

**Subject line options (pick one):**

- Your AI receptionist is ready
- [BUSINESS_NAME] - custom demo inside

**Body:**

```
Hi [NAME],

No cost, no commitment. Just wanted to show you what it sounds like for your specific business.
```

## 3. Follow-Up (After They Engage)

**Use when:** Prospect responded positively, tried the demo, or asked a question.

### If they liked the demo:

```
Glad you liked it! That was built specifically for [BUSINESS_NAME] using info from your website. Here's what it can do in production:

- Answer every call 24/7 (no more missed calls or voicemails)
- Book appointments directly into your calendar
- Handle your top FAQs so your staff isn't repeating the same answers all day
- Transfer urgent calls to you or your team instantly
- Speak Spanish (or other languages) if your customers need it

Want me to walk you through how it would work day-to-day for your business? I can do a quick 10-minute call whenever works for you.
```

### If they tried it but didn't respond:

```
Hey [NAME], just checking in - did you get a chance to call the demo line? Curious what you thought. If anything was off (wrong hours, missing services), I can update it in a few minutes. It's built to match your business exactly.
```

### If they responded but seem on the fence:

```
Totally understand wanting to think about it. One thing worth mentioning - the demo you tried is basically production-ready. If you wanted to turn it on tomorrow, we could. Most of our clients start seeing results (fewer missed calls, more bookings) in the first week.

Happy to answer any questions, no pressure either way.
```

## 4. Pricing Response

**Use when:** Prospect asks "How much does this cost?" or "What's your pricing?"

### Standard pricing response:

```
Great question. Here's how it works:

Setup: $2,500 one-time (covers building your custom AI receptionist, configuring it for your business, integrating with your booking system, and getting your phone line active)

Monthly: $497/month (includes unlimited calls, 24/7 coverage, updates whenever your business info changes)

That's it. No per-minute charges, no hidden fees.

For context, a part-time receptionist costs $3,000+/month and doesn't work nights, weekends, or holidays. This handles every call, every time, for a fraction of the cost.

Want me to set yours up? Since I already built the demo, we can have you live in about 48 hours.
```

### If they push back on price:

```
I hear you. Here's how most owners think about it: if this catches even 2-3 calls per month that would've gone to voicemail, it's already paid for itself. The average missed call costs a service business $500-1,500 in lost revenue.

At $497/mo, you need to recover ONE missed customer per month to be in the green. Most of our clients recover 10-30x that. Want me to run the numbers for your specific business?
```

### If they ask for a discount:

```
The pricing reflects the level of customization and support you get. This isn't a generic chatbot - it's built specifically for your business with your services, your hours, your voice.

That said, if you're serious about moving forward this week, I can work with you on the setup fee. The monthly stays at $497 because that's what keeps the system running and maintained. Fair?
```

## 5. Objection Handling

### "I already have a receptionist."

```
That's great - this isn't meant to replace them. Think of it as backup. It handles after-hours calls, overflow during busy times, and weekends/holidays when your receptionist isn't there. Your receptionist handles the personal touch during business hours, this catches everything else. Most businesses miss 30-40% of their calls - this closes that gap.
```

### "I don't get that many calls."

```
That actually makes each call more valuable. If you're only getting 20-30 calls a month, every single one matters. Even missing 2-3 of those could mean hundreds in lost revenue. This makes sure zero go unanswered, 24/7.
```

### "I need to talk to my partner/think about it."

```
Totally fair. I'll leave the demo active so you (or your partner) can call anytime and hear it. Here's a quick summary you can share: [send the 3-sentence email script]. No rush.
```

### "Can it really handle my specific business?"

```
That's literally what the demo proves. The one you called was built using your actual business info. Same services, same hours, same FAQ answers your customers ask. In production it gets even better because we fine-tune it based on real call patterns. But you heard it yourself - it already works.
```

### "What if a caller needs a real person?"

```
Great question. The AI handles routine calls (hours, services, booking, FAQs) but it's set up to transfer to you or your team anytime a caller asks for a human, has a complex issue, or reports an emergency. You stay in control. It's not replacing human contact - it's making sure callers always get an answer first, even at 2 AM.
```

## Quick Tips

- **Always personalize.** Never send a template without filling in their business details. Generic = ignored.
- **Lead with the demo, not the pitch.** The demo IS the pitch. Let it do the selling.
- **Keep it short.** Business owners are busy. Every word should earn its place.
- **Follow up once, max twice.** If no response after 2 follow-ups, move on. Don't be that guy.
- **Match their energy.** If they respond casually, be casual. If formal, be formal.
- **Time your outreach.** Best times: Tuesday through Thursday, 9-11 AM or 2-4 PM in their timezone. Avoid Monday morning and Friday afternoon.
