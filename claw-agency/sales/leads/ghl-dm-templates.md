# GHL Agency Owner DM Templates
*Created: 2026-02-28 | Agent: Flint (SDR)*
*Target: GoHighLevel agency owners (highest-leverage prospect segment)*

---

## Rules for Using These Templates

1. **Never send any template word-for-word.** Always customize the bracketed sections and adapt the tone to match how the person writes.
2. **Read their last 3-5 posts/comments first.** Reference something specific. Generic DMs get ignored.
3. **Never pitch in the first message.** These templates are conversation starters, not sales pitches. Product talk happens in message 2 or 3, only if they engage.
4. **Cap at 5-10 DMs per day.** Quality over volume. Reddit users check your post history.
5. **No links, no company name, no CTAs in message one.** Just be a helpful person who works in this space.
6. **Log every DM** in the lead tracking spreadsheet (see `lead-monitoring-setup.md`).

## Template 1: Agency Owner Complaining About GHL Voice AI Quality

**When to use:** Someone in r/GoHighLevel posts about GHL's native voice AI being robotic, unreliable, having latency issues, or dropping calls. This is the most common complaint in the sub and our strongest opening.

**What to reference:** Their specific complaint (latency, robotic voice, call drops, inaccurate transcription, etc.)

```
Hey, saw your post about [specific issue, e.g., "the voice AI sounding robotic on the first 2 seconds of every call"]. We ran into the exact same thing.

Ended up switching to a custom voice stack outside of GHL's native integration. Way lower latency, more natural voice, and it still pushes everything back into GHL workflows (call notes, tags, pipeline updates, the whole thing).

If you want I can break down the setup. It's not complicated, just a different approach than what GHL ships out of the box.
```

**Why it works:** Validates their frustration instead of dismissing it. Positions you as someone who solved the problem, not someone selling something. Offers to share knowledge, not a product.

## Template 2: Agency Owner Asking About Voice AI Pricing

**When to use:** Someone asks about pricing for voice AI solutions, per-minute costs, or whether voice AI is worth the expense for their clients. Common in threads comparing GHL's built-in pricing vs. third-party options.

**What to reference:** Their pricing concern or the specific use case they're pricing out (e.g., inbound only, outbound calling, appointment booking).

```
Good question. Pricing in this space is all over the place right now.

The per-minute model (Vapi, Bland, etc.) looks cheap on paper but gets expensive fast once your clients are handling 200+ calls/month. We moved a few agency clients to a flat-rate model and their margins got way healthier.

What kind of call volume are your clients doing? That changes the math a lot. Happy to share what we've seen work for agencies in the [their niche if visible, otherwise "home services"] space.
```

**Why it works:** Answers their actual question with useful context. Shows you understand agency economics (margins, per-seat math). The follow-up question keeps the conversation going without any pitch.

## Template 3: Agency Owner Looking for White-Label Fulfillment Partner

**When to use:** Someone posts asking who can build/fulfill voice AI, automations, or AI services for their agency clients. They want a "you sell, we build" partner. This is the highest-intent signal for our agency model.

**What to reference:** What they're specifically looking to outsource and what their client base looks like.

```
Hey, this is pretty much exactly what we do.

We handle the build and ongoing management for [specific service they mentioned, e.g., "AI voice agents"] so the agency can sell it under their brand. Right now we're working with a few agencies in the [their vertical if visible] space doing it this way.

What does your client base look like? That'll help me figure out if the setup would actually make sense for you or not.
```

**Why it works:** Direct and simple because the intent is already there. No need to be coy when someone is literally asking for what you offer. Still keeps it conversational by asking about their situation rather than listing features.

## Template 4: Agency Owner Sharing a Client Win

**When to use:** Someone posts a success story (new client signed, hit a revenue milestone, SaaS mode growth, etc.). This is a relationship-building play, not a sales play. You're planting a seed for future conversation.

**What to reference:** Their specific win. Be genuine. Don't make it about you.

```
That's solid, congrats. [Reference specific detail, e.g., "Going from 12 to 40 sub-accounts in 6 months is no joke."]

Quick question since you're scaling fast: are you offering any voice AI or call handling as part of your packages? I've been seeing agencies add it as a retention play and it seems to stick really well. Curious if that's on your radar or if you're focused on other stuff right now.
```

**Why it works:** Leads with a genuine compliment tied to a specific detail (not generic "great job!"). The question is casual and exploratory. If they say "no, not yet" that's a future opening. If they say "yes, but it's been rough," that's Template 1 territory. Either way, you're in a conversation.

## Template 5: Agency Owner Asking About Vapi/Bland/Retell Comparison

**When to use:** Someone is evaluating voice AI platforms and asking the community which one to choose. These threads pop up weekly in r/GoHighLevel and r/SaaS. The person is in active buying mode but hasn't committed yet.

**What to reference:** Which platforms they're comparing and what use case they're building for.

```
We tested all three pretty extensively. Short version:

Vapi has the best developer tooling but the per-minute costs add up once you're past 50+ concurrent calls. Bland is solid for outbound campaigns but their inbound handling is still rough. Retell has the cleanest voice quality right now but their integration layer needs more work.

What's the main use case for your clients? Inbound reception, outbound booking, or both? That narrows it down a lot. I can share what stack ended up working best for agencies doing [their use case if visible].
```

**Why it works:** Gives a genuine, balanced comparison that respects the person's intelligence. No trash-talking competitors. Demonstrates real experience with these tools. The follow-up question positions you as someone who can help them make the right choice based on their specific situation, not someone pushing a single option.

## Follow-Up Templates (For Message 2+, Only After They Reply)

### If They Ask "What Do You Use?" or "Tell Me More"

```
Sure. We built a custom voice stack using [keep it vague or technical depending on their level, e.g., "Pipecat + a custom orchestration layer"] that handles inbound calls, qualifies the caller, books appointments, and pushes everything into GHL.

The main difference from the off-the-shelf options is latency (sub-500ms response time) and voice quality. Clients can't tell it's not a person on most calls.

Want me to send you a demo number you can call? Easier to hear it than read about it.
```

### If They Ask About Pricing / How It Works for Agencies

```
For agencies we do a white-label setup. Your branding, your client relationship, we handle the tech and ongoing management.

Setup is a one-time build fee per client template (so you pay once for "restaurant" and deploy it to all your restaurant clients). Then a flat monthly per seat that gives you room to mark up however you want.

Most agencies we're working with sell it at $297-$497/mo to their clients and keep solid margin. Want me to walk you through the numbers for your setup?
```

## What NOT to Do

- Don't DM someone who posted a win just to pitch them. Congratulate first, pitch never (in the first message).
- Don't trash GHL's native voice AI. Agency owners are loyal to the platform. Position alternatives as "supplements" not "replacements."
- Don't claim to be a user if you're a vendor. If asked directly, be honest: "I work in this space" is fine.
- Don't follow up if they don't reply. One DM. That's it. If they come back later, great.
- Don't use the word "revolutionary," "game-changing," "cutting-edge," or any other marketing fluff.
- Don't use em dashes. Keep punctuation simple and casual.
