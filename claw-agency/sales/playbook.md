## Overview

ClawOps sells AI automation services to small and mid-size businesses. Our sales cycle is 7-21 days. Every deal follows: **Lead > Qualify > Discovery > Propose > Close > Onboard.**

We win by being fast, honest, and results-focused. We don't sell "AI" as a buzzword. We sell hours saved, errors eliminated, and revenue recovered.

---

## 1. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Best-Fit Companies
- Revenue: $500K-$10M/year
- Team size: 5-200 employees
- Industries: Marketing agencies, e-commerce, real estate, SaaS, coaching, professional services, home services
- Tech stack: Already using 3+ software tools (CRM, email, project management, etc.)
- Pain: Spending 10+ hours/week on manual, repetitive processes

### Best-Fit Contacts
- Founders, CEOs, COOs, Operations Managers
- People who feel the pain directly
- Decision-makers who can approve $500-$5K without a committee

### Red Flags (Disqualify Fast)
- No measurable problem, just "curious about AI"
- Budget under $500 with no flexibility
- Want free consulting disguised as a "discovery call"
- Internal IT team that wants to build it themselves
- No timeline ("maybe someday")

## 2. Lead Qualification (BANT Framework)

Score each criterion 1-3. Minimum score of 8/12 to pursue.

| Criteria | 1 (Weak) | 2 (Moderate) | 3 (Strong) |
|----------|----------|--------------|-------------|
| **Budget** | No budget discussed, price-sensitive | Has budget but unclear allocation | Confirmed budget of $500+ |
| **Authority** | Employee with no buying power | Manager, needs approval | Owner/founder, decides alone |
| **Need** | Vague interest in "AI" | Identified pain point, no urgency | Clear bottleneck costing time/money NOW |
| **Timeline** | "Maybe next quarter" | Within 60 days | Within 30 days or immediate |

## 3. The Sales Process

### Stage 1: Lead Generation
- Sources: LinkedIn outbound, cold email, Reddit, referrals, inbound (website, content)
- Daily activity: 20 connection requests, 10 comments, 30 cold emails, 5 Reddit engagements
- See: lead-gen-plan.md and prospect-list.md

### Stage 2: Qualification
- Apply BANT scoring within the first 2 interactions
- Score below 8: nurture list (monthly content)
- Score 8+: book discovery call within 48 hours

### Stage 3: Discovery Call (25-30 min)
- Full script in call-scripts.md
- Key objectives: understand their workflow, quantify their pain, confirm BANT, paint the solution
- End with: proposal delivery date + next meeting booked

### Stage 4: Proposal
- Send within 24 hours of discovery call
- Use proposal-template.md
- Always present 2-3 options (Starter, Growth, Enterprise)
- Include ROI calculation based on their numbers from discovery

### Stage 5: Follow-Up
- Start the 5-Touch post-proposal sequence (see follow-up-sequences.md)
- Day 1: proposal + personal note
- Day 3: check-in
- Day 7: value-add (case study)
- Day 14: direct ask
- Day 30: graceful exit

### Stage 6: Close
- Send contract (contract-template.md)
- Collect payment (50% upfront for projects, first month for Growth)
- Schedule kickoff within 48 hours

### Stage 7: Onboard and Handoff
- Welcome email
- Kickoff call
- Project setup in PM tool
- Sales owns relationship until kickoff is complete

## 4. Objection Handling

### "Too expensive."
> "What's this problem costing you right now in time or money? [Let them answer.] So you're spending $[X]/month on this manually. Our solution pays for itself in [timeframe]. It's not a cost, it's an offset."

### "We don't trust AI / it's too new."
> "Totally fair. We build specific, tested automations for your exact workflow. You approve every step. We start small so you see results before committing more."

### "We can build it ourselves."
> "You could. But should you? Your team's time is worth $[X]/hr. We do this daily and can have it running in [timeframe]. Building internally takes 3-5x longer and pulls your team off revenue work."

### "I need to think about it."
> "Of course. What specifically do you want to think through? Let me address that now so you have everything you need."

### "We tried automation before and it didn't work."
> "What happened? [Listen.] That's common with generic tools. We build around YOUR workflow, not force you into a template. And we support it ongoing so it doesn't break."

### "Can you do a free trial?"
> "We don't do free work, but our Starter package at $500 is designed for exactly this. One focused automation so you see the quality and results before going bigger. Lowest risk possible."

### "Your competitor is cheaper."
> "Cheaper how? [Listen.] We focus on results, not hours. A $500 automation that saves you $3K/month is a better deal than a $200 automation that breaks in two weeks."

### "I need to talk to my partner/team."
> "Absolutely. Would it help if I joined a quick call with them to answer questions directly? That way you don't have to play telephone."

## 5. Closing Techniques

### The Assumptive Close
> "I'll send the contract over today. Would you prefer to kick off next Monday or the following week?"

### The Summary Close
> "To recap: we're automating [X], saving you [Y hours/week], for [Z]. Everything look right? Let's get started."

### The Urgency Close (only when real)
> "We have capacity for two new projects this month. After that, onboarding pushes to [date]. Want to lock in your spot?"

### The Starter Bridge
> "Let's start with Starter. $500, one automation. If you love it, we scale up. If not, you still got a working automation."

### The ROI Close
> "You told me this costs you $[X]/month in manual labor. Our fee is $[Y]/month. That's a [Z]x return. When do you want to start saving?"

## 10. Key Principles

1. **Sell outcomes, not features.** "Save 15 hours/week" beats "we use Make.com and GPT-4."
2. **Disqualify fast.** Bad-fit clients cost more than no clients.
3. **Follow up relentlessly.** 80% of deals close after the 3rd-7th touch.
4. **Always add value.** Every interaction should give them something useful.
5. **Be honest.** If we can't help, say so. Referrals from honesty are worth more than forced deals.
6. **Speed wins.** Respond within 1 hour during business hours. Send proposals within 24 hours.
7. **Document everything.** If it's not in the CRM, it didn't happen.
