# Mystery Shopper Script Template

A proven outreach approach: call a prospect's business after hours, document what happens, then DM the owner with a gap analysis they can't ignore.

---

## Why This Works

Most business owners have no idea what happens when their phone rings after 5 PM. They assume voicemail catches everything. In reality, 85% of callers who hit voicemail never call back. They just call the next business on Google.

You're not selling. You're showing them a problem they didn't know they had, backed by firsthand evidence. That changes the entire dynamic from "stranger pitching me" to "someone who just revealed a leak in my business."

## Step 1: Build Your Call List

**Target businesses that depend on inbound calls:**
- HVAC, plumbing, electrical, home services
- Dental offices, med spas, chiropractors
- Auto repair shops, body shops
- Law firms (personal injury, family law, criminal defense)
- Restaurants, salons, barbershops
- Veterinary clinics, pet groomers
- Real estate agents, property management

**Where to find them:**
- Google Maps (search "[industry] near [city]")
- Yelp listings
- Google Business profiles
- Reddit (r/smallbusiness, industry subs, local subs)
- Facebook business pages

**What to note before calling:**
- Business name
- Phone number
- Listed hours (from Google or their website)
- Owner name (from Google, LinkedIn, or their website)
- Their social media handles (for DM delivery)

## Step 2: Make the Call (After Hours)

**When to call:**
- 30 minutes to 2 hours after their posted closing time
- Weekends (especially Sunday)
- Holidays
- Early morning (before their listed opening time)

**What to listen for and document:**

### Scenario A: Rings to Generic Voicemail
- "You've reached [number]. Please leave a message."
- No business name, no hours, no direction to book online
- **Score: Critical gap.** Caller has zero confidence they reached the right business.

### Scenario B: Rings to Business Voicemail (Recorded Message)
- "Thanks for calling [Business Name]. We're closed right now. Leave a message and we'll call you back."
- Better, but still loses most callers. No way to book, no alternative contact.
- **Score: Significant gap.** They acknowledge the business exists but offer no solution.

### Scenario C: No Answer, No Voicemail
- Phone rings 8-10 times and disconnects
- Or goes to a "this number is not available" message
- **Score: Severe gap.** Caller gets nothing. Complete dead end.

### Scenario D: Answering Service (Human)
- A third-party receptionist answers
- Usually can only take a message, sometimes books appointments
- Note: Is the person knowledgeable about the business? Can they answer basic questions?
- **Score: Partial coverage.** Better than nothing, but expensive and limited.

### Scenario E: IVR / Phone Tree
- "Press 1 for appointments, press 2 for billing..."
- Note: Does it actually lead anywhere after hours, or just loop back to voicemail?
- **Score: Varies.** Often creates a dead end with extra steps.

**Document everything:**
- Date and time of call
- What happened (exact experience, in your words)
- How many rings before something happened
- Whether you could book an appointment: yes or no
- Whether you got any useful information: yes or no
- Overall caller experience rating: terrible / poor / mediocre / acceptable

## Step 3: Write the Gap Analysis DM

Send via Instagram DM, Facebook Messenger, LinkedIn, Reddit DM, or email, depending on where you found them.

### Template A: Direct to Business Owner

```
Hey [OWNER_NAME], this is going to sound weird but I think you'll appreciate it.

I called [BUSINESS_NAME] last night at [TIME] to see what happens when a potential customer calls after hours. Here's what I got:

[DESCRIBE EXACTLY WHAT HAPPENED - e.g., "The phone rang 8 times and went to a generic voicemail with no business name, no hours, and no way to book an appointment."]

I do this research for businesses in [THEIR_INDUSTRY] because it's the #1 revenue leak nobody talks about. Industry data shows 62% of calls to service businesses go unanswered, and 85% of those callers never try again. They just call the next result on Google.

I'm not saying this to be critical. Your [BUSINESS_NAME] clearly has a solid reputation. But if even 5 calls a month are falling through the cracks, that's [ESTIMATED_LOST_REVENUE] in lost revenue depending on your average ticket.

I set up AI phone systems that answer every call 24/7 in your brand voice, book appointments, answer FAQs, and send you a summary of every conversation. $2,500 setup, $497/mo, no contracts.

Would it be worth a 10-minute conversation? I can even build a free demo for [BUSINESS_NAME] so you can hear exactly what your callers would experience.
```

Hey [AGENCY_CONTACT], I did something kind of unusual and wanted to share the results with you.

- [CLIENT_2]: No answer, no voicemail. Dead end.

This isn't an attack on your work. You're doing the marketing to get the phone ringing. The problem is what happens when nobody's there to pick up.

We white-label AI receptionist systems for agencies exactly like yours. Your clients get 24/7 call answering under your brand. You charge them $300-500/mo, your cost is $100/mo per seat. $5,000 setup gets you fully branded with training and your first client deployment.

## Step 4: Follow-Up Sequence

### If they respond with interest:
Move directly into the personalized demo workflow (see `personalized-demo/delivery-checklist.md`). Build their demo within 15 minutes and send it.

### If they respond with skepticism or pushback:
```
Totally fair to be skeptical. I'm a stranger who called your business, I get it.

Here's the thing though: I'm not the only one calling after hours. Your actual customers are too. And unlike me, they don't send you a DM about it. They just move on.

I can build a free demo for [BUSINESS_NAME] in about 15 minutes. You call a number, hear your business name, your services, your hours, and watch it book an appointment. If it's not impressive, no hard feelings. Deal?
```

### If no response after 48 hours:
```
Hey [NAME], just bumping this. I know a random DM about your phone system is probably low on the priority list.

Quick question though: do you know what percentage of your calls go unanswered? Most [INDUSTRY] businesses are shocked when they see the data. If you're curious, I'm happy to run a full analysis. No cost, no strings.
```

### If still no response after 5 days:
Move on. Add them to a long-term nurture list. They might come back when they notice the problem themselves.

## Tips for Maximum Impact

1. **Call from a real number, not a blocked one.** You want the experience to mirror what a real customer gets.
2. **Try calling during business hours too** and note how long it takes to get answered. If it takes 6+ rings during business hours, that's also a gap worth mentioning.
3. **Screenshot the Google listing** showing their hours next to your call log timestamp. Visual proof is powerful.
4. **Batch your calls.** Do 10-15 in one evening session. You'll have a full pipeline of outreach ready for the next morning.
5. **Lead with empathy, not criticism.** You're helping them see a blind spot, not attacking their business. The tone should be "thought you'd want to know" not "you're doing it wrong."
6. **The best time to DM is the morning after the call.** Business owners check messages in the morning. Lead with the call from last night and the data is fresh.
7. **If they have a good after-hours setup** (rare), acknowledge it and move on. Don't force a pitch where no gap exists.
