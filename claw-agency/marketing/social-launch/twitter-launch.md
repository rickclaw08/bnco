### Day 3: Educational Thread (Thread Format)

Before you automate anything, ask these 3 questions:

🧵

---

**1/ Is it repetitive?**

If the task happens >5x per week with the same steps every time, automate it.

If it's unique every time, automation won't help. Focus on human judgment tasks.

---

**2/ What's the failure mode?**

One automation mistake = lose a client? Keep humans in the loop.

One mistake = re-send an email? Automate away.

Match the risk to the oversight level.

---

**3/ Can you document it?**

If you can't write down the exact steps, you can't automate it.

Documentation first. Automation second.

Most "automation failures" are actually "we didn't understand the process" failures.

---

Start with one task. Prove it works. Expand.

That's how you build reliable systems instead of expensive chaos.

## Metrics to Track

**Weekly:**
- Follower growth
- Engagement rate (likes, retweets, replies per tweet)
- Profile visits
- Link clicks (to website, ROI calculator, audit tool)

**What success looks like:**
- 50-100 followers/month (organic, no follow/unfollow tactics)
- 2-5% engagement rate on tweets
- 10-20 website visits per week from Twitter
- 3-5 meaningful replies/DMs per week

**Don't obsess over vanity metrics.** Quality followers > follower count. Engagement > impressions.

## DMs and Replies Strategy

**When someone DMs asking about services:**
- Respond within 24 hours
- Ask 2-3 qualifying questions (what's the bottleneck, team size, timeline)
- Offer a free 15-minute audit call
- Don't pitch immediately (build trust first)

**When someone replies to your tweets:**
- Respond to every reply in the first 48 hours
- Be helpful and conversational (not robotic)
- Continue the conversation if they're engaged
