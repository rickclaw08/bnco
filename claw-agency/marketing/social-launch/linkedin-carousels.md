**Design specs:**
- 1080 x 1080 pixels per slide (square format)

**Publishing tip:** Create the graphics in Canva, Figma, or similar tool. Upload as a PDF or multiple images to LinkedIn.

## Publishing Strategy

**When to post carousels:**
- 1-2 carousels per week
- Post on Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday (best engagement days)
- Post between 9-11 AM EST (peak business hours)

**How to promote them:**
- Share in relevant LinkedIn Groups
- Tag relevant people (ask first) if appropriate
- Repurpose into Twitter threads (text-only version)
- Pin top-performing carousels to your profile

**Track performance:**
- Impressions
- Engagement rate (likes, comments, shares)
- Click-through rate to website/tools
- Which carousels get the most saves (saves = high value content)

**Iterate:**
- If a carousel performs well, create similar content
- If it flops, analyze why (too long? too sales-y? unclear value?)
- Test different hooks, visuals, and CTAs

---

## Design Tips

- **Keep text minimal** (people skim on LinkedIn)
- **Use large, readable fonts** (mobile-first design)
- **Stick to 2-3 brand colors** (consistency builds recognition)
- **Use icons and visuals** (not just text walls)
- **End with a strong CTA** (tell people exactly what to do next)

**Tools:**
- Canva (easiest, has LinkedIn carousel templates)
- Figma (more control, better for custom designs)
- Adobe Express (similar to Canva)
