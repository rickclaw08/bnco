### Post 4: Educational / Thought Leadership

**Text:**

The 3 questions to ask before automating anything:

**1. Is this task repetitive?**  
If it happens more than 5 times per week, it's a candidate for automation. If it's ad-hoc or unique every time, automation won't help.

**2. Is the failure mode acceptable?**  
Automation isn't perfect. If one mistake costs you a client or legal trouble, you need human oversight. If a mistake means re-sending an email, automation is fine.

**3. Can I document the steps?**  
If you can't write down exactly how the task works, you can't automate it. Documentation is step one, automation is step two.

Too many businesses try to automate everything at once and end up with fragile systems that break constantly.

Start with one task. Prove it works. Expand from there.

That's how you build reliable automation instead of expensive chaos.
