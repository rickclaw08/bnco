## Part 1: Testimonial Collection Framework

### When to Ask
- **Trigger point:** 30 days after deployment, once measurable results exist
- **Second ask:** 90 days (for updated numbers)
- Never ask before they've seen results. Empty testimonials are worthless.

### How to Ask (Email Template)

**Subject:** Quick favor (2 minutes)

Hey [Name],

It's been about a month since we deployed [system name]. Last I checked, [specific metric — e.g., "your ticket response time is down to 4 minutes from 6 hours"].

Would you be open to sharing a quick testimonial about your experience? It helps us a ton and takes about 2 minutes.

Here are 3 questions — answer any or all:

1. What was the biggest problem we solved for you?
2. What specific result has stood out the most?
3. Would you recommend Claw Systems to other business owners? Why?

You can reply to this email, or if you prefer, I can send a quick form. Totally fine to keep it to 2-3 sentences.

Thanks,
Rick

### The 5-Star Framework (Guide for Clients)

When clients aren't sure what to write, give them this structure:

1. **Before:** What was the problem? ("We were spending X hours on...")
2. **After:** What changed? ("Now our system handles...")
3. **Number:** One specific metric ("Saved us X hours/week" or "Response time went from X to Y")
4. **Feeling:** How does it feel? ("Our team finally has time to..." or "I stopped worrying about...")
5. **Recommend:** Would you recommend it? ("If you're dealing with X, talk to Rick.")

### Formats to Collect
- **Written testimonial** (2-4 sentences) — Minimum ask
- **LinkedIn recommendation** — Ask them to post on LinkedIn and tag Claw Systems
- **Video testimonial** (60-90 seconds) — Premium social proof. Offer to do a quick Zoom recording.
- **Case study interview** (15 minutes) — For detailed write-ups. Record, transcribe, edit.

### Testimonial Storage
Keep a running doc with:
- Client name + company + title
- Date of project
- Services provided
- Testimonial text (verbatim)
- Permission level (name OK, company OK, anonymous only)
- Photo/headshot (if provided)

## Part 2: Case Study Outlines

### Case Study 1: E-Commerce Support Automation

**Title:** "How [Brand] Cut Support Costs by 60% While Improving Customer Satisfaction"

**Client Profile:**
- DTC e-commerce brand
- 500-1,000 orders/day
- 2-person support team drowning in tickets

**The Problem:**
- 200+ support tickets/day, 80% repetitive (order status, returns, sizing)
- Average response time: 6 hours
- Support team burnt out, turnover risk
- Customer satisfaction declining

**What We Built:**
- AI-powered ticket classification system
- Auto-response engine for 8 common ticket types
- Smart routing for complex issues (with full context)
- Customer sentiment detection for priority escalation

**Implementation:**
- Week 1: Audit existing tickets, identify patterns, classify ticket types
- Week 2: Build classification + auto-response system, train on historical tickets
- Week 3: Deploy with human review layer, tune accuracy
- Week 4: Remove review layer for high-confidence responses, monitor

**Results:**
- 80% of tickets auto-resolved (160/day)
- Response time: 6 hours → 3 minutes
- Support team reduced from 2 → 1 (reassigned, not fired)
- Customer satisfaction: +18% (measured via post-ticket survey)
- Annual savings: ~$52,000

**Client Quote:** *"I was skeptical about AI handling our customer conversations. But the responses are better than what we were sending manually — because they're consistent, fast, and always accurate. Our one remaining support person actually enjoys the job now because she only deals with interesting problems."*

### Case Study 2: Real Estate Lead Qualification

**Title:** "From 15 to 45 Booked Showings/Month — Same Ad Spend, Zero Extra Headcount"

**Client Profile:**
- Real estate team (1 lead agent, 3 buyer's agents)
- Spending $4,000/month on lead gen (Zillow, Google, Facebook)
- Getting 300+ leads/month, converting poorly

**The Problem:**
- Leads responded to in 4-6 hours (often next day)
- No systematic qualification — agents called everyone
- 70% of calls were unqualified or unreachable
- Agents frustrated, spending more time dialing than showing

**What We Built:**
- Unified lead capture from all sources (Zillow, website, Facebook, Google)
- Instant response system (personalized text + email within 60 seconds)
- AI qualification flow (budget, timeline, pre-approval status, location preferences)
- Lead scoring algorithm
- Automated calendar booking for qualified leads
- 90-day nurture sequence for not-yet-ready leads

**Implementation:**
- Week 1: Integrate all lead sources, build response templates
- Week 2: Build qualification flow and scoring logic
- Week 3: Connect to team calendars, deploy nurture sequences
- Week 4: Launch, monitor, tune qualification criteria

**Results:**
- Lead response time: 4-6 hours → 47 seconds
- Booked showings: 15/month → 45/month (3x)
- Agent time on unqualified leads: reduced by 75%
- Closed deals: +40% over 90 days
- ROI: System paid for itself in first 2 closed deals

**Client Quote:** *"We were throwing away leads we paid good money for, just because we couldn't follow up fast enough. Now every lead gets handled instantly and my agents only talk to people who are actually ready to buy. It's a completely different business."*

### Case Study 3: SaaS Onboarding & Churn Reduction

**Title:** "23% Less Churn in 90 Days: How Behavior-Based Onboarding Changed Everything"

**Client Profile:**
- B2B SaaS tool (project management space)
- $49-199/month plans
- 500+ new signups/month
- 60-day churn rate: 35%

**The Problem:**
- Generic time-based onboarding emails (Day 1, Day 3, Day 7)
- No visibility into which users were struggling
- CS team manually checking in with every new user (45 min each)
- High churn, no clear signal of who would stay vs. leave

**What We Built:**
- Behavioral event tracking (5 key activation events identified)
- User scoring system based on activation progress
- Behavior-triggered email sequences (not time-based)
- Stall detection: alerts CS team when active users go quiet
- At-risk user dashboard with suggested talking points

**Implementation:**
- Week 1: Identify activation events through data analysis, set up tracking
- Week 2: Build scoring system and email sequences
- Week 3: Build CS alerting system and dashboard
- Week 4: Deploy, A/B test against old onboarding flow

**Results:**
- 90-day churn: 35% → 27% (23% reduction)
- Users hitting activation milestone: +40%
- CS team time per user: 45 min → 10 min (focused on high-value)
- Net revenue retention improved by 12 points
- Expansion revenue up (more users reaching advanced features)

**Client Quote:** *"Our old onboarding was basically 'spray and pray.' Now we know exactly which users need help and which ones are thriving. Our CS team went from reactive firefighting to proactive relationship building. The churn numbers speak for themselves."*

- "47-second average lead response time"
- "8-week average payback period"

- [ ] Partner badges (Make.com, Zapier, OpenAI)
- [ ] "X businesses automated" counter on website
- [ ] LinkedIn recommendations (aim for 10+ in first 90 days)
