## Part 1: Testimonial Collection Framework

### When to Ask
- **Trigger point:** 30 days after deployment, once measurable results exist
- **Second ask:** 90 days (for updated numbers)
- Never ask before they've seen results. Empty testimonials are worthless.

### How to Ask (Email Template)

**Subject:** Quick favor (2 minutes)

Hey [Name],

It's been about a month since we deployed [system name]. Last I checked, [specific metric, e.g., "your ticket response time is down to 4 minutes from 6 hours"].

Would you be open to sharing a quick testimonial about your experience? It helps us a ton and takes about 2 minutes.

Here are 3 questions, answer any or all:

1. What was the biggest problem we solved for you?
2. What specific result has stood out the most?
3. Would you recommend ClawOps to other business owners? Why?

You can reply to this email, or if you prefer, I can send a quick form. Totally fine to keep it to 2-3 sentences.

Thanks,
Rick

### The 5-Star Framework (Guide for Clients)

1. **Before:** What was the problem? ("We were spending X hours on...")
2. **After:** What changed? ("Now our system handles...")
3. **Number:** One specific metric ("Saved us X hours/week" or "Response time went from X to Y")
4. **Feeling:** How does it feel? ("Our team finally has time to..." or "I stopped worrying about...")
5. **Recommend:** Would you recommend it? ("If you're dealing with X, talk to Rick.")

### Formats to Collect
- **Written testimonial** (2-4 sentences) - Minimum ask
- **LinkedIn recommendation** - Ask them to post on LinkedIn and tag ClawOps
- **Video testimonial** (60-90 seconds) - Premium social proof. Offer to do a quick Zoom recording.
- **Case study interview** (15 minutes) - For detailed write-ups. Record, transcribe, edit.

### Testimonial Storage
Keep a running doc with:
- Client name + company + title
- Date of project
- Services provided
- Testimonial text (verbatim)
- Permission level (name OK, company OK, anonymous only)
- Photo/headshot (if provided)

## Part 2: Case Study Outlines

### Case Study 1: E-Commerce Support Automation

**Title:** "How [Brand] Cut Support Costs by 60% While Improving Customer Satisfaction"

**Client Profile:**
- DTC e-commerce brand
- 500-1,000 orders/day
- 2-person support team drowning in tickets

**The Problem:**
- 200+ support tickets/day, 80% repetitive
- Average response time: 6 hours
- Support team burnt out, turnover risk

**What We Built:**
- AI-powered ticket classification system
- Auto-response engine for 8 common ticket types
- Smart routing for complex issues with full context
- Customer sentiment detection for priority escalation

**Results:**
- 80% of tickets auto-resolved (160/day)
- Response time: 6 hours to 3 minutes
- Support team reduced from 2 to 1 (reassigned, not fired)
- Customer satisfaction: +18%
- Annual savings: ~$52,000

**Client Quote:** *"I was skeptical about AI handling our customer conversations. But the responses are better than what we were sending manually, because they're consistent, fast, and always accurate."*
