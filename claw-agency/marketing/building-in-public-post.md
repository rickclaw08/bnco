## Post Body:

I've been building AI automation for small businesses for about two months. Wanted to share what I've learned because I see a lot of "I made $50K in my first month" posts and not enough "here's what actually happens when you try this."

**The idea**

Service businesses (plumbers, HVAC techs, roofers, dentists) miss 30-40% of their inbound calls. They're on job sites. They're with customers. The phone rings and nobody picks up. That caller moves on to the next company.

I built an AI receptionist that answers the phone, asks the right questions, books the appointment, and sends a confirmation text. Sounds simple. Took way longer than I expected to make it not sound like a robot.

**The tech stack and real costs**

Here's what the system actually costs me to run per client:

- Twilio (phone number + call minutes): ~$15-20/month
- Speech-to-text (Deepgram): ~$5-10/month per client depending on volume
- LLM (GPT-4o-mini for conversation): ~$3-8/month per client
- Text-to-speech (ElevenLabs): ~$10-15/month per client
- Hosting and infra: ~$5/month

**Total COGS per client: roughly $40-55/month.**

I charge $2,000 setup and $300/month. So margins are solid IF you can get clients.

**What I tried for getting clients**

1. **Cold email.** Sent about 200 emails to local contractors. Response rate was basically zero. Not even angry replies. Just silence. Turns out every AI agency on the planet is doing this right now and business owners are filtering all of it straight to trash. One guy on r/smallbusiness said he gets 15+ AI pitch emails a week. We're all just noise at this point.

2. **Upwork/Fiverr.** Requires verified identity and the race to the bottom on pricing makes it hard to sell a $2K setup. Didn't pursue this seriously.

3. **Reddit.** This is where things started to click. Not from posting about my product, but from just answering questions in subreddits where business owners hang out. Someone asks "how do I handle calls when I'm on a job?" and I give a real answer. No link. No pitch. A few people checked my profile and reached out.

**What I learned**

- The product demo matters more than anything else. When I let someone call my AI and hear it answer with their business name, their tone, their services, the reaction is always "whoa." A pitch deck or cold email will never come close to that.

- Building for a specific vertical is 10x easier than being a "general AI agency." When I say "I build AI receptionists for plumbing companies" people get it instantly. When I said "I do AI automation for businesses" people glazed over.

- The real competitor is not other AI companies. It's voicemail. Most of these business owners have accepted missed calls as a fact of life. The sale is convincing them it doesn't have to be that way.

- After-hours calls are the killer feature. A plumber at 10 PM is not answering the phone. But that homeowner with a burst pipe is desperate. An AI that picks up at 10 PM and dispatches someone is worth 10x the monthly fee in a single call.

- The hardest part of the entire build was making the AI handle interruptions naturally. If someone talks over the AI and it keeps going with its script, it sounds terrible. Getting the voice activity detection right took more time than building the actual conversation logic.

**Where I am now**

Being honest: no paying clients yet. I have a working product, a demo anyone can call, and a pipeline of interested people but no signed contracts. The gap between "this is cool" and "here's my credit card" is wider than I expected.

What I'm doing next: building personalized demos for specific businesses (scrape their site, use their name and services, give them a number to call), posting useful content in places where my target customers already hang out, and stopping all cold outreach.

**The numbers I'm watching**

- Inbound messages from people who found me organically
- Demo calls completed (people who actually call the AI)
- Time from first contact to signed deal

**What would you do differently?** I'm especially curious if anyone has sold done-for-you AI services to local/service businesses and what channel actually worked for closing. Not leads. Closing.
