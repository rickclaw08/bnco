# LinkedIn Cold DM Templates

## Strategy Overview
These messages are designed to feel personal, not automated. Each template targets a specific persona and pain point. Goal: 20-30% reply rate, 10-15% conversion to discovery call.

**Rules for Success:**
- Customize the first line (comment on their profile/post)
- Keep under 100 words
- One specific value prop
- Low-pressure CTA
- NO generic "I help businesses" openers

---

## Template 1: The Scaling Business Owner

**Best for**: Founders/owners of 15-100 employee companies showing growth signals

**Subject**: Quick question about [Company Name]'s operations

Hi [First Name],

Saw you recently [hired/expanded/raised funding/specific observation from their profile]. Congrats on the growth.

Quick question: as you scale, how are you handling [specific operational task relevant to their industry - e.g., "client onboarding" for agencies, "invoice processing" for service businesses]?

Most companies at your stage are drowning in manual work. We just helped [similar company] automate their [specific process] and saved them 25 hours/week.

Worth a 15-minute conversation? I can share what we're seeing work for [their industry] right now.

[Your Name]

## Customization Checklist

Before sending ANY of these, customize:

**✅ First line** - Reference something specific from their profile:
- Recent post or article they shared
- Job change or promotion
- Company milestone (funding, hiring, expansion)
- Shared connection or interest

**✅ Pain point** - Make it specific to their:
- Industry
- Company size
- Role/title
- Recent activity

**✅ Social proof** - Use relevant examples:
- Same industry
- Similar company size
- Comparable challenge

**✅ CTA** - Make it low-friction:
- "20 minutes" not "demo"
- "Happy to share" not "I want to show you"
- "Quick call?" not "Are you available for a consultation"

---

## Message Timing Strategy

**Day 1**: Send initial message (morning, 8-10 AM)  
**Day 4**: If no reply, view their profile (they get notification)  
**Day 7**: Send follow-up (use Template 10 or variation)  
**Day 14**: Final touchpoint, then move to nurture sequence  

**Best days**: Tuesday-Thursday  
**Best times**: 8-10 AM or 4-6 PM (before/after core work hours)  

**Avoid**: Monday mornings, Friday afternoons, weekends

## Reply Handling Scripts

### When they say "Send me more info"
"Happy to. What specifically would be most useful - case studies from [their industry], pricing overview, or a quick video walkthrough of our process? Want to make sure I send what's actually relevant."

### When they say "Not right now"
"Totally understand. Mind if I ask - is it timing, budget, or just not a priority at the moment? Just helps me know whether to check back in a month or six months."

### When they ask about pricing
"Depends on what we're automating. Projects typically range from $3K-$15K depending on complexity. Want to hop on a quick call so I can give you a real number based on your specific needs?"

### When they say "We're already working with someone"
"That's great - good automation partners are hard to find. If you ever want a second opinion on what you've got set up, happy to do a free audit. No strings attached."

## Success Metrics

Track these for each template:

- **Send → Reply rate**: Target 20-30%
- **Reply → Discovery call booked**: Target 40-50%
- **Discovery → Paid engagement**: Target 30-40%

If a template consistently underperforms (sub-15% reply rate), kill it or revise.

---

## Don'ts (Common Mistakes)

❌ "I hope this message finds you well" - generic and boring  
❌ "I'd love to connect" - sounds like spam  
❌ "Let me know if you're interested" - weak CTA  
❌ Writing a novel - keep it under 100 words  
❌ Pitching your service in the first message - lead with value  
❌ Asking "Do you have time for a call?" - gives them an easy out  
❌ Using emojis - unless their profile suggests they use them  
❌ Sending LinkedIn voice notes - too personal for cold outreach  

---

## LinkedIn Profile Optimization (Required for Cold DM Success)

Before sending any DMs, make sure YOUR profile is dialed in:

**Headline**: Not "CMO at ClawOps" - instead "I help [target audience] save 20+ hours/week through automation | [X] companies scaled"

**About section**: 
- Lead with results, not credentials
- Include 3-5 specific case studies
- Clear CTA to book a call

**Featured section**:
- Case studies
- Video testimonials
- Free resources (automation assessment, guides)

**Activity**:
- Post 3-5x per week about automation, efficiency, scaling
- Engage with target audience posts
- Share client wins (with permission)

Remember: They WILL check your profile before replying. Make it convert.

---

**Bottom line: These templates work because they're specific, valuable, and low-pressure. Customize heavily, test constantly, and kill what doesn't work. Aim for 15-20 qualified discovery calls per week from 75-100 messages sent.**
