### Slide 26-30: The Framework (How to Think About Automation)

**Slide 26: The Automation Decision Tree**
- Visual flowchart:
  - Is this task repetitive? (Yes → continue, No → delegate or eliminate)
  - Does it follow clear rules? (Yes → automate, No → document process first)
  - Is it worth the setup time? (Yes → automate, No → simplify or batch)

**Slide 27: What TO Automate**
✅ High-frequency tasks (daily/weekly)  
✅ Rule-based decisions (if X, then Y)  
✅ Data transfer between systems  
✅ Notifications and reminders  
✅ Report generation  
✅ Repetitive communication  

**Slide 28: What NOT to Automate (Yet)**
❌ Tasks you do once per quarter  
❌ Complex judgment calls  
❌ Customer-facing interactions that need personal touch  
❌ Processes that change frequently  
❌ Things you haven't documented  

**Slide 29: The ROI Formula**
- Time saved per week × hourly value × 52 weeks = annual value
- Cost of setup (one-time) + cost of tools (ongoing)
- Payback period calculation
- Example: $3K setup saves 15 hrs/week at $75/hr = $58K/year ROI

**Slide 30: How to Prioritize**
- Matrix: Impact (time saved) vs. Effort (complexity)
- Focus on high-impact, low-effort first (quick wins)
- Then tackle high-impact, high-effort (big projects)
- Ignore low-impact tasks (not worth it)
