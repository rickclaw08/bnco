## Post 5: How to Calculate ROI on Business Automation (With Template)

**Target Keyword:** automation ROI calculator  
**Search Intent:** Transactional/Informational (wants tool + understanding)  
**Word Count:** 2,000-2,500  
**Meta Description:** Wondering if automation is worth it? Learn how to calculate ROI on business automation with our free template. Includes formulas, examples, and real case studies.

### Outline

- Hook: "Is automation worth the investment?" Here's how to know for sure

**Section 1: Why ROI Matters for Automation Decisions (300 words)**
- Automation requires upfront investment (time or money)
- Without ROI tracking, you can't prove value
- CFOs and stakeholders need numbers, not feelings
- Good ROI calculation prevents bad investments
- Helps prioritize: which automation first?

**Section 2: The Automation ROI Formula (400 words)**

**Basic Formula:**
```
ROI = (Total Benefits - Total Costs) / Total Costs x 100
```

**Total Benefits Include:**
- Labor hours saved x hourly cost
- Error reduction (cost of mistakes prevented)
- Revenue gained (faster response times, more capacity)
- Customer satisfaction improvements (retention value)
- Opportunity cost recovered (what else team can now do)

**Total Costs Include:**
- Tool subscription fees (monthly or annual)
- Implementation costs (agency fees or internal dev time)
- Training time (hours x hourly cost)
- Maintenance and support
- Downtime during transition

**Example Calculation:**
- **Process:** Invoice follow-ups
- **Manual time:** 8 hours/week x $25/hour = $200/week = $10,400/year
- **Automation cost:** $3,000 setup + $600/year subscription = $3,600 total year 1
- **Time saved:** 95% (7.6 hours/week) = $9,880/year value
- **ROI Year 1:** ($9,880 - $3,600) / $3,600 x 100 = **174% ROI**
- **Payback period:** 18 weeks

**Section 3: Step-by-Step: How to Calculate Your Automation ROI (600 words)**

**Step 1: Define the Process**
- Name it specifically ("Monthly financial reporting" not just "reporting")
- Document current steps
- Identify who's involved

**Step 2: Measure Current State**
- **Time spent:** Track for 2 weeks, average it
- **Frequency:** Daily, weekly, monthly?
- **People involved:** How many, what's their hourly cost?
- **Current error rate:** How often does it go wrong?
- **Cost of errors:** What does a mistake cost to fix?

**Example tracking table:**
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Hours per cycle | 6 hours |
| Cycles per month | 12 |
| Total hours/month | 72 hours |
| Hourly cost (loaded) | $35 |
| Monthly labor cost | $2,520 |
| Annual labor cost | $30,240 |

**Step 3: Estimate Automation Efficiency**
- Most automations save 70-95% of time
- Be conservative: assume 70% for your calculation
- Example: 72 hours/month x 70% = 50.4 hours saved

**Step 4: Calculate Time Savings Value**
- Hours saved per month x hourly cost x 12 months
- Example: 50.4 hours x $35 x 12 = $21,168/year

**Step 5: Factor in Error Reduction**
- Current error rate and cost per error
- Estimated reduction (often 90%+)
- Example: 5 errors/month x $200/fix x 90% reduction x 12 = $10,800/year

**Step 6: Add Revenue or Capacity Gains**
- Can team now handle more clients with freed time?
- Faster response time = better customer retention?
- Conservative estimate or exclude if hard to quantify

**Step 7: Calculate Total Costs**
- Get quotes from vendors or agencies
- Include training time (hours x cost)
- First-year costs vs. ongoing costs
- Example: $8,000 implementation + $1,200/year tool + $500 training = $9,700 year 1

**Step 8: Run the ROI Formula**
```
Total Benefits Year 1: $21,168 (time) + $10,800 (errors) = $31,968
Total Costs Year 1: $9,700
ROI = ($31,968 - $9,700) / $9,700 x 100 = 229% ROI
Payback Period = $9,700 / ($31,968/12 months) = 3.6 months
```

**Mistake 1: Only Counting Direct Labor**
