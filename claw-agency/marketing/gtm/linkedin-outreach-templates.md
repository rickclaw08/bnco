## General Outreach Best Practices

### Before You Send

- [ ] Research the prospect (LinkedIn profile, company website, recent posts)
- [ ] Confirm they match ICP (ideal customer profile)
- [ ] Personalize at least one line (no spray-and-pray)
- [ ] Check for mutual connections or warm intro paths
- [ ] Have a clear CTA (call, case study, scorecard, etc.)

### After You Send

- [ ] Log in CRM with sent date and template used
- [ ] Set follow-up reminder (3-5 days if no response)
- [ ] Track response rate by template (optimize over time)
- [ ] Move to different cadence if no response after 2 follow-ups

### What NOT to Do

- ❌ Send generic copy-paste messages
- ❌ Pitch in the first message (build rapport first)
- ❌ Write walls of text (keep it scannable)
- ❌ Use aggressive language ("Just following up AGAIN...")
- ❌ Lie or exaggerate results
- ❌ Forget to proofread (typos kill credibility)

### Response Rate Benchmarks

- **Cold outreach:** 10-20% response rate is solid
- **Warm intros:** 40-60% response rate expected
- **Event/trigger-based:** 25-35% response rate

Track your numbers monthly and optimize the lowest performers.

## Objection Handling Scripts

### "We're already using [Tool X]"

"That's great, [Tool X] is solid for [what it does]. Most of our clients use tools like that too. Where we come in is connecting those tools together and building the custom logic that tools alone can't handle. Would it be worth exploring what that could look like for you?"

---

### "We don't have budget right now"

"Totally understand. Most of our clients didn't have 'automation budget' either, they reallocated from planned hires or operational costs once they saw the ROI. Would it be helpful to run the numbers together so you know what it'd take to make it cash-flow positive?"

---

### "We're too small for this"

"I hear that a lot, but actually, smaller teams benefit most because every hour saved has bigger impact. We've worked with teams as small as 3 people. If you're spending even 10 hours a week on repetitive stuff, the math works. Want to see a quick breakdown?"

---

### "Can't we just use Zapier?"

"You absolutely can, and we recommend it for simple stuff. Where we add value is when workflows get complex, need custom logic, or require integrations Zapier doesn't support. Think of us as the team that builds what Zapier can't. Make sense?"

---

### "We tried automation before and it didn't work"

"That's frustrating. Can I ask what went wrong? Most failed automation projects fail because of bad process design, not bad tech. We start with fixing the process first, then automating it. Would it be worth a fresh look with that approach?"

---

### "Send me some info"

"Happy to. What's most useful, a case study from [industry], an ROI breakdown, or a quick video walkthrough of a similar project? I don't want to spam your inbox with stuff that's not relevant."

(Forces them to pick, increases engagement.)

## Follow-Up Cadence (If No Response)

**Day 0:** Initial outreach  
**Day 4:** Follow-up #1 (add value, no pressure)  
**Day 9:** Follow-up #2 (new angle, case study, or question)  
**Day 16:** Follow-up #3 (breakup email: "Should I close your file?")  
**Day 30+:** Move to nurture campaign (quarterly check-ins, content shares)

### Breakup Email Template

Hi [First Name],

I've reached out a few times about automation opportunities for [Company], but haven't heard back.

Totally fine if now's not the right time or it's just not a fit.

Should I close your file, or would you rather I check back in a few months?

Either way, no hard feelings. Good luck with [specific thing about their business].

Best,  
Victoria

---

**Why breakup emails work:**
- Gives them an easy out (reduces guilt)
- Often triggers a response ("Sorry, been swamped, let's talk")
- Shows respect for their time
- Ends the loop professionally

## A/B Test Ideas

Run these tests over 50+ sends each to optimize:

1. **Short vs. Long:** 3-sentence message vs. 6-sentence message
2. **Question vs. Statement:** Open with question vs. open with statement
3. **Case Study vs. Pain Point:** Lead with proof vs. lead with problem
4. **Casual vs. Formal:** "Hey" vs. "Hi" or "Hello"
5. **CTA Placement:** End with CTA vs. middle of message

Track response rate, meeting book rate, and close rate for each variant.
