5 automations every small business should have running by now (and most don't):

1. **Instant lead response**
When someone fills out your form or sends an inquiry, they should get a personalized reply within 60 seconds. Not a generic "thanks, we'll get back to you." A real, context-aware response that moves them forward.

2. **Invoice and payment follow-up**
Stop chasing payments manually. Set up automated reminders at 3 days, 7 days, and 14 days overdue. Include payment links. Escalate to a human only when needed. Most businesses recover 15-25% more revenue just from consistent follow-up.

3. **Customer onboarding sequences**
New client signs up? Trigger a welcome flow: send contracts, collect info, schedule kickoff calls, grant system access. All without someone on your team touching it.

4. **Report generation**
If anyone on your team spends more than 30 minutes a week pulling numbers into a spreadsheet, that's automated in 2026. Connect your data sources, define the template, schedule delivery. Done.

5. **Meeting prep and follow-up**
Before every sales call: pull CRM data, recent interactions, company info, and prep a one-page brief. After every call: send a summary, update CRM, trigger next steps. Your reps close more when they show up prepared.

Here's what all five have in common: they're not expensive, they're not complicated, and they pay for themselves within weeks.

The gap between businesses that automate and businesses that don't is growing fast. If you're still doing these manually, 2026 is the year to fix that.
