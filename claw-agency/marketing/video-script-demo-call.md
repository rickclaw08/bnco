# Video Script: AI Receptionist Demo Call

**Duration:** 60 seconds
**Format:** Screen recording of phone call + text overlays
**Tone:** Real, no hype, let the product speak for itself

---

## Scene 1: The Problem (0:00 - 0:08)

**ON SCREEN:** Split screen. Left side: contractor on a roof, phone buzzing in pocket. Right side: phone screen showing incoming call from "New Lead - Google."

**TEXT OVERLAY:** "You're on a job. The phone rings. You can't answer."

**AUDIO:** Phone ringing twice, then silence. Voicemail beep.

**TEXT OVERLAY:** "85% of callers won't leave a voicemail. They call your competitor instead."

---

## Scene 2: The Solution - Dialing In (0:08 - 0:14)

**ON SCREEN:** Close-up of a phone screen. Finger taps the dialer. Number shown: (888) 457-8980. Call connects.

**TEXT OVERLAY:** "But what if every call got answered?"

**AUDIO:** One ring, then pickup.

---

## Scene 3: Sarah Answers (0:14 - 0:22)

**ON SCREEN:** Call screen active, audio waveform visual showing AI speaking.

**SARAH (AI):** "Thanks for calling! This is Sarah. How can I help you today?"

**CALLER:** "Yeah, hi. My AC unit stopped blowing cold air this morning. I need someone to come take a look."

**TEXT OVERLAY:** "AI receptionist. Answers in under 5 seconds. 24/7."

---

## Scene 4: Qualifying the Lead (0:22 - 0:38)

**ON SCREEN:** Continues on call screen. Small animated checklist appears on the right side, items checking off as Sarah asks questions.

**SARAH:** "I can definitely help with that. Is this for a residential or commercial property?"

**CALLER:** "Residential. It's a single-family home."

**SARAH:** "Got it. And what is the address?"

**CALLER:** "742 Oakwood Drive."

**SARAH:** "Thanks. Do you know the make or model of the unit, or would you like the technician to identify it on site?"

**CALLER:** "No idea. It's whatever came with the house."

**SARAH:** "No problem at all. Let me check the schedule and get you set up."

**CHECKLIST ITEMS (animating in):**
- Service type: AC repair ✓
- Property: Residential ✓
- Address: Captured ✓
- Unit info: On-site ID ✓

---

## Scene 5: Booking the Appointment (0:38 - 0:48)

**ON SCREEN:** Calendar view briefly flashes, then back to call screen.

**SARAH:** "I have an opening tomorrow between 9 and 11 AM. Does that work for you?"

**CALLER:** "Tomorrow morning works great."

**SARAH:** "Perfect. You are booked for tomorrow, 9 to 11 AM. I will send you a confirmation text right now with all the details. Is there anything else I can help with?"

**CALLER:** "Nope, that's it. Thanks, Sarah."

**SARAH:** "You're welcome. Stay cool out there!"

**TEXT OVERLAY:** "Lead qualified. Appointment booked. Confirmation sent. Zero hold time."

---

## Scene 6: The CTA (0:48 - 0:60)

**ON SCREEN:** Dark background with ClawOps logo. Text fades in line by line.

**TEXT LINE 1:** "That call took 40 seconds."

**TEXT LINE 2:** "No voicemail. No missed lead. No phone tag."

**TEXT LINE 3:** "This could be YOUR receptionist."

**BOLD TEXT:** "$1,997 one-time. No monthly fees."

**CTA BUTTON (animated):** "Claim Your Founding Member Spot"

**URL:** theclawops.com/founding

**SMALL TEXT:** "20 spots available. Call the demo yourself: (888) 457-8980"

**AUDIO:** Subtle, confident outro music (2-3 seconds, fade out).

---

## Production Notes

- Keep the call audio clean and natural. Sarah should sound warm, professional, and human, not robotic.
- The checklist animation during Scene 4 reinforces that the AI is doing real qualification work, not just taking a message.
- No background music during the call itself. Let the conversation carry the scene. Music only on the final CTA card.
- Total runtime target: 55-60 seconds. Cut any pauses to keep pacing tight.
- Color palette: dark navy background (#050810), green accents (#4ade80), white text.
