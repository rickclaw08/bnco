## Post 2: AI Automation Reality Check

"We tried AI automation and it failed."

I hear this weekly. Here's why it fails (and how to fix it):

**What doesn't work:**
- Throwing ChatGPT at everything hoping it sticks
- Automating broken processes (garbage in, garbage out)
- No human oversight or quality checks

**What actually works:**
- Start with ONE painful, repetitive task
- Map the process before automating
- Keep humans in the loop for decisions
- Measure results ruthlessly

Real example: A client wanted to "automate customer support."

We started smaller. Just auto-categorizing support tickets.

Results: 40% faster response times, zero AI hallucinations, support team loved it.

Then we added auto-drafts for common questions.
Then FAQ generation from ticket patterns.

6 months later, their 3-person support team handles 5x the volume.

AI automation isn't all-or-nothing. It's incremental.

Start with the lowest-hanging fruit. Prove value. Scale from there.

What's one task your team does daily that makes everyone groan?

## Post 5: The 5-Minute Automation Test

Not sure if AI automation is right for your business?

Take this 5-minute test:

**Question 1:** Is someone on your team doing the same task more than 3x per week?
- Yes = 1 point

**Question 2:** Does that task follow a predictable pattern? (Same steps each time)
- Yes = 1 point

**Question 3:** Is the task taking 30+ minutes each time?
- Yes = 1 point

**Question 4:** Would mistakes in this task create problems?
- Yes = 1 point (quality matters)

**Question 5:** Is this task blocking other work or slowing down your team?
- Yes = 1 point

**Your score:**

0-1 points: Not worth automating yet
2-3 points: Good candidate, low priority
4-5 points: Prime automation opportunity

Here's the thing: most businesses have 5-10 tasks that score 4-5.

That's 5-10 opportunities to free up hours every week.

We help businesses identify and automate their highest-value opportunities first.
