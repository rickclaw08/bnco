## Thread 2: Why Your AI Automation Failed (And How to Fix It)

Your AI automation failed. Here's why (and how to fix it):

[THREAD]

1/ You automated a broken process

AI makes bad processes faster, not better.

Fix: Map your current workflow first. Find inefficiencies. THEN automate the improved version.

Example: Don't automate a 15-step approval process. Cut it to 5 steps, THEN automate.

2/ You didn't involve the actual users

Builders love complex solutions. Users want simple ones.

Fix: Shadow the person doing the work for 2 hours. Ask what frustrates them. Build THAT solution.

Reality check: If they don't cheer when you demo it, start over.

3/ No quality checks

AI hallucinates. It makes mistakes. You shipped with zero oversight.

Fix: Human-in-the-loop for anything customer-facing or important.

AI drafts, human approves. AI suggests, human decides.

Accuracy > speed.

4/ You tried to automate EVERYTHING at once

Big automation projects fail. Small ones succeed then scale.

Fix: Pick ONE task. Automate it completely. Prove value. Then move to task #2.

Stop building "AI platforms." Start automating Tuesday's annoying work.

5/ No ongoing maintenance

AI tools need tuning. APIs change. Edge cases appear.

Fix: Plan for 2-4 hours/month of maintenance.

Monitor errors. Gather feedback. Iterate.

Automation isn't "set and forget." It's "set and improve."

The pattern I see work:
- Start small (1 task)
- Involve users early
- Keep humans in loop
- Measure results weekly
- Iterate based on feedback

Automation is a process, not a project.

What automation have you tried? What happened?

## Thread 5: Automate Your Business in 30 Days (Roadmap)

🚀 How to automate your business in 30 days (week-by-week roadmap):

[THREAD]

WEEK 1: Audit & Identify

Days 1-3: Shadow your team
- Watch what they actually do (not what you think they do)
- Note every repeated task
- Ask: "What work feels like a waste of time?"

Days 4-7: Score opportunities
- Frequency (how often?)
- Time cost (how long?)
- Error risk (what if it's wrong?)
- Skill level (does it need expertise?)

Pick your top 3 automation targets.

WEEK 2: Design & Test

Days 8-10: Map the process
- Document every step of task #1
- Identify decision points
- Spot edge cases

Days 11-14: Build MVP automation
- Use no-code tools first (Zapier, Make)
- Keep it simple
- Test with dummy data

Goal: Working prototype by end of week.

WEEK 3: Deploy & Train

Days 15-17: Launch with 1 user
- Train them thoroughly
- Monitor closely
- Fix bugs immediately

Days 18-21: Gather feedback
- What works?
- What's annoying?
- What's missing?

Iterate based on real usage, not assumptions.

WEEK 4: Scale & Measure

Days 22-24: Roll out to full team
- Document the workflow
- Create troubleshooting guide
- Set up error monitoring

Days 25-30: Measure results
- Time saved per week
- Error rate
- User satisfaction

Then start on automation #2.

The pattern:
Week 1: Find it
Week 2: Build it
Week 3: Test it
Week 4: Scale it

Repeat monthly.

By month 6, you'll have automated 6 major workflows.

That's 20-40 hours/week back.
