# Partnership Playbook - Referral Network Strategy

## Overview
Build a network of referral partners who send us clients in exchange for 15% commission on first-year revenue. Target: 10-15 active partners generating 5-10 qualified leads per month.

**Why This Works:**
- Partners get passive income for referrals they'd make anyway
- We get pre-qualified leads with built-in trust
- Win-win-win (us, partner, client)

---

## Ideal Partner Profile

### Who Makes a Great Referral Partner?

**1. Web Design / Development Agencies**
- They build sites but don't handle ongoing operations
- Clients often need backend automation after launch
- Natural handoff point: "site is done, now let's optimize your workflows"

**2. Marketing Agencies**
- Focus on lead generation, need ops support for lead processing
- Clients scale marketing but operations can't keep up
- We solve the "marketing is working but we're drowning" problem

**3. Bookkeepers / Accounting Firms**
- See client inefficiencies firsthand (manual invoicing, data entry hell)
- Want to add value beyond tax prep
- Trust factor is high - if accountant recommends us, client listens

**4. Business Coaches / Consultants**
- Identify operational bottlenecks but don't implement solutions
- We become their implementation arm
- High-trust relationship with clients

### Partner Red Flags
❌ Direct competitors (other automation agencies)  
❌ Partners who want exclusivity (we need multiple partners per vertical)  
❌ "Tire kickers" who won't actively refer  
❌ Companies with bad reputations (reflects on us)  

---

## Commission Structure

### Base Offer: 15% of First-Year Revenue

**How It Works:**
- Partner refers qualified lead
- We close the deal
- Partner receives 15% of total first-year revenue from that client
- Paid monthly as we invoice the client

**Example Scenarios:**

**Scenario 1: $5K Project**
- Project fee: $5,000
- Partner commission: $750 (one-time)
- Paid within 30 days of project completion

**Scenario 2: $2K/month Retainer**
- Monthly retainer: $2,000
- Annual value: $24,000
- Partner commission: $3,600 (paid $300/month for 12 months)

**Scenario 3: $10K Setup + $1K/month Ongoing**
- Year 1 total: $22,000
- Partner commission: $3,300
- Paid: $1,500 upfront (from setup fee) + $165/month for 12 months

### Tracking & Payment
- Partner portal access to see their referrals and commissions
- Monthly statements emailed automatically
- Payment via ACH, PayPal, or check (partner's choice)
- Net 15 terms (paid 15 days after we receive client payment)

### Special Incentives

**Volume Bonuses:**
- 3+ qualified referrals in 90 days: +2% commission (17% total)
- 5+ qualified referrals in 90 days: +5% commission (20% total)

**First Referral Bonus:**
- $500 bonus for first successful referral (closes within 90 days)
- Proves they're serious, gets them invested

## Partner Pitch (Call Script)

### Opening (2 minutes)
"Thanks for taking the time. I'll keep this quick. 

We work with businesses that are growing but drowning in manual work. Think: spreadsheets everywhere, data entry, things falling through the cracks, too many tools that don't talk to each other.

We come in and automate their operations. Usually saves them 20-40 hours a week.

The reason I wanted to chat is because I think there's a natural fit between what you do and what we do."

### The Opportunity (3 minutes)
"Here's what I'm thinking:

Your clients [specific to their business - examples below] probably run into operational bottlenecks all the time. 

- **For web agencies**: 'You build them a beautiful site, then they realize their inquiry forms don't connect to their CRM, their invoicing is still manual, and they're copying data between five different tools.'

- **For marketing agencies**: 'You generate leads for them, but then their follow-up process is a mess. They can't scale because their operations can't keep up with the marketing.'

- **For bookkeepers**: 'You see clients wasting hours on manual invoice entry, chasing down receipts, and updating spreadsheets when all of that could be automated.'

- **For coaches**: 'You identify the problems and create the strategy, but your clients need someone to actually implement the systems.'

**That's where we come in.**

We handle all of that. And instead of you saying 'sorry, can't help with that,' you can introduce us and earn 15% of whatever they pay us in the first year.

Make sense so far?"

### The Details (3 minutes)
"Here's how it actually works:

**Step 1**: You identify a client who has operational pain. Usually comes up naturally in conversation.

**Step 2**: Warm intro - email introduction or three-way call. We take it from there.

**Step 3**: We run the sales process, do the work, invoice the client.

**Step 4**: You get 15% of first-year revenue, paid monthly. So if they're on a $2K/month retainer, you make $300/month for 12 months. If it's a $10K project, you get $1,500.

**Step 5**: We send you a monthly statement and payment. You do nothing except cash the check.

No exclusivity. No non-compete. You refer when it makes sense. We handle everything else."

### Objection Handling (5 minutes)

**"How do I know you'll take care of my clients?"**
"Great question. Here's our process: [explain discovery process, communication cadence, client satisfaction guarantees]. Plus, you're welcome to stay looped in as much or as little as you want. Some partners want weekly updates, others just want to know when it's done. Your call."

**"What if my client isn't happy?"**
"Then we fix it. And if we genuinely drop the ball, we'll refund them AND still pay your commission. Your reputation is on the line when you refer us, so we treat that seriously."

**"What's a 'qualified referral'?"**
"Good question. Qualified means:
- They have a clear operational problem (we'll help you identify this)
- Budget of at least $2K for a project or $500+/month for ongoing
- They're ready to move forward in the next 30-90 days

We're NOT picky. If you think they might be a fit, send them over. Worst case, we tell them it's not a fit and point them in the right direction. Best case, you make money."

**"I don't want to be 'salesy' with my clients."**
"You're not selling. You're solving a problem they already have. Here's how to position it: 'Hey, you mentioned [specific pain point]. I know a team that specializes in exactly that. Would it be helpful if I introduced you?' That's it. No pitch. Just a helpful introduction."

### Closing (2 minutes)
"So here's what I'm thinking:

Let's do a test run. I'll send you the partnership agreement to review (it's simple, 2 pages). If it looks good, we're official partners.

Then, over the next 30-60 days, if you come across a client who's a good fit, send them my way. We'll treat them like gold, and you'll make some extra money.

If it works, great. We keep going. If not, no harm done.

Sound good?"

**[Pause for response]**

"Perfect. I'll send the agreement this afternoon. Anything else you want to know before we wrap up?"

## Partnership Agreement (Outline)

### Key Terms to Include

**1. Parties**
- ClawOps LLC (referred to as "Company")
- [Partner Name/Entity] (referred to as "Partner")

**2. Referral Arrangement**
- Partner will refer qualified leads to Company
- Company will handle all sales, delivery, and client management
- No exclusivity - Partner may work with other service providers

**3. Commission Structure**
- 15% of first-year gross revenue from referred clients
- Paid monthly, Net 15 terms
- Volume bonuses: 17% at 3 referrals, 20% at 5 referrals per 90 days
- First referral bonus: $500 upon first successful close

**4. Qualified Referral Definition**
- Client must be a new customer (not existing ClawOps client)
- Client must explicitly be referred by Partner (tracked via unique link or intro)
- Client must have budget of $2K+ (project) or $500+/month (retainer)

**5. Payment Terms**
- Commission calculated on revenue received (not invoiced)
- Monthly statements provided by 5th of each month
- Payment issued by 20th of each month for prior month's revenue
- Payment method: ACH, PayPal, or check (Partner's choice)

**6. Relationship**
- Independent contractor relationship (not employee, not JV)
- Partner responsible for own taxes and liabilities
- No authority to bind Company in any way

**7. Confidentiality**
- Both parties agree to keep client information confidential
- Exception: Partner may use client name as social proof with client's permission

**8. Term & Termination**
- Agreement effective upon signature, no fixed end date
- Either party may terminate with 30 days written notice
- Commissions continue for clients referred prior to termination

**9. Liability & Indemnification**
- Company responsible for quality of work delivered to referred clients
- Partner not liable for Company's work product or mistakes
- Partner liable for any misrepresentations made about Company services

**10. Miscellaneous**
- Governed by [State] law
- Amendments require written consent from both parties
- Entire agreement (supersedes any prior discussions)

## Partner Objection Handling Guide

### "I don't want to risk my reputation"
**Response**: "I totally get that. Here's what we do to protect your reputation: [explain quality control, client communication process, satisfaction guarantee]. Plus, we'll do a trial referral first. If you're not 100% happy with how we treated your client, we'll part ways. No hard feelings."

### "15% seems low"
**Response**: "Fair point. Industry standard for referral partnerships is 10-20%, and we're at the higher end of that. Plus, it's recurring for 12 months on retainers. But I'm open to discussing a higher rate if you're going to be sending consistent volume. If you can commit to 3+ referrals per quarter, we can look at 20%."

### "I tried a referral partnership before and it didn't work"
**Response**: "What went wrong?" [Listen] "Here's how we're different: [address their specific concern]. Plus, there's no long-term commitment here. Let's try one referral and see how it goes."

### "My clients won't pay for this"
**Response**: "You might be surprised. Our average client ROI is 3-5x in the first year. If we save them 20 hours a week, that's $4K/month in recovered labor costs for a $2K investment. Most of the time, the objection isn't money - it's understanding the value. That's why the intro call is important."

### "I don't have time to actively refer"
**Response**: "You don't need to. This is passive. When a client mentions operational struggles, you say, 'I know someone who can help.' That's it. We handle the rest. The partners who do best just keep us top of mind and make intros when it's natural."
