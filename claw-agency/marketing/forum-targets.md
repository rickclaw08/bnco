# Forum & Facebook Group Targets for ClawOps SMB Outreach

## 1. Facebook: "Small Business Automation & AI Tools"
**Link:** https://www.facebook.com/groups/smallbusinessautomation/
**Members:** ~25K+ (estimated)
**Strategy:** This group focuses specifically on automation tools for small businesses. Join and establish credibility by answering questions about workflow automation for 1-2 weeks before mentioning ClawOps. Share case studies as "lessons learned" posts. Best posting times are Tuesday-Thursday mornings when business owners are planning their week.

## 2. Facebook: "Automate Your Agency"
**Link:** https://www.facebook.com/groups/automateyouragency/
**Members:** ~15K+ (estimated)
**Strategy:** Agency owners looking to streamline operations. This is a high-intent group where people are actively seeking automation solutions. Focus on sharing n8n/Make.com tips and workflow templates. Position ClawOps as the team that builds custom automations for agencies that outgrow DIY solutions. Engage in comments before posting original content.

## 3. Facebook: "AI for Small Business Owners"
**Link:** https://www.facebook.com/groups/aiforsmallbusinessowners/
**Members:** ~10K+ (estimated)
**Strategy:** Non-technical business owners exploring AI. This audience needs hand-holding and plain-language explanations. Share "before and after" workflow diagrams showing manual vs automated processes. Avoid jargon. Lead with outcomes ("saved 12 hours/week") not technology ("deployed an n8n workflow with webhook triggers"). Offer free 15-minute audits as a lead magnet.

## 4. Reddit: r/AiForSmallBusiness
**Link:** https://www.reddit.com/r/AiForSmallBusiness/
**Members:** Growing community
**Strategy:** Already active here. Continue posting helpful answers in threads. Create original posts sharing automation case studies with real numbers (hours saved, cost reduction). Reddit rewards authenticity, so avoid salesy language. The subreddit is small enough that consistent helpful contributions will build recognition quickly. Post 2-3 comments per week answering questions, then 1 original post per month with a detailed case study.

## 5. IndieHackers Community
**Link:** https://www.indiehackers.com/
**Members:** 100K+ entrepreneurs and builders
**Strategy:** IndieHackers is full of solo founders and small team operators who are building products and services. Many are looking for ways to automate operations so they can focus on product development. Engage in the "How do you handle X?" threads. Share a detailed post about how ClawOps started (the story sells here). The community values transparency about revenue, challenges, and real numbers. Consider posting a "building in public" update monthly. Focus on the Groups section, particularly "Marketing" and "Operations" categories.

---

## General Notes
- For all Facebook groups: request to join with the RickClaw_Dev personal profile, not a business page
- Wait 5-7 days after joining before posting original content
- Engage genuinely in 10+ comment threads before self-promoting
- Never lead with a pitch. Lead with value, let curiosity do the selling
- Track which groups drive DMs and website visits using UTM parameters (clawops.com?utm_source=facebook&utm_medium=group&utm_campaign=groupname)
