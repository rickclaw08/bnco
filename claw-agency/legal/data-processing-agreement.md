## 1. Definitions

For purposes of this DPA:

- **"Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person as defined by applicable Data Protection Laws.
- **"Data Protection Laws"** means all applicable laws and regulations relating to data protection and privacy, including but not limited to the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), UK GDPR, and California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA).
- **"Processing"** means any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection, storage, use, disclosure, and deletion.
- **"Data Subject"** means an identified or identifiable natural person to whom Personal Data relates.
- **"Sub-processor"** means any third party engaged by ClawOps to process Personal Data on behalf of Client.
- **"Data Breach"** means a breach of security leading to accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to Personal Data.

## 2. Scope and Applicability

### 2.1 Scope of Processing
This DPA applies to the Processing of Personal Data by ClawOps on behalf of Client in connection with the Services described in the Service Agreement.

### 2.2 Roles
- **Client** acts as the Data Controller, determining the purposes and means of Processing Personal Data.
- **ClawOps** acts as the Data Processor, Processing Personal Data only on documented instructions from Client.

### 2.3 Precedence
In the event of conflict between this DPA and the Service Agreement, this DPA shall prevail with respect to data protection matters.

## 3. Client's Instructions and Compliance

### 3.1 Processing Instructions
ClawOps shall Process Personal Data only:
- In accordance with Client's documented instructions as set forth in this DPA and the Service Agreement
- To provide the Services
- As required by applicable law (in which case ClawOps shall notify Client before Processing, unless prohibited by law)

### 3.2 Unlawful Instructions
If ClawOps believes that any instruction from Client violates Data Protection Laws, ClawOps shall promptly notify Client and may suspend Processing until the parties resolve the issue.

### 3.3 Client Obligations
Client represents and warrants that:
- It has complied with all applicable Data Protection Laws
- It has provided all necessary notices and obtained all necessary consents for the Processing described in this DPA
- Its instructions to ClawOps comply with Data Protection Laws

## 5. Security Measures

### 5.1 Technical and Organizational Measures
ClawOps shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect Personal Data against Data Breaches, including:

**Technical Measures:**
- Encryption of Personal Data in transit (TLS/SSL) and at rest (AES-256 or equivalent)
- Access controls and authentication mechanisms
- Regular security testing and vulnerability assessments
- Secure API connections with third-party AI services
- Logging and monitoring of system access
- Secure data backup and recovery procedures

**Organizational Measures:**
- Security policies and procedures
- Employee training on data protection
- Confidentiality obligations for personnel
- Incident response procedures
- Vendor management and due diligence
- Regular review and update of security measures

### 5.2 Security Standards
ClawOps shall maintain security measures that are:
- Appropriate to the risks presented by the Processing
- Compliant with industry standards and best practices
- In accordance with applicable Data Protection Laws

### 5.3 Updates
ClawOps may update security measures from time to time, provided that such updates do not result in degradation of the overall level of protection.

## 7. Data Subject Rights

### 7.1 Assistance with Requests
ClawOps shall, to the extent legally permitted and commercially reasonable, assist Client in responding to Data Subject requests to exercise their rights under Data Protection Laws, including:
- Right of access
- Right to rectification
- Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")
- Right to restrict processing
- Right to data portability
- Right to object

### 7.2 Procedure
If ClawOps receives a Data Subject request directly, it shall:
- Promptly notify Client (within forty-eight (48) hours)
- Not respond to the request without Client's prior written authorization
- Provide Client with reasonable assistance in responding

### 7.3 Fees
ClawOps may charge reasonable fees for assistance with Data Subject requests if such requests are manifestly unfounded, excessive, or require significant resources beyond normal service provision.

## 8. Data Breach Notification

### 8.1 Notification Obligation
ClawOps shall notify Client without undue delay and in any event within seventy-two (72) hours after becoming aware of a Data Breach affecting Client's Personal Data.

### 8.2 Notification Content
Data Breach notifications shall include, to the extent known:
- Nature of the Data Breach, including categories and approximate number of Data Subjects and Personal Data records affected
- Name and contact details of ClawOps' data protection contact
- Likely consequences of the Data Breach
- Measures taken or proposed to address the Data Breach and mitigate its effects

### 8.3 Cooperation
ClawOps shall:
- Cooperate with Client in investigating and remediating the Data Breach
- Provide reasonable assistance to Client in notifying Data Subjects or authorities as required by law
- Take measures to prevent further Data Breaches

### 8.4 No Acknowledgment of Fault
Notification of a Data Breach under this Section 8 shall not constitute an acknowledgment of fault or liability by ClawOps.

## 9. Data Transfers

### 9.1 International Transfers
Personal Data may be transferred to and processed in countries outside the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, or Client's country of residence.

### 9.2 Transfer Mechanisms
When transferring Personal Data internationally, ClawOps shall implement appropriate safeguards, including:
- **Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs):** EU Commission-approved Standard Contractual Clauses for transfers from the EEA
- **UK Addendum:** UK International Data Transfer Addendum for transfers from the UK
- **CCPA Compliance:** Appropriate contractual provisions for transfers subject to CCPA
- **Other mechanisms:** Any other valid transfer mechanism under applicable Data Protection Laws

### 9.3 Data Localization
Upon Client's request and where commercially feasible, ClawOps shall process Personal Data within specific geographic regions subject to additional fees and availability.

### 9.4 Sub-processor Transfers
Sub-processors may process Personal Data in various locations as specified in Section 6.2. ClawOps ensures appropriate transfer mechanisms are in place with all Sub-processors.

## 10. Audits and Inspections

### 10.1 Audit Rights
Client may, upon reasonable notice and during normal business hours, audit ClawOps' compliance with this DPA up to once per calendar year, or more frequently if:
- Required by applicable Data Protection Laws
- A Data Breach has occurred
- A supervisory authority requests an audit

### 10.2 Audit Procedure
Audits shall be conducted:
- With at least thirty (30) days' prior written notice
- In a manner that does not interfere with ClawOps' business operations
- Subject to reasonable confidentiality obligations
- At Client's expense (unless the audit reveals material non-compliance)

### 10.3 Documentation
Instead of on-site audits, Client may request and ClawOps shall provide:
- Copies of relevant certifications (e.g., SOC 2, ISO 27001)
- Summaries of third-party audit reports
- Completed audit questionnaires
- Documentation demonstrating compliance with this DPA

### 10.4 Remediation
If an audit reveals non-compliance, ClawOps shall, at its expense, take commercially reasonable steps to remediate identified issues within a mutually agreed timeframe.

## 11. Data Retention and Deletion

### 11.1 Retention Period
ClawOps shall retain Personal Data only for as long as necessary to:
- Provide the Services
- Comply with legal obligations
- Resolve disputes
- Fulfill the purposes described in this DPA

### 11.2 Return or Deletion
Upon termination or expiration of the Service Agreement, or upon Client's written request, ClawOps shall, at Client's option:
- **Return:** Provide Client with a copy of all Personal Data in a commonly used, machine-readable format
- **Delete:** Securely delete or anonymize all Personal Data

### 11.3 Retention Exceptions
ClawOps may retain Personal Data to the extent required by applicable law or for legitimate business purposes, including:
- Backup copies for disaster recovery (deleted in accordance with retention schedules)
- Data required for legal compliance or litigation
- Aggregated or anonymized data that no longer constitutes Personal Data

### 11.4 Certification
Upon completion of deletion, ClawOps shall, upon request, provide written certification that Personal Data has been deleted in accordance with this Section 11.

## 13. Liability and Indemnification

### 13.1 Liability
Each party's liability under this DPA shall be subject to the limitations and exclusions set forth in the Service Agreement, except where Data Protection Laws prohibit such limitations.

### 13.2 GDPR-Specific Liability
Under GDPR Article 82:
- Each party shall be liable for damages caused by Processing that violates the GDPR
- A party shall be exempt from liability if it proves it is not responsible for the event giving rise to the damage
- Where both parties are involved in the same Processing, they shall be jointly and severally liable

### 13.3 Indemnification
ClawOps shall indemnify and hold Client harmless from third-party claims, fines, or penalties arising from ClawOps' violation of this DPA or applicable Data Protection Laws, except to the extent caused by Client's instructions or actions.

## Appendix C: Security Measures (Detailed)

### Technical Security Measures
1. **Encryption:** AES-256 encryption for data at rest; TLS 1.3 for data in transit
2. **Access Control:** Multi-factor authentication, role-based access control, principle of least privilege
3. **Network Security:** Firewalls, intrusion detection/prevention systems, network segmentation
4. **Vulnerability Management:** Regular security scanning, penetration testing, patch management
5. **Logging and Monitoring:** Centralized logging, security information and event management (SIEM), alerts for suspicious activity

### Organizational Security Measures
1. **Policies and Procedures:** Written information security policy, data protection procedures, incident response plan
2. **Personnel Security:** Background checks, confidentiality agreements, security awareness training
3. **Physical Security:** Secure data center facilities, access controls, environmental controls
4. **Vendor Management:** Due diligence on Sub-processors, contractual security requirements
5. **Business Continuity:** Backup and disaster recovery procedures, redundancy and failover systems
