# LLC Formation Guide

A step-by-step guide to forming a Limited Liability Company (LLC) in the United States for an AI Automation Agency.

---

## 1. Why an LLC?

- **Liability protection:** Personal assets are shielded from business debts and lawsuits
- **Tax flexibility:** Default pass-through taxation (no double taxation); can elect S-Corp status for tax optimization
- **Credibility:** Clients and partners take an LLC more seriously than a sole proprietorship
- **Simplicity:** Less paperwork than a corporation; fewer compliance requirements
- **Flexibility:** No restrictions on number of members; operating agreement is customizable

| Factor | Details |

### Recommendation
For a lean AI agency: **Wyoming LLC** if you want privacy and low costs. **Home state** if you want simplicity. **Delaware** only if investors are on the roadmap.

## 3. Step-by-Step Formation

### Step 1: Choose Your LLC Name
- Must be unique in your state of formation
- Must include "LLC" or "Limited Liability Company"
- Check availability: search your state's Secretary of State business name database
- Consider reserving the name ($25-$50 in most states) if you're not filing immediately
- Secure the matching domain name and social media handles

### Step 2: Appoint a Registered Agent
A registered agent receives legal/government documents on behalf of your LLC. Required in every state where you're registered.

**Options:**

| Provider | Cost/Year | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Northwest Registered Agent | $125 | Popular, includes free mail forwarding, privacy |
| ZenBusiness | $199 | Bundled with formation packages |
| Incfile / LegalZoom | $119-$299 | Well-known but higher upsell |
| Yourself | Free | Must have a physical address in the state; you're publicly listed |

**Recommendation:** Use **Northwest Registered Agent** — reliable, affordable, and they keep your home address off public records.

### Step 3: File Articles of Organization
- File with the Secretary of State in your chosen state
- Information typically required:
  - LLC name
  - Registered agent name and address
  - Principal office address
  - Member/manager names (varies by state)
  - Organizer signature
- **Online filing** is available in most states and is fastest

**Cost:** $50–$500 depending on state (see state comparison above)

### Step 4: Get an EIN (Employer Identification Number)
- Free from the IRS: [irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online)
- Required for bank accounts, hiring, and tax filings
- Takes ~5 minutes online; EIN issued immediately
- Apply as soon as your LLC is approved

### Step 5: Draft an Operating Agreement
Even if your state doesn't require it (most don't), **always have one**. It:
- Defines ownership percentages and member roles
- Establishes management structure (member-managed vs. manager-managed)
- Sets profit/loss distribution rules
- Outlines voting rights and decision-making processes
- Covers what happens if a member leaves, dies, or wants to sell
- Strengthens your liability protection (courts look for this)

**For single-member LLCs:** Still draft one. It demonstrates the LLC is a separate entity from you personally.

### Step 6: Open a Business Bank Account
- Required to maintain the "corporate veil" (separating personal and business finances)
- Bring: EIN letter, Articles of Organization, Operating Agreement, government ID
- **Recommended banks:**
  - **Mercury** — best for tech/digital businesses, excellent API
  - **Relay** — great for small businesses, free plan
  - **Chase Business** — traditional option, wide branch network

### Step 7: Set Up Accounting and Compliance
- **Accounting software:** QuickBooks Self-Employed or Xero
- **Separate all business and personal finances** from day one
- Track all income, expenses, and invoices
- Set aside **25-30% of revenue** for taxes (self-employment + income)

### Step 8: Obtain Business Licenses/Permits
- Most AI/consulting agencies need minimal licensing
- Check your city/county for a general business license
- Some states require a state business license
- No professional license typically required for AI consulting (but verify in your state)

### Step 9: Get Business Insurance
- **General liability insurance:** $500-$1,500/year — covers third-party bodily injury/property damage claims
- **Professional liability (E&O) insurance:** $500-$2,000/year — **essential for a consulting/services agency** — covers claims of negligence, errors, or failure to deliver
- **Cyber liability insurance:** Consider if handling client data
- **Providers:** Hiscox, Next Insurance, Hartford

## 6. S-Corp Election (Tax Optimization)

Once your agency earns **$40,000–$50,000+/year in profit**, consider electing S-Corp tax status (IRS Form 2553). Benefits:
- Pay yourself a "reasonable salary" and take remaining profits as distributions
- Distributions are **not subject to self-employment tax** (15.3%)
- Potential savings: thousands of dollars per year
- **Downside:** Requires payroll, additional tax filings (Form 1120-S), and accounting costs ($1,000–$3,000/year)

**When to do it:** Talk to a CPA when you're consistently profitable above $40K/year.
