# Referral & Partnership Agreement

**Between:** [Referring Partner Company/Individual] ("Partner")  
**And:** ClawOps ("Provider")  
**Date:** [Date]  
**Commission Rate:** 15% of initial contract value

---

## 1. Partnership Overview

This agreement establishes a referral relationship where Partner introduces qualified prospects to Provider for automation services. In exchange, Partner receives a commission on closed deals.

**Purpose:** Create mutually beneficial relationships with agencies, consultants, and professionals who serve clients that need automation solutions.

---

## 2. Referral Commission Structure

### Standard Commission: 15%

Partner earns **15% of the initial contract value** for each successfully closed deal.

**What Counts:**
- Sprint contracts: 15% of sprint fee
- Retainer agreements: 15% of first 3 months of fees ($5,000/mo × 3 = $2,250 commission)
- Custom projects: 15% of total project fee
- Enterprise MSAs: 15% of first project delivered under the MSA

**What Doesn't Count:**
- Renewals or extensions beyond initial term
- Add-on services purchased more than 90 days after initial contract
- Ongoing retainer months beyond the first 3

### Commission Tiers (Optional)

For partners generating significant volume, higher tiers may apply:

| Deals Closed (12 months) | Commission Rate |
|--------------------------|-----------------|
| 1-3 deals | 15% |
| 4-6 deals | 18% |
| 7+ deals | 20% |

Tiers reset annually and require separate written agreement.

---

## 3. Qualified Referrals

A referral is considered "qualified" when:

1. **First Contact:** Partner makes the introduction and clearly identifies themselves as referring party
2. **New Client:** Prospect has not previously engaged with Provider or been in Provider's pipeline
3. **Legitimate Interest:** Prospect has genuine need and budget for automation services
4. **Contact Info:** Partner provides prospect's name, company, email, and basic need description

**Duplicate Referrals:** If multiple partners refer the same prospect, the first documented introduction receives credit.

**Attribution Window:** Partner receives credit if prospect signs within 12 months of initial introduction.

---

## 4. Referral Process

### Step 1: Introduction
Partner introduces prospect to Provider via:
- Email introduction (copying both parties)
- Warm handoff call
- Submission through partner portal (if available)

**Required Info:**
- Prospect name, title, company
- Contact details (email, phone)
- Brief description of their automation needs
- Budget range (if known)

### Step 2: Provider Follow-Up
Provider will:
- Respond within 1 business day
- Conduct discovery and qualification
- Provide proposal and pricing
- Keep Partner informed of status

### Step 3: Deal Close
When prospect signs:
- Provider notifies Partner within 2 business days
- Commission is locked in and scheduled for payment
- Partner receives copy of signed agreement (with financial details redacted)

### Step 4: Commission Payment
Partner receives payment within **30 days** of:
- Client's contract signature, AND
- Client's initial payment received by Provider

---

## 5. Payment Terms

**Payment Schedule:** Commissions paid within 30 days after Provider receives client's initial payment.

**Payment Method:** ACH transfer, wire transfer, or PayPal (Partner covers any transaction fees)

**Tax Reporting:** Partner is responsible for all taxes. Provider will issue 1099 (US) or equivalent tax documents as required by law.

**Statements:** Partner receives monthly statements showing:
- Active referrals in pipeline
- Closed deals and commission earned
- Payment history

---

## 6. Partner Responsibilities

Partner agrees to:

### Ethical Representation
- Represent Provider accurately and professionally
- Not make false claims or guarantees about Provider's services
- Only share marketing materials approved by Provider
- Disclose Partner relationship to prospects

### No Conflicts
- Not refer prospects to competing automation providers during this agreement
- Disclose any conflicts of interest with referred prospects
- Not solicit Provider's clients for competing services

### Compliance
- Comply with all applicable laws and regulations
- Not engage in spam, deceptive marketing, or unethical practices
- Protect confidential information shared by Provider

---

## 7. Provider Responsibilities

Provider agrees to:

### Quality Service
- Deliver high-quality automation services to referred clients
- Treat referred clients with same care as direct clients
- Honor quoted prices and terms

### Communication
- Respond promptly to referred prospects
- Keep Partner updated on deal status
- Provide support to help Partner understand services

### Fair Attribution
- Honor Partner's referral credit per attribution rules
- Pay commissions on time
- Provide transparent reporting

### Brand Protection
- Maintain professional reputation
- Not engage in practices that damage Partner's reputation
- Deliver on promises made during sales process

---

## 8. Use of Names & Marketing

**Provider May:**
- List Partner as an official referral partner (with permission)
- Mention partnership in case studies or testimonials (with approval)
- Feature Partner logo on website partners page (with written consent)

**Partner May:**
- Describe the partnership to prospects during referrals
- Use approved Provider marketing materials
- State they are an official ClawOps referral partner

**Approval Required:**
- Any public marketing mentioning the other party requires prior written approval
- Both parties may request removal from partner lists at any time

---

## 9. Confidentiality

Both parties agree to protect confidential information including:
- Pricing structures and commission rates
- Client details and contract terms
- Business strategies and proprietary processes
- Financial information

**Duration:** Confidentiality obligations continue for 2 years after agreement termination.

**Exceptions:** Public information, independently developed information, or legally required disclosures.

---

## 10. Non-Circumvention

**Provider Agreement:**
Provider will not circumvent Partner by:
- Contacting referred prospects directly before introduction
- Encouraging prospects to work directly to avoid commission
- Re-engaging referred prospects through other channels to bypass attribution

**Partner Agreement:**
Partner will not circumvent Provider by:
- Introducing prospects and then offering competing services
- Requiring prospects to use specific subcontractors that benefit Partner
- Steering clients away from Provider after receiving commission

**Penalty:** Circumvention results in immediate termination and forfeiture of unpaid commissions.

---

## 11. Term & Termination

**Initial Term:** This agreement begins on [Date] and continues indefinitely until terminated.

**Termination Rights:**
Either party may terminate by providing **30 days written notice**.

**Effect of Termination:**
- Partner stops making new referrals immediately
- Existing referrals in pipeline remain eligible for commission per attribution rules
- Commissions for closed deals before termination are still paid
- Confidentiality and non-circumvention obligations survive

**Immediate Termination For:**
- Breach of confidentiality
- Unethical or illegal conduct
- Non-payment (Provider only)
- Competitive conflicts

---

## 12. Relationship of Parties

**Independent Contractors:** Partner is an independent contractor, not an employee, agent, or representative of Provider.

**No Authority:** Partner has no authority to:
- Bind Provider to contracts or commitments
- Make promises or guarantees on Provider's behalf
- Accept payment or negotiate terms

**Separate Businesses:** Each party operates its own independent business and is responsible for its own expenses, taxes, and liabilities.

---

## 13. Limitation of Liability

**Provider Not Liable For:**
- Partner's business expenses or opportunity costs
- Lost commissions due to prospect's decision not to buy
- Delays in client decision-making or procurement processes

**Commission Disputes:**
If Partner disputes commission calculation or attribution, parties will work in good faith to resolve. Provider's records are considered authoritative unless proven incorrect.

**Maximum Liability:**
Each party's total liability is limited to commissions paid or owed in the prior 6 months.
