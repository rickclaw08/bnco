## 2. Services Included

Provider will deliver the following on an ongoing basis:

### Core Services
- **Automation Monitoring:** Weekly check-ins on workflow performance and error tracking
- **Optimization:** Continuous improvement of existing automations for speed and reliability
- **Troubleshooting:** Resolution of automation failures or integration issues
- **Updates & Maintenance:** Adapting workflows to platform updates and API changes
- **Strategic Planning:** Monthly review of automation roadmap and new opportunities
- **Priority Support:** Email and Slack support with response times per SLA (Section 4)

### Additional Services (as hours allow)
- New automation builds (scoped within monthly hours)
- Team training sessions
- Documentation updates
- Integration of new tools

**Hour Tracking:** Provider will track and report hours monthly. Unused hours do not roll over.

## 3. Service Level Agreement (SLA)

Provider commits to the following response and resolution times:

| Priority Level | Response Time | Resolution Target |
|---------------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Critical** (system down, revenue impact) | 2 hours | 24 hours |
| **High** (major feature broken, workaround available) | 4 hours | 3 business days |
| **Normal** (minor bug, optimization request) | 1 business day | 5 business days |
| **Low** (enhancement, documentation) | 2 business days | Best effort |

**Business Hours:** Monday-Friday, 9 AM - 6 PM [Timezone]  
**After-Hours:** Critical issues only; response within 4 hours

**Priority Determination:** Client may designate priority; Provider may adjust if classification doesn't match impact.

## 4. Escalation Process

If SLA targets are not met or Client needs additional attention:

1. **Level 1:** Your assigned automation manager (primary contact)
