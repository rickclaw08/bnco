# Master Services Agreement (MSA)
## Enterprise Automation Services

**Between:** [Client Company Name] ("Client")  
**And:** ClawOps ("Provider")  
**Effective Date:** [Date]

## 1. Agreement Overview

This Master Services Agreement establishes the general terms and conditions governing all automation and AI services provided by Provider to Client. Specific projects will be detailed in separate Statements of Work (SOWs) that reference this MSA.

**Purpose:** Create a streamlined framework for ongoing collaboration on multiple automation projects without renegotiating terms for each engagement.

---

## 2. Services Framework

### Types of Services

Provider may deliver any combination of the following:

**Automation Development**
- Workflow automation using no-code/low-code platforms
- API integrations and data synchronization
- AI agent deployment and configuration
- Custom automation solutions

**Strategic Consulting**
- Automation opportunity audits
- Process optimization and redesign
- Technology stack evaluation
- ROI modeling and business case development

**Ongoing Management**
- Monitoring and maintenance of deployed automations
- Performance optimization
- Troubleshooting and support
- Regular strategic reviews

**Training & Enablement**
- Team training on automation tools and processes
- Documentation and SOPs
- Change management support

### Project Structure

Each project will be defined in a **Statement of Work (SOW)** that includes:
- Scope and deliverables
- Timeline and milestones
- Pricing and payment terms
- Success criteria
- Any project-specific terms

SOWs are incorporated into and governed by this MSA.

---

## 3. Statements of Work (SOWs)

### SOW Requirements

Each SOW must include:
1. Project description and objectives
2. Detailed scope of work
3. Deliverables and acceptance criteria
4. Timeline and key milestones
5. Fees and payment schedule
6. Project team and points of contact
7. Any project-specific terms or exceptions

### SOW Approval

SOWs become binding when signed by authorized representatives of both parties. Email approval is acceptable if it clearly references the SOW document.

### Changes to SOWs

Changes to an active SOW require a written change order signed by both parties, including:
- Description of change
- Impact on timeline
- Impact on cost
- Revised acceptance criteria (if applicable)

### Conflicts

If any SOW conflicts with this MSA, the MSA controls unless the SOW explicitly states otherwise and is signed by both parties' authorized representatives.

## 4. Pricing & Payment Terms

### Fee Structure

Projects may be priced using any of the following models:

**Fixed Price:** Set fee for defined scope and deliverables  
**Time & Materials:** Hourly or daily rates for flexible scope work  
**Retainer:** Monthly fee for ongoing services and defined allocation  
**Value-Based:** Fee tied to measured outcomes or ROI

Specific pricing for each project will be detailed in the applicable SOW.

### Standard Rates (for Time & Materials)

Unless otherwise specified in an SOW:
- Senior Automation Architect: $250/hour
- Automation Engineer: $175/hour
- Junior Automation Specialist: $125/hour
- Strategic Consultant: $300/hour

Rates may be adjusted annually with 60 days written notice.

### Payment Terms

**Standard Terms:** Net 30 from invoice date  
**Payment Schedule:** As specified in each SOW (typically milestone-based or monthly)  
**Methods:** ACH transfer, wire transfer, check, or credit card (3% processing fee applies)

**Retainers & Fixed Price Projects:**
- Initial payment typically required before work begins
- Remaining payments tied to milestones or monthly billing

**Late Payment:**
- 1.5% monthly interest on balances over 30 days past due
- Services may be paused for invoices over 45 days past due
- Collections costs and legal fees are Client's responsibility

### Expenses

Out-of-pocket expenses (travel, third-party tools, licensing) will be billed at cost plus 10% administrative fee, unless otherwise specified in SOW. Expenses over $500 require pre-approval.

## 5. Term & Termination

### Agreement Term

This MSA:
- Becomes effective on the date signed
- Continues indefinitely until terminated by either party
- May be terminated with 30 days written notice

### Effect on Active Projects

Terminating the MSA does not automatically terminate active SOWs. Options:
1. Complete active projects under existing SOW terms
2. Negotiate early termination of active projects
3. Transition work to Client or another provider

### Termination for Cause

Either party may terminate immediately for:
- Material breach not cured within 30 days of written notice
- Bankruptcy, insolvency, or cessation of business operations
- Fraud, gross negligence, or illegal conduct

### Survival

The following sections survive termination:
- Intellectual Property (Section 7)
- Confidentiality (Section 8)
- Limitation of Liability (Section 10)
- General Terms (Section 13)

## 7. Intellectual Property

### Ownership of Deliverables

**Work Product:** Custom automations, workflows, integrations, and documentation created specifically for Client under an SOW become Client's property upon full payment for that project.

**Transfer of Rights:** Provider assigns all rights, title, and interest in work product to Client, including the right to modify, distribute, and create derivative works.

### Provider Retained Rights

Provider retains ownership of:

**Pre-Existing Materials:** Tools, frameworks, templates, methodologies, and code libraries that existed before the project or were developed independently

**Generic Work:** Reusable components, design patterns, or techniques that are not specific to Client's business

**Know-How:** General skills, knowledge, and experience gained during projects

**License Grant:** Client receives a perpetual, non-exclusive license to use any pre-existing materials embedded in deliverables.

### Third-Party Components

Work may incorporate third-party software, APIs, or platforms:
- Client is responsible for obtaining and maintaining all necessary licenses
- Provider will identify any third-party dependencies in deliverables
- Client assumes compliance responsibility for third-party components

### Restrictions

Client may not:
- Resell or redistribute Provider's proprietary tools or frameworks as standalone products
- Remove proprietary notices or attributions from Provider's pre-existing materials
- Reverse engineer Provider's proprietary methodologies

## 8. Confidentiality

### Confidential Information

Both parties agree to protect confidential information including:
- Business strategies, financial data, and proprietary processes
- Technical designs, source code, and system architectures
- Customer data, employee information, and business relationships
- Terms of this agreement and associated SOWs

### Obligations

Receiving party will:
- Use confidential information only for purposes of this agreement
- Limit disclosure to employees and contractors with legitimate need to know
- Protect information with same care used for own confidential data (minimum: reasonable care)
- Return or destroy confidential information upon request after termination

### Exceptions

Obligations do not apply to information that:
- Is or becomes publicly available through no breach
- Was rightfully known before disclosure
- Is independently developed without use of confidential information
- Must be disclosed by law or court order (with prompt notice to disclosing party)

### Duration

Confidentiality obligations continue for **3 years** after termination of this agreement.

### Data Handling

Provider will:

- Comply with applicable data protection laws
- Not use Client data for any purpose other than providing services

- Regular security updates and patches
- Restricted access to production systems

### Data Breaches

Provider will notify Client within 48 hours of discovering any:

- Data breach affecting Client information
- Security incident that may impact Client

### Client Responsibility

Client remains the data controller and is responsible for:
- Ensuring lawful collection and processing of data
- Obtaining necessary consents and authorizations
- Compliance with GDPR, CCPA, and other applicable regulations
- Notifying affected individuals in case of breach (Provider will assist as needed)

### Data Retention

Unless otherwise specified in an SOW:
- Provider will retain project-related data for 90 days after project completion
- Client may request early deletion of data
- Provider will securely delete data upon request (within 30 days)

For projects under 12 months, liability is limited to total fees paid for the specific project giving rise to the claim.

### Excluded Damages

**PROVIDER IS NOT LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, SPECIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING:**
- Lost profits or revenue
- Lost data or business interruption
- Cost of substitute services
- Loss of business opportunity
- Damage to reputation

This limitation applies even if Provider was advised of the possibility of such damages.

### Exceptions

Liability limitations do not apply to:
- Gross negligence or willful misconduct
- Breach of confidentiality obligations
- Infringement of third-party intellectual property rights
- Violations of applicable law that cannot be contractually limited

### Client Mitigation

Client agrees to:
- Maintain adequate insurance for business risks
- Implement backup and disaster recovery procedures
- Validate critical automations before full production deployment

### Provider Indemnification

Provider will indemnify and defend Client against third-party claims that Provider's work product directly infringes a patent, copyright, or trade secret, provided Client:
- Promptly notifies Provider of the claim
- Gives Provider sole control of defense and settlement
- Reasonably cooperates with Provider's defense

**Remedy Options:** Provider may, at its option:

- Modify work product to be non-infringing

- Refund fees paid for the infringing component

**Exclusions:** Provider has no obligation for infringement claims arising from:
- Client's modification of deliverables
- Combination of deliverables with non-Provider materials

- Compliance with Client's specifications or instructions

Client will indemnify and defend Provider against third-party claims arising from:
- Client's data or content used in projects
- Client's use of deliverables outside scope of this agreement

### Independent Contractor

Provider is an independent contractor, not an employee, agent, partner, or joint venturer of Client. Provider:
- Controls the manner and means of performing services
- Is responsible for own taxes, insurance, and benefits
- May use subcontractors (with Client's consent for key personnel)

- Solicit the other's employees who worked on projects under this agreement
- Hire such employees without prior written consent

**Exception:** General job postings and unsolicited applications do not violate this provision.

**Remedy:** Violating party will pay the other party an amount equal to 6 months of the hired employee's salary.

### Force Majeure

- Natural disasters, pandemics, or acts of God
- War, terrorism, or civil unrest
- Government actions or regulations

Affected party must notify the other promptly and make reasonable efforts to minimize impact.
