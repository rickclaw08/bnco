## 1. Overview

This Master Service Agreement ("Agreement") establishes the terms under which ClawOps LLC, a Delaware limited liability company ("Provider"), will deliver services to [CLIENT_NAME] ("Client").

Individual engagements will be defined in Statements of Work ("SOWs") executed under this Agreement. Each SOW is incorporated into and governed by this Agreement. If a SOW conflicts with this Agreement, the SOW controls for that engagement unless it expressly states otherwise.

---

## 2. Services

2.1. Provider will perform services as described in one or more SOWs referencing this Agreement.

2.2. Each SOW will specify the scope of work, deliverables, timeline, pricing, acceptance criteria, and any service-specific terms.

2.3. Provider will perform all services in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted industry standards.

2.4. Provider may use qualified subcontractors to perform portions of the services, provided that Provider remains responsible for all obligations under this Agreement.

---

## 3. Payment Terms

3.1. **Setup Fee.** Client will pay a one-time setup fee of $[SETUP_FEE] as specified in the applicable SOW. Setup fees are due upon execution of the SOW and are non-refundable once Provider begins work.

3.2. **Monthly Fee.** Client will pay a recurring monthly fee of $[MONTHLY_FEE] (or as specified in the applicable SOW), invoiced on the first business day of each month.

3.3. **Net 15.** All invoices are due within fifteen (15) calendar days of the invoice date ("Due Date").

3.4. **Late Payments.** Invoices unpaid after the Due Date will accrue interest at 1.5% per month (or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, whichever is lower). Provider may suspend services if any invoice remains unpaid for more than thirty (30) days past the Due Date.

3.5. **Taxes.** Fees do not include taxes. Client is responsible for all applicable sales, use, and similar taxes, excluding taxes based on Provider's income.

3.6. **Payment Method.** Payments will be made via ACH, wire transfer, credit card, or other method agreed upon by the parties.

3.7. **Fee Adjustments.** Provider may adjust recurring fees upon thirty (30) days' written notice, effective at the start of the next renewal term. Client may terminate the affected SOW if it does not accept the adjusted fees, by providing written notice before the adjustment takes effect.

## 4. Intellectual Property

4.1. **Provider IP.** Provider retains all rights in its pre-existing and independently developed tools, software, frameworks, AI models, methodologies, and know-how ("Provider IP"). Nothing in this Agreement transfers ownership of Provider IP to Client.

4.2. **Client Data.** Client retains all rights in its data, content, brand assets, and materials provided to Provider ("Client Data"). Provider will use Client Data solely to perform services under this Agreement.

4.3. **Work Product.** Custom configurations, integrations, workflows, and documentation created specifically for Client under a SOW ("Work Product") become Client's property upon full payment of all fees for that SOW.

4.4. **License to Provider IP.** Where delivered services incorporate Provider IP, Provider grants Client a non-exclusive, non-transferable, royalty-free license to use such Provider IP solely in connection with the delivered services, for the duration of this Agreement (and any applicable SOW).

4.5. **Feedback.** If Client provides suggestions or feedback regarding Provider's services, Provider may freely use such feedback to improve its services without obligation to Client.

## 5. Confidentiality

5.1. **Definition.** "Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by one party ("Disclosing Party") to the other ("Receiving Party") in connection with this Agreement, whether in writing, orally, or by inspection. This includes business plans, technical data, customer information, pricing, and trade secrets.

5.2. **Obligations.** The Receiving Party will: (a) use Confidential Information solely to fulfill its obligations under this Agreement, (b) protect it using at least the same degree of care it applies to its own confidential information (and no less than reasonable care), and (c) limit disclosure to employees, contractors, and agents who need access and are bound by comparable confidentiality obligations.

5.3. **Exclusions.** Confidential Information does not include information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party, (b) was already known to the Receiving Party before disclosure without restriction, (c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of Confidential Information, or (d) is received from a third party without confidentiality restrictions.

5.4. **Required Disclosures.** If the Receiving Party is legally compelled to disclose Confidential Information, it will promptly notify the Disclosing Party (where permitted), cooperate with efforts to limit disclosure, and disclose only the minimum information required.

5.5. **Duration.** Confidentiality obligations survive termination of this Agreement for two (2) years. Trade secrets remain protected for as long as they qualify as trade secrets under applicable law.

6.2. **By Client.** Client represents that: (a) it has the authority to enter into this Agreement, (b) it has all necessary rights in Client Data provided to Provider, and (c) its use of the services will comply with applicable law.

## 7. Limitation of Liability

7.1. **Liability Cap.** PROVIDER'S TOTAL AGGREGATE LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT WILL NOT EXCEED THE TOTAL FEES ACTUALLY PAID BY CLIENT TO PROVIDER DURING THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING THE CLAIM.

7.2. **Exclusion of Consequential Damages.** NEITHER PARTY WILL BE LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOSS OF PROFITS, REVENUE, DATA, OR BUSINESS OPPORTUNITY, REGARDLESS OF THE THEORY OF LIABILITY, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

7.3. **Exceptions.** The limitations in Sections 7.1 and 7.2 do not apply to: (a) breaches of confidentiality obligations (Section 5), (b) indemnification obligations (Section 8), (c) willful misconduct or gross negligence, or (d) Client's obligation to pay fees.

## 8. Indemnification

8.1. **By Provider.** Provider will indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Client from third-party claims arising from Provider's: (a) gross negligence or willful misconduct in performing services, or (b) infringement of a third party's intellectual property rights through the services (excluding claims arising from Client Data or Client's instructions).

8.2. **By Client.** Client will indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Provider from third-party claims arising from: (a) Client Data, (b) Client's use of the services in violation of this Agreement or applicable law, or (c) Client's gross negligence or willful misconduct.

8.3. **Process.** The indemnified party will promptly notify the indemnifying party of any claim, cooperate in the defense, and allow the indemnifying party to control the defense and settlement (provided no settlement admits liability on behalf of the indemnified party without consent).

## 9. Term and Termination

9.1. **Term.** This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues for twelve (12) months ("Initial Term"). After the Initial Term, this Agreement automatically renews for successive twelve (12) month periods ("Renewal Terms") unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least thirty (30) days before the end of the current term.

9.2. **Termination for Convenience.** Either party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW by providing thirty (30) days' written notice to the other party.

9.3. **Termination for Cause.** Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other party: (a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within fifteen (15) days after receiving written notice of the breach, or (b) becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or ceases normal business operations.

9.4. **Effect of Termination.** Upon termination:
- (a) Client will pay all fees for services rendered through the effective date of termination.
- (b) Provider will deliver all completed Work Product and return all Client Data in its possession within fifteen (15) business days.
- (c) Each party will return or destroy the other party's Confidential Information and certify destruction in writing upon request.
- (d) Sections 4, 5, 7, 8, 10, and 11 survive termination.

9.5. **Wind-Down.** Upon termination, Provider will cooperate in reasonable transition activities for up to thirty (30) days at Client's expense, unless termination results from Provider's material breach.

## 10. Dispute Resolution

10.1. **Good Faith Negotiation.** The parties will first attempt to resolve any dispute through good faith negotiation between designated representatives for thirty (30) days after written notice of the dispute.

10.2. **Mediation.** If negotiation fails, the parties will submit the dispute to mediation with a mutually agreed mediator, sharing costs equally, before pursuing other remedies.

10.3. **Governing Law.** This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict-of-law principles.

10.4. **Jurisdiction.** Any legal action arising from this Agreement will be brought exclusively in the state or federal courts located in the State of Delaware, and both parties consent to the personal jurisdiction of such courts.

10.5. **Attorneys' Fees.** In any legal proceeding to enforce this Agreement, the prevailing party is entitled to recover reasonable attorneys' fees and costs.

## 11. General Provisions

11.1. **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, together with all SOWs and written amendments signed by both parties, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior proposals, negotiations, and understandings.

11.2. **Amendments.** This Agreement may only be modified by a written amendment signed by authorized representatives of both parties.

11.3. **Assignment.** Neither party may assign this Agreement without the other party's prior written consent, except that either party may assign to a successor in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all its assets, provided the successor agrees to be bound by this Agreement.

11.4. **Notices.** All notices must be in writing and will be effective when delivered by email (with read receipt or reply confirmation) or recognized overnight courier to the addresses listed above, or to updated addresses provided in writing.

11.5. **Force Majeure.** Neither party is liable for delays or failures in performance caused by events beyond its reasonable control, including natural disasters, pandemics, government actions, cyberattacks, or internet service disruptions. The affected party will promptly notify the other and use reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact.

11.6. **Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is found unenforceable, it will be modified to the minimum extent necessary to be enforceable, and the remaining provisions continue in full force.

11.7. **Waiver.** A party's failure to enforce any provision does not waive that provision or any other provision. Waivers must be in writing to be effective.

11.8. **Independent Contractors.** The parties are independent contractors. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship.

11.9. **Counterparts.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, including by electronic signature, each of which is an original and all of which together constitute one agreement.
