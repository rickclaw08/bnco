## 1. How Our Service Works

ClawOps provides an AI-powered telephone receptionist service ("Service") that answers inbound calls on behalf of our business clients. When you call a phone number operated by the Service:

- An AI system answers and processes your call in **real time**
- Your spoken words are converted to text (speech-to-text) so the AI can understand your request
- The AI generates a spoken response using text-to-speech technology
- This processing happens **live during the call** and is transient in nature

---

## 2. Biometric Data - What We Do NOT Collect

**ClawOps does NOT collect, capture, store, or use biometric identifiers or biometric information.** Specifically:

- **No voiceprints.** We do not create, generate, or store voiceprints or voice templates that could be used to identify you.
- **No voice-based identification.** We do not use your voice to uniquely identify you or distinguish you from other callers.
- **No biometric matching.** We do not compare your voice against any database or stored template.
- **No facial geometry, fingerprints, retina/iris scans, or other biometric identifiers** are collected by our Service.

The speech-to-text conversion used by our Service is a **general-purpose transcription technology** that converts audio to text without analyzing or storing any characteristics unique to your voice.

---

## 3. What Happens to Your Voice Data

### Default Mode (No Recording)

By default, calls processed by our Service are **not recorded**. Your voice audio is:

1. Received in real time by the AI system
2. Converted to text for the purpose of understanding your request
3. **Discarded immediately** after processing - no audio is retained

No voice data is stored, archived, or preserved after the call ends.

### If Call Recording Is Enabled by the Client

Some of our business clients may choose to enable an optional call recording feature. If recording is enabled:

- **You will be notified** at the beginning of the call that the call may be recorded
- **Retention period:** Call recordings are stored for a maximum of **ninety (90) days** from the date of the call
- **Storage:** Recordings are stored in encrypted storage with access restricted to the client and authorized ClawOps personnel
- **Destruction schedule:** After the 90-day retention period, recordings are **automatically and permanently deleted** using industry-standard secure deletion methods (overwrite and purge)
- **Early deletion:** The business client or ClawOps may delete recordings before the retention period expires
- **No biometric extraction:** Even when calls are recorded, we do **not** extract, derive, or store any biometric identifiers or biometric information from the recordings

---

## 4. Purpose of Voice Data Processing

Voice data is processed **solely** for the following purposes:

- Answering and responding to your telephone call
- Understanding your spoken request or inquiry
- Routing your call to the appropriate person or department
- Scheduling appointments on your behalf
- Providing information you have requested

Voice data is **never** used for:

- Identifying you by your voice
- Building a voice profile or voiceprint
- Marketing, advertising, or profiling purposes
- Sale, lease, or trade to third parties

---

## 5. No Sale or Profit from Voice Data

ClawOps does not sell, lease, trade, or otherwise profit from voice data, call recordings, or any information derived from voice processing. Voice data is used exclusively to deliver the Service.

## 6. Third-Party Processors

Voice data may be processed by the following categories of third-party service providers solely to deliver the Service:

- **Speech-to-text providers** (for real-time transcription)
- **Text-to-speech providers** (for AI-generated voice responses)
- **Cloud infrastructure providers** (for real-time compute processing)

All third-party processors are bound by contractual obligations to process data only as instructed, to maintain appropriate security measures, and to delete data in accordance with our retention policies.

## 7. Security Measures

ClawOps implements commercially reasonable security measures to protect any voice data during processing, including:

- Encryption in transit (TLS 1.2+)
- Encryption at rest (AES-256) for any stored recordings
- Role-based access controls
- Regular security assessments

2. If call recording is enabled, provide appropriate notice to callers that calls may be recorded

You may end the call at any time if you do not consent to AI processing.
