## 1. Purpose

The Parties wish to explore a potential business relationship related to [BRIEF DESCRIPTION, e.g., "AI automation and receptionist services"] (the "Purpose"). In connection with the Purpose, each Party may share confidential information with the other. This Agreement sets forth the terms under which such information will be protected.

---

## 2. Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by one Party (the "Disclosing Party") to the other (the "Receiving Party") in connection with the Purpose, regardless of the form of disclosure (written, oral, visual, electronic, or otherwise). This includes, but is not limited to:

- Business plans, strategies, and financial data
- Technical information, algorithms, software, and system architecture
- Customer and prospect lists, pricing, and sales data
- Product roadmaps, unreleased features, and trade secrets
- Proprietary processes, methodologies, and know-how
- Any materials marked "Confidential," "Proprietary," or with a similar designation
- Information disclosed orally that is identified as confidential at the time of disclosure and confirmed in writing within ten (10) days

---

## 3. Exclusions

Confidential Information does not include information that:

(a) Was publicly available at the time of disclosure, or becomes publicly available afterward, through no breach by the Receiving Party;

(b) Was already in the Receiving Party's possession before disclosure, without restriction, as shown by the Receiving Party's records;

(c) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information;

(d) Is lawfully received from a third party who is not under a confidentiality obligation to the Disclosing Party; or

(e) Is approved for release by the Disclosing Party in writing.

---

## 4. Obligations

The Receiving Party agrees to:

4.1. **Protect.** Use at least the same degree of care to protect the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information as it uses for its own (and no less than reasonable care).

4.2. **Limit Use.** Use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose described in Section 1, and for no other purpose.

4.3. **Limit Disclosure.** Share Confidential Information only with its employees, contractors, and advisors who (a) have a genuine need to know for the Purpose, and (b) are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as this Agreement.

4.4. **No Reverse Engineering.** Not reverse engineer, decompile, or disassemble any prototypes, software, or other tangible items provided under this Agreement, unless expressly permitted in writing.

---

## 5. Required Disclosures

If the Receiving Party is legally compelled (by court order, subpoena, or regulatory authority) to disclose Confidential Information, the Receiving Party will:

- Promptly notify the Disclosing Party in writing (where legally permitted) to allow the Disclosing Party to seek protective relief;
- Cooperate with the Disclosing Party's reasonable efforts to limit the scope of disclosure;
- Disclose only the minimum information legally required; and
- Use reasonable efforts to obtain confidential treatment for any disclosed information.

---

## 6. Return or Destruction

Upon written request by the Disclosing Party, or upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, the Receiving Party will promptly:

- Return or destroy all Confidential Information in its possession, including copies, notes, summaries, and derivative materials; and
- Certify in writing that it has done so.

**Exception:** The Receiving Party may retain one archival copy solely for legal compliance purposes, subject to the ongoing confidentiality obligations of this Agreement.

---

## 7. No Rights Granted

This Agreement does not:
- Grant any license or ownership interest in Confidential Information;
- Require either Party to disclose any particular information;
- Obligate either Party to enter into any further agreement or transaction; or
- Create any partnership, joint venture, agency, or employment relationship.

All Confidential Information remains the sole property of the Disclosing Party.

---

## 8. No Warranty

ALL CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION IS PROVIDED "AS IS" WITHOUT WARRANTY OF ANY KIND, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED. THE DISCLOSING PARTY MAKES NO REPRESENTATIONS REGARDING THE ACCURACY, COMPLETENESS, OR FITNESS FOR ANY PURPOSE OF ANY CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION.

---

## 9. Term

9.1. **Agreement Term.** This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and remains in effect for two (2) years, unless terminated earlier by either Party with thirty (30) days' written notice.

9.2. **Survival.** The confidentiality obligations in this Agreement survive termination or expiration for a period of two (2) years from the date of disclosure of the applicable Confidential Information. Trade secrets remain protected for as long as they qualify as trade secrets under applicable law.

---

## 10. Remedies

Both Parties acknowledge that a breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages alone would be inadequate. In addition to all other remedies available at law or in equity, the non-breaching Party is entitled to seek injunctive or other equitable relief without the requirement of posting a bond.

---

## 11. General Provisions

11.1. **Governing Law.** This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict-of-law principles.

11.2. **Jurisdiction.** Any legal action arising from this Agreement will be brought exclusively in the state or federal courts located in the State of Delaware, and both Parties consent to the personal jurisdiction of such courts.

11.3. **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the Parties regarding confidentiality and supersedes all prior discussions or agreements on this subject.

11.4. **Amendments.** This Agreement may only be amended in writing, signed by both Parties.

11.5. **Severability.** If any provision is found unenforceable, it will be modified to the minimum extent necessary, and the remaining provisions continue in full force.

11.6. **Waiver.** A Party's failure to enforce any provision does not waive that or any other provision. Waivers must be in writing.

11.7. **Assignment.** Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the other Party's written consent, except to a successor entity in a merger or acquisition, provided the successor agrees to be bound by these terms.

11.8. **Notices.** All notices must be in writing and sent to the addresses listed above (email is acceptable with read receipt or reply confirmation).

11.9. **Counterparts.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, including by electronic signature, each of which is an original.
