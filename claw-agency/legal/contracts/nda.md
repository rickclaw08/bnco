## 2. Definition of Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any information, in any form, disclosed by one Party (the "Disclosing Party") to the other Party (the "Receiving Party") that:

- Is marked or identified as "Confidential," "Proprietary," or similar designation
- Would reasonably be understood to be confidential given its nature and the circumstances of disclosure
- Is disclosed orally and confirmed as confidential in writing within 15 days

**Examples include:**
- Business plans, strategies, and financial information
- Technical data, algorithms, code, and system architectures
- Customer lists, user data, and business relationships
- Product roadmaps and unpublished features
- Pricing, costs, and negotiations
- Trade secrets and proprietary methodologies

---

## 3. Exclusions

Confidential Information does NOT include information that:

1. **Was publicly known** at the time of disclosure or becomes publicly known through no breach by Receiving Party
2. **Was rightfully known** by Receiving Party before disclosure, without confidentiality restrictions
3. **Is independently developed** by Receiving Party without use of or reference to Confidential Information
4. **Is rightfully received** from a third party without confidentiality obligations
5. **Is approved for release** by written authorization from Disclosing Party

---

## 4. Obligations of Receiving Party

The Receiving Party agrees to:

1. **Maintain Confidentiality:** Keep all Confidential Information strictly confidential using at least the same degree of care it uses for its own confidential information (but no less than reasonable care)

2. **Limit Disclosure:** Disclose Confidential Information only to employees, contractors, or advisors who:
   - Have a legitimate need to know for the Purpose
   - Are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as this Agreement
   - Have been informed of the confidential nature of the information

3. **Limit Use:** Use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose and not for any other purpose without prior written consent

4. **Protect Information:** Take reasonable precautions to prevent unauthorized disclosure or use

5. **No Reverse Engineering:** Not reverse engineer, disassemble, or decompile any prototypes, software, or samples unless expressly permitted in writing

---

## 5. Required Disclosures

If Receiving Party is legally compelled (by court order, subpoena, or government authority) to disclose Confidential Information, Receiving Party will:

- Promptly notify Disclosing Party in writing (if legally permitted)
- Cooperate with Disclosing Party's efforts to seek protective orders or other remedies
- Disclose only the minimum information required
- Use reasonable efforts to obtain confidential treatment for disclosed information

---

## 6. Return or Destruction of Information

Upon written request by Disclosing Party, or upon termination of this Agreement, Receiving Party will:

- Promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information (including copies, notes, and derivatives)
- Certify in writing that all such materials have been returned or destroyed

**Exception:** Receiving Party may retain one archival copy for legal compliance purposes, subject to continued confidentiality obligations.

---

This Agreement does not:

- Grant any license, rights, or ownership interest in Confidential Information
- Require either Party to disclose any information
- Obligate either Party to proceed with any transaction or relationship
- Create any agency, partnership, or joint venture relationship

All Confidential Information remains the sole property of the Disclosing Party.
