## 1. Purpose

This Agreement establishes the general terms and conditions under which the Company will provide AI automation services to the Client. Specific projects and engagements will be defined in one or more Statements of Work ("SOW") executed under this Agreement.

## 2. Statements of Work

### 2.1 SOW Execution
Each SOW shall reference this Agreement, describe the scope of services, deliverables, timeline, fees, and any project-specific terms. Each SOW, once executed by both Parties, is incorporated into and governed by this Agreement.

### 2.2 Conflicts
In the event of a conflict between this Agreement and a SOW, the SOW shall control with respect to that specific engagement, unless the SOW expressly states otherwise.

### 2.3 Change Orders
Any changes to an executed SOW must be documented in a written Change Order signed by both Parties, specifying the impact on scope, timeline, and fees.

## 3. Scope of Services

### 3.1 Services
The Company will perform the services described in each SOW with reasonable skill and care, consistent with generally accepted industry standards.

### 3.2 Client Responsibilities
The Client shall:
- Provide timely access to necessary information, systems, personnel, and resources
- Designate an authorized point of contact for decisions and approvals
- Review and provide feedback on deliverables within the timeframes specified in the SOW
- Ensure compliance with applicable laws regarding data and materials provided to the Company

### 3.3 Delays
If the Client's failure to fulfill its responsibilities causes a delay, the Company may adjust timelines and, if applicable, fees, upon written notice.

## 4. Fees and Payment

### 4.1 Project-Based Fees
- **50% of the total project fee** is due upon execution of the SOW ("Deposit")
- **50% of the total project fee** is due upon delivery of final deliverables ("Final Payment")
- Alternative milestone-based payment schedules may be specified in the SOW

### 4.2 Retainer Fees
- Monthly retainer fees are invoiced on the **1st of each month** and are due within **15 calendar days**
- Retainer hours are for the designated month and **do not roll over** unless agreed in writing
- Overage hours are billed at the rate specified in the SOW

### 4.3 Expenses
Pre-approved, reasonable out-of-pocket expenses incurred in performing the services will be reimbursed by the Client upon submission of receipts. Expenses exceeding **$250** require prior written approval.

### 4.4 Late Payment
- Invoices unpaid after the due date accrue interest at **1.5% per month** (or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is lower)
- The Company may suspend work after **15 days** of non-payment with written notice
- The Client is responsible for all costs of collection, including reasonable attorney's fees

### 4.5 Taxes
All fees are exclusive of taxes. The Client is responsible for all applicable sales, use, VAT, or similar taxes, excluding taxes based on the Company's income.

## 5. Intellectual Property

### 5.1 Client Deliverables
Upon **full payment** of all fees under the applicable SOW, the Company assigns to the Client all right, title, and interest in the custom deliverables created specifically for the Client under that SOW ("Client Deliverables").

### 5.2 Company Materials
The Company retains all rights to:
- Pre-existing tools, frameworks, libraries, methodologies, and code ("Company Materials")
- General knowledge, skills, and experience gained during the engagement
- Any improvements to Company Materials made during the engagement

Where Company Materials are incorporated into Client Deliverables, the Company grants the Client a **non-exclusive, perpetual, royalty-free, worldwide license** to use such Company Materials solely as part of the delivered solution.

### 5.3 Client Materials
The Client retains all rights to materials, data, and content provided to the Company ("Client Materials"). The Client grants the Company a limited license to use Client Materials solely to perform the services.

### 5.4 Feedback
Any suggestions, ideas, or feedback provided by the Client regarding Company Materials may be freely used by the Company without obligation.

## 6. Confidentiality

### 6.1 Definition
"Confidential Information" means all non-public information disclosed by one Party ("Disclosing Party") to the other ("Receiving Party"), whether oral, written, or electronic, including business plans, technical data, trade secrets, client lists, financial information, and the terms of this Agreement.

### 6.2 Obligations
The Receiving Party shall:
- Use Confidential Information solely to perform obligations under this Agreement
- Protect it with at least the same degree of care used for its own confidential information (and no less than reasonable care)
- Limit disclosure to employees, contractors, and advisors with a need to know, who are bound by comparable obligations
- Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent

### 6.3 Exclusions
Confidential Information does not include information that:
- Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party
- Was already known to the Receiving Party without restriction
- Is independently developed without use of Confidential Information
- Is received from a third party without restriction

### 6.4 Required Disclosures
The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information as required by law, regulation, or court order, provided it gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice (where legally permitted) and reasonable assistance to seek protective measures.

### 6.5 Duration
Confidentiality obligations survive termination of this Agreement for a period of **three (3) years**, except for trade secrets, which are protected for as long as they remain trade secrets.

## 7. Representations and Warranties

### 7.1 Mutual
Each Party represents and warrants that it has the authority to enter into this Agreement and perform its obligations.

### 7.2 Company Warranties
The Company warrants that:
- Services will be performed in a professional and workmanlike manner
- Deliverables will substantially conform to the specifications in the applicable SOW for **30 days** after delivery ("Warranty Period")
- It will not knowingly infringe any third-party intellectual property rights

### 7.3 Warranty Remedy
During the Warranty Period, the Company will, at no additional cost, correct any deliverable that fails to substantially conform to the SOW specifications. This is the Client's **sole and exclusive remedy** for warranty claims.

### 7.4 Disclaimer
EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY SET FORTH IN THIS AGREEMENT, THE COMPANY DISCLAIMS ALL WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY, FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE, AND NON-INFRINGEMENT.

## 8. Limitation of Liability

### 8.1 Cap
THE COMPANY'S TOTAL AGGREGATE LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE **TOTAL FEES PAID BY THE CLIENT UNDER THE APPLICABLE SOW** GIVING RISE TO THE CLAIM.

### 8.2 Exclusion of Consequential Damages
IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOSS OF PROFITS, REVENUE, DATA, OR BUSINESS OPPORTUNITIES, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

### 8.3 Exceptions
The limitations in this Section 8 do not apply to:
- Breaches of Section 6 (Confidentiality)
- Obligations under Section 9 (Indemnification)
- Willful misconduct or gross negligence
- Infringement of intellectual property rights

## 9. Indemnification

### 9.1 By the Company
The Company shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Client from third-party claims alleging that the deliverables (as delivered) infringe any US intellectual property right, provided the Client gives prompt written notice, cooperates in the defense, and grants the Company sole control of the defense and settlement.

### 9.2 By the Client
The Client shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Company from third-party claims arising from:
- Client Materials provided to the Company
- The Client's use of deliverables in a manner not contemplated by the SOW
- The Client's violation of applicable laws

### 9.3 Mitigation
If a deliverable becomes subject to an infringement claim, the Company may, at its option: (a) obtain a license for continued use, (b) modify the deliverable to be non-infringing, or (c) refund the fees paid for the infringing deliverable.

## 10. Term and Termination

### 10.1 Term
This Agreement is effective from the Effective Date and continues until terminated by either Party.

### 10.2 Termination for Convenience
Either Party may terminate this Agreement (or any SOW) by providing **30 days' written notice** to the other Party.

### 10.3 Termination for Cause
Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if:
- The other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within **15 days** of written notice
- The other Party becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or ceases operations

### 10.4 Effect of Termination
Upon termination:
- The Client shall pay for all services performed and expenses incurred through the termination date
- If the Client terminates for convenience, the Deposit is **non-refundable** for work already commenced
- If the Company terminates for cause (Client breach), **all outstanding fees become immediately due**
- Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party's Confidential Information within 30 days
- The Company shall deliver all completed and in-progress deliverables for which Client has paid

### 10.5 Survival
Sections 5, 6, 7.4, 8, 9, and 12 survive termination.

## 11. General Provisions

### 11.1 Independent Contractor
The Company is an independent contractor. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship.

### 11.2 Assignment
Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the other's prior written consent, except to a successor in a merger or acquisition.

### 11.3 Force Majeure
Neither Party is liable for delays caused by events beyond its reasonable control, including natural disasters, pandemics, government actions, or internet/infrastructure failures.

### 11.4 Notices
All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and sent to the addresses above (or updated addresses provided in writing) via email with confirmation or certified mail.

### 11.5 Non-Solicitation
During the term and for **12 months** after termination, neither Party shall directly solicit the other's employees or contractors who were involved in the engagement, without prior written consent.

### 11.6 Severability
If any provision is found invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions remain in full force.

### 11.7 Waiver
Failure to enforce any provision is not a waiver of future enforcement.

### 11.8 Entire Agreement
This Agreement and all executed SOWs and Change Orders constitute the entire agreement between the Parties and supersede all prior agreements, proposals, and communications.

### 11.9 Amendments
This Agreement may only be modified by a written instrument signed by both Parties.

## 12. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

### 12.1 Governing Law
This Agreement is governed by the laws of the **State of [State]**, without regard to conflict of law principles.

### 12.2 Dispute Resolution
The Parties agree to first attempt resolution through good-faith negotiation for **30 days**. If unresolved, disputes shall be submitted to **binding arbitration** under the AAA Commercial Arbitration Rules in **[City, State]**. The arbitrator's award is final and enforceable in any court of competent jurisdiction.
