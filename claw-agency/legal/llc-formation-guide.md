# LLC Formation Guide for ClawOps

A step-by-step guide to forming a Limited Liability Company (LLC) for ClawOps.

---

## 1. Why an LLC?

- **Liability protection:** Personal assets are shielded from business debts and lawsuits
- **Tax flexibility:** Default pass-through taxation (no double taxation); can elect S-Corp status later for tax optimization
- **Credibility:** Clients and partners take an LLC more seriously than a sole proprietorship
- **Simplicity:** Less paperwork than a corporation; fewer compliance requirements
- **Flexibility:** No restrictions on number of members; operating agreement is customizable

## 2. Delaware vs. Your Home State

### Option A: Delaware

**Pros:**
- Business-friendly legal system with the Court of Chancery (specialized business court)
- Well-established body of corporate law and legal precedent
- Preferred by investors and venture capital
- No state income tax on revenue earned outside Delaware
- Strong privacy protections (members not listed in public filings)
- Fast filing (same-day available)

**Cons:**
- $300/year franchise tax (minimum)
- If you operate from another state, you must also **foreign-qualify** there (extra $100 to $500+ depending on state, plus an additional registered agent fee)
- Two sets of compliance: Delaware annual report + home state annual report
- Registered agent required in Delaware ($50 to $150/year)

**Best for:** Agencies planning to raise investment, bring on partners, or scale significantly.

**Costs:**
- Filing fee: $90
- Registered agent: $50 to $150/year
- Annual franchise tax: $300/year
- Foreign qualification in home state: $100 to $500 (one-time) + annual fees

### Option B: Your Home State (State of Residence)

**Pros:**
- No foreign qualification needed (you only register once)
- One set of compliance requirements
- Often cheaper overall for a single-member LLC
- Simpler setup and maintenance

**Cons:**
- State-specific rules vary widely
- Some states have higher filing fees or annual taxes (e.g., California $800/year franchise tax)
- May have less favorable business law precedent than Delaware

**Best for:** Solo founders operating locally or online, who want simplicity and lower costs.

### Recommendation for ClawOps

**Start with your home state** unless you are actively pursuing investors. You can always re-domesticate to Delaware later if needed. For a lean AI agency, the savings and simplicity of a home-state LLC outweigh Delaware's advantages at the early stage.

If your home state has high fees (like California's $800 annual franchise tax), consider **Wyoming** as a low-cost alternative ($100 filing, $60/year annual report, no state income tax, strong privacy).

## 3. Step-by-Step Formation

### Step 1: Choose and Verify Your LLC Name

- Name must include "LLC" or "Limited Liability Company"
- Must be unique in your state of formation
- Search your state's Secretary of State business name database
- Verify domain availability (theclawops.com)
- Consider reserving the name ($25 to $50 in most states) if not filing immediately

### Step 2: Appoint a Registered Agent

A registered agent receives legal and government documents on your behalf. Required in every state where you are registered.

**Recommended providers:**
- **Northwest Registered Agent** - $125/year. Reliable, privacy-focused, includes free mail forwarding.
- **ZenBusiness** - $199/year. Bundled with formation packages.
- **Yourself** - Free, but your address becomes public record and you must be available during business hours.

### Step 3: File Articles of Organization

- File with the Secretary of State in your chosen state
- Information typically required:
  - LLC name (ClawOps LLC)
  - Registered agent name and address
  - Principal office address
  - Organizer signature
- Online filing is available in most states and is fastest
- **Cost:** $50 to $500 depending on state

### Step 4: Get an EIN (Employer Identification Number)

- Free from the IRS: https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online
- Required for bank accounts, hiring, and tax filings
- Takes about 5 minutes online; EIN issued immediately
- Apply as soon as your LLC is approved

### Step 5: Draft an Operating Agreement

Even if your state does not require one, **always have an operating agreement**. It:
- Defines ownership and management structure
- Establishes profit/loss distribution rules
- Covers what happens if members leave or join
- Strengthens your liability protection (courts look for this to confirm the LLC is a real separate entity)

For a single-member LLC, a simple operating agreement is sufficient. Templates are available online, or have an attorney draft one for $500 to $1,500.

### Step 6: Open a Business Bank Account

This is critical for maintaining the "corporate veil" (keeping personal and business finances separate).

**Bring:** EIN letter, Articles of Organization, Operating Agreement, government ID.

**Recommended banks:**
- **Mercury** - Best for tech/digital businesses, excellent API, no fees
- **Relay** - Great for small businesses, free plan
- **Chase Business** - Traditional option with wide branch network

### Step 7: Set Up Accounting

- **Software:** QuickBooks Self-Employed, Wave (free), or Xero
- Separate all business and personal finances from day one
- Track all income, expenses, and invoices
- Set aside **25 to 30% of revenue** for taxes (self-employment + income)

### Step 8: Obtain Business Licenses and Permits

- Most AI/consulting agencies need minimal licensing
- Check your city/county for a general business license
- No professional license is typically required for AI consulting (verify in your state)

### Step 9: Get Business Insurance

- **Professional liability (E&O):** $500 to $2,000/year. Essential for a services agency. Covers claims of negligence, errors, or failure to deliver.
- **General liability:** $500 to $1,500/year. Covers third-party bodily injury and property damage claims.
- **Cyber liability:** Consider if handling sensitive client data.
- **Providers:** Hiscox, Next Insurance, Hartford

## 6. S-Corp Election (Future Tax Optimization)

Once ClawOps earns **$40,000 to $50,000+ per year in profit**, consider electing S-Corp tax status (IRS Form 2553).

**How it works:**
- Pay yourself a "reasonable salary"
- Take remaining profits as distributions
- Distributions are **not subject to self-employment tax** (15.3%)
- Potential savings: thousands of dollars per year

**Downside:** Requires payroll, additional tax filings (Form 1120-S), and accounting costs ($1,000 to $3,000/year).

**When to do it:** Talk to a CPA when you are consistently profitable above $40K/year.
