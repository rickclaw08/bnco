## 1. Introduction

Claw Agency LLC ("Company," "we," "us") provides AI automation services to clients. This policy describes how we collect, process, store, and protect personal data and client information in compliance with applicable data protection laws, including the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), and other relevant regulations.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All personal data processed by the Company on behalf of clients
- All employees, contractors, and agents of the Company
- All systems, tools, and platforms used to deliver services
- Data from EU/EEA residents (GDPR), California residents (CCPA), and all other jurisdictions where we operate

## 3. Definitions

- **Personal Data:** Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person
- **Processing:** Any operation performed on personal data (collection, storage, use, transfer, deletion)
- **Data Controller:** The entity that determines the purposes and means of processing (typically the Client)
- **Data Processor:** The entity that processes data on behalf of the controller (typically the Company)
- **Data Subject:** The individual whose personal data is being processed
- **Sub-processor:** A third party engaged by the Company to process data

## 4. Our Role

In most engagements, our clients are the **Data Controller** and Claw Agency acts as a **Data Processor**. We process client data only under their instructions and for the purposes defined in our service agreements.

## 5. Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR Article 6)

We process personal data only when we have a lawful basis:
- **Contractual necessity:** Processing required to fulfill our service agreement with the client
- **Legitimate interest:** Processing necessary for our legitimate business interests, balanced against data subject rights
- **Consent:** When explicitly provided by the data subject
- **Legal obligation:** When required by applicable law

## 6. Data We May Process

In the course of providing AI automation services, we may process:
- Client business data (CRM records, customer lists, contact information)
- Communication data (emails, messages being automated)
- Analytics data (website traffic, user behavior data)
- Financial data (invoices, transaction records)
- Any other data provided by clients for automation purposes

## 7. Data Processing Principles

We adhere to the following principles:

### 7.1 Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency
- Process data only with a valid legal basis
- Be transparent about what data we process and why

### 7.2 Purpose Limitation
- Process data only for the specific purposes agreed upon with the client
- Never use client data for our own marketing, AI training, or other unauthorized purposes

### 7.3 Data Minimization
- Collect and process only the minimum data necessary for the agreed service
- Regularly review data holdings and purge unnecessary data

### 7.4 Accuracy
- Take reasonable steps to ensure data is accurate and up to date
- Correct or delete inaccurate data promptly

### 7.5 Storage Limitation
- Retain data only as long as necessary for the agreed purpose
- Default retention period: **90 days after project completion** unless otherwise agreed
- Delete or anonymize data when no longer needed

### 7.6 Integrity and Confidentiality
- Implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures
- Protect data against unauthorized access, loss, or destruction

## 8. Security Measures

### 8.1 Technical Measures
- Encryption of data in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest (AES-256)
- Multi-factor authentication on all systems handling client data
- Regular software updates and security patches
- Access controls based on principle of least privilege
- Secure deletion of data when no longer needed
- Regular backups with encryption

### 8.2 Organizational Measures
- Background checks for team members handling sensitive data
- Mandatory confidentiality agreements for all personnel
- Regular security awareness training
- Documented incident response procedures
- Annual security reviews

### 8.3 AI-Specific Measures
- Client data is never used to train, fine-tune, or improve our AI models without explicit written consent
- API calls to third-party AI services (OpenAI, Anthropic, etc.) are made with data processing agreements in place
- We verify that third-party AI providers do not retain or train on data sent via API
- Prompts containing client data are not logged beyond the retention period
- We maintain a register of all AI tools and third-party services used in client engagements

## 9. Sub-processors

We may engage third-party sub-processors to deliver services. We will:
- Maintain an up-to-date list of sub-processors (available upon request)
- Ensure sub-processors are bound by data processing agreements with equivalent protections
- Notify clients before adding new sub-processors (with reasonable notice)
- Remain liable for sub-processor compliance

### Current Sub-processor Categories:
- Cloud infrastructure providers (e.g., AWS, Google Cloud)
- AI/ML API providers (e.g., OpenAI, Anthropic)
- Automation platforms (e.g., Make, Zapier, n8n)
- Communication tools (e.g., Slack, email providers)

## 10. International Data Transfers

When transferring data outside the EU/EEA:
- We rely on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or other approved transfer mechanisms
- We assess the data protection laws of the recipient country
- We implement supplementary measures where necessary

## 11. Data Subject Rights

We support our clients in fulfilling data subject requests, including:
- **Right of Access:** Confirm what data is held and provide a copy
- **Right to Rectification:** Correct inaccurate data
- **Right to Erasure:** Delete data when no longer necessary ("right to be forgotten")
- **Right to Restrict Processing:** Limit how data is used
- **Right to Data Portability:** Provide data in a structured, machine-readable format
- **Right to Object:** Stop processing based on legitimate interest
- **Right related to Automated Decision-Making:** Not be subject to solely automated decisions with legal effects

**Response timeline:** We will respond to data subject requests within **30 days**.

## 12. Data Breach Response

In the event of a personal data breach:

1. **Detection & Containment (0-4 hours):** Identify the breach, contain it, and begin assessment
2. **Internal Notification (4-12 hours):** Notify Company leadership and legal counsel
3. **Client Notification (within 24 hours):** Notify affected clients with details of the breach
4. **Supervisory Authority Notification (within 72 hours):** If required under GDPR, notify the relevant data protection authority
5. **Data Subject Notification (without undue delay):** If the breach is likely to result in high risk to individuals
6. **Post-Incident Review (within 30 days):** Conduct root cause analysis and implement corrective measures

## 13. Data Processing Agreement (DPA)

For client engagements involving personal data, we will execute a Data Processing Agreement that specifies:
- Subject matter and duration of processing
- Nature and purpose of processing
- Types of personal data processed
- Categories of data subjects
- Obligations and rights of the controller
- Technical and organizational measures

A DPA template is available upon request.

## 14. Record Keeping

We maintain records of processing activities (GDPR Article 30), including:
- Categories of processing activities
- Purposes of processing
- Description of data categories and data subject categories
- Recipients of data
- International transfers
- Retention periods
- Security measures

## 15. Training and Awareness

All Company personnel who handle client data must:
- Complete data protection training before accessing client data
- Acknowledge this policy in writing
- Complete annual refresher training
- Report any suspected breaches immediately

## 16. Policy Review

This policy is reviewed and updated:
- At least annually
- When significant changes occur in our processing activities
- When relevant laws or regulations change
- After any data breach incident
