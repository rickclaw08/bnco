# HONE — Brand Identity Guide

**Date:** February 18, 2026
**Rebrand:** FORGE → HONE

---

## 1. Brand Name & Story Shift

### Why HONE

HONE means to sharpen, refine, perfect. It's a verb — an ongoing action, not a finished state. Where FORGE implied force, heat, and hammering raw material into shape, HONE implies precision, intention, and the daily discipline of getting sharper.

A man who hones his craft isn't performing strength. He's practicing care. He's refining what's already there — not starting from scratch, but getting better every day. That's what good grooming actually is: not a transformation, but a refinement.

### FORGE vs. HONE — The Shift

| Element | FORGE | HONE |
|---------|-------|------|
| **Core metaphor** | Hammering metal (force, heat) | Sharpening a blade (precision, care) |
| **Energy** | Industrial, aggressive, sparks | Deliberate, skilled, controlled |
| **Implied virtue** | Strength, toughness | Discipline, craftsmanship, refinement |
| **Relationship to user** | "We built something tough" | "You're getting sharper every day" |
| **Material palette** | Steel, iron, dark metals | Leather, whetstone, oiled wood, honed steel |
| **Tagline energy** | "Built, not born." | "Hone your craft." |

HONE doesn't abandon the craft ethos — it sharpens it. FORGE was about creation. HONE is about refinement. Both are about intention, but HONE acknowledges that the man is already something — he's just getting better.

## 2. Tagline Options

### The Five

1. **"Hone your craft."** — Direct. Action-oriented. Works as tagline, CTA, and philosophy. Implies grooming is a skill worth practicing.

2. **"Sharpen daily."** — Minimal. Routine-focused. Frames grooming as discipline, not vanity.

3. **"Refined, not reinvented."** — Positions HONE as the anti-makeover brand. You're already good — we just help you get sharper.

4. **"The edge is in the details."** — Double meaning: edge (sharpness) and edge (competitive advantage). Speaks to men who care about craft.

5. **"Better every pass."** — References honing (each pass of the blade gets sharper). Implies daily improvement without being preachy.

### ✅ Winner: "Hone your craft."

It works everywhere — homepage hero, email sign-off, social bio, packaging. It's a command and an invitation. It frames grooming as part of a larger ethos of doing things well, not as a chore or a vanity project. It's the kind of line a man would actually repeat.

## 3. Brand Voice & Tone Guide

### Who HONE Sounds Like
A skilled craftsman who's also well-read. He doesn't brag — his work speaks. He explains things clearly because he respects your intelligence. He has opinions but holds them lightly. Think: the friend who makes his own cutting boards and also knows which beard oil is bullshit.

### Voice Attributes

| Attribute | What It Means | What It Doesn't Mean |
|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| **Precise** | Every word earns its place. No filler. | Cold, clinical, robotic |
| **Confident** | We know what we made is good | Arrogant, chest-thumping, "alpha" |
| **Dry wit** | Humor through understatement and observation | Sarcastic, mean, trying too hard |
| **Grounded** | Honest about what we are and aren't | Self-deprecating, apologetic |
| **Craft-minded** | We care about process and materials | Pretentious, gatekeeping, elitist |

### Tone Spectrum

- **Product copy:** Confident, specific, clean. Let ingredients and performance do the talking.
- **Email:** Conversational, direct, occasionally funny. Like a text from a friend who happens to know about grooming.
- **Social:** Witty, observational, culturally aware. Never thirsty.
- **About/Brand:** Thoughtful, slightly philosophical. The one place we earn the right to go deeper.

### Writing Rules

1. **Short sentences win.** If a sentence has a comma, ask if it needs one.
2. **No superlatives without proof.** Don't say "best" — say why it's good.
3. **Verb energy.** HONE is a verb. Use active voice. "We made" not "it was crafted."
4. **Respect the reader.** Never explain something they already know. Never talk down.
5. **Humor is dry, never loud.** The joke is funnier when you don't point at it.
6. **No grooming clichés.** No "unleash," no "warrior," no "alpha," no "rugged."
7. **Specificity > vagueness.** "Cedar, black pepper, bergamot" > "masculine scent."

### Sample Voice Comparisons

❌ "HONE — Unleash the best version of yourself with our premium grooming essentials!"
✅ "HONE — Three products. Clean ingredients. Ninety seconds. Done."

❌ "Our artisanally handcrafted beard oil is lovingly blended..."
✅ "Seven ingredients. All pronounceable. None of them synthetic."

❌ "For the modern man who demands excellence in every aspect of his life..."
✅ "For men who'd rather spend 90 seconds on their beard than 90 minutes choosing between 47 products."

## 4. Visual Identity

### Color Palette

| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| **Honed Steel** (dark charcoal) | #2C2C2C | Primary text, logo |
| **Whetstone** (warm mid-gray) | #7A7568 | Secondary text, borders |
| **Raw Linen** (warm off-white) | #F4F0E8 | Backgrounds, packaging |
| **Oiled Leather** (warm brown) | #8B6914 | Accents, CTAs, highlights |
| **Cedar** (muted warm wood) | #A0522D | Secondary accent, seasonal |
| **Black Pepper** (near-black warm) | #1A1A18 | Bold headers, packaging |

**Palette philosophy:** Warm neutrals grounded in natural materials. Nothing neon, nothing cold. Every color should feel like something you could touch — leather, stone, linen, wood.

### Typography

**Primary (Headlines):** A strong, slightly condensed serif. Recommendations:
- **Druk Text Wide** (bold, architectural)
- **GT Sectra** (sharp serifs, editorial feel)
- **Freight Display** (classic but not stuffy)

**Secondary (Body):** A clean geometric sans-serif. Recommendations:
- **Söhne** (successor to Helvetica, warmer)
- **Inter** (open source, excellent readability)
- **Neue Haas Grotesk** (the original Helvetica, refined)

**Rules:**
- Headlines: uppercase or title case, tracked slightly wide
- Body: sentence case, generous line height (1.5-1.6)
- No script fonts. No distressed fonts. No fonts that say "I own a motorcycle."

### Photography Style

**Mood:** Quiet confidence. Natural light. Warm tones. Close-up textures.

**Do:**
- Textural close-ups: grain of wood, leather, stone, oil on skin
- Hands at work: applying product, trimming, holding the bottle
- Environmental context: clean bathroom shelf, workshop bench, morning light
- Real skin, real beards, real imperfections
- Desaturated warmth — like a Kodak Portra 400 film stock

**Don't:**
- Lifestyle shots of men laughing at nothing
- Over-produced studio shots with dramatic lighting
- Shirtless gym selfie energy
- Stock photo "diverse group of friends hanging out" vibes
- Heavy filters or overly moody darkness

**Product photography:** Clean, elevated, minimal. Products on natural surfaces — raw wood, concrete, linen. Slight shadow. No floating-in-white-void sterility.

### Logo Direction

**The word HONE in a custom or modified typeface.** No icon, no emblem, no shield, no crossed axes.

- Letterforms should feel honed — precise edges, intentional geometry
- Slight customization to the H and E to create distinctiveness
- Consider a subtle blade-edge quality to the letterforms without being literal
- Works at all sizes: embossed on packaging, favicon, social avatar
- One color: Honed Steel (#2C2C2C) on light backgrounds, Raw Linen (#F4F0E8) on dark

**Wordmark only.** The name is strong enough to be the mark. An icon can come later if the brand earns one.

### Packaging Direction

- **Beard Oil:** Amber glass dropper bottle, matte label, debossed HONE wordmark
- **Beard Balm:** Matte black aluminum tin, embossed lid
- **Beard Wash:** Amber glass bottle, matte pump
- **Secondary packaging:** Uncoated kraft or raw linen-textured stock
- **Details:** No foil stamping. Embossing or debossing only. Ingredient lists printed clearly, not hidden.
- **Unboxing:** Tissue paper in Raw Linen shade. Simple routine card on heavy stock. No confetti, no stickers, no "you're amazing" inserts.

## 5. Brand Narrative

> HONE is beard care built on a simple idea: grooming is a craft, and craft improves with practice.
>
> We make three products with clean ingredients, subtle scent, and formulas that work in 90 seconds. No gimmicks. No Viking branding. No 14-step routines designed to sell you more things.
>
> The name is the philosophy. To hone is to sharpen — not through force, but through care, repetition, and attention to detail. Your beard doesn't need a makeover. It needs a better daily practice.
>
> Hone your craft.
