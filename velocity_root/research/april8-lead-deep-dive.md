### Habitat for Humanity Greater Cincinnati - Potential Resources

**Organization:** Habitat for Humanity of Greater Cincinnati
**Website:** https://www.habitatcincinnati.org/
**Programs Page:** https://www.habitatcincinnati.org/programs

Habitat Cincinnati offers "a wide range of programs and services" but their website is light on foreclosure prevention specifics. Key considerations:

1. **Habitat's Own Foreclosure:** Since Habitat IS the lender, they theoretically have full discretion on workout options. They don't have to foreclose - they could modify, forbear, or accept a reduced payoff.

2. **Ohio Save the Dream (HAF):** Ohio Housing Finance Agency runs the "Save the Dream" program (https://savethedream.ohiohome.org/). This is the state's Homeowner Assistance Fund - funded by the American Rescue Plan Act. May provide mortgage reinstatement assistance. **Status as of April 2026 needs verification - HAF funds were initially expected to be depleted by mid-2025 in many states.**

3. **City of Cincinnati Emergency Mortgage Assistance:**
   - Community and Economic Development Department
   - Phone: (513) 352-6146 (general questions)
   - Phone: (513) 591-6000 (building/zoning)
   - Could provide emergency mortgage reinstatement funds

4. **513Relief (513relief.org):**
   - Housing & Utility Relief programs
   - "Qualify for several mortgage, rent and/or utility programs designed to keep families safe in their homes"
   - Covers Hamilton County

**Strategy Note:** With only ~$29K owed on a 0% Habitat loan, the reinstatement amount is likely very small (maybe just a few months of missed payments plus legal fees). This could be a deal where:
- Hudson reinstates with help from HAF/city programs (we help facilitate, build goodwill)
- OR we negotiate a subject-to or assumption of the Habitat loan
- OR we purchase at auction for potentially well below market
