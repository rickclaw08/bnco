"Hey William, this is Brand. I'm a local investor here in Cincinnati. I was doing some research on properties in the West Price Hill area and your name came up on a place over on Saint Martins. I know these situations can be stressful, and I'm not trying to add to that. I just wanted to reach out and see if there's anything I can help with. Hope you're having a good day."

### Key Points:
- **DO NOT mention foreclosure, sheriff's sale, or auction** - let him bring it up
- **DO NOT mention the case number or court**
- Keep it warm, casual, non-threatening
- "Your name came up" is vague enough to be true without being creepy
- Offer the solution (cash, fast close) without naming the problem
- One callback number, say it twice

### If He Calls Back:
- Use the Straight Line method
- Qualify: Does he want to sell? What's his timeline? What does he owe?
- Don't quote a number on the first call - "I'd need to see the property first"
- Goal: Get him to agree to a walkthrough or at minimum confirm he's interested in an offer
- If he asks how you got his number: "Public records research. I look for opportunities to help homeowners in the area."

### Timing:
- Best time: Before 5 PM on a weekday (construction workers are up early, home by late afternoon)
- Avoid: Monday mornings, Friday afternoons
- If no callback within 48 hours: Text follow-up with same tone
