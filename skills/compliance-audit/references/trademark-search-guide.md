# Trademark Search Guide

**Version:** 0.1.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-21

Step-by-step procedures for conducting trademark searches before adopting a new name, logo, or slogan.

---

## When to Conduct a Trademark Search

- Before naming a new product, service, or business
- Before creating a new logo or brand identity
- Before registering a domain name for a brand
- Before launching marketing with a new tagline or slogan
- Before entering a new market or geography with existing branding

---

## Search Levels

### Level 1: Quick Knock-Out Search (30 minutes)

Use this for initial screening. If you find obvious conflicts here, stop and choose a different name.

**Steps:**

1. **Google Search**
   - Search: "[name]" + your industry keywords
   - Search: "[name]" + competitors
   - Check first 3 pages of results
   - Look for: existing businesses, products, or services with the same or confusingly similar name

2. **Domain Check**
   - Search for [name].com, [name].io, [name].co, [name].net
   - If the exact .com is taken by a business in your space, that is a yellow flag

3. **Social Media Check**
   - Search the name on Twitter/X, Instagram, LinkedIn, Facebook, TikTok
   - Look for established accounts using the name in your industry

4. **App Store Check** (if applicable)
   - Search Apple App Store and Google Play
   - Look for apps with the same or similar name in your category

**Decision point:** If obvious conflicts exist, stop here and pick a different name.

### Level 2: Trademark Database Search (1-2 hours)

If Level 1 is clear, search official trademark databases.

**Steps:**

1. **USPTO TESS (United States)**
   - URL: https://tmsearch.uspto.gov
   - Search types to run:
     - **Exact match:** Search the exact name
     - **Phonetic match:** Search how the name sounds (e.g., "Klear" for "Clear")
     - **Root word:** Search the core word without prefixes/suffixes
   - For each result, check:
     - Registration status (Live vs Dead)
     - International Class (is it in your goods/services class?)
     - Owner (who owns it?)
     - Filing date (how long ago?)

2. **EUIPO (European Union)**
   - URL: https://euipo.europa.eu/eSearch
   - Run the same searches as USPTO
   - Important if you plan to operate in Europe

3. **WIPO Global Brand Database (International)**
   - URL: https://branddb.wipo.int
   - Searches multiple countries at once
   - Good for broad international screening

4. **State Trademark Databases** (if US-based)
   - Check the Secretary of State database for your state
   - Some businesses only register at the state level

**For each potential conflict found, document:**
- Mark name
- Registration number
- Status (Live/Dead/Pending)
- International Class
- Owner
- Filing/Registration date
- Goods/Services description

### Level 3: Comprehensive Search (Professional)

For high-stakes brands (major product launch, significant investment), recommend a professional comprehensive search through a trademark attorney or search firm.

**What they add:**
- Common law trademark search (unregistered marks)
- Business name registrations across all states
- Industry publication searches
- International coverage beyond WIPO
- Legal opinion on registrability and risk

**Cost:** Typically $500-2,000 per mark
**Timeline:** 1-2 weeks

## Understanding Trademark Classes

Trademarks are registered in specific International Classes. A name can coexist across unrelated classes.

### Most Relevant Classes for Digital/Service Businesses

| Class | Covers | Examples |
|-------|--------|----------|
| 9 | Software, apps, digital content | Mobile apps, SaaS platforms, downloadable content |
| 35 | Advertising, business management, consulting | Marketing agencies, business consulting |
| 38 | Telecommunications, streaming | Chat platforms, video streaming, podcasts |
| 41 | Education, entertainment, publishing | Online courses, media, events |
| 42 | Technology services, SaaS, hosting | Cloud services, web development, IT consulting |
| 45 | Legal services, security | Legal consulting, compliance services |

**Key principle:** Two businesses can use the same name if they operate in different classes AND there is no likelihood of confusion. But identical names in the same class are almost always a conflict.

## Analyzing Search Results

### Risk Assessment Matrix

| Scenario | Risk Level | Recommendation |
|----------|------------|----------------|
| Identical name, same class, live registration | CRITICAL | Do not use. Choose another name. |
| Similar name, same class, live registration | HIGH | Likely conflict. Consult attorney or choose another name. |
| Identical name, different class, live registration | MEDIUM | Possible to use, but assess for famous mark issues. |
| Similar name, different class, live registration | LOW | Generally safe, but document the analysis. |
| Any name, dead registration | LOW | Generally available, but check why it died. |
| Identical name, pending application | HIGH | Monitor closely. May conflict if it registers. |
| Unregistered but actively used business | MEDIUM | Common law rights may exist. Research further. |

### "Likelihood of Confusion" Factors

Courts consider these factors when assessing trademark conflicts:

1. **Similarity of marks** (sight, sound, meaning)
2. **Similarity of goods/services**
3. **Similarity of trade channels** (where products are sold)
4. **Strength of the existing mark** (famous marks get broader protection)
5. **Evidence of actual confusion**
6. **Intent of the adopter** (did you know about the existing mark?)
7. **Sophistication of consumers**

## After the Search

### If Clear
1. Consider filing a trademark application (USPTO, EUIPO, etc.)
2. Register the domain name
3. Secure social media handles
4. Begin using the mark in commerce (use builds rights)
5. Use (TM) symbol immediately; use (R) only after registration

### If Conflicts Found
1. Document the conflict and risk assessment
2. Consider alternative names
3. If the conflict is borderline, consult a trademark attorney
4. Do not invest heavily in branding until the name is cleared
5. Consider a coexistence agreement if the conflict is minor

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------|
| Only searching exact matches | Similar-sounding names can still conflict |
| Ignoring dead registrations | The name may still be in use without registration |
| Assuming different class = safe | Famous marks get protection across all classes |
| Not searching before investing in branding | Rebranding is expensive and disruptive |
| Using (R) without registration | Illegal in most jurisdictions; use (TM) instead |
| Assuming a domain name = trademark rights | Domain ownership does not equal trademark rights |
