# Tone Examples

## Version: 0.1.0

## Purpose

This file provides before-and-after examples showing off-brand writing corrected to on-brand writing. Use these as calibration when reviewing content.

## Example 1: Blog Introduction

### Off-Brand (Too Corporate)
"In today's rapidly evolving technological landscape, businesses of all sizes are leveraging cutting-edge AI solutions to transform their operational paradigms. At ClawOps, we're at the forefront of this revolution, delivering best-in-class agent-powered automation that drives unprecedented efficiency gains."

### On-Brand
"Most small agencies spend 10+ hours a week on tasks an AI agent could handle in minutes. We know because we used to be one of them. Here is what we changed, and how you can do it too."

### What Changed
- Removed jargon: "leveraging," "cutting-edge," "paradigms," "best-in-class," "unprecedented"
- Added specifics: "10+ hours a week"
- Made it personal: "We know because we used to be one of them"
- Shortened from 47 words to 38 words
- Replaced em dashes with periods and commas

## Example 2: Product Description

### Off-Brand (Too Salesy)
"ClawOps is the ultimate AI automation platform -- designed to revolutionize your workflow and supercharge your productivity. Our cutting-edge agents seamlessly integrate with your existing tools to deliver a transformative experience that will change the way you do business forever."

### On-Brand
"ClawOps builds AI agents that handle your repetitive work: client onboarding, weekly reports, outreach tracking. They connect to your existing tools and run in the background. You review the output and move on."

### What Changed
- Removed em dash
- Replaced hype words: "ultimate," "revolutionize," "supercharge," "cutting-edge," "seamlessly," "transformative"
- Added concrete examples: "client onboarding, weekly reports, outreach tracking"
- Described what actually happens instead of making promises
- Used colon instead of em dash

## Example 3: Social Media Post

### Off-Brand (Too Enthusiastic)
"WE'RE SO EXCITED!! 🚀🔥 Our AMAZING new AI agent platform just dropped and it's going to DISRUPT THE ENTIRE INDUSTRY!! Check it out NOW and prepare to have your mind BLOWN!!! 🤯💥 #AI #GameChanger #Revolution"

### On-Brand
"Shipped the first version of our reporting agent today. It pulls data from 3 sources, generates a client-ready summary, and sends it by Friday morning. Took us 2 weeks to build. Thread on what we learned:"

### What Changed
- Removed all caps shouting
- Removed excessive emojis and exclamation marks
- Replaced hype with specifics: what it does, how long it took
- Added a genuine hook: "Thread on what we learned"
- Sounds like a real person sharing real work

## Example 4: Client Email

### Off-Brand (Too Formal)
"Dear Mr. Johnson, I hope this email finds you well. Per our previous conversation, I wanted to circle back regarding the deliverables we discussed during our last touchpoint. Please find attached the relevant documentation for your perusal at your earliest convenience."

### On-Brand
"Hi Mark, following up on our call Thursday. Attached is the project timeline we discussed. Let me know if the dates work for your team, and I will lock them in. Happy to adjust if anything has shifted on your end."

### What Changed
- Dropped overly formal greeting
- Removed jargon: "circle back," "touchpoint," "perusal," "at your earliest convenience"
- Made it specific: "call Thursday," "project timeline"
- Added a clear action: "Let me know if the dates work"
- Sounds like a person, not a form letter

## Example 5: Error Message

### Off-Brand (Too Technical / Cold)
"Error 503: Service temporarily unavailable. The server is currently unable to handle the request due to a temporary overloading or maintenance of the server."

### On-Brand
"Something went wrong on our end. We are looking into it and expect to have things back to normal within a few minutes. If this keeps happening, reach out to us at support@clawops.com."

### What Changed
- Removed technical jargon (error codes, server terminology)
- Added reassurance: "We are looking into it"
- Set expectations: "within a few minutes"
- Gave a clear next step: contact support
- Sounds helpful instead of robotic

## Example 6: Em Dash Replacement

### Off-Brand
"Our agents -- built specifically for small teams -- handle the work you hate doing."

### Fix Option A (Commas)
"Our agents, built specifically for small teams, handle the work you hate doing."

### Fix Option B (Separate Sentences)
"Our agents are built specifically for small teams. They handle the work you hate doing."

### Fix Option C (Parentheses)
"Our agents (built specifically for small teams) handle the work you hate doing."

All three options are acceptable. Choose based on which reads most naturally in context.

## Quick Calibration Test

Read your content and ask:
1. Would I say this to a friend at a coffee shop? If not, simplify
2. Can I replace any word with a shorter, simpler one? Do it
3. Are there any em dashes? Remove them
4. Is there a specific number or example I could add? Add it
5. Does it sound like a press release? Rewrite it
