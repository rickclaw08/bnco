# Brand Voice Guide

## Version: 0.1.0

## Core Identity

ClawOps sounds like a smart, experienced colleague who explains things clearly and gets to the point. Not a press release. Not a sales pitch. Not a textbook.

## Voice Characteristics

### Pragmatic
We focus on what works, not what sounds impressive. Every claim should be backed by specifics. "Saves 3 hours per week on client reporting" beats "dramatically improves your workflow efficiency."

### Direct
Say what you mean. Short sentences are fine. Get to the point, then stop.

### Technical but Accessible
We talk to people who build things, but we do not assume everyone has the same background. Explain concepts without being condescending. Use technical terms when they are the right terms, but define them when the audience may not know them.

### Honest
We admit limitations. We say "this works best for X" instead of "this works for everything." Honesty builds trust faster than hype.

### Human
Write like a person, not a brand. Contractions are fine. Starting sentences with "And" or "But" is fine. Personality is welcome.

## Absolute Rules

### 1. No Em Dashes
Never use em dashes (the long dash). Not ever. Not in any content, on any platform, for any reason.

**Instead of em dashes, use:**
- Commas for brief asides
- Periods to break into separate sentences
- Colons to introduce explanations
- Parentheses for supplementary info
- Semicolons to connect related ideas

**Wrong:** "Our platform -- built for small teams -- handles everything."
**Right:** "Our platform, built for small teams, handles everything."
**Also right:** "Our platform handles everything. It is built for small teams."

### 2. No Corporate Jargon

Banned words and phrases:
- Synergy / synergize
- Leverage (as a verb)
- Paradigm / paradigm shift
- Disrupt / disruptive
- Ecosystem (unless talking about actual biology)
- Holistic
- Best-in-class
- World-class
- Cutting-edge
- Bleeding-edge
- Move the needle
- Circle back
- Touch base
- Low-hanging fruit
- Deep dive (acceptable in technical contexts only)
- At the end of the day
- It is what it is

### 3. No Hype

Do not exaggerate. Do not use superlatives without evidence.

**Wrong:** "The most powerful AI agent platform ever built."
**Right:** "An AI agent platform that handles client onboarding, reporting, and outreach."

### 4. Active Voice

Use active voice by default. Passive voice is acceptable only when the actor is genuinely unknown or irrelevant.

**Wrong:** "Reports are generated by the agent automatically."
**Right:** "The agent generates reports automatically."

## Sentence Guidelines

- Keep sentences under 25 words when possible
- One idea per sentence
- Break up paragraphs after 3-4 sentences max
- Read it aloud. If you stumble, simplify
- Target 8th-grade reading level

## Tone by Context

| Context | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Blog post | Conversational, informative, slightly casual |
| Social media | Casual, punchy, engaging |
| Client email | Professional, warm, direct |
| Technical docs | Clear, precise, structured |
| Marketing copy | Confident, specific, action-oriented |
| Error messages | Helpful, calm, solution-focused |

## Examples

### Blog Post Opening
**Wrong:** "In today's rapidly evolving digital landscape, leveraging cutting-edge AI solutions has become paramount for businesses seeking to maintain a competitive edge."

**Right:** "Most small agencies spend 10+ hours a week on tasks an AI agent could handle in minutes. Here is how we fixed that."

### Social Media
**Wrong:** "We're thrilled to announce our revolutionary new platform that will disrupt the way you think about business operations!"

**Right:** "Built an agent that handles our weekly client reports. Went from 3 hours to 5 minutes. Here is what we learned."

### Client Email
**Wrong:** "Per our previous correspondence, I wanted to circle back regarding the deliverables we discussed."

**Right:** "Following up on our call yesterday. Here is the updated timeline for the three deliverables we discussed."
