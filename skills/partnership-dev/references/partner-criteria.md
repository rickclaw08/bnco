# Partner Criteria

Evaluation framework for identifying and scoring potential partners.

---

## Ideal Partner Profile

The best partners for RickClaw AI:

1. **Serve businesses that need AI** but don't have AI capabilities themselves
2. **Have an active client base** (not just an audience, actual paying clients)
3. **Offer complementary services** (web dev, marketing, consulting, design)
4. **Value quality** and won't send us bad-fit clients for a quick buck
5. **Are responsive** and professional in communication

---

## Partner Categories

### Tier 1: High-Value Partners (Prioritize)

**Web Development Agencies**
- Their clients already have digital presence
- AI features are natural upsells
- They can't build AI themselves
- High deal values (Professional+ tier)

**Marketing Agencies**
- Content automation is a hot need
- Lead generation and analytics are AI-ready
- Large client portfolios
- Recurring need for AI tools

**Business Consultants / Operations Consultants**
- They identify problems, we build solutions
- High-trust relationships with clients
- Clients have budget for solutions
- Natural co-selling opportunities

**SaaS Companies**
- Technology integration opportunities
- Large user bases
- Mutual referral potential
- API-based collaboration is natural

### Tier 2: Good Partners (Pursue Selectively)

**Freelancers / Solopreneurs**
- Lower volume but personal relationships
- Good for testing referral program
- May become clients themselves
- Community influencers

**Industry Communities / Groups**
- Access to targeted audiences
- Speaking and content opportunities
- Brand building
- Not direct revenue but pipeline building

**Complementary Tech Providers**
- CRM companies, email platforms, etc.
- Integration partnerships
- Co-marketing opportunities
- Technical documentation partnerships

### Tier 3: Opportunistic Partners (If They Come to Us)

**Influencers / Content Creators**
- Affiliate-style partnerships
- Good for brand awareness
- Lower conversion quality
- Harder to track ROI

**Educational Institutions**
- Training partnerships
- Student pipelines
- Long sales cycles
- Lower deal values

## Scoring Criteria (Detailed)

### 1. Audience Overlap (Weight: 25%)

How well does their client base match our ideal client?

| Score | Description |
|-------|------------|
| 5 | Perfect match - their clients are exactly who we serve |
| 4 | Strong overlap - 70%+ of their clients could use AI |
| 3 | Moderate overlap - 40-70% potential fit |
| 2 | Some overlap - 20-40% potential fit |
| 1 | Minimal overlap - <20% potential fit |

### 2. Complementary Services (Weight: 20%)

Are their services complementary (not competitive)?

| Score | Description |
|-------|------------|
| 5 | Perfectly complementary - fills a gap in our offering |
| 4 | Strongly complementary - natural pairing |
| 3 | Somewhat complementary - some overlap but mostly different |
| 2 | Partially competitive - significant overlap in some areas |
| 1 | Directly competitive - same services, same market |

### 3. Reputation & Quality (Weight: 20%)

Are they reputable and do they deliver quality work?

| Score | Description |
|-------|------------|
| 5 | Excellent reputation, known for quality |
| 4 | Good reputation, solid work |
| 3 | Average reputation, acceptable quality |
| 2 | Mixed reputation, quality concerns |
| 1 | Poor reputation or unknown |

### 4. Active Client Base (Weight: 15%)

Do they have active clients who could become our clients?

| Score | Description |
|-------|------------|
| 5 | Large active client base (50+ active clients) |
| 4 | Solid client base (20-50 active clients) |
| 3 | Growing client base (10-20 active clients) |
| 2 | Small client base (5-10 active clients) |
| 1 | Very small or no established client base |

### 5. Willingness to Partner (Weight: 10%)

Are they open to and excited about partnering?

| Score | Description |
|-------|------------|
| 5 | Actively seeking partners, enthusiastic |
| 4 | Open to partnership, interested |
| 3 | Neutral, willing to explore |
| 2 | Hesitant, needs convincing |
| 1 | Not interested or unresponsive |

### 6. Ease of Integration (Weight: 10%)

How easy is it to work together operationally?

| Score | Description |
|-------|------------|
| 5 | Seamless - shared tools, similar processes |
| 4 | Easy - minor adjustments needed |
| 3 | Moderate - some process alignment required |
| 2 | Difficult - significant process differences |
| 1 | Very difficult - fundamentally different operations |

## Red Flags in Potential Partners

Do not partner with businesses that:
- Have a history of client complaints or legal issues
- Expect us to work for free "for exposure"
- Want exclusive arrangements (we need flexibility)
- Can't clearly describe their client base
- Are in financial trouble (risky for commission payments)
- Have conflicting values or ethics
- Want to control our pricing or process
