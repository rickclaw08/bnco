## When to Use

Use this skill when:
- Looking to create inbound lead flow without direct prospecting
- A potential partner has been identified
- Setting up or managing a referral program
- Evaluating a partnership opportunity
- Building an affiliate or commission structure

## Why Partnerships Matter

Partnerships create leverage:
- **Referral partners** send pre-qualified leads (highest conversion rate)
- **Technology partners** expand our capabilities
- **Agency partners** bring clients we couldn't reach alone
- **Community partners** build reputation and trust

## Workflow

### Step 1: Identify Partnership Opportunities

Look for businesses that:
1. Serve the same clients but offer different services
2. Have audiences that need AI automation
3. Would benefit from adding AI to their offering
4. Are complementary, not competitive

Use criteria from `references/partner-criteria.md` to evaluate.

#### Partner Types

| Type | Description | Example | Revenue Model |
|------|------------|---------|---------------|
| Referral | Sends clients to us for a fee | Web dev agency | Commission per deal |
| Co-Selling | We sell together, split revenue | Consulting firm | Revenue share |
| White-Label | We build, they sell under their brand | Marketing agency | Wholesale pricing |
| Technology | We integrate with their platform | SaaS companies | Mutual referrals |
| Affiliate | Anyone who refers via a tracked link | Bloggers, influencers | Flat fee or % |

### Step 2: Research Potential Partners

For each potential partner, gather:
- What services they offer
- Who their clients are
- Their company size and maturity
- Their reputation and quality
- How a partnership would benefit both sides
- Any existing partnership programs they run

Sources to find partners:
- LinkedIn (search for complementary businesses)
- Industry directories and communities
- Client recommendations ("who else do you work with?")
- Competitor partner pages (who partners with our competitors?)
- Conference speaker lists and sponsor lists

### Step 3: Evaluate Fit

Score each potential partner using `references/partner-criteria.md`:

| Criteria | Weight | Score (1-5) |
|----------|--------|------------|
| Audience overlap with our ideal client | 25% | __ |
| Complementary (not competitive) services | 20% | __ |
| Reputation and quality | 20% | __ |
| Active client base | 15% | __ |
| Willingness to partner | 10% | __ |
| Ease of integration | 10% | __ |
| **Weighted Total** | 100% | __ |

Minimum score to pursue: 3.5 out of 5.0

### Step 4: Draft Partnership Proposal

Use templates from `references/partnership-templates.md`. Include:

1. **Who we are** (brief, 2-3 sentences)
2. **Why we're reaching out** (specific to them)
3. **The opportunity** (what's in it for them)
4. **Partnership structure** (referral fee, revenue share, etc.)
5. **How it works** (simple process, low friction)
6. **Next steps** (call, meeting, trial period)

#### Key Principles
- Lead with what's in it for THEM
- Make the process dead simple
- Start small (trial period)
- Provide everything they need to refer us (collateral, talking points)

### Step 5: Structure the Deal

#### Referral Program Structure

See `references/referral-structure.md` for full details. Quick summary:

| Tier of Referred Deal | Referral Fee | Payment Timing |
|----------------------|-------------|----------------|
| Starter ($500-$1,500) | 10% ($50-$150) | On payment receipt |
| Professional ($2,500-$7,500) | 15% ($375-$1,125) | On payment receipt |
| Enterprise ($10K-$25K/mo) | 10% of first 3 months | Monthly for 3 months |
| Custom ($25K+) | 10% of initial project | On first payment |

#### Revenue Share (Co-Selling)

| Scenario | Our Share | Partner Share |
|----------|----------|--------------|
| Partner brings client, we deliver | 70% | 30% |
| We bring client, partner helps deliver | 80% | 20% |
| Joint effort, joint delivery | 50% | 50% |

#### White-Label Pricing

| Tier | Retail Price | Wholesale to Partner | Partner Margin |
|------|-------------|---------------------|----------------|
| Starter | $500-$1,500 | $350-$900 | 30-40% |
| Professional | $2,500-$7,500 | $1,750-$4,500 | 30-40% |
| Enterprise | $10K-$25K/mo | Negotiated | 25-35% |

### Step 6: Outreach and Negotiation

1. Send partnership proposal (see templates)
2. Schedule a call to discuss
3. Negotiate terms (stay within guidelines above)
4. Agree on trial period (3 months recommended)
5. Set up tracking method (how we attribute referrals)
6. Create partner collateral (one-pager, talking points)
7. Launch and monitor

### Step 7: Manage Active Partnerships

Once a partnership is active:

**Monthly tasks:**
- Check in with partner contact
- Report on referrals and commissions
- Share updates on our capabilities
- Ask about their pipeline and upcoming opportunities

**Quarterly tasks:**
- Review partnership performance
- Discuss what's working and what's not
- Adjust terms if needed
- Plan joint initiatives

**Tracking metrics:**
- Referrals received per month
- Conversion rate of partner referrals
- Revenue from partner channel
- Commission paid out
- Partner satisfaction (informal check)

### Step 8: Scale What Works

When a partnership proves successful:
1. Increase commitment (more co-marketing, joint content)
2. Formalize the agreement (if started informally)
3. Create case studies together
4. Explore deeper integration (technology, co-selling)
5. Use the model to find similar partners

When a partnership isn't working:
1. Diagnose why (wrong audience? low effort? poor fit?)
2. Try adjustments (better collateral, higher commission, more training)
3. If still not working after 3 months, gracefully wind down
4. Thank them and leave the door open for the future
