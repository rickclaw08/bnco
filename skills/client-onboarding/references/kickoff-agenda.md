# Kickoff Meeting Agenda

## Version: 0.1.0

## Meeting Details

- **Title:** [Company Name] x ClawOps - Project Kickoff
- **Duration:** 60 minutes
- **Attendees:** Client team + ClawOps project team
- **Format:** Video call

---

## Agenda

### 1. Welcome and Introductions (5 min)
- ClawOps team introductions (name, role, what they will do on this project)
- Client team introductions
- Quick icebreaker if appropriate

### 2. Project Overview and Goals (10 min)
- Recap the project scope from the proposal
- Confirm the primary objectives:
  - What problem are we solving?
  - What does success look like?
  - How will we measure it?
- Client confirms or adjusts the scope

### 3. Timeline Review (10 min)
- Walk through the proposed project timeline
- Key milestones and dates:
  - Phase 1: Discovery and requirements ([dates])
  - Phase 2: Design and architecture ([dates])
  - Phase 3: Build and iterate ([dates])
  - Phase 4: Testing and QA ([dates])
  - Phase 5: Launch and handoff ([dates])
- Hard deadlines or constraints from the client
- Adjust timeline as needed based on discussion

### 4. Communication Plan (10 min)
- Preferred communication channel (Slack, email, etc.)
- Response time expectations:
  - Business hours: within 4 hours
  - Urgent issues: within 1 hour
- Meeting cadence:
  - Weekly sync (30 min) on [Day] at [Time]
  - Ad-hoc meetings as needed
- Status report delivery:
  - Written updates every [Day]
  - Format walkthrough
- Escalation path:
  - Who to contact for day-to-day questions
  - Who to contact for urgent issues
  - Who to contact for scope or contract changes

### 5. Requirements and Discovery (15 min)
- Discuss initial requirements:
  - What systems or tools does the client currently use?
  - What are the most painful manual processes?
  - What integrations are needed?
  - What data sources will we work with?
  - Are there security or compliance requirements?
- Identify what we need from the client:
  - Access credentials
  - Documentation
  - Subject matter expert availability
  - Decision-making authority and process
- Schedule dedicated discovery sessions if needed

### 6. Next Steps and Action Items (10 min)
- Summarize agreed-upon next steps
- Assign owners and deadlines for each action item:
  - [ ] [Action item] - [Owner] - [Deadline]
  - [ ] [Action item] - [Owner] - [Deadline]
  - [ ] [Action item] - [Owner] - [Deadline]
- Confirm the first weekly sync date and time
- Set expectations for what happens between now and the next meeting

### 7. Questions (remaining time)
- Open floor for any questions from the client
- Address any concerns or uncertainties
- Confirm everyone is aligned and comfortable

---

## Post-Meeting Checklist

Within 2 hours of the meeting:
- [ ] Send meeting notes to all attendees
- [ ] Send action items with owners and deadlines
- [ ] Send calendar invite for recurring weekly sync
- [ ] Set up the communication channel (if not done)
- [ ] Share any documents referenced during the meeting
- [ ] Log the kickoff in the project file

## Notes for the Facilitator

- Keep the meeting moving. Do not let any section run more than 5 minutes over
- If a topic needs deeper discussion, note it and schedule a follow-up
- Make sure the client talks more than we do (especially in sections 2 and 5)
- End on time or early. Never run over without asking permission
- Be prepared with answers to common questions:
  - "What if the scope changes?"
  - "What if we need to pause?"
  - "How do we handle feedback and revisions?"
  - "What does handoff look like?"
