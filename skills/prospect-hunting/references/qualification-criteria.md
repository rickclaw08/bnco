# Qualification Criteria

How to evaluate whether a lead is worth pursuing.

---

## Must-Have Criteria (All Required)

### 1. Real Business Problem
- [ ] The prospect has a specific, identifiable problem
- [ ] The problem is something AI/automation can actually solve
- [ ] The problem is causing measurable pain (time, money, or missed opportunity)
- [ ] They can articulate what success looks like

**Red flags:**
- "I just want to explore AI" (no specific problem)
- "Build me something like ChatGPT" (unrealistic scope)
- "I have an idea for an app" with no business behind it

### 2. Budget Available
- [ ] They have budget allocated or can allocate budget
- [ ] Budget aligns with our tier pricing ($500 minimum)
- [ ] They understand that quality AI work costs money
- [ ] Payment method is available (credit card, wire, etc.)

**Red flags:**
- "What's the cheapest option?"
- "I have a $50 budget"
- "Can you do this for free/equity only?"
- No payment history on Upwork

### 3. Decision-Making Authority
- [ ] The contact can approve the project
- [ ] Or they have direct access to the decision maker
- [ ] Decision timeline is clear (not "someday")

**Red flags:**
- "I need to check with my boss's boss's boss"
- "Our committee meets quarterly"
- No clear decision-making process

### 4. Timeline Within 30 Days
- [ ] They want to start within the next 30 days
- [ ] There's urgency or a deadline driving the project
- [ ] They're actively looking for solutions (not just browsing)

**Red flags:**
- "We're planning for next year"
- "Just doing research right now"
- "No rush at all"

### 5. Capability Match
- [ ] The project is within our technical capabilities
- [ ] We can deliver what they need at quality
- [ ] No dependencies on technologies we don't have access to
- [ ] Scope is reasonable for our team size

**Red flags:**
- Requires technology we don't know
- Scope is a 6-month project for a 50-person team
- Needs 24/7 human support we can't provide
- Requires regulatory compliance we're not equipped for

## Nice-to-Have Criteria (Bonus Points)

### Repeat Business Potential
- Business has ongoing needs beyond this project
- Multiple departments that could use AI
- Growing company that will need more over time
- Retainer upsell is natural next step

### Portfolio Value
- Interesting or impressive project
- Good for case studies
- Demonstrates a new capability
- Client willing to provide testimonial

### Industry Fit
- Industry we want to specialize in
- Industry with high AI adoption potential
- Niche with less competition
- Industry where we have domain knowledge

### Referral Potential
- Well-connected founder/executive
- Active in business communities
- Part of a network or accelerator
- Likely to recommend us if satisfied

### Clear ROI Story
- Easy to measure the impact of our work
- Client can quantify the problem cost
- Before/after comparison is straightforward
- Makes a compelling case study

## Lead Scoring Matrix

### Score Calculation

| Criteria | Points |
|----------|--------|
| Each must-have met | 20 points each (100 max) |
| Each nice-to-have met | 5 points each (25 max) |
| Maximum score | 125 points |

### Score to Grade

| Score | Grade | Action |
|-------|-------|--------|
| 100-125 | A (Hot) | Drop everything, respond in 2 hours |
| 80-99 | B (Warm) | High priority, respond in 24 hours |
| 60-79 | C (Cool) | Standard priority, respond in 48 hours |
| 40-59 | D (Lukewarm) | Low priority, nurture |
| Below 40 | F (Cold) | Skip or add to long-term nurture list |

## Quick Qualification Questions

Ask these early to qualify fast:

1. **"What problem are you trying to solve?"**
   - Tests: Real problem + capability match

2. **"What's your timeline for getting this done?"**
   - Tests: Timeline urgency

3. **"Do you have a budget range in mind?"**
   - Tests: Budget availability

4. **"Who else is involved in this decision?"**
   - Tests: Decision authority

5. **"What have you already tried?"**
   - Tests: Seriousness + understanding of the problem

---

## Disqualification Triggers

Immediately disqualify if:
- They want work done for free or well below our minimums
- The project requires capabilities we fundamentally lack
- They are abusive, disrespectful, or dishonest
- The project is illegal or unethical
- They want an NDA before even discussing the project scope
- They refuse to describe the project but want a price
- Multiple red flags across must-have criteria

## Industry Prioritization

### High Priority Industries
- SaaS companies (understand tech, have budgets)
- Marketing agencies (need AI for content, analytics)
- E-commerce (automation for operations, customer service)
- Real estate (lead gen, document processing)
- Professional services (efficiency, automation)

### Medium Priority Industries
- Healthcare (opportunity but regulatory complexity)
- Finance (high value but compliance heavy)
- Education (growing need but lower budgets)
- Manufacturing (automation needs but less tech-savvy)

### Lower Priority Industries
- Government (slow procurement, bureaucracy)
- Non-profits (limited budgets)
- Extremely regulated industries (high compliance overhead)

## Post-Qualification Checklist

After qualifying a lead:
- [ ] Assign lead score (A/B/C/D)
- [ ] Determine tier fit (Starter/Professional/Enterprise/Custom)
- [ ] Add to pipeline tracker with all details
- [ ] Draft personalized outreach or response
- [ ] Set follow-up reminder
- [ ] Note qualification details in memory file
