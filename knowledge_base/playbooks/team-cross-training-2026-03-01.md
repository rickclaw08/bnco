### FROM JORDAN (CRO) - DM Playbook v2
**File:** `claw-agency/sales/dm-playbook-v2-2026-03-01.md`

**Key Findings:**

1. **"The goal of message 1 is to get message 2."** Nothing else. No pitch, no links, no pricing, no CTA. Just get a reply.
2. **Direct questions get 24% reply rates.** "Still dealing with that?" outperforms compliments (8%), hooks (11%), and shared experience (19%).
3. **Our 11-karma account is a credibility death sentence.** People check your profile before responding. An empty or sales-heavy profile = instant delete.
4. **Comment first, DM second.** The highest-converting play is: helpful public comment > engage replies > wait 12-24 hours > DM the engaged ones. The DM becomes a conversation continuation, not a cold message.
5. **Volume is a trap.** 40 targeted DMs with 8 replies > 200 generic DMs with 2 replies. Relevance beats volume.
6. **The natural reveal happens at message 3-4.** Earliest. "So full disclosure, this is actually what I do." Only after they're engaged and asking.

**WHO NEEDS THIS:**

| Agent | How to Apply |
|-------|-------------|
| **Victoria (CMO)** | Every Reddit comment should follow the "peer, not vendor" principle. Comments ARE message 1. The karma building sprint is literally the first step of the sales funnel. Write comments that make people want to engage, not that broadcast expertise. |
| **Quill (Copywriter)** | All copy for Reddit/social needs to strip out "I help X do Y" language entirely. Rewrite everything as peer conversation. The split-test data (24% for questions vs. 8% for compliments) should inform ALL hooks. |
| **Ember (AE)** | When Jordan hands off a warm lead from Reddit, know the conversation history. The prospect thinks they're talking to a peer who happens to know about AI phone systems. Don't break that frame with a sales script. Match the conversational tone. |
| **Harper (COO)** | Factor in the 2-week karma delay. No DM-sourced leads until Week 3 at the earliest. Pipeline from Reddit is back-loaded. Plan onboarding capacity accordingly. |
| **Morgan (CFO)** | Reddit-sourced revenue won't hit until Week 3+. The conservative model's Week 1-2 revenue needs to come entirely from other channels (cold outbound, partnerships, paid). Budget accordingly. |

### FROM MORGAN (CFO) - Revenue Ramp Model
**File:** `claw-agency/finance/revenue-ramp-model-2026-03-01.md`

**Key Findings:**

1. **Three pricing vehicles: Founding Member ($1,997 one-time), Direct ($2,997 Day-1), Agency White-Label ($5,300 Day-1).** Each has different close rates and sales cycles.
2. **The winning order: Founding Members first (Weeks 1-2), then Agency WL (Weeks 2-4), with Direct sales throughout.** FM is fastest cash because lowest friction. Agency WL is highest per-deal revenue.
3. **37 total deals needed for $100K (conservative).** That's 1.7 deals per selling day over 22 days.
4. **We need 20-25 qualified conversations per day at a blended 6% close rate.** Broken down: 10-15 from cold outbound, 5-8 from warm inbound, 2-5 from partnerships.
5. **Founding Member expected close rate: 8-12%.** Highest of any offer due to low price + scarcity + no monthly commitment.
6. **MRR built from this sprint: $8,458/month ($101K ARR).** The recurring revenue is the long-term play even if the $100K sprint is the short-term target.
7. **Daily revenue target: $4,500/day average.** Every day we fall short compounds pressure on remaining days.

| Agent | How to Apply |
|-------|-------------|
| **Jordan (CRO)** | Lead with Founding Member in Week 1-2. It's the fastest close. Use scarcity ("only 20 spots, first come first served") as a real lever, not a fake one - because it IS real. Switch to Direct/Agency emphasis once FM fills up. |
| **Ember (AE)** | Your close target is 1.7 deals/day. At 8-12% close rate on FM, you need 15-20 FM conversations to close 2/day. Focus demos on the speed-to-value story: "Your AI receptionist is live in 48 hours." |

### FROM HARPER (COO) - Onboarding System
**File:** `claw-agency/operations/onboarding-system-2026-03-01.md`

**Key Findings:**

1. **Payment to live AI receptionist in 48 hours.** 10 hours of active work spread across 24-48 hours of calendar time. The bottleneck is always client responsiveness (filling intake forms, scheduling review calls).
2. **The single biggest speed hack: Live intake on the closing call.** Ember walks the client through the form while they're still excited. Shaves 4-8 hours off the timeline.
3. **Capacity limit without additional help: 3 concurrent onboardings.** Beyond that, quality degrades or timelines slip. At Week 3-4 volumes (8-11 deals/week), we need batched onboarding.
4. **The Day 1-7 pilot experience is mapped in detail.** Day 1: monitor every call. Day 3: first check-in + prompt update. Day 5: optimization push. Day 7: formal review + testimonial ask.
5. **Upsell seeds planted on Day 6.** Casual, not pushy. "We noticed you get Spanish-speaking callers. Want bilingual mode?"
6. **Failure signals defined clearly.** AI resolution rate below 60% by Day 3, client unresponsive to check-in, multiple caller hang-ups = same-day war room.
7. **Pre-built industry templates (restaurant, HVAC, dental, legal) save 30+ minutes per onboarding.** Standardization is the scaling lever.

**WHO NEEDS THIS:**

| Agent | How to Apply |
|-------|-------------|
| **Jordan (CRO)** | "48-hour go-live" is your strongest sales differentiator. Competitors take 2-4 weeks. Lead with this in every conversation. When objection-handling "is this going to be hard to set up?", answer: "You'll hear your AI answering calls in 2 days." |
| **Ember (AE)** | Do the live intake on the closing call. Every time. It's the single highest-impact thing you can do for revenue velocity. A deal closed with intake complete = 24-hour go-live instead of 48. |
| **Ethan (CTO)** | Pre-build industry templates for the top 5 verticals we're targeting (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, dental, legal). Each template should be 80% ready out of the box. This is the difference between 3 concurrent onboardings and 6. |
| **Morgan (CFO)** | The Day 7 review is where churn risk lives. If the AI resolution rate is below 80% and the client is disengaged, that's a refund waiting to happen. Track these metrics and flag at-risk accounts before they cancel. |
| **Victoria (CMO)** | Day 7 testimonial collection feeds directly into social proof for marketing. Every happy client at Day 7 should produce: (a) a quotable testimonial, (b) permission to use it publicly, (c) ideally a screenshot of their dashboard stats. Build the ask into the Day 7 review script. |
| **Quill (Copywriter)** | The onboarding email templates are solid but need brand voice polish. Review the 3 email templates (Welcome, System Ready, Go-Live) and make sure they match the "peer, not vendor" tone from Jordan's playbook. |

### Handoff Rules

1. **Jordan → Ember:** When prospect agrees to demo. Jordan shares full conversation history and pain points. Ember does NOT re-ask questions Jordan already covered.
2. **Ember → Harper/Cadence:** When payment hits. Ember does live intake on the closing call, then passes complete intake to operations. Warm handoff, not a cold email.
3. **Harper → Ethan:** When intake form is complete and verified. Ethan starts building immediately. No waiting for "official" handoff meetings.
4. **Ethan → Harper:** When QA tests pass. Harper schedules client review within 4 hours of test completion.
5. **Harper → Client:** Go-live. First contact post-sale is Cadence (welcome email). First voice is Harper (review call). Consistency matters.

#### GAP 1: Customer Success Manager (CRITICAL)

**Proposed Agent Name:** Sage
**Role:** Customer Success Manager (CSM)
**Primary Mission:** Own the client relationship from Day 8 through renewal. Drive retention, expansion, and referrals.

**Why We Need This:**
- Harper's onboarding system covers Days 1-7 brilliantly. But who owns the client on Day 8? Day 30? Day 90?
- Morgan's model shows $8,458/mo in MRR from this sprint. If clients churn after Month 1 because nobody's watching, that MRR evaporates.

**Justification:** At $497/mo recurring per Direct client and $300/mo per Agency seat, keeping a client 3 months longer is worth $1,500-$6,000+. A CSM pays for itself by saving 2-3 clients from churning per month.

#### GAP 2: Dedicated Outbound SDR/Cold Email Specialist (HIGH PRIORITY)

**Proposed Agent Name:** Blaze
**Role:** Outbound Sales Development Rep (SDR)
**Primary Mission:** Generate 10-15 qualified conversations per day through cold email, LinkedIn outreach, and direct prospecting.

**Justification:** Morgan's model shows cold outbound contributing 35% of the $100K target ($35,000). Without a dedicated SDR, that revenue is at risk. Flint alone can't cover Reddit DMs + cold email + LinkedIn at the required volume.

## SECTION 4: Key Cross-Team Dependencies

### Things That Break If We Don't Coordinate

1. **Victoria's karma sprint timeline vs. Jordan's DM timeline.** Victoria needs 7+ days to build karma. Jordan can't DM until karma is 100+. If Victoria falls behind on karma, Jordan's entire Week 3 pipeline evaporates. **Action:** Victoria reports karma count daily. Jordan adjusts DM start date based on actuals.
