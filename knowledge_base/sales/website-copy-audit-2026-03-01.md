# Website Copy Audit: theclawops.com
**Date:** 2026-03-01 | **Auditor:** Victoria (CMO)
**Source URL:** https://rickclaw08.github.io/claw-systems/ (redirects to theclawops.com)

---

## Current Copy Assessment

The site is solid structurally. Clear pricing, specific verticals, a logical flow from problem to solution to CTA. The "terminal" branding (clawops diagnose) is unique and memorable. But three specific copy changes would meaningfully improve conversion.

---

## Change 1: The Hero Headline Needs a Dollar Amount, Not a Feature List

**Current:**
> "AI systems that run your operations while you grow revenue."
> "AI receptionists, revenue automation, and custom agents for service businesses. Never miss a lead again."

**Problem:** The headline speaks in features ("AI systems," "revenue automation") instead of the one thing a contractor landing on this page cares about: money they're losing right now. The subheadline "Never miss a lead again" is closer, but it's generic and could be on any SaaS landing page.

**Proposed:**
> **"Your business missed 23 calls last month. That's $8,000+ in lost jobs."**
> "ClawOps answers your phone 24/7, books appointments, and texts customers back in under 60 seconds. For HVAC, plumbing, electrical, dental, and more."

**Why:** The specific dollar figure ($8,000) already appears lower on the page in the stats section, but it's buried. Leading with a loss-aversion number in the hero creates immediate emotional urgency. Contractors think in dollars, not "AI systems." Move the pain to the top, the solution below it.

---

## Change 2: The "About" Section Should Be a Trust Signal, Not a Bio

**Current:**
> "Rick Claw - Founder and lead engineer at ClawOps. I help service businesses stop losing money to missed calls..."
> "Deep expertise in AI voice agents, CRM automation, and revenue operations."
> "You have revenue leaks. I fix them with AI. That is the whole pitch."

**Problem:** The about section reads like a LinkedIn bio. For a contractor who has been burned by SEO companies and "marketing gurus," this doesn't build trust. No social proof, no case numbers, no before/after. The terminal code block below it is cool branding, but it's doing the selling that a real testimonial should do.

**Proposed:**
Replace the bio paragraph with a results-led introduction:

> **"I'm Rick. I built a system that answers your phone, books the job, and sends confirmations while you're on a ladder."**
> "In the last 90 days, ClawOps systems have captured [X] leads, booked [X] appointments, and recovered an estimated $[X] in revenue that would have gone to voicemail. I work with HVAC, plumbing, electrical, and dental businesses."
> "You have revenue leaks. I fix them with AI. That is the whole pitch."

**Why:** Even placeholder numbers are better than no numbers. Once we have real client data, this section becomes the most powerful trust builder on the page. For now, the "I'm Rick. I built a system that answers your phone while you're on a ladder" line speaks the contractor's language. It says "I understand your actual day."

**Note:** If we don't have real numbers yet, use the industry stat framing: "The average HVAC company loses $8,400/mo to missed calls. I built the system that stops that."

---

## Change 3: The CTA Should Address the Objection, Not Just Repeat the Offer

**Current (bottom CTA):**
> "Stop Losing Money to Missed Calls"
> "Every day without an AI receptionist is revenue walking out the door."
> "How many calls did you miss this week?"

**Problem:** By the time a prospect reaches the bottom of the page, they already understand the product. The question isn't "should I fix my missed calls?" The question is "is this worth $2,500 to set up, and is this guy legit?" The current CTA doesn't address either concern. It just re-states the problem.

**Proposed:**
> **"Find out exactly how much you're losing. 15 minutes. No charge."**
> "Book a call. I'll pull your Google Business data, estimate your missed-call volume, and show you the math on what it's costing you. If it doesn't make sense, you'll still walk away with real numbers about your business."
> **[Book Your Free Audit]**

**Why:** This does three things: (1) Makes the ask feel low-risk ("15 minutes, no charge"), (2) Promises something of value even if they don't buy ("walk away with real numbers"), (3) Positions the call as diagnostic, not a sales pitch. Contractors are allergic to sales calls. They'll show up for a free business diagnosis.

---

## Bonus Observations

- **Pricing is visible and clear.** This is a strength. Don't hide it. Most competitors gate pricing behind a call. Keeping it transparent builds trust with the trades market.
- **The stats section (40% missed calls, $8K-$15K lost, 5-min response window)** is powerful but sits below the fold. Consider pulling one stat into the hero or making it a sticky banner.
- **"ClawOps Shield included free"** is mentioned on every pricing tier but never explained. If it's meaningful, explain it. If it's not, cut it. Unexplained badges reduce trust.
- **Industry pages (contractors, legal, healthcare) are linked but may not exist yet.** Verify these don't 404. A dead link kills credibility instantly.
- **No testimonials or case studies anywhere on the page.** This is the single biggest conversion gap. Even one real quote from a real business owner would outperform every other copy change combined. Prioritize getting a beta client testimonial above all else.

---

## Priority Order

1. **Get a testimonial** (biggest impact, not a copy change but critical)
2. **Change 1: Hero headline** (immediate first-impression improvement)
3. **Change 3: Bottom CTA** (converts the people who read the whole page)
4. **Change 2: About section** (builds trust for skeptical buyers)
